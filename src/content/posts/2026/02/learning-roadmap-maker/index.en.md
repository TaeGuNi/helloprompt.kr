### ü•á Pro Version

Use this for a detailed, fail-proof curriculum tailored to your exact schedule and goals.

> **Role (Role):** You are a world-class accelerated learning expert and elite personal tutor. You specialize in micro-learning and curriculum design that completely eliminates procrastination.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I have zero knowledge about `[Topic/Skill]`, but I need to learn the core fundamentals as quickly as possible.
> - Goal: My ultimate goal is to `[Specific Goal, e.g., build a simple Python script, understand basic financial statements]`.
> - Time Commitment: I can study for `[Number]` hours per day.
> - Timeline: I want a `[3-day OR 3-week]` intensive roadmap.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Identify the 20% of core concepts that will yield 80% of the results in this field.
> 2. Break down the learning journey into a daily schedule.
> 3. For each day, provide:
>    - **Focus Area:** What is the main theme of the day?
>    - **Core Concepts:** 2-3 specific things to understand.
>    - **Actionable Output:** A micro-project or specific exercise to prove I learned it (No passive reading).
>    - **Resources:** Keywords or specific types of materials I should search for.
> 4. Include a "Motivation & Burnout Prevention" tip for every 3rd day.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Output the daily schedule as a clean Markdown format.
> - Do not include filler words. Be concise, direct, and highly actionable.
> - If `[Topic/Skill]` is too broad to learn in the given timeline, explicitly tell me and narrow the scope down to something achievable.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Do not recommend non-existent books or broken links. Stick to general resource categories if specific ones are uncertain.

## üí° Writer's Insight

This prompt is a game-changer because it shifts the focus from _passive consumption_ (reading, watching) to _active production_ (doing). The biggest reason we quit learning a new skill is that the gap between starting and feeling a sense of accomplishment is too wide. By explicitly asking the AI for an "Actionable Output" every single day, you are guaranteed a quick win. ## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work for complex subjects like Quantum Physics or advanced programming?**
  - A: Yes, but the AI will (and should) narrow the scope. You won't master Quantum Physics in 3 weeks, but you can master a highly specific sub-topic or the foundational vocabulary. Make sure your `[Specific Goal]` is realistic.

- **Q: What if the AI generates a schedule that is too intense?**
  - A: Simply reply to the AI with: _"This is a bit too heavy for my schedule. Please reduce the daily workload by 30% and focus strictly on the absolute minimum viable knowledge."_

- **Q: Should I choose the 3-day or 3-week roadmap?**
  - A: Choose 3 days for crash courses on single tools (e.g., Notion, basic Git commands). Choose 3 weeks for broader concepts (e.g., Digital Marketing basics, conversational Spanish).

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **The 80/20 Rule Integration:** By forcing the AI to identify the 20% of concepts that yield 80% of the results, it automatically cuts out the academic fluff that usually causes burnout.
2. **Actionable Outputs:** Learning is verified by doing. The prompt mandates daily outputs, ensuring you aren't just passively nodding along to a tutorial.
3. **Built-in Reality Check:** The constraint telling the AI to "narrow the scope" if the topic is too broad prevents it from hallucinating an impossible curriculum.
