## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials. Copy the prompt below and simply fill in the `[Variables]` to use it immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want quick menu ideas without complex calculations.

> **Role:** You are a creative chef.
>
> **Task:** I have `[2 eggs, 1 slice of bread, cherry tomatoes]` in my fridge. Recommend 3 breakfast menus I can make in 15 minutes.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This is a powerful prompt for when you want accurate nutritional analysis, intuitive recipes, and calorie calculations all at once.

> **Role:** You are a clinical nutritionist with 10 years of experience and a head brunch chef responsible for a 5-star hotel.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: It's the morning rush where every second counts. I want a quick, easy, yet healthy breakfast.
> - Goal: Create a high-nutrient meal plan that maximizes the use of leftover ingredients in the fridge.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Suggest 3 breakfast menus that can be cooked immediately using the `[Fridge Ingredients]` I provide.
> 2. Each menu must feature an intuitive, super-simple recipe that can be completed within 15 minutes.
> 3. Include a nutritional analysis result with precisely calculated ratios of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats.
> 4. Assume that the basic seasonings and kitchen tools listed in `[Additional Options]` are already available.
>
> **Variables:**
>
> - `[Fridge Ingredients]`: 2 eggs, a handful of spinach, 1 slice of bread, 1 slice of cheese, 5 cherry tomatoes
> - `[Additional Options]`: Salt, pepper, olive oil, butter, air fryer, microwave
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Strictly exclude specialized ingredients that are hard to find or cumbersome cooking methods.
> - Set the difficulty level so anyone can attempt it immediately, considering realistic morning preparation time.
> - Follow the `[Output Format]` strictly and use Markdown for the response.
>
> **Output Format:**
>
> - **Menu Name:** (A creative name to stimulate the appetite)
> - **Cooking Time:** (Under 15 minutes)
> - **Simple Recipe:** (Explain intuitively in 3 steps or less)
> - **Nutritional Info:** (Estimated total calories and major nutrient ratios)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The true value of this prompt goes beyond listing generic recipes; it uses **'strictly controlled constraints'** to suppress AI "hallucinations" and maximize real-world utility. When I first asked an AI for a recipe, it nonchalantly suggested a complex oven dish that took 45 minutes, completely ignoring my actual fridge situation. This is where prompt engineering comes in—to pull the AI's output down into our "reality."

The first secret is the **'Fusion of Multi-dimensional Personas.'** If you look at the prompt, I didn't give it a single role. I layered two contrasting personas: "clinical nutritionist" and "5-star hotel head brunch chef." The nutritionist persona handles **health and data**, such as calorie counts and macro ratios. Meanwhile, the chef persona works to extract **layers of flavor and visual satisfaction** even from humble ingredients. The fusion of these two personas results in high-end output that finds the perfect balance.

The second key is **'Strong and Clear Time and Resource Constraints.'** By setting limits like `[Cook within 15 minutes]`, `[Exclude cumbersome methods]`, and `[3-step intuitive explanation]`, I've fundamentally blocked the AI from exercising unnecessary imagination. Without clear boundaries, AI generates recipes where the "tail wags the dog." Through these constraints, we've successfully tamed the AI into being a **realistic, survival-oriented personal chef.**

Furthermore, this prompt offers incredible **scalability through the use of variables (`[ ]`)** that you can adapt to your lifestyle. If you're on a diet or maintaining a specific plan, try adding a condition like `[Goal: Low-carb, high-fat meal under 300kcal]` to the Context section. The AI will adjust the ratios automatically. Also, if you only have a microwave, specify **"Kitchen tools are microwave only"** in the Additional Options. Custom recipes that require no stove will appear like magic.

Every morning, we stand at a crossroads of choices about how to spend our day. This system not only prevents unnecessary spending on food but also acts as a core weapon to drastically reduce the **Decision Fatigue** you waste every morning by outsourcing the trivial task of choosing a breakfast menu to AI.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I get recommendations for hearty Korean meals with rice and soup?**
  - A: Yes, absolutely! Just change the `[Fridge Ingredients]` to things like kimchi, tofu, or canned tuna, and add an instruction like "Focus the menu on warm, hearty Korean food" to the Context section.

- **Q: I want a new menu every day, but the AI keeps repeating similar recipes.**
  - A: Try providing your previous meal history in the Constraints section, like: "I had fried eggs yesterday, so strictly exclude dishes where eggs are the main ingredient." This easily prevents menu overlap.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Persona Fusion (Role):** By combining the "Clinical Nutritionist" (health and precision) with the "Hotel Head Chef" (taste and creativity), it induces high-end results that satisfy both nutrition and flavor.
2. **Controlled Constraints (Constraints):** By setting specific limits like 'under 15 minutes' and '3-step explanations,' it prevents AI hallucinations—like generating complex recipes that are impossible to attempt during a busy morning.
