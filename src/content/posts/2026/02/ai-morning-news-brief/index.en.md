## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

### 🥉 Basic Version

Useful for quickly grasping core headlines and industry flows during a busy commute without complex details.

> **Role:** You are a `[News Anchor]` who delivers news quickly and accurately.
>
> **Task:** Summarize the top 3 core news stories in the field of `[IT/Artificial Intelligence]` from this morning in 3 lines each.

### 🥇 Pro Version

A powerful prompt for professionals who need to see beyond the surface of events and derive business insights that can be immediately applied to their work.

> **Role:** You are an `[Economics Reporter]` with sharp insight and a `[Trend Analyst]` who helps companies establish strategies.
>
> **Context:**
> - Areas of Interest: `[Global Generative AI Trends]`, `[Investment Trends in Local and International AI Startups]`
> - My Job: `[Product Manager at a B2B SaaS Company]`
> - Purpose: Discover industry insights valuable enough to share with team members at this morning's team meeting.
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Select the Top 3 core news stories from overnight (last 24 hours) corresponding to the areas of interest above.
> 2. For each news item, clearly distinguish between 'Objective Fact' and 'Business Implication' in your summary.
> 3. Add 1-2 sentences on the potential impact or opportunity factors the selected news has for my job and our company's (`[SaaS Company]`) strategic direction.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Must include the original source (URL link) for each item.
> - Refine difficult technical jargon or translated phrasing from foreign media into simple business terms that a middle school student could understand.
> - Use Markdown lists and bold text (`**`) to structure it cleanly for high readability.
>
> **Warning:**
> - Strictly exclude unconfirmed rumors or fake news from unreliable media. If the latest information cannot be found, do not make it up; instead, answer honestly that "There is no significant major news in that field."

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work smoothly on the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: To handle real-time news, **Web Browsing functionality is essential.** For free version users, there are limitations on searching for the latest information, so there is a very high risk of hallucinations. Therefore, I strongly recommend using **Perplexity** or **ChatGPT Plus**, which are AI models specialized for searching, for the latest news curation tasks.

- **Q: Can I specify only articles from certain media outlets like Bloomberg or the NYT to be summarized?**
  - A: Yes, it is definitely possible. Try specifying a condition in the Constraints section of the prompt like `- Limit search sources strictly to authoritative economic media such as [Bloomberg, Reuters, Financial Times].` You can bring the reliability and depth of the extracted information to another level.

- **Q: It's a hassle to repeat the same prompt every morning. Any tips?**
  - A: Actively use the Shortcut (iOS) function on your smartphone or text expansion extensions provided by the Chrome Web Store (e.g., Text Blaze). For example, if you set it up so that the entire prompt is automatically completed just by entering a short keyword, you can start your personalized briefing in just one second.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Contextual Targeting:** Goes beyond giving simple interest keywords by clearly defining **'My Job (SaaS Product Manager).'** This elevates the quality of the results from a generic news summary for the public to a customized business report written only for you.
2. **Structured Thinking (Fact vs. Implication):** Instructs the output to forcibly separate objective facts from subjective interpretations. This powerful constraint prevents the AI's biased views from mixing with facts at the source and enables much more multi-dimensional and multifaceted information analysis.
