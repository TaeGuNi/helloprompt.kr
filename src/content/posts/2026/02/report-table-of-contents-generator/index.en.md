## 🚀 How Real Pros Write

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` in brackets to fit your situation, and deploy it to your work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to get a rough feel for it quickly.

> **Role:** You are a 10-year veteran ace team leader in a corporate strategy office.
> **Task:** I am writing a report on `[Topic]`. Design the most logical table of contents to persuade my boss. Use a Bottom-Line Up Front (BLUF) structure where the conclusion comes first.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you want to build a flawless defensive logic structure before writing a report. Copy the prompt below and modify only the content within brackets `[ ]` to fit your situation.

> **Role:**
> You are a 10-year veteran ace team leader in a corporate strategy office who has successfully led numerous projects.
> You are a master of report writing, praised by executives for your sharp insight and flawless logic flow.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - I am currently writing a planning report regarding `[Introduction of a new company welfare system]`.
> - The core conclusion this report ultimately wants to deliver is `[By redistributing the existing welfare budget and introducing a workation system combined with flexible work, employee satisfaction and work efficiency can be increased by over 20% without additional costs]`.
> - The final decision-maker (boss) who will read the report is `[highly sensitive to cost increases and conservative, valuing specific data-driven expected effects]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Organize a report table of contents with the most logical flow (BLUF structure with the conclusion first) that can instantly persuade the final decision-maker.
> 2. Structure it in detail with major categories (I, II, III...) and sub-categories (1, 2, 3...).
> 3. Provide a clear writing guideline of 1–2 lines for each sub-item in the TOC, specifying what core content and keywords I should fill in.
> 4. Finally, suggest three impactful and professional title candidates suitable for the cover of this report.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Maintain a dry, firm, and professional business tone and manner (bullet point format).
> - Minimize lengthy background explanations and follow an intuitive flow: "Problem Identification -> Solution (Conclusion) -> Detailed Execution Plan -> Expected Effects."
> - Output in Markdown format to maximize readability on mobile and web.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What should I do if it's hard to grasp the boss's exact personality?**
  - A: Rather than leaving the `[Decision-maker's personality]` field blank, it's better to set it to a universally applicable target, such as "An executive who prioritizes ROI (Return on Investment) and makes conservative decisions." Since this is a point most decision-makers empathize with, it helps greatly in building a solid defensive logic.

- **Q: I need to write a report in English. Is English planning possible with this prompt?**
  - A: Just add a single line at the bottom of the Constraints: `- The output format must be written in Professional Business English.` You will immediately receive sophisticated English table of contents and high-end English titles that meet global business standards.

- **Q: The generated TOC is too long. Can I change it for a 1-page summary report (1-Pager)?**
  - A: Yes, simply add an instruction to the Task section: "This is a 1-page summary report (1-Pager). Limit the major categories to a maximum of 3 and compress the content extremely." You will receive a perfectly condensed core framework ideal for a one-page report.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role (The Magic of Persona):** By assigning a detailed persona—not just a generic 'expert' but an 'ace planning team leader who has persuaded conservative executives'—it elevates the level of tone, manner, and logic the AI produces to the highest professional rank.
2. **Context (Target and Situation Setting):** By pre-identifying tendencies like 'cost-sensitive and data-centric decision-maker,' it induces the AI to preemptively place target-tailored defensive logic (Risk Management) in the TOC rather than a generic structure.
3. **Constraints (Control):** By enforcing a bulleted format and a BLUF structure, it strictly controls the quality of the output so that it doesn't look awkward even when copied and pasted directly into Word or PowerPoint.
