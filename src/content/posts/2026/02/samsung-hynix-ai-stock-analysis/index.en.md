## 🚀 The Solution: "Semiconductor Stock Analysis Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you just want a quick core forecast.

> **Role:** You are a `[Senior Semiconductor Analyst]`.
> **Task:** Based on the latest news, summarize the 6-month stock outlook for `[Samsung Electronics]` and `[SK Hynix]`, focusing only on the core points.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need deep business diagnostics and investment points.

> **Role:** You are a `[Lead Semiconductor Sector Analyst at a Top Tier Brokerage]` with 20 years of experience.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: `[Current global AI semiconductor demand status and position in the memory cycle]`
> - Goal: `[Write a detailed analysis report that is easy for retail investors to understand]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Comparatively analyze the current valuation (PER, PBR, etc.) of Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix.
> 2. Summarize the strengths and weaknesses of both companies' core future growth momentums (e.g., HBM, DDR5) in a table.
> 3. Provide 3 rational investment reasons why one should Buy/Hold/Sell `[Target Company, e.g., Samsung Electronics]` at this current point in time.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use a mix of Markdown tables and bullet points to make the output visually clean.
> - Explain technical jargon using everyday analogies so even beginners can easily understand.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never fabricate uncertain figures like the latest earnings releases; reply with "Requires checking latest search" instead. (Hallucination prevention)

The semiconductor industry is a cyclical business with very high technological barriers. It is nearly impossible for a normal retail investor to track all technology trends and financial statements. ## 🧬 Why it works?

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning the persona of a 'Lead Analyst,' we induce a professional tone filled with investment insights rather than simple knowledge listing.

3. **Constraints:** Forcing the simplification of technical terms guarantees a highly readable report format.
