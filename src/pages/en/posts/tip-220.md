_"Thinking they will raise it on their own... and it's been frozen for 3 years?"_

Companies don't give more money first to employees who stay still. A salary raise is not 'begging' but a legitimate **'Performance Report'**. Send an email persuading with facts, not emotions, to make an excuse to have coffee with the team leader.

### ğŸ¥‡ Pro Version

Copy the **PROMPT** below and use it.

> **Role:** You are a negotiation communication expert.
>
> **Context:** I intend to send an email requesting a salary raise interview to my team leader for the salary contract renewal season.
>
> **Key Achievements:**
>
> 1. `[Achievement 1: e.g. - Achieved 1 billion sales with new project launch]`
> 2. `[Achievement 2: e.g. - Cost reduction of 20% with work process automation]`
> 3. `[Additional Role: e.g. - In charge of mentoring new employees]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Write an email in a humble and professional tone while clearly showing my achievements.
> 2. Don't just say "Give me more money", approach with a nuance of "I want to review past achievements and discuss future goals".
> 3. Put a specific meeting request at the end of the email.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Exclude complaints or dissatisfaction (e.g., "Prices went up too..."). Speak only with achievements.
> - Pick a title that is intuitive but not burdensome.
