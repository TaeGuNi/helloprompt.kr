## üöÄ The Solution: "The Google OKR Coach"

### ü•â Basic Version (Quick Start)

Use this when you need a fast, solid baseline for your OKRs.

> **Role:** You are an expert OKR Coach from Google.
> **Request:** I need to establish an inspiring Objective and 3 measurable Key Results for my `[Insert Team/Project Name]`. The main goal is to `[Insert High-Level Goal]`.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need highly detailed, professional-grade OKRs tailored to your specific organizational context.

> **Role (Persona):** You are an elite OKR Professional Coach with years of experience at Google.
>
> **Context (Situation):**
>
> - Our team's primary function is `[e.g., B2B SaaS Marketing]`.
> - Our most critical mission this quarter is `[e.g., successfully launching our new enterprise tier]`.
>
> **Task (Instructions):**
>
> 1. Suggest 3 inspiring, qualitative, and challenging Objectives that will motivate the team.
> 2. For each Objective, provide 3 specific, measurable, and time-bound Key Results (KRs).
> 3. Clearly specify the exact metrics, numbers, or KPIs used to measure the success of each KR.
> 4. Wrap placeholders like `[Metric]` or `[Date]` in brackets if I need to fill them in later.
>
> **Constraints (Rules):**
>
> - Output the final OKRs in a clean Markdown format (use bullet points, not tables).
> - Objectives MUST be qualitative and ambitious (no numbers).
> - Key Results MUST be strictly quantitative (must contain numbers or percentages).
>
> **Warning (Anti-Hallucination):**
>
> - Do not invent industry benchmarks. If a standard metric is unknown, use `[Target %]` as a placeholder and advise me to set it based on historical data.

## üí° Writer's Insight

Drafting OKRs from scratch often leads to "analysis paralysis," where teams spend weeks debating wording instead of executing. The real magic of this prompt is how it strictly enforces the fundamental rule of OKRs: **Objectives must inspire, and Key Results must measure.** By explicitly separating the qualitative ambition from the quantitative metrics in the prompt's constraints, the AI prevents you from creating hybrid, confusing goals like "Improve UX by 20%." I highly recommend running this prompt collaboratively with your team during quarterly planning to brainstorm and refine your goals together.
