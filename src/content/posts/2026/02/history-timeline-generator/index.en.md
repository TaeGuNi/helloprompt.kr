## ‚ö°Ô∏è 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. Chronologically sort major events of a specific historical era or topic.

3. Cross-reference contemporary situations in other countries to provide a comprehensive, three-dimensional historical perspective.

## üöÄ The Solution: "Timeline Master"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, high-level chronological summary.

> **Role:** You are a `[Historian and Data Visualization Expert]`.
>
> **Request:** I need to understand `[Historical Event or Era]`. Organize its major events in chronological order and explain their causal relationships in a simple timeline.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this when you need an in-depth, structured, and comparative historical analysis.

> **Role:** You are a top-tier Historian and Data Visualization Expert.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I want to grasp the overarching flow of `[Topic/Era]` at a glance.
> - Goal: To create a comprehensive timeline that shows not just when things happened, but _why_ they happened and how they influenced the world.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Timeline Creation:** Select the 10 most critical events related to the topic and generate a Markdown table. The columns must be `[ Year | Event Name | Key Content | Note ]`.
> 2. **Causal Relationship:** Summarize how these events connect to one another using arrows (‚Üí) to indicate cause and effect. (e.g., Event A occurred ‚Üí Became the direct cause of Event B).
> 3. **Contemporary Context (Cross-check):** Briefly explain what was happening simultaneously in `[Target Region/Country, e.g., East Asia, Europe, or the Americas]` to provide a global perspective.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Enter the years as accurately as possible. If an exact year is debated, state the most widely accepted timeline.
> - Keep the "Key Content" summary concise, limiting it to a single, impactful sentence.
> - Output the timeline strictly in a Markdown table format.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not hallucinate historical facts. If you are unsure about a specific date or causal link, explicitly state that historical consensus is mixed or that you do not know.

## üí° Writer's Insight

This prompt is a game-changer for anyone trying to study for history exams, write a historical essay, or simply build a mental model of world events. By forcing the AI to output a Markdown table and specifically requesting "Contemporary Context," you elevate the result from a basic Wikipedia-style summary to a holistic, university-level study guide. I highly recommend tweaking the `[Target Region/Country]` variable; comparing European events to simultaneous occurrences in Asia often yields mind-blowing connections that standard textbooks miss.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I use this for fictional timelines or world-building?**
  - A: Absolutely! Just change the role to "Fantasy Worldbuilder" and input your own lore instead of real-world history. It's fantastic for keeping your D&D campaigns or novels logically consistent.

- **Q: What if the AI generates too many minor events?**
  - A: You can tighten the constraints. Add a rule like "Only include events that directly resulted in a change of government or a major war" to filter out the noise.

- **Q: Does this work well with standard free AI models?**
  - A: Yes, it works perfectly. However, models with larger context windows tend to draw much better global, cross-cultural connections in the "Contemporary Context" section.
