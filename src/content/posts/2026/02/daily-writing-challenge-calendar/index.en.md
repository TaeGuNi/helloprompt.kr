## üöÄ The Solution: "30-Day Topic Curator"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick burst of inspiration without complex parameters.

> **Role:** You are an expert writing coach and content curator.
> **Request:** I want to build a daily writing habit. Please generate a 30-day list of engaging writing topics based on my interest in `[Insert Interest]`.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this prompt for a highly structured, scalable, and personalized editorial calendar.

> **Role:** You are a veteran writing coach and senior content curator.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am trying to build a consistent daily writing habit but struggle with idea generation.
> - Goal: I need a compelling 30-day topic calendar that keeps me motivated without feeling overwhelmed.
>
> **Input Information:**
>
> - **My Interests:** `[e.g., Startup Culture, Psychology, Productivity]`
> - **Writing Purpose:** `[e.g., To share personal insights and build a personal brand]`
> - **Target Audience:** `[e.g., Junior developers and ambitious professionals]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Create a **30-day writing schedule** presented in a Markdown table with columns: (Day | Topic | Guiding Question).
> 2. Structure the pacing: Start with light, easy-to-answer topics for Days 1-5 to build momentum, gradually introducing deeper, more reflective topics toward the end.
> 3. Incorporate a "Breather/Retrospective" topic once a week (e.g., Days 7, 14, 21, 28) to prevent burnout.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - The 'Guiding Question' must be highly specific and evocative. (Bad: "Write about your career." / Good: "What is a piece of career advice you received that sounded great but turned out to be terrible?")
> - Do not generate the actual articles; only provide the calendar.

By incorporating "breather" days every week, the Pro Version mimics a realistic workout routine for your brain, significantly increasing the likelihood that you will actually complete the 30-day challenge. ## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well on free AI models like ChatGPT-3.5 or Gemini Flash?**
  - A: Absolutely. Because the prompt provides a strict structural framework, even lighter models excel at generating the calendar. However, more advanced models like GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet tend to provide more creative and nuanced "Guiding Questions."

- **Q: Can I change the duration from 30 days to 100 days?**
  - A: Yes! Just change the number in the prompt. For longer challenges, I highly recommend adding a constraint like "Group the topics into weekly themes" to keep the calendar organized.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Strategic Pacing:** By explicitly instructing the AI to start easy and scale up the difficulty, the prompt prevents early burnout.
2. **Specific Constraints:** Forcing the AI to write specific, evocative guiding questions prevents generic, uninspiring prompts that lead to writer's block.
3. **Rest Intervals:** Scheduling "retrospective" days ensures the writing habit remains sustainable over a long period.
