## 🚀 This is How Real Professionals Write

This prompt was completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variable]` parts in parentheses to match your situation, and deploy it immediately in your work.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you just need quick results.

> **Role:** You are a `[Professional Brand Naming Consultant]` with 20 years of experience. You are skilled at wordplay and trendy word combinations.
>
> **Request:** Please quickly suggest 5 attractive and trendy product names that capture `[Product Features]`.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need detailed quality and a draft for a proposal.

> **Role:** You are a `[Professional Brand Naming Consultant]` with 20 years of experience. You are skilled at wordplay and trendy word combinations.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am launching a `[New Product/Service]`, and I need a powerful name that will stick in the customer's mind instantly.
> - Goal: Derive attractive naming that can capture the target customer's attention and induce clicks.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Product Description: `[Enter Core Functions and Features of the Product]`
> 2. Target Customer: `[Enter Main Target Audience]`
> 3. Brand Tone & Manner: `[Enter Desired Atmosphere (e.g., Luxurious, Friendly, Hip)]`
> 4. Based on the above information, suggest 10 product name ideas.
> 5. For each name, specifically explain the **Reason for Proposal** and the **Associated Image**.
> 6. Organize the names into various categories such as pure native words, intuitive English, and portmanteaus.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Exclude names that are too long or difficult to pronounce.
> - Avoid anything similar to already well-known famous brand names.
> - Use Markdown Table for the output format. (Columns: Naming, Style, Reason for Proposal, Associated Image)
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up information that is not certain, and never use forced wordplay or obscure metaphors that are difficult for the target customer to understand.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

Having worked as a marketer and brand strategist on numerous projects, I've found that naming is a subjective area with few "correct" answers. Team members have different tastes, and dozens of names prepared over several days can easily turn into scrap paper with a single word from a decision-maker. That's why the core of naming lies in **'Persuasion.'** It shouldn't just be a pretty name that sounds good or a combination of easy-to-pronounce words; it must be backed by a solid and logical background of **"Why must our product have this specific name?"** Only then can you convince a demanding boss or client at once and lead to final approval.

This is precisely where the value of the prompt I designed comes into play. Its most powerful weapon is that the AI doesn't stop at listing naming candidates. It perfectly organizes the **'Reason for Proposal'** and the **'Associated Image'** in a table format along with the name. You can copy this table result and use it directly as a draft for a naming proposal or pitch. Controlling the variables to fill in the blanks essentially completes 80% of your proposal work.

I'll share a few of my own variable control "cheat codes" to maximize prompt utilization. First, in the `[Enter Core Functions and Features of the Product]` variable, don't just write one-dimensional specs like "lightweight laptop" or "gentle cosmetics." Instead, it is much more effective to specifically describe the **'Emotional and Physical Benefits the customer actually gains.'** For example, if you input "A 900g ultra-lightweight laptop for college students that can be carried in one hand without shoulder pain," the AI will derive a three-dimensional name that captures both the physical characteristic of 'lightness' and the emotional characteristic of 'freedom.'

Also, one of the most important points is the `[Enter Desired Atmosphere]` variable. Rather than just writing "luxuriously" or "cutely," **mentioning a popular reference brand that everyone knows** is excellent for setting the AI's direction. Try entering "A minimalist and sophisticated feel like Apple," or "A witty, popping, and friendly feel like LUSH." Since the AI has accurately learned the unique tone and brand persona of those brands, it will perfectly pick up on the context you imagined and provide overwhelming results beyond expectations.

If the generated results feel somewhat plain, a good way is to add extreme hurdles to the Constraints, such as **"Never use Sino-Korean words; use only pure Korean and English combinations"** or **"Limit the number of syllables to exactly three."** This is because AI tends to squeeze out more creative and roundabout ideas as constraints get stronger. I hope you hone this prompt into a one-of-a-kind powerful weapon through various variable combinations and additional constraint testing. I am certain that this prompt will be a ray of light for the many professionals struggling in the swamp of naming.

Finally, here's a small tip you must follow before taking the generated naming candidates to an internal meeting. Read the names recommended by the AI out loud several times. Even names that look perfect visually may not roll off the tongue easily, or specific pronunciations might get slurred, reducing clarity. Consumers will eventually call out the brands they read. If you meticulously verify ease of pronunciation and auditory appeal, the name you choose will have an even more powerful impact in the market. I support you in creating the best brand by adding your delicate human touch to the AI's analytical power.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does it also check if the name can be registered as a trademark?**
  - A: No, AI is not perfectly linked with real-time trademark databases (like KIPRIS). If you find a name you like, you must consult a patent attorney or directly search for trademark registrability through patent information search services to prevent fatal legal disputes.

- **Q: The results feel somewhat cliché and ordinary. How can I improve them?**
  - A: Instead of listing only functional features in the `[Enter Core Functions and Features of the Product]` field, try adding the 'emotional benefit the customer can gain.' For example, instead of just "lightweight laptop," inputting a specific value like "a 900g ultra-lightweight laptop that can be carried all day without shoulder pain" will lead to much more attractive and sharp names.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **The Power of Role Assignment:** By assigning a clear persona as a 'Naming Consultant with 20 years of experience' rather than a simple chatbot, it draws out sophisticated and professional results from a branding perspective rather than a generic list of words.
2. **Segmentation of Context and Task:** By specifying the target audience and tone, it sharply narrows the 'texture' of the words the AI will generate. By forcing various styles (pure native, English, portmanteau, etc.), it greatly expands the spectrum and diversity of ideas.
3. **The Magic of Format Specification:** It is designed to output in a Markdown Table format. This is a key element that maximizes the readability and practical utility of the document, allowing team members to intuitively compare and quickly evaluate candidates during busy meetings.
