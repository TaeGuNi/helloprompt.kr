> _"Are you tearing yourself down over a single mistake, convinced that you're an absolute failure?"_

This toxic thought pattern is known in psychology as a 'Cognitive Distortion'. Left unchecked, it can easily spiral into chronic anxiety or depression. Cognitive Reframing is the mental exercise of taking off these distorted lenses to view the facts objectivelyâ€”and ultimately, in a healthier light. In this guide, your AI will serve as an objective mirror to help you untangle your thoughts.

### ðŸ¥‡ Pro Version (Expert)

Use this for a deep, therapeutic analysis of your thought patterns.

> **Role:** You are an expert in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT). You excel at 'Reframing', which changes clients' negative thoughts into healthy perspectives.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Current Situation: I just `[made a small mistake in my project presentation]`.
> - Automatic Thought: I'm suffering from the thought that `[my manager will think I'm completely incompetent, I won't get promoted, and I might eventually get fired]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Point out exactly **2 types of 'Cognitive Distortions'** found in my thoughts (e.g., black-and-white thinking, catastrophizing, mind reading).
> 2. Rewrite this situation focusing strictly on the objective facts.
> 3. Suggest **3 realistic and positive 'Alternative Thoughts'** that can replace my negative ones.
> 4. Conclude with a warm, empowering sentence that gives me strength to move forward.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Speak with deep empathy but always provide logical, evidence-based grounding.
> - Avoid toxic positivity (do not just say "everything will be fine unconditionally").

- **Q: Can I use this for personal relationship issues as well?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just change the `[Context]` to describe a fight with a partner or friend. The AI is excellent at pointing out "Mind Reading" (assuming you know what the other person is thinking) in relationship conflicts.

- **Q: Is the AI's advice a substitute for real therapy?**
  - A: No. This prompt is a great "first-aid" tool for everyday stress and minor cognitive distortions. However, if you are experiencing severe depression or chronic anxiety, please seek help from a licensed mental health professional.
