## 📝 Resume vs. JD: AI-Powered 'Skill Gap' Diagnosis Prompt

- **🎯 Recommended For:** Job Seekers, Career Changers, Junior to Mid-level Professionals
- **⏱️ Time Required:** 5 minutes
- **🤖 Recommended Model:** All AI Models (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.5 Pro)

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 🚀 **Utility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

> _"Should I even bother applying for this role? What exactly am I missing?"_

Stop agonizing in isolation and let AI provide an objective diagnosis. By analyzing the delta between your current resume and the target Job Description (JD), you can clearly identify your weaknesses to preemptively address them in your cover letter, while pinpointing your strengths to double down on during the interview.

---

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. Execute a precise 1:1 comparative analysis between your resume and the target JD.
2. Instantly map out your perfectly aligned strengths and glaring skill gaps.
3. Unlock actionable strategies to bridge those gaps—whether by recontextualizing past experiences or framing a compelling short-term learning plan.

---

## 🚀 The Solution: "Skill Gap Analyzer"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, high-level overview of your fit for a role.

> **Role:** You are a pragmatic career coach and senior technical recruiter.
> 
> **Request:** Compare my resume against the provided Job Description (JD). Identify my missing competencies (skill gaps) and suggest a strategy to overcome them in my application.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need a highly detailed, actionable analysis before submitting your application.

> **Role:** You are a pragmatic career coach and senior technical recruiter.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Goal: I want to apply for the **Target JD** provided below. I need to know my realistic chances of passing the resume screening based on my **Current Resume**, and exactly what I am lacking.
>
> **Input Material:**
>
> 1. **Target JD:**
>    [Paste the entire job description here]
> 2. **Current Resume:**
>    [Paste your complete resume content here]
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Matching Analysis:** Create a clear breakdown using bullet points, categorizing the JD requirements into two sections: what I fully satisfy (Match) and what I lack (Gap).
> 2. **Weakness Mitigation Strategy:** Provide actionable advice on how to reinterpret my existing experiences to bridge the identified gaps. If a gap cannot be bridged by past experience, suggest how I should frame my short-term learning plan in my cover letter or interview.
> 3. **Pass Probability:** Give me a brutally honest evaluation out of 100 points for my current pass probability, along with a detailed rationale.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Provide "tough love"—prioritize objective, cool-headed analysis over comforting words.
> - Suggest highly specific, actionable steps rather than vague advice.
> - Format the output using clean, readable Markdown lists. Do not use tables.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

When job hunting, it’s remarkably common to self-reject simply because you don’t check 100% of the boxes on a JD. This prompt is a game-changer because it forces a critical mindset shift: from a defeated "I'm not qualified" to a strategic "How do I prove I can bridge this gap?"

Speaking from the perspective of a hiring manager, discovering a candidate who perfectly satisfies every single bullet point is a rarity. What actually grabs attention is a candidate who demonstrates self-awareness regarding a missing skill, and immediately counters it with a highly relevant **transferable skill** or a **proactive learning plan**. Leverage the insights generated by this prompt to craft a laser-focused cover letter. Rather than sweeping your weaknesses under the rug, confront them strategically and reframe them into a compelling narrative of adaptability and rapid growth.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I use this for completely different industries (e.g., when changing careers)?**
  - A: Absolutely. In fact, it's even more powerful for career pivoters. Just add a line to the `Context` section stating: _"I am transitioning from [Current Industry] to [Target Industry]. Please focus heavily on identifying transferable skills."_

- **Q: Should I include the company name and background in the JD input?**
  - A: Yes! Including the company overview helps the AI understand the corporate culture and the implicit soft skills the employer might value, leading to a much more nuanced analysis.

- **Q: The AI gave me a very low pass probability (under 40 points). Should I still apply?**
  - A: A low score usually means your resume doesn't utilize the same terminology as the JD. First, try rewriting your resume to organically incorporate the keywords the AI highlighted in the 'Gap' section, and run the prompt again. If the core experience is genuinely missing, it might be a signal to aim for a slightly more junior role or invest time in targeted upskilling before applying.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing (Senior Recruiter):** By setting the persona as a "pragmatic career coach and senior recruiter," the AI bypasses overly generic advice and adopts a strict evaluator's perspective.
2. **Structured Inputs:** Clearly separating the JD and Resume prevents the AI from conflating what you _have_ done with what you _need_ to do.
3. **Tough Love Constraint:** The instruction to provide "brutal honesty" forces the AI to highlight fatal flaws rather than offering false hope, giving you a genuine opportunity to prepare solid defenses.
