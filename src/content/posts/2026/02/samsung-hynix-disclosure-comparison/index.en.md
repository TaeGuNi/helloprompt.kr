## 🚀 The Solution: "Corporate Disclosure Data Comparison Analysis Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, high-level comparison of the two companies' revenue and profit scales.

> **Role:** You are an `[Expert in Financial and Corporate Disclosure Analysis]`.
> **Task:** Based on their most recent quarterly reports, create a comparative table showing the revenue and operating profit of the `[Samsung Electronics Semiconductor Division]` and `[SK Hynix]`.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Deploy this prompt when you need a deep, qualitative and quantitative comparison covering business structures, factory utilization rates, and market share beyond surface-level numbers.

> **Role:** You are a `[Lead Portfolio Manager at a Top-Tier Value Investing Firm]` with 15 years of industry experience.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: `[I need to rigorously compare fundamental health to determine which of these two semiconductor companies is the better long-term investment]`
> - Advice: Your analysis must be strictly based on the core data found in official 'Business Reports' from corporate disclosure systems (such as DART or EDGAR).
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Compare the following metrics based on the latest quarterly/annual data for the `[Samsung Electronics DS (Memory) Division]` and `[SK Hynix]` in a well-structured table.
>    - Metrics: Revenue, Operating Profit Margin, R&D Investment Ratio, Factory Average Utilization Rate, Core Product Market Share (DRAM, NAND, etc.)
> 2. Based on the data above, synthesize the **'Strongest Competitive Moat'** that each company is projecting to the market through their disclosures, summarized in exactly two sentences per company.
> 3. Identify and explicitly list the potential risks hidden within each company's financial statements (e.g., rising inventory assets, increasing debt ratios, etc.).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - The output format must utilize a clean mix of Markdown headings and tables.
> - Whenever professional financial or regulatory terms (like IFRS standards) are used, provide a beginner-friendly explanation in parentheses.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Base all data on the most recently disclosed confirmed figures (or consensus estimates). If specific numbers are unavailable or uncertain, you must explicitly state "Requires Disclosure Check." Do not hallucinate or arbitrarily invent numbers under any circumstances.

## 💡 Insight

The true power of this prompt lies in the **'Comparison'**. Analyzing a single company in isolation makes it incredibly difficult to gauge whether its metrics are actually competitive. However, by placing the vital statistics (such as R&D ratios or factory utilization rates) of two fierce industry rivals side-by-side in a table, you instantly reveal who is dominating the market. In practice, if you download the original PDF documents from the disclosure system and upload them directly to your AI (Claude is especially highly recommended for this) along with this prompt, the accuracy and depth of the resulting analysis will give you goosebumps.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Can I just upload the original PDF or Excel disclosure files directly to the AI?**
  - A: Absolutely! If you upload the raw business report files for both Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix to an AI that supports file attachments (like ChatGPT Plus or Claude 3.5 Sonnet) and feed it the Pro prompt above, you will receive a flawless, hallucination-free analysis.

- **Q: Is it strictly necessary to specify the exact business division, like `[Samsung Electronics DS Division]`?**
  - A: It is absolutely crucial. Because Samsung Electronics is a massive conglomerate that also sells smartphones and home appliances, you must explicitly restrict the scope to the 'DS Division (specifically Memory)' to ensure an accurate, apples-to-apples comparison with a pure-play semiconductor company like SK Hynix.

---

## 🧬 Why it works?

1. **Metrics Segmentation:** By clearly specifying the exact core metrics that real-world fundamental investors care about—such as 'Utilization Rate' and 'R&D Ratio'—we prevent the AI from generating generic, unhelpful responses.
2. **Apples-to-Apples Condition:** We architected a perfectly logical comparison baseline by isolating Samsung Electronics' 'DS Division' rather than evaluating the entire corporate entity.
3. **Risk Extraction:** By forbidding the AI from only highlighting positive metrics and forcing it to actively hunt for 'risks within the financial statements', we built a highly objective investment judgment tool.
