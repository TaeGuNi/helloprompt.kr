## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to use)

The most fatal mistake novice writers make when using AI is **blank-check delegation**, vaguely asking it to _"write the next chapter"_ from an empty canvas. This is the typical way of amateurs, and the result is almost certainly a boring, bland piece of writing. The true power of this prompt lies not in simply letting the AI write, but in a structural control that instructs it to **'first find the most obvious development yourself, and then force three new paths that perfectly avoid that banality.'** In a sense, you are exploiting the AI's data bias to squeeze its creativity to the limit.

The core know-how of **Variable Control** that I realized after running this prompt dozens of times during actual web novel serialization is to input the `[Current Situation]` and `[Key Keywords]` as sharply as possible. Instead of vaguely writing 'crisis situation,' apply extreme constraints like _"The protagonist's right arm is broken, his mana is depleted, and the only exit is a trap that will collapse in 10 seconds."_ The stronger the constraints, the more brilliant and unconventional the solutions the AI calculates. Freedom without constraints actually breeds obvious clichés, but an extreme control situation where all sides are blocked maximizes the AI's reasoning ability to create a **logical reversal without Deus Ex Machina** that even the author couldn't imagine.

Also, a point to look closely at while using this prompt is the gradual change in the three options the AI presents. The first and second proposals are likely to still have that safe, mediocre 'playing it safe' feel typical of AI. However, by the time it reaches the **third proposal**, after eliminating all previous possibilities, a shocking and radical idea often pops out that makes even the author slap their knee and gasp, _"Wow, twisting the setting like this?"_

Of course, you must not make the mistake of copying and pasting the text of the AI-generated plot sentence by sentence into your serialization. That is an act of self-sabotage for your life as a writer. What you should take is only the **'inversion of thought'** and the **'causality of events'** hidden within it. The subtle emotional lines between characters, the snappy dialogue, and the detailed descriptions using unique proper nouns must be thoroughly infused with your own style. Remember that this prompt is merely a massive stick of dynamite that clears a blocked tunnel; beautifying that tunnel and bringing light to it is the writer's job. When you perfectly digest this overwhelming reference with your own color, readers will give your work an enthusiastic cheer.

One more tip: sometimes none of the three generated plots are perfectly to your liking. In that case, try a **'Mash-up strategy'** that combines only the strengths of each proposal. Throw an additional prompt asking to combine the 'unexpected helper' setting from the 1st proposal with the 'cliffhanger (new hook)' ending from the 3rd proposal. If you command, _"Combine the timing of the helper's appearance from the 1st plot with the final explosive ending of the 3rd plot to form a new plot,"_ a masterpiece draft with impeccable, tight, and explosive tension is born. Use the AI not just as a machine that answers questions, but as a loyal sub-writer who constantly revises planning documents according to your instructions.
