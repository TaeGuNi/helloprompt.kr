## 🚀 How Real Experts Write Prompts

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` in the brackets to fit your situation, and put it into practice immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly scan the most basic differences in revenue and profit scales between the two companies.

> **Role:** You are a `[Financial and Disclosure Analysis Expert]`.
>
> **Task:** Based on the latest quarterly reports, summarize the revenue and operating profit of `[Samsung Electronics Semiconductor Division]` and `[SK Hynix]`.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This prompt goes beyond simple numbers to perform a more three-dimensional and in-depth qualitative and quantitative fundamental analysis, including fundamental profit structures, plant utilization rates, and market shares.

> **Role:** You are a Senior Portfolio Manager at a `[Value Investment Institutional Firm]` with 15 years of experience.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: `[A situation where you are precisely comparing the fundamentals of two semiconductor companies to decide which one to invest in for the long term.]`
> - Advice: You must analyze based on the core contents of the 'Business Report' from DART (Electronic Disclosure System).
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Clearly compare the following metrics for `[Samsung Electronics DS (Memory) Division]` and `[SK Hynix]` based on the most recent quarterly/annual data.
>    - Analysis Metrics: Revenue, operating margin, R&D investment ratio, average plant utilization rate, and market share of major products (DRAM, NAND, etc.)
> 2. Based on the data above, summarize in 2 sentences each what **'Strongest Economic Moat'** each company is currently promoting to the market through its disclosures.
> 3. Identify and sharply point out potential risks lurking behind the financial statements for each company (e.g., increasing inventory assets, rising debt ratios, etc.).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - For mobile readability, do not use tables; organize in a high-readability bulleted list.
> - If professional financial accounting (IFRS) terms appear, always add a simple explanation in parentheses that even a beginner investor can understand immediately.
> - **Bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Base the data on the most recently disclosed finalized figures (or consensus). If the basis for a figure is uncertain, do not make it up; specify "Need to check DART." (Hallucination Prevention)

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to use)

The true destructive power and value of this prompt lie in perfectly forming a **'context of precise comparison'** that goes beyond the analysis of a single stock. No matter how excellent a single company's financial statements may be, looking at them in isolation makes it difficult to gauge whether those figures are truly superior to industry peers or relatively poor. However, the moment you contrast the core metrics (e.g., **R&D investment as a percentage of revenue**, **average plant utilization rate**) of two rival companies that dominate the global memory semiconductor market on the same baseline, you gain the insight to see where the dominant market power and future growth engines are leaning.

As a practical investment tip, never rely solely on the AI's base knowledge. Download the entire original PDF document for the most recent quarter from DART and attach it with this prompt to **Claude 3.5 Sonnet**, which is unparalleled in document decoding and data analysis. You can immediately obtain analysis results from your own room that are as sophisticated and data-dense as expensive paid reports written by analysts at major Yeouido brokerage firms.

There is a core detail of prompt engineering you must not miss here. If you simply instruct it to compare all of `[Samsung Electronics]` and `[SK Hynix]` one-on-one, the analysis will fall into a swamp of serious statistical errors from the start. This is because Samsung Electronics is a massive group operating various global business divisions besides semiconductors, such as smartphones (MX), consumer electronics (CE), display (SDC), and Harman. Therefore, to fairly evaluate capabilities on the same baseline as SK Hynix, which focuses solely on memory semiconductors, you must sharply narrow the target range in the prompt variables to the **'DS Division (especially Memory)'** to establish an accurate **Apple-to-Apple** comparison without distortion. This tiny detail completely changes the logical reliability and quality of the entire output generated by the prompt.

Furthermore, note the ironclad safeguards for **Hallucination Prevention** layered within the constraints section at the bottom of the prompt. LLMs have a fatal instinct to fill in gaps they don't know with plausible-sounding lies and inferences. Especially in financial data analysis where a single decimal point is vital, if the AI makes up an operating margin even by 1%, it becomes a deadly poison that eats away at an investor's hard-earned capital. Therefore, by hardcoding the strong constraint **"If the basis for a figure is uncertain, do not make it up; specify 'Need to check DART,'"** we have raised the integrity of the data and the purity of the facts to the highest level.

In addition, I have secretly embedded the **'Law of Forced Risk Extraction'** into the core instructions. In the cold stock market, identifying looming bad news is the only survival secret to protecting your account, rather than finding rosy good news. I forced the AI to scan and point out time bombs hidden in 10-point font in corners of hundreds of pages of financial statements, such as a sharp slowdown in inventory turnover or a silent surge in short-term debt ratios. This prevents us from falling into confirmation bias—unconditionally optimistic about the stocks we bought—and allows us to weigh both sides of the fundamentals with icy cool logic.

In investment, information asymmetry leads directly to differences in returns.
