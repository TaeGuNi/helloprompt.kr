## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Pain Point (Agitation):** Accurately pinpoint the exact problem that keeps your customers up at night. ("Still doing manual labor in Excel?")
2. **Solution (Resolution):** Pitch a magical solution that ends their suffering. ("Done in a single click.")
3. **Social Proof (Validation):** Provide the psychological safety that they aren't the only ones taking the leap. ("Already trusted by 10,000 teams.")

## 🚀 The Solution: "Conversion Booster Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need powerful headline ideas quickly.

> **Role:** You are a `[10-year experienced professional copywriter]`.
> **Context:** I am writing the main headline for the landing page of an `[AI English conversation app]`. The target audience is `[professionals who freeze up when speaking English]`.
> **Task:** Emphasize the core value that "English flows naturally without needing a native speaker," and write 5 attention-grabbing main headlines.


### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when structuring the persuasive flow of an entire landing page using proven marketing frameworks.

> **Role:** You are a top-tier Silicon Valley Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) expert and copywriter.
>
> **Context (Product & Target):**
> 
> - Product: `[Posture-correcting ergonomic chair]`
> - Features: `[Ergonomic design, results with just 10 minutes a day, 100% money-back guarantee]`
> - Target: `[Office workers suffering from chronic back pain from sitting in front of a monitor all day]`
>
> **Task:**
> 
> Write section-by-section landing page copy based on the classic AIDA marketing model (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action).
> 
> 1. **Attention (Headline):** A powerful hook that grabs the attention of people with back pain within 3 seconds.
> 2. **Interest (Problem Agitation):** Build empathy by warning them of the terrible future (slipped discs, surgery costs) if they ignore their slouching posture.
> 3. **Desire (Solution & Benefits):** Vividly describe how comfortable they will feel and how much their productivity will soar when sitting in this chair.
> 4. **Action (CTA Button):** Button copy that makes clicking irresistible. (e.g., explicitly state the benefit like "Get 50% Off Today").
>
> **Constraints:**
> 
> - Every sentence must be strictly customer-centric (Use "You" instead of "We" or "Our").
> - Avoid technical jargon; use simple words that a middle schooler would intuitively understand.

---

## 💡 Writer's Insight

The eternal golden rule of copywriting is **"You"**. 
If there's a sentence on your landing page that starts with "Our product (We)...", delete it immediately. You must make the customer the hero, shifting the narrative to something like, "You deserve to feel more comfortable." By simply adding a single constraint line—**"Write in a customer-centric manner"**—when prompting AI, you will experience a complete transformation in the tone and texture of your copy.

Crucially, never confuse a Feature with a Benefit. Don't sell a "1000mAh battery" (Feature); sell a "2-day weekend camping trip without needing a charger" (Benefit).

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Should landing page copy be long or short?**
  - A: The purpose varies by section. The hero headline at the very top needs to be short and punchy (under 15 words), while the body copy needs to be long enough to erase customer doubts through comprehensive storytelling. When instructing AI, clearly separate the tone and manner: "Make the headline impactful, and the body copy friendly and detailed."

- **Q: Does changing a single CTA button really affect the conversion rate?**
  - A: Yes, dramatically. Instead of rigid words that introduce friction like "Buy Now" or "Sign Up", the key is to **explicitly state the immediate benefit the customer gets upon clicking**, such as "Get My 50% Discount" or "Unlock My Free Report".

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Forcing the AIDA Framework:** By instructing the AI to use the timeless AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action) model, you extract a landing page with a **"perfect logical structure of persuasion"** rather than just smooth-sounding fluff.
2. **Providing Specific Target and Context:** Instead of vaguely saying "sell this chair," the prompt dramatically increases the resolution of the target audience ("sell to office workers with chronic back pain"), resulting in sharp, hard-hitting copy that resonates deeply and directly.
