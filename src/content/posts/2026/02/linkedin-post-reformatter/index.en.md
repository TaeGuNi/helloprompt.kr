## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

Use the Basic version when you want to lightly refine the overall tone and manner of a short note or blog post you've already written. Use the Pro version when you want to plan a high-quality post that generates many 'likes' by imbuing a fragmentary experience with powerful storytelling.

Copy the prompts below and fill in the brackets `[Variables]` to fit your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a professional SNS content editor in the B2B IT industry.
>
> **Input:** `[Enter the rough note or short text you want to post here]`
>
> **Task:** Refine this rough note into a sophisticated style suitable for posting on LinkedIn. Maintain a tone that is professional yet humble, without being too stiff. Apply appropriate line breaks to maximize readability in mobile environments, and recommend 3 hashtags that go well with the text.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a 'top-tier career influencer' with 100,000 followers in the IT/career field on LinkedIn and a professional ghostwriter.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Persona: `[Enter your job and years of experience, e.g., 5th-year senior backend developer]`
> - Core Problem: `[Enter current struggle or situation, e.g., recently took on a manager role and realized managing people is harder than dealing with code]`
> - Goal: `[What you want to achieve through the post, e.g., sharing my failure and realization to gain empathy from new leaders]`
>
> **Keywords:**
>
> - `[Keyword 1: e.g., Transition from IC to manager]`
> - `[Keyword 2: e.g., 1on1 meetings are 100 times more terrifying than catching bugs]`
> - `[Keyword 3: e.g., Code doesn't lie, but people are entangled with complex emotions and contexts]`
> - `[Keyword 4: e.g., The lesson that the essence of leadership is sincere listening, not fluent speaking]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Powerful Hook:** Start the first 1-2 sentences with a curiosity-piquing statement or a striking paradox that instantly stops the reader's scroll.
> 2. **Storytelling Structure:** Develop the text naturally in the following order: My arrogance and struggle → Disastrous failure and trial and error → Decisive epiphany → Positive change in behavior.
> 3. **Actionable Advice:** Don't end with just a simple reflection; finish by presenting one practical leadership tip that the reader can apply immediately when they go to work tomorrow.
> 4. **Format and Styling:** Readability on mobile is life. Avoid long, suffocating paragraphs of more than 3 lines; keep paragraphs short. Use appropriate emojis to reduce visual fatigue, but do not overdo it.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Maintain the tone and manner of the writing as 'Professional yet Humble.' Absolutely avoid any nuance of bragging or showing off.
> - Strictly exclude cliché expressions that sound like a principal's sermon, such as "In this day and age..."
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not artificially exaggerate or make up non-existent facts or figures. 'Authenticity' is the most important factor in a LinkedIn post.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The core of a successful LinkedIn post lies in the sincere **'sharing of experience,'** not just 'bragging about my achievements.' The most common mistake people make when starting personal branding is trying to show only their perfect side. A one-sided notification tone like "I led this massive traffic project and successfully completed it without any issues" doesn't move readers at all.

Instead, when you take an altruistic and honest stance like "I made this ridiculous mistake in the field, went through days of intense struggle, and I hope you can avoid the same painful mistake through this post," you can draw much more explosive virality and deep respect from your colleagues. This is exactly why you must strongly demand a **'Humble tone'** and the **'exposure of vulnerability'**—admitting your shortcomings without filtering—when applying this prompt to your work.

Readers scrolling quickly are much more fascinated by the story of an ordinary colleague who was wounded in the field, reflected on themselves, and overcame it, rather than a flawless hero's narrative. Revealing vulnerability is not a weakness; it is the most powerful weapon for instantly narrowing the psychological distance with the reader. Because AI inherently tends to output 'correct answers' based on input data, there is a risk it might churn out overly textbook-like, stiff writing if you are not careful.

Therefore, users must include specific and vivid emotional descriptions that only they know in the `[Context]` or `[Keywords]` section of the prompt. For example, instead of the dry fact 'I fixed a bug,' input raw emotions like 'the moment I checked the error log, I felt a chill down my spine' or 'my face flushed at a colleague's sharp code review.' The AI will capture these detailed emotional lines and serve as a great amplifier to maximize the immersion of the text.

Also, the conclusion of the writing should always result in **'Actionable Advice'** for the reader. A post that simply ends with 'it was a good experience' does not stay in the reader's memory for long. Extract the single core lesson from your failures and successes and offer it like a gift in a clear sentence. Readers will 'like' and willingly share your post with their network only when they feel they have gained practical value from it.

AI is just a great assistant that builds a logical and perfect skeleton. Never forget that the task of breathing warm human life into the writing and giving it a soul called authenticity belongs solely to the author.

Furthermore, the LinkedIn algorithm highly evaluates the **'initial dwell time'** and **'initial reaction speed'** of a post. It takes only about 3 seconds for a reader to click your post and read the first three lines. If you don't convince them that *"this is a story for me"* within that short time, the reader will heartlessly leave. The reason we placed the 'Powerful Hook' as the very first instruction in the prompt we provided is to win this 3-second battle.

A striking first sentence like *"I thought I'd rather have the server blow up"* throws an intense question mark into the reader's mind, making it impossible not to read the next sentence. When the AI's strategic hook structure and your sincere episode are combined, a perfect LinkedIn viral post with overwhelming suction power is finally born. I hope you complete your own attractive narrative.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Can I apply this same way to an English LinkedIn profile?**
  - A: Absolutely. If you add a single instruction at the very end of the prompt: `Translate the output to professional, native-sounding English suitable for LinkedIn.`, you can get a perfect English post at a native level. For your information, when writing English text, Claude 3.5 Sonnet tends to capture subtle business nuances more naturally than ChatGPT.

- **Q: I'm worried about leaking company secrets if I put sensitive content into the prompt. What should I do?**
  - A: **Never** enter exact figures from internal company databases, real names of clients, or code names of private projects directly into the prompt. Instead, even if you provide abstract keywords that are thoroughly anonymized, such as `[Company A]` or `[Large-scale traffic]`, the AI can grasp the context and complete a sufficiently great and persuasive storytelling.

- **Q: What if the finished post obviously looks like it was written by AI?**
  - A: Even if the AI writes a great draft, you must directly add **one or two specific episodes that only you know** as the flesh. (e.g., *"That one remark Senior Kim casually made during yesterday's 1on1 meeting..."*) AI is just a great assistant for the skeleton; don't forget that the 'details' that breathe warm life into the final writing are solely your responsibility.

3. **Powerful Constraint of Humility:** By fundamentally blocking 'arrogance' or a 'preachy attitude'—the biggest pitfalls in professional SNS writing—in advance, we prepared a solid safety device so that readers do not feel unconscious rejection while reading the post.
