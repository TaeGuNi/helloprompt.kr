_If you've been struggling for 30 minutes writing, deleting, and rewriting a single email, copy this prompt right now._

Do you feel stuck when you have to politely decline an unreasonable request from a client, or when you suddenly need to send a business email in English? Writing an email that completely excludes personal emotions while maintaining perfect business etiquette is one of the most exhausting tasks for junior employees. Just give the AI the context, and it will generate a clean, ready-to-send draft in an instant.

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)
- 1️⃣ Assign the AI the persona of a 'B2B Communication Expert with 10 years of experience'.
- 2️⃣ Casually toss in the purpose, tone and manner, and core points as bullet points.
- 3️⃣ Get a perfectly nuanced business email in 1 minute without any emotional drain.

## 🚀 The Solution: "Business Email Automation Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version
Use this when you just need quick results. When you're busy, simply copying and pasting this is enough.

> **Role:** You are a `[Working Professional at a Global Company with 10 years of experience]`.
> **Task:** Write a `[polite but firm]` business email based on the following details.
> **Context:** `[Request to postpone a meeting due to an internal project delay]`

### 🥇 Pro Version
Use this when you need detailed, high-quality output for situations like handling client complaints, important English emails, or polite refusals.

> **Role:** You are a Senior Communication Manager with 10 years of experience, highly proficient in B2B communication.
>
> **Context:**
> - Recipient: `[Client Representative / Partner / Internal Executive]`
> - Purpose: `[Refusing an unreasonable request / Postponing a schedule / Sending a cold email in English, etc.]`
> - Core Points: 
>   1. `[Alternative solution we can provide]`
>   2. `[Reason why the current request is impossible]`
>   3. `[Proposed next steps]`
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Write a flawless business email draft that fits the context above.
> 2. Provide up to 2 subject line suggestions so the recipient can intuitively grasp the email's purpose.
> 3. Write the email in `[English / Korean]`.
> 4. Keep the tone and manner `[polite but firm / soft and friendly / extremely formal]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Strictly exclude any emotional vocabulary or nuance of making excuses.
> - Keep the sentences concise and remove unnecessary fluff.
> - Clearly mark the parts I need to fill in, such as `[Company Name]`, `[Representative Name]`, and `[Date]`, with brackets (`[ ]`).
>
> **Warning:**
> - Do not invent fake reasons that were not provided, and do not make promises that cannot be kept.

## 💡 Author's Insight
Stop draining your emotions while writing emails. Especially when replying to an infuriating client email, if we write it ourselves, our emotions inevitably bleed into the text. AI has no emotions. It churns out sentences that are thoroughly dry and professional. 

The most important thing when using this prompt in practice is to just roughly jot down the 'core points' in a bulleted list. Even if you just write, "Running late because of a problem. Ask to meet next Wednesday instead," the AI will brilliantly package it into something like, "Due to an unexpected issue during the project's progression, we inevitably need to adjust our schedule..." When you need an email in English, just turn on the `[English]` option, and you'll get a flawless, native-level business email with zero grammatical errors. Stop wasting time overthinking and just copy and use this.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
- **Q: Does this work well on the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it does. However, the Claude 3.5 Sonnet model is overwhelmingly superior when it comes to subtle business nuances and the expressiveness of polite refusals. For important emails, I highly recommend using Claude.
- **Q: Can I trust the quality of the English emails?**
  - A: It is far better than clumsy translators like Papago or Google Translate. Just add the condition, "Refine this using expressions native speakers use in a business environment," and it will be perfect.
- **Q: What if the generated email is too long?**
  - A: Simply add a condition in the prompt constraints saying, "Write it in a maximum of 3 paragraphs and within 10 sentences."

## 🧬 Anatomy of the Prompt (Why it works?)
- 1️⃣ **Assigning a Role:** By applying the persona of a 'Manager with 10 years of experience,' we completely block out awkward word choices typical of students or beginners.
- 2️⃣ **Specifying Tone & Manner:** The core of a business email is its nuance. A single directive like 'polite but firm' creates sentences that aren't rude, yet prevent you from looking like a pushover.
- 3️⃣ **Constraints:** The constraint to "exclude emotional vocabulary or excuses" forces a professional output that doesn't look pathetic.
