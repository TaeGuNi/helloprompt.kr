## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Clear Definition of Situation and Target:** Provide context to the AI by clearly defining the nature of the gathering (company dinner, blind date, etc.) and the age group of participants.
2. **Instruction for the Golden Balance Principle:** Avoid clichés by explicitly asking the AI to "extract Golden Balance questions where opinions conflict sharply at exactly 50:50."
3. **Ask for the Reason (Follow-up):** After posing a question, ask participants "why they chose that option" to lead to a much richer conversation.

## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in brackets to fit your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version (Spicy/Casual)

A casual prompt perfect for light drinks with close acquaintances, ready to use without complex settings.

> **Role:** You are a witty and charismatic recreation instructor.
>
> **Task:** 4 close friends are having drinks. Create 5 "spicy" and annoying Balance Game questions that aren't too serious but will cause a heated debate among them.

### 🥇 Pro Version (TPO-Customized Curation)

A sophisticated prompt that shines in situations where you must strictly follow TPO (Time, Place, Occasion), such as when bosses or awkward colleagues are present.

> **Role:** You are a veteran variety show PD of 10 years and a witty recreation specialist.
>
> **Context:**
> 
> - Nature of Gathering: `[Company welcome dinner for new employees]`
> - Participant Composition: `[Diverse mix from a boss in his 50s to new hires in their 20s]`
> - Current Atmosphere: `[Slightly awkward since it's the beginning, lacking common interests due to generational gaps]`
>
> **Task:**
> 
> 1. Create Balance Game questions that everyone can participate in with light laughter without feeling a generation gap.
> 2. Write them categorized into the following 3 types:
>    - **Daily Life of Office Workers:** (e.g., Unemployed with $2,000/month vs. Office worker with $5,000/month working 6 days a week with overtime)
>    - **Playful Imagination/Fantasy:** (e.g., Gaining mind-reading skills with 100% social awareness vs. Becoming invisible for one day a month)
>    - **Food/Taste:** (e.g., Eating only ramen for life vs. Eating only chicken for life)
> 3. For each category, provide 3 **"Golden Balance"** questions where opinions are likely to split **exactly 50:50**.
> 4. Add one "mood-lifting comment" for the host to say under each question.
>
> **Constraints:**
> 
> - **Never include** topics that belittle specific individuals, or are political, religious, or sexually explicit.
> - Focus on dilemmas that cause light laughter rather than serious conflicts of values.
> - Do not use tables for mobile readability; organize in a high-readability bulleted list format.
> - **Bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
> 
> - Do not make up information that isn't certain; answer "I don't know" if necessary. (Hallucination prevention)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight)

The true value of this prompt goes beyond simply extracting a few plausible question texts from an AI. The core is that it acts as a **catalyst for communication**, opening the floodgates of a stuck conversation and instantly narrowing the psychological distance between people.

Practically, the most important part of using this prompt is **Variable Control**. The quality of the AI's output varies wildly depending on how specifically and vividly you describe the three variables: `[Nature of Gathering]`, `[Participant Composition]`, and `[Current Atmosphere]`. Instead of just writing "It's a company dinner," you get much sharper and more relatable questions when you inject **specific context**, such as "It's Friday evening, we're at a BBQ place for round one, and everyone is tired from a recent project deadline but needs to boost the energy."

Additionally, specifying "Exclude political, religious, and sexually explicit topics" in the Constraints is a **Safety Net** gained through numerous trials and errors. AI can sometimes misunderstand context and throw out provocative questions, and in a professional setting, a single small slip of the tongue can invite irreversible disaster. This one line of constraint will save you from a crisis in your performance review.

When you run the prompt and the AI pours out brilliant questions, your role as the host (MC) shouldn't end with being a parrot that reads them. The real fun of a Balance Game is not the choice itself, but the process of hearing the reason: **"Why did you choose that?"** After posing a question to participants, you must individually ask, **"Which did you choose between A and B, and what is the reason?"**

Imagine throwing a question like "Guaranteed on-time departure for life (total exemption from company dinners) vs. Top-tier Omakase on the corporate card for every dinner (but strictly no second round)" at a real company dinner. Someone will shout for the early exit, saying "Work-life balance is life," while someone else will say, "I'll welcome the dinner if it's Omakase I can't afford with my own money." Sharing these answers naturally and enjoyably offers a glimpse into your colleagues' work styles, hidden lifestyles, and values.

If the results are too obvious and everyone leans to one side, provide immediate feedback to the AI for **Prompt Tuning**. Instruct it by saying, "Everyone chose A for the last question. Make the conditions harsher so A looks more balanced and attractive," and the AI will immediately adjust the difficulty to present a perfect 'Golden Balance' again.

If you wittily add the 'mood-lifting comments' pointed out by the AI and induce reactions by lightly mentioning the real names of participants according to the situation, you will surely establish yourself as the unrivaled mood maker of the gathering.

The skill of handling prompts is ultimately linked to the skill of handling people's hearts. AI is just a tool; it is entirely up to you, the prompt engineer and leader of the gathering, to use it to form strong bonds between people. Try freely changing the `[variables]` according to the nature of the meeting, and sometimes add your own brilliant categories. This prompt only exerts 100% of its power when your imagination and wit are added. Don't just stare at your smartphone in silence at the next meeting. ## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I generate provocative or 19+ rated questions?**
  - A: Major conversational AI models like ChatGPT and Claude have strict ethical guidelines (safety filters), so there's a high probability they will refuse to generate overly provocative questions. In such cases, we recommend writing indirect prompts like "An honest and humorous personality test for adult couples," or reducing the AI's defensiveness by strongly emphasizing the "Context" as a 'very intimate and private setting where everyone knows each other's boundaries.'

- **Q: Can I reflect the MBTI types of participants in the prompt?**
  - A: Absolutely! Try adding a specific setting like "Most participants are T (Thinking) types" in the `[Context]` section, or instruct the `[Task]` to "Create a Balance Game focused on emotional empathy vs. practical solutions that T (Thinking) and F (Feeling) types can heatedly debate." You can get much more diverse and interesting results that target specific personality traits than expected.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Detailed Contextualization:** By clearly defining the age group and nature of the gathering, the AI is induced to coordinate the appropriate tone and manner. This is because humor among peers and jokes in front of a boss are fundamentally different in nature.
2. **Strict Constraints:** A strong safety net of "Political, religious, and sexually explicit topics strictly prohibited" was established. This fundamentally blocks fatal slips of the tongue or disasters where the mood turns cold in a public setting.
3. **Golden Balance Instruction:** Instead of just requesting a dilemma, a specific benchmark of "splitting opinions 50:50" was provided. Thanks to this, the AI excludes cliché answers that lean to one side and completes higher-quality questions.
