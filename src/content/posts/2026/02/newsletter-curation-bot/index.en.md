## 🚀 How Real Experts Write This

### 🥉 Basic Version (Simple Summary)

A useful prompt when you want to quickly grasp just the core content of an article.

> **Role:** You are an `[IT Professional Summarizer]`.
> **Request:** Summarize the article below in 3 lines so a busy professional can read it in 10 seconds. Explain difficult technical terms simply so even a middle schooler can understand.
>
> **Full Article:**
> `[Copy and paste news body here]`

### 🥇 Pro Version (Newsletter Draft Generation)

Use this when you need high-quality curated content that is good enough to be sent to subscribers immediately. (Enter this into a Zapier or Make prompt node.)

> **Role:** You are 'Tech Fairy,' a senior newsletter editor who sharply analyzes the latest IT and marketing trends.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: Every Monday morning, I need to send a newsletter delivering industry trends to subscribers (junior marketers and planners).
> - Goal: To maximize newsletter open rates and trust by providing deep insights that can be applied immediately to real-world tasks, going beyond simple information delivery.
>
> **Task:**
>
> Based on the provided article, perfectly write one section of the newsletter.
>
> 1.  **Headline:** Create a witty and attractive title that can induce reader clicks. (Example: "Apple Did It Again? The Hidden Meaning of Vision Pro 2")
> 2.  **Core Summary (What):** Summarize the 3 most important points based on the facts of the article using bullet points.
> 3.  **Editor's Insight (Why & How):** Add your own sharp perspective (View) on why this news is important, what impact it will have on the industry, and what professionals should prepare for.
>
> **Article Content:**
>
> - Title: `[Input variable for article title from RSS]`
> - Content: `[Input variable for article body from RSS]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use Markdown for output format to make it highly readable.
> - Tone & Manner: Maintain a friendly and humorous tone that is professional yet not boring. Use 2-3 appropriate emojis.
> - Never make up information or content not present in the original article. (Strictly prohibit hallucinations)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The true value of newsletter curation lies not in 'simple delivery of information,' but in presenting the publisher's own unique and sharp **'Perspective (View).'** In the past, when information was scarce, there was value in just quickly translating and delivering overseas articles, but now we live in an era where anyone can get the information they want with a few searches. If you just instruct the AI to summarize an article, the result will inevitably be dry and obvious, no different from the main news on a portal site. Readers will find no reason to open your email among the many newsletters.

Therefore, the part you should put the most effort into when designing a prompt is **'Context Injection.'** You must strongly demand interpretations specialized for a specific job group in the prompt, such as **"What does this news mean for a marketer (or target reader)?"** or **"What should professionals prepare for right now?"** Instead of just 'Summarize this,' you should ask specific and pointed questions like 'Analyze the impact this article will have on next year's marketing budget allocation in 3 scenarios.' Only then can the AI provide breakthrough insights that go beyond the obvious.

Also, **Variable Control** is very important. In the Pro version prompt provided above, areas like `[Input variable for article title from RSS]` play a key role when linking with automation tools like Zapier or Make.com. The quality of the output varies wildly depending on how you set these variable areas. It’s best to pass the entire body of the article, but if you're worried about character limits, it's also a great strategy to extract and pass only the RSS Description or the first 3 paragraphs of the article. AI is smart enough to infer the flow and summarize it as long as the core context is given.

And never forget to **'Prevent Hallucinations.'** AI sometimes tends to plausibly make up information it doesn't know. If information that hasn't been properly fact-checked is sent out in a newsletter, it will deal a fatal blow to your credibility. Therefore, you must clearly state **"Never make up content not in the original article"** in the constraints. If the insight derived by the AI is not in the original text, a process to meticulously inspect whether it is an 'interpretation' or a 'creation (lie)' is essential. We must remember that while automation makes our hands and feet comfortable, the **responsibility as an editor-in-chief** who hits the final publish button rests entirely on us.

In conclusion, this prompt and automation system are not magic wands that create perfect finished products. They are powerful assistants that draw an excellent **'Draft.'** Once the AI sets a solid framework and throws out a draft of insights, you can simply add a spoonful of your own vivid professional experience or the real reactions of industry colleagues you discussed at a gathering last night. When that 10% 'Human Touch' is added, unique content is born that receives the reader's choice and survives in the flood of numerous newsletters. Invest the time secured through automation into refining your own unique View. That is the true path of a content creator.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Are there copyright issues if I summarize someone else's article and publish it in a newsletter?**
  - A: Unauthorized distribution by copying the original text as-is is a clear copyright infringement. You must go through the process of summarizing and recreating it in your own language using AI. Also, it is proper and safe curation etiquette to clearly state the source and attach the **'Original Link'** to send traffic back to the original author.

- **Q: Are there powerful automation tools I can use for free besides Zapier?**
  - A: I highly recommend the free plan of Make.com (formerly Integromat). It provides 1,000 free operations per month, which is enough to build a personal newsletter automation pipeline. Try setting up a perfect system without additional costs by connecting `RSS Module` ➡️ `OpenAI (ChatGPT) Module` ➡️ `Notion Module`.

- **Q: How do I prevent the AI from misinterpreting article content or making up lies?**
  - A: Hallucinations can occur at any time. To minimize this, I specified a strong control phrase in the constraints of the Pro version prompt: "Never make up content not in the article." However, a human-in-the-loop defense process, where a person directly and meticulously inspects the content before final sending, is necessary to prevent fatal errors.
