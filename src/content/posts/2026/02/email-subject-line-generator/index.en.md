## 🚀 This is How Real Pros Write

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in parentheses to match your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you just need a quick idea sketch.

> **Role:** You are an `[Email Marketing Expert]`.
>
> **Task:** Based on the following `[Email Content Summary]`, recommend 5 email subject lines and preview texts that customers can't help but click.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a sophisticated A/B testing plan and professional-level quality ready for immediate sending.

> **Role:** You are a 10-year veteran `[CRM Email Marketing Expert]`. You perfectly understand subject line patterns and copywriting psychology proven to deliver overwhelming open rates through A/B testing.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Email Type: `[Email Type (e.g., Season-off Promotion)]`
> - Target Customer: `[Target Customer Traits (e.g., Women in their 20s-30s who haven't purchased in the last 3 months)]`
> - Key Content & Benefits: `[Main Benefit (e.g., Up to 70% off storewide and free shipping)]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Based on the information above, write 5 attractive email subject lines and corresponding preview texts (Preheaders) that make target customers want to click immediately.
> 2. Propose a variety of options by applying at least one of the following four copywriting psychological triggers to each:
>    - Curiosity (Making them die to know more)
>    - Urgency and Scarcity (FOMO, making them feel like they'll lose out if they don't open now)
>    - Personalization (Making them feel like it's a custom email just for them)
>    - Specific numbers and clear benefit presentation
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Considering the mobile environment, subject lines must be within 30 characters (including spaces).
> - Replace words likely to trigger spam filters like 'Ad', 'Free', 'Special Price', '100%' with sophisticated and indirect expressions.
> - Provide the output in a Markdown Table format.
> - Organize the table columns in the order of `[Psychological Trigger | Email Subject Line | Preview Text]`.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never write subject lines that use excessive clickbait and create a disconnect with the body content. This severely damages brand trust.

## 💡 Author's Commentary (Insight & How to Use)

The most daunting and sweat-inducing moment in CRM marketing is standing in front of a blank subject line field one hour before sending. In the past, I too used to huddle in a meeting room with colleagues for over an hour just to decide on one subject line. However, since introducing this prompt, the time spent on copywriting has been reduced to one minute, and I’ve experienced the miraculous leap of open rates from an average of 15% to 38%. In this section, I will share the <b>cheat-key know-how to control variables perfectly and boost AI performance by 200%</b>.

The first key to focus on is the <b>strategic use of A/B testing</b>. When the AI suggests 5 great pieces of copy, it is very risky to choose just one based on your intuition. A pro tip for practitioners: select two strategies that are the most different from each other—for example, one focused purely on **'Curiosity'** (Option A) and one maximizing **'Urgency (FOMO)'** (Option B). Then, conduct a preliminary A/B test by sending Option A to 10% of your total audience and Option B to another 10%. Two hours later, send the final email to the remaining 80% using the 'Winner' subject line that recorded the higher open rate. This simple process dramatically increases the success probability of your campaign.

The second know-how is the <b>ultra-precision targeting of the `[Target Customer Traits]` variable</b>. A common mistake is entering vague information like 'Women in their 20s-30s' or 'Existing customers.' AI produces results exactly proportional to the resolution of the input information. Therefore, you must describe the target as specifically and sharply as possible. For example, fill in the variable based on behavioral data like 'Working women in their 20s who have put summer sandals in their cart but haven't checked out for 3 days' or 'Potential male VIP customers in their 30s who made their first purchase last month but haven't repurchased yet.' Based on this detailed context, the AI will generate chilling <b>hyper-personalized copy</b> that makes the customer think, "Wait, is this talking about me?" and click.

Third is maximizing the <b>synergy with the Preview Text (Preheader)</b>, the hidden golden territory of email. In a mobile environment, preview text takes up as much visual weight as the subject line. Many marketers leave the first line of the body to be exposed as-is or pointlessly repeat words from the subject line. However, this prompt plans the subject line and preview text as a 'One-Two Punch' combo. If the subject line hooks the customer with intense curiosity or FOMO, the preview text should subtly drop a specific benefit or solution to <b>complete the justification for the final action: the click</b>.

Lastly, I want to warn you about the <b>tightrope walk between credibility and clickbait</b>. If you overuse overly provocative or irrelevant clickbait titles just to increase open rates, your open rate might rise temporarily, but your spam report and unsubscribe rates will skyrocket, completely destroying your brand's trust capital. This is exactly why 'No excessive clickbait' is strictly stated in the prompt's Constraints. If you feel that a title suggested by the AI is too light or provocative compared to your brand's Tone & Manner, don't hesitate to give an <b>additional instruction (Re-prompting)</b> like _"Refine this into a more professional and trustworthy tone."_ Ultimately, the AI is just a great assistant; the role of the final Brand Guardian belongs to you, the marketer.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if the subject line created by AI gets caught in a spam filter and doesn't reach the customer?**
  - A: We have included clear instructions in the **Constraints** to cleverly bypass spam keywords, drastically reducing filtering risk. However, before the final send, we recommend <b>cross-verifying</b> using the spam check function of your current email platform, such as Stibee or Mailchimp.

- **Q: What if the output subject line is longer than the specified 30 characters?**
  - A: Sometimes, due to the nature of AI models, they may not perfectly recognize character limit constraints. In this case, don't panic. Simply provide <b>additional feedback (Re-prompting)</b> like _"The first subject line is too long. Make it much shorter and punchier, within 20 characters including spaces, to fit the mobile environment,"_ and it will be perfectly revised.
