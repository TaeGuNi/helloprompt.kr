## 📝 Algorithm-Free Product Planning Prompt

- **🎯 Recommended for:** PMs, Product Designers, Marketers, Developers
- **⏱️ Time Saved:** 2 hours → 3 minutes
- **🤖 Best Performance:** Claude 3.5 Sonnet, ChatGPT (GPT-4o)

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 🚀 **Versatility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

> _"Users are exhausted by dopamine-drenched feeds. It's time to boldly shift product direction toward 'quiet consumption' without algorithms."_

Endless feeds filled with non-stop, stimulating short-form content, unwanted ads, and random recommendations. Even with smartphones in our hands every day, we've fallen into a state of severe dopamine addiction where we don't even clearly perceive 'what' we are consuming. From the moment we launch an app, sophisticated recommendation algorithms ruthlessly hijack our attention and subtly drain our precious daily time and energy. The initial novelty has long since vanished, and users have begun to feel intense fatigue and even resentment. The deep dissatisfaction of "Where is the information I actually want to see?" is leading directly to the worst outcomes: a decline in trust and app uninstalls.

Despite this, most platform companies, product managers (PMs), and marketers are still morbidly obsessed with outdated success formulas and metrics. To boost seemingly glamorous **'Vanity Metrics'** like **'Time Spent'** and **'Monthly Active Users (MAU)'**, they cling to more provocative thumbnail tests and force 'dark patterns' like infinite scrolling into every corner of their services. These are merely short-term fixes, like pouring water into a leaky bucket. Traffic squeezed out by compromising a user's mental health can never lead to long-term business growth. Core target users, tired of the tyranny of meaningless algorithms, are silently leaving before even voicing their complaints, scattering to find more 'quiet' alternative platforms they can control themselves. Can you be sure your product is truly safe from this massive 'Silent Exodus'?

Now is the golden time to break the mold and completely flip your service's direction. You must sharply capture the hidden, powerful need for **'Quiet Consumption'**—a trend where users want to regain sovereignty in the digital environment, obtaining only the information they want, when they want it, without algorithmic interference. Look at the recent trends: the surprising revival of RSS reader apps, the explosive growth of decentralized social networks without algorithms like Mastodon and Bluesky, and the popularity of minimalist, text-based static websites that have stripped away cluttered widgets. We must essentially consider and design **'Algorithm-Free' architectures** that cut out meaningless recommendations and return full transparency and choice to the user.

This innovative prompt goes beyond simple feature suggestions; it is a powerful tool optimized for planning next-generation products that completely escape provocative, aggressive grammars and place 'user autonomy' as the highest value. In just 3 minutes, you can discard outdated blueprints that cause dopamine addiction and complete the framework for a solid, elegant service plan that creates true **'Value Gained'** for the user. A product that users willingly seek out when they truly need it, even without desperate attempts to trap them in the app. A powerful product where they feel deep satisfaction and utility from just one visit without meaningless scrolling and are willing to pay for premium features. Copy and paste it now to take the first step toward true product innovation, free from the slavery of algorithms.

## 🚀 This Is How True Professionals Write

This prompt was completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variable]` parts in brackets to match your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need to quickly analyze trends and set the skeleton of an initial plan.

> **Role:** You are a `[Senior PM]` expert in trend analysis.
>
> **Request:** Suggest 3 core ideas for completely removing algorithmic recommendation feeds or introducing 'Quiet Consumption' features to `[Service Name]`.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need detailed feature specifications, UX planning, and solid defensive logic to persuade management.

> **Role:** You are a `[Senior Product Manager (PM)]` and `[UX Researcher]` with a keen eye for user psychology.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: Users are feeling extreme fatigue from constant recommendation algorithms and ad saturation, and they want autonomous, transparent 'Quiet Consumption.'
> - Goal: I intend to completely reorganize the existing `[Current Product/Service Name]` to be Algorithm-Free or plan new core features reflecting this.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Plan 3 core features that can significantly lower algorithm dependency and maximize 'User Control (Autonomy)' in the current service.
> 2. Propose 2 new 'Value Gained Metrics' that can measure whether users obtained practical utility, instead of Time Spent.
> 3. Provide an in-depth analysis of the expected positive user reactions and business risks (e.g., short-term PV drop) when these features are introduced, and set a strategy to defend against them.
> 4. Write a single, powerful sentence for the marketing Unique Selling Point (USP) that `[Target Audience]` will find most attractive.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use Markdown to organize the output for high readability.
> - Avoid abstract conceptual explanations; write in the form of a practical planning document that can be reviewed with the development and marketing teams tomorrow.
> - For feature descriptions, clearly separate 'Why (why is it needed)' and 'How (how to implement it).'
> - For mobile readability, do NOT use tables; organize in a high-readability bulleted list.
> - **Bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up unverified information; if you don't know, say "I don't know." (Hallucination prevention)
> - I will reject one-dimensional ideas like "add a chronological sort tab." You must include specific, technically deep alternatives such as RSS integration, user-configurable middleware, or consent-based consumption filters.

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to use)

This prompt is absolutely not for shallow feature improvements like just adding a 'sort filter button' to a screen. This is a master key for highly strategic and heavy planning that completely reverses and innovates the **fundamental philosophy** and business model trajectory of the product you are responsible for. In the field, I have witnessed all too painfully and frequently the disaster of numerous IT companies becoming slaves to traditional vanity metrics like **'Monthly Active Users (MAU)'** or **'Average Session Duration,'** eventually leading to skyrocketing churn rates and damaged brand value.

A sophisticatedly designed recommendation algorithm can induce user scrolling in the short term to create a flash of traffic pumping. However, like chemical seasoning, it fails utterly in building long-term, solid 'trust' with the user. Instead, it only accumulates dissatisfaction from users who feel tricked by clickbait content. Therefore, when using this prompt, don't just write the app name in the `[Current Product/Service Name]` variable; include the core pain points you are currently struggling with (e.g., "A commerce app where PV per user is high, but the actual conversion rate or scrap rate is significantly low") to get a much sharper and more context-appropriate solution from the AI.

Furthermore, one of the most powerful weapons of this prompt is that it rejects existing metrics and creates entirely new ones. Take the innovative metrics derived here, such as **'Value Gained'** or **'Intent Completion Rate' (how quickly and usefully the user used the app before turning it off)**, to your data analysis team and management tomorrow and discuss them fiercely. Initially, you will face significant pushback, with people asking, "Does it make sense to reduce time spent?" However, if you use the 'Risk Defense Logic' and 'Insights on Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) Increase' generated by the prompt as your weapons, you can break through management's outdated thinking and improve the fundamental constitution of the service.

**Constraint Control Tip:**
In addition to the basic role of `[Senior Product Manager (PM)]`, try sharpening the persona to fit your industry. If you are in charge of a B2B SaaS product, modify the variable to `[B2B Workflow Optimization Expert]`; if you are in charge of subscription media, change it to `[Media Journalism and Ethics Expert]`. The AI will pour out 'Algorithm-Free' solutions from completely different angles through the lens of the persona you assigned.

Now, boldly step away from the obsession with numbers created by algorithms. Abandon the shallow tricks of trying to trap users in the app, and design a **'Solid and Transparent Product'** that users think of first and seek out themselves when they truly need to solve their problems. This prompt will be the perfect first step and the most reliable weapon in that great and bold journey.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Won't reducing time spent have a fatal impact on ad revenue?**
  - A: Short-term page views (PV) and impressions will certainly decrease. However, user loyalty and **LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)** tend to skyrocket instead. Since the target audience seeking 'Quiet Consumption' usually has high purchasing power, you should simultaneously consider strategies to shift the business model toward high-margin premium subscription models or high-intent targeted advertising rather than inducing meaningless banner ad clicks.

- **Q: Which service domains is this most effective for?**
  - A: It is essential for newsletter platforms, professional knowledge curation communities, and productivity tool apps. Interestingly, when this concept is applied in the form of a user-controllable 'Focus Mode' to **E-commerce apps**, where user fatigue has reached its peak due to excessive and indiscriminate product recommendations, it can surprisingly lead to very positive and explosive turnarounds in final purchase conversion rates.

## 🧬 Anatomy of the Prompt (Why it works?)

1. **Forcing New Metrics:** I explicitly specified discarding outdated metrics (Time Spent) and forcibly setting a philosophical alternative standard called **'Value Gained.'** Thanks to this single constraint, the AI is guided to move away from one-dimensional traffic-centered thinking and deep-dive into planning from an entirely new perspective focused on user value.
2. **Business Risk Defense Logic:** A great practical plan always addresses negative business impacts head-on. I included a powerful mechanism in the prompt to simultaneously ask about risks (user churn, short-term metric drops, etc.) and specific defense strategies. This elevates the quality of the result beyond simple brainstorming to a level where it can be used as a **'Planning Document for Persuading Management'** immediately.
3. **Requirement for Specific Technical Alternatives (Warning):** Typical AI answers (e.g., "Make a simple chronological sort filter") were completely blocked in advance through the Warning section. By throwing out specific and heavy technical keywords like RSS integration and middleware, I perfectly controlled the AI to derive much more in-depth and practice-friendly architecture-level solutions.
