## 📝 The First Step to Success: A Prompt for Crafting a Captivating '1-Minute Self-Introduction'

- **🎯 Recommended for:** Job seekers preparing for interviews, junior and mid-level career switchers
- **⏱️ Time required:** Reduced from 1 hour to 3 minutes
- **🤖 Top Performance:** Claude 3.5 Sonnet (excellent for subtle nuances), ChatGPT (GPT-4o)

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 🚀 **Utility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

> _"Hello, I am a candidate who is like a sponge... (Interviewer's internal thought: 'Ugh, the third sponge today...') Is your 1-minute self-introduction still just soaking up water?"_

The moment you step into the interview room, the first question that resonates through the silent air is always the same: **"Now, candidate, please introduce yourself for about a minute."** For many job seekers and career switchers, their minds go completely blank at this simple request. Even if you practiced dozens of times in front of the mirror last night, the words you prepared vanish the moment you face the sharp gaze of the interviewers.

Eventually, out of panic, the first words that come out are usually something like: **"Hello, I am [Name], a candidate who absorbs any task like a sponge!"** or **"I am like a vitamin that provides vitality to the organization!"** Have you ever looked closely at the interviewers' expressions? They meet dozens of 'sponges' and 'vitamins' every single day. In that **first 3 seconds** when you bring up a stale metaphor, the interviewer's brain has already 'auto-skipped' your answer. It's the moment your only chance to leave a charming first impression vanishes into thin air.

Many candidates mistake the 1-minute self-introduction as merely a 'formal ritual to show prepared diligence.' However, think about the real reason why hiring managers and executives participate in interviews. They are extremely tired, weighed down by their daily workload, and want to quickly determine if the candidate sitting in front of them is a **'real expert'** who can solve their team's immediate problems. The 1-minute self-introduction is the most decisive weapon to wake up the brains of these exhausted interviewers and trigger intense curiosity: *"Wait, this person is a bit different?"* Depending on how you start, the level of the interviewer's questions will change, and the final outcome of the interview will be decided.

**Why do we fail so often at the 1-minute self-introduction?** The problem isn't your writing skills; it's the **'absence of planning.'** It's because we fail to objectively identify what to discard and what to keep among the many experiences on our resume, and what core competencies the interviewer actually wants to hear. We repeat the painful mistake of talking at length about what we want to say, or conversely, reciting a soul-less summary of the resume. For those who aren't used to writing, even starting the first sentence while looking at the blinking cursor of a blank document can be a struggle.

But there is no need to despair any longer. This prompt boldly discards the old, stale metaphors that everyone uses and instantly generates an optimal script that strikes your unique concept into the interviewer's mind through a **'job-oriented, head-first competency summary.'** It doesn't just polish sentences. This prompt transforms the AI into a **top-tier advertising copywriter with 15 years of experience** and a **professional image consultant.** Using your plain resume text as ingredients, it perfectly designs an overwhelming <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">Opening Hook</span> that will make the interviewer look up from the papers and stare at you.

Invest just 3 minutes to run this prompt. It will complete a magical 1-minute self-introduction script that invites predictable questions from interviewers and allows you to lead the conversation with the job competencies you are most confident in. Your experiences and the value of your efforts are too precious to be buried under the same words as everyone else. Now, it's time to prove your true skills.

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Eliminate Clichés:** Thoroughly exclude predictable metaphors like 'Vitamin' or 'Sponge' and complete a powerful hook based on job experience.
2. **Structured Speech:** Densely compress your two core competencies and the actual experiences that prove them into an optimal duration of 45–50 seconds.
3. **Utilize Persona:** By assigning the professional roles of 'Advertising Copywriter' and 'Image Consultant' to the AI model, you derive a perfect tone and manner that is confident yet humble.

## 🚀 How the Real Experts Write

This prompt was completed after dozens of trials and errors. If your interview is tomorrow or if you want to plan your own unique concept, copy the prompt below, customize only the `[Variables]`, and use it immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need an urgent 1-minute self-introduction draft for an interview tomorrow.

> **Role:** You are an HR manager at a large corporation and a veteran interviewer.
> 
> **Request:** Based on my resume, write a 45-second self-introduction script that highlights my job competencies the most. Never use cliché and abstract metaphors like 'Vitamin' or 'Passion.'

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you want to plan a unique interview concept and desire the highest quality with living detail. After copying the prompt below, enter the content in the `[ ]` customized to your actual situation.

> **Role (Role):** You are a top-tier advertising copywriter with 15 years of experience and a professional image consultant.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Goal: Write a 1-minute self-introduction script for a `[Job Position, e.g., Performance Marketer]` interview.
> - Target: Practical-level and executive interviewers who are extremely exhausted from hearing the same predictable answers from candidates all day.
>
> **My Core Weapons (Keywords):**
>
> 1. Competency 1: `[e.g., Data-driven analytical skills with experience achieving 300% ROAS]`
> 2. Competency 2: `[e.g., Flexible cross-functional communication skills with development and design teams]`
>
> **Request (Task):**
>
> 1. Create a powerful and professional **Opening (Hook)** sentence that will make the interviewers look up from the documents and stare at me immediately.
> 2. Construct a logical body by naturally weaving the two 'Core Weapons' I provided above with actual experiences.
> 3. Conclude the speech cleanly with a clear post-hiring aspiration of how I will practically contribute to the company.
> 4. Write it in the optimal length (approx. 350–400 characters) that ends exactly between **45 and 50 seconds** when spoken by a person.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Thoroughly exclude cliché and pleading abstract expressions such as "I am like a vitamin" or "If you just hire me."
> - Skip predictable greetings like "Hello" and start powerfully with the opening hook right away. (I will add the actual interview greeting myself.)
> - Maintain a strictly "Polite but Confident" tone so as not to appear overly arrogant while remaining assured.

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

The core of this prompt's power lies not in treating the AI as a simple writing machine, but in perfectly positioning it as a professional persona: a **'top-tier advertising copywriter.'** A 1-minute self-introduction is ultimately a 1-minute voice advertisement. An advertising copywriter is a magician of language who extracts the most attractive keywords within a limited time to move the target's heart. The moment you apply this persona to the AI, the density and suction of the generated text far exceed the level of a typical chatbot.

When I personally demonstrated this prompt during an interview consulting session for a junior marketer preparing for a career switch, the results were amazing. A mediocre script that initially stayed at the level of "I will learn everything diligently" and "I love data" was transformed 180 degrees into a sophisticated, agency-level punchline: **"I am candidate [Name], who finds the exact direction of marketing with the compass of data."** This opening, which struck the interviewers' ears, immediately led to advantageous follow-up questions like "Could you tell us a specific case of how you used data like a compass?" and eventually resulted in a final job offer.

The key to utilizing this prompt 200% is the **precise control of variables (`[ ]`)**. Do not simply write "Excellent communication skills" in the `[My Core Weapons]` section. Instead, include specific episodes and numbers, such as "Data-driven communication skills that reconciled a conflict between developers and designers in just 3 days." The more specific and vivid the materials given to the AI, the exponentially higher the persuasiveness of the resulting script.

**💡 Tuning Tips & Variations:**

1. **Fine-tuning Tone and Manner:** If the company you are applying to is a conservative large corporation, add a condition to the prompt constraints: *"Avoid overly flashy or aggressive expressions, and use refined words that convey trust and weight."* Conversely, if it is a young and trendy startup, instruct it to *"Write a creative and provocative opening that breaks existing molds"* to customize the script to the company's vibe.

2. **Personalizing Breath and Length:** You must never finish by just reading the script generated by the AI with your eyes. Once the draft is out, turn on the recorder on your smartphone and read it out loud yourself. Everyone has a different speaking speed and a different length of breath they feel comfortable with. If there is a sentence that doesn't feel right or makes you out of breath, give an additional request (Re-prompting) to the AI: *"I tend to speak a bit slowly, and if the sentences are long, I run out of breath. Keep the meaning of what you just wrote, but rewrite it by breaking all sentences into short ones."* This will allow for a much more fluid, authentic, and relaxed speech.

3. **Planting Follow-up Question Triggers:** The best 1-minute self-introduction is one that triggers the interviewer's curiosity and makes them ask the questions you want. Try giving a secret instruction in the 'Request' section of the prompt: *"Construct the conclusion with a slightly lingering finish so that the interviewer will definitely ask a follow-up question about my core weapon #1."* You can become the master who controls the flow of the interview.

A 1-minute self-introduction is not a place to show everything about who you are. it is a time to show a trailer of how **'interesting'** you are and how **'attractive a colleague you are to work with.'** This prompt is the best tool to refine the raw diamond of your brilliant competencies most gorgeously. Now that you have completed the perfect plan with the help of AI, all that's left is to breathe life into the completed script with your own confident voice in front of the mirror.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: My career or experience is a bit thin; what if the AI exaggerates the script?**
  - A: Before entering the prompt, it is important to build a framework based on specific figures or clear facts, no matter how small the experience. To prevent the AI from inventing non-existent facts through hallucinations, record strictly only facts in the `[Core Weapons]` input field. Additionally, you can use it much more safely by adding a strong constraint: "Never exaggerate my experience or invent non-existent facts."

- **Q: Which AI model is best optimized for writing 1-minute self-introduction scripts?**
  - A: When it comes to capturing the subtle nuances of spoken language and striking the difficult tone and manner of being 'humble yet confident,' **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** currently boasts overwhelming performance that is unrivaled. If you use ChatGPT, you need to refine the prompt instructions to be a bit more firm and sharp.

- **Q: If the output script is too long to fit into 1 minute, how should I shorten it?**
  - A: It's simple. Give a light second instruction to the AI: "I tend to speak a bit slowly, so please summarize what you just wrote within 300 characters, leaving only the core parts." It will be adjusted to the perfect length.

## 🧬 Prompt Dissection (Why it works?)

1. **Role (Role Assignment):** By giving the AI the professional identity of an 'Advertising Copywriter' rather than a simple HR person, it induces a powerful opening at the level of a 'copy' that sticks deep into the interviewer's mind instead of flat, plain text.
2. **Context (Target Analysis):** By setting a very specific persona—an 'interviewer tired of interviews all day'—as the target audience, it forces the AI to avoid lengthy background explanations and strategically choose an impactful, head-first structure.
3. **Constraints (Constraints):** By explicitly blocking words like 'Vitamin' and 'Sponge,' which are representative of clichés and taboos in the job market, the quality of the derived answers is forcibly pulled up from the bottom.
