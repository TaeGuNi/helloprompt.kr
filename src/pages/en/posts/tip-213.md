_"If the interviewer's face darkens while you are explaining hard?"_

The reason for failing most interviews is **'rambling'**. You explain the situation for 3 minutes and end up unable to say what you actually did. Build the skeleton of your answer with the all-purpose cheat key **STAR Method**.

3. Complete it as a script of about 1 minute that you can say right away in the interview room.

### ğŸ¥‡ Pro Version

Use this when you need detailed quality.

Copy the **PROMPT** below and use it.

> **Role:** You are a speech coach and interview consultant.
>
> **Context:** I am organizing an episode to answer when asked `[Question Content]` in an interview. I want to structure it so I don't speak incoherently.
>
> **My Experience Episode:**
> [Write in full sentences as you think of it here]
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1.  Reconstruct the above content according to the **STAR Method**.
>     - **Situation:** The background at the time in 1 sentence.
>     - **Task:** The problem I had to solve in 1 sentence.
>     - **Action:** Specifically what I did and how in 2~3 sentences. (Most Important!)
>     - **Result:** Quantitative achievement or what was learned from it in 1 sentence.
> 2.  Make this into a **colloquial script** that can be spoken naturally.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Adjust the total length to be **40 seconds ~ 1 minute** when spoken.
> - Put more than 50% weight on 'Action'. (Keep situation explanation short)
