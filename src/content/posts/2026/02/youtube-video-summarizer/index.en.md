> _"Are you still listening to a 20-minute intro for a 1-hour video just to get the main point?"_

The biggest drawback of video media is its **'low information density.'** We often endure more than 20 minutes of a YouTuber's unnecessary chatter and repetitive introductions for content that could be fully understood in just 3 minutes if scanned as text. You've likely experienced **'thumbnail bait'**—clicking a video lured by an attractive title and provocative thumbnail, only to find the core information you actually wanted appearing briefly in the last minute of the video or not existing at all. In this flood of information, these inefficient situations we face daily are more than just annoying; they are a major cause of draining our most precious resources.

For busy modern people, time is the most scarce and expensive asset, irreplaceable by anything else. Whether on a crowded subway during a commute or searching for a quick reference during intense work, falling into the trap of a cleverly designed algorithm and wasting an hour on YouTube brings a deep sense of self-reproach. You might try to increase the playback speed to 1.5x or 2x out of frustration, but the **physical time constraint** of having to stare at the screen until the video ends never truly disappears. Your valuable focus and energy [are consumed by] the watch-time extension strategies of YouTubers, meticulously designed to increase views and retention...
