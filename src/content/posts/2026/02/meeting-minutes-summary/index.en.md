## 🚀 The Solution: "The Ultimate Meeting Minutes Generator"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a rapid, no-frills summary for a brief sync.

> **Role:** You are a highly efficient `[Executive Assistant]`.
>
> **Task:** Summarize the following `[Meeting Transcript]`. Extract the core topics, key decisions, and immediate action items.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Deploy this for complex, hour-long deep dives involving multiple stakeholders and tangled agendas. It guarantees zero lost context and crystal-clear accountability.

> **Role:** You are a Senior Project Manager renowned for impeccable organization and crystal-clear communication.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: We have just concluded a `[Meeting Type, e.g., Weekly Sync / Client Pitch]`.
> - Goal: Transform the raw transcript into structured, actionable meeting minutes to ensure total alignment on next steps across all participants.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Carefully review the provided `[Meeting Transcript]`.
> 2. Categorize the content into the following specific sections: Meeting Objective, Key Decisions Made, Unresolved Issues/Risks, and Action Items.
> 3. Within the 'Action Items' section, explicitly map out WHO is responsible for WHAT by WHEN.
> 4. `[Meeting Transcript]`: (Paste your transcript here)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Deliver the final output in a clean, highly readable Markdown format.
> - Strictly utilize bullet points to maximize scannability.
> - Structure the 'Action Items' rigorously so that individual responsibilities and deadlines are instantly obvious.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not hallucinate or fabricate any dates, names, or decisions that are not explicitly stated in the transcript. If a deadline or assignee is ambiguous, mark it precisely as "TBD" (To Be Determined).

## 💡 Writer's Insight

This prompt is an absolute lifesaver for chaotic cross-functional meetings. The single biggest mistake professionals make when leveraging AI for notes is simply typing, "Summarize this." That lazy approach inevitably yields a generic, dense paragraph that no one actually wants to read.

By explicitly demanding an "Action Items" breakdown—specifically formatted as "Who, What, When"—and enforcing strict Markdown styling, you essentially force the AI to operate as a ruthless project manager. Pair this prompt with a native transcription tool like Otter.ai, Zoom, or MS Teams, and it will single-handedly reclaim hours of your workweek.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if the raw transcript is riddled with typos or filler words (like "ums" and "ahs")?**
  - A: Advanced models like GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet are incredibly adept at ignoring filler words and transcription errors. They focus strictly on semantic meaning, so you never need to manually clean up the text before pasting it.

- **Q: The meeting lasted over two hours. Will the AI cut off mid-summary?**
  - A: Extremely long transcripts might exceed the context window limits of standard AI models. For marathon meetings, we highly recommend using Claude; its massive context window ensures absolutely no detail is left behind.

- **Q: Can the AI accurately distinguish between different speakers?**
  - A: Yes. As long as your transcript includes basic speaker labels (e.g., "Speaker 1: ...", "John: ..."), the AI will flawlessly attribute statements and assign tasks to the correct individuals.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** Injecting the "Senior Project Manager" persona guarantees a tone that is authoritative, concise, and hyper-focused on execution, rather than settling for a passive, low-energy summary.
2. **Strict Formatting Requirements:** By enforcing a rigid structural hierarchy (Objective, Decisions, Action Items), the resulting output is instantly ready to be copy-pasted directly into a team email, Notion page, or Jira ticket.
3. **Anti-Hallucination Guardrails:** The explicit warning clause completely blocks the AI from guessing or fabricating missing information. Forcing it to mark ambiguous details as "TBD" safely highlights blind spots instead of creating a dangerous illusion of alignment.
