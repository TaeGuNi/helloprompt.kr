## 📝 Working Like a Team of One: AI Agent Workflow Basics

- **🎯 Target Audience:** Solopreneurs, freelancers, and overwhelmed PMs/marketers
- **⏱️ Time Saved:** 1 hour → 5 minutes (including planning & review)
- **🤖 Recommended AI:** GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.5 Pro (High-reasoning models strongly recommended)

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 🚀 **Versatility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

> _"Disappointed after dumping all your tasks onto AI at once? The real pros don't treat AI like a chatbot—they command it as a 'virtual team.'"_

Most people issue AI simple, one-off commands like "Do this for me." However, as tasks grow in complexity, the AI's output inevitably becomes vague and generic. This is precisely where the productivity gap widens.

Top-tier professionals don't treat AI as a single, monolithic brain. Instead, they view it as an assembly of **'Agents.'** They architect their own **'virtual team'** where a Planner Agent outlines the structure, a Copywriter Agent fleshes out the narrative, and an Editor Agent relentlessly critiques the work. Today, I will introduce a foundational 'Agentic Workflow' prompt that will drastically slash your working hours.

---

## ⚡️ TL;DR

1. **Deconstruct and Chain Tasks:** Sequential, interlocking prompt chains are vastly superior to single, overarching instructions.
2. **Assign Multiple Personas (Roles):** Strictly divide roles into Planner, Executor, and Critic so they can debate and complement one another.
3. **Force a Feedback Loop:** Never accept the first draft. Compel the AI to self-critique, identify its own blind spots, and refine the output autonomously.

---

## 🚀 The Solution: "One-Person Agent Team Setup Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this for a rapid project outline and role assignment.

> **Role:** You are now a senior `[Project Manager]` with 10 years of experience.
>
> **Task:** To successfully achieve the `[Project Goal]`, outline 3 core milestones. For each phase, specify the required specialist (role) and detail their specific action items in a markdown table format.

### 🥇 Pro Version

A powerful prompt that forces the AI to embody three distinct experts within a single session, autonomously handling everything from initial strategic planning to ruthless final editing.

> **Role:** You are a **'Super Agent'** embodying a top-tier team of three experts: a Strategic Planner, an Expert Copywriter, and a Chief Critic.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I need a high-resolution `[Output Format, e.g., Sales Landing Page Copy]` for my `[Topic/Product]`.
> - Goal: To produce a compelling, persuasive outcome that outshines competitors and deeply resonates with the target audience.
>
> **Task:**
> Execute the following 3 phases sequentially. (Do not dump everything at once; think deeply through each step.)
>
> 1. **Phase 1 (Strategic Planner):** Logically outline a clear target persona, Unique Selling Proposition (USP), and the overarching structure for the `[Topic]`.
> 2. **Phase 2 (Expert Copywriter):** Based on the Phase 1 outline, write the actual draft. You must strictly adhere to a `[Desired Tone & Manner, e.g., professional yet persuasive]` tone.
> 3. **Phase 3 (Chief Critic):** Ruthlessly deconstruct the Phase 2 draft. Point out any logical leaps, cliché expressions, or lack of evidence. Then, **absorb all these criticisms and output a dramatically improved 'Final Masterpiece.'**
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Clearly mark the start of each phase with a markdown header: **"### Phase X: [Phase Name]"**.
> - The Chief Critic must NEVER offer meaningless praise like "This is well written." Focus strictly on exposing weaknesses and vulnerabilities.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Between Phase 2 and Phase 3, you MUST first output the **"Criticism Summary & Improvement Direction"** highlighted by the Critic, so I can verify your thought process.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

The true brilliance of this prompt lies in the final phase: **'Self-Refinement.'**
When you deploy this in the field, you will be amazed not just by the caliber of the writing, but by how the AI autonomously spots its own logical loopholes and fortifies its arguments against them.

Forcing the 'Chief Critic' persona specifically empowers the AI to preemptively catch edge cases or customer objections you might have overlooked. This is the most essential, immediately actionable manifestation of the "Agentic Workflow" championed by industry leaders like Dr. Andrew Ng. Even though it requires a slightly longer processing time, the resulting output boasts such overwhelming quality that it can often be deployed directly into your workflow with zero human edits.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well on free models (e.g., ChatGPT Free)?**
  - A: It functions, but the performance ceiling is immediately apparent. This prompt demands advanced reasoning capabilities and high context retention. To prevent the AI from conflating the three roles, I strongly recommend leveraging top-tier models like GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet.

- **Q: What if the AI ignores the sequential instruction and spits everything out at once?**
  - A: This happens occasionally. If it does, simply append this constraint to the Task section: _"Wait for my approval at the end of each Phase before moving on to the next."_ This drastically amplifies your control over the output generation.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Multi-Persona Assignment:** By assigning three complementary roles simultaneously rather than relying on a single identity, we compel the model to undergo internal context switching, which drastically minimizes bias.
2. **Chain-of-Thought:** Enforcing the human-expert workflow of [Planning → Execution → Ruthless Verification] mitigates hallucinations and dramatically increases logical density.
3. **Iterative Refinement Loop:** An AI's first draft typically plateaus at "good enough." Introducing a Chief Critic to tear down the draft and rebuild it is the secret sauce for achieving an exponential leap in quality.
