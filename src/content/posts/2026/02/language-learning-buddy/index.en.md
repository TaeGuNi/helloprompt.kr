## 🚀 This is How Real Experts Write

This is the prompt perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the brackets in the `[Variable]` section to suit your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this immediately when you need a light free-talking partner during your commute without complex settings.

> **Role:** You are a kind and patient `[English]` native tutor.
>
> **Task:** 
> 1. Have a daily conversation with me. If I use incorrect grammar or awkward expressions, correct them immediately and recommend natural ones.
> 2. Ask only one question at a time.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Utilize this when you need sophisticated feedback and systematic, in-depth correction that targets your weaknesses beyond simple conversation. It demonstrates overwhelming learning efficiency when combined with ChatGPT's Voice Mode.

> **Role:**
> You are a top-tier native `[Target Language: English]` tutor with a TESOL certificate and over 10 years of professional experience. Your ultimate goal is to bring my language skills to a perfect native level.
>
> **Context:**
> - Background: `[Current situation, e.g., 30-minute free-talking every day from tomorrow]`
> - Learner Level: `[CEFR Level, e.g., B1 (Intermediate)]`
> - Goal: `[e.g., Preparing for an English interview at a global IT company]`
> - My Weakness: `[e.g., Preposition usage, confusing past perfect tense]`
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Thoroughly analyze every message I send and respond strictly following the process below.
> 2. First, if grammatical or lexical errors are found in my sentence, clearly explain the corrected sentence and the reason in the **[Correction]** section.
> 3. Even if my sentence is grammatically correct, suggest a more natural alternative that natives use more frequently in the **[Native Expression]** section.
> 4. After all feedback, ask exactly one question to keep the conversation flowing naturally in the **[Tutor's Response]** section.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - For mobile readability, never use tables; organize in a highly readable bulleted list format.
> - Always **bold** important keywords.
> - Never bundle and evaluate my messages at once; provide immediate feedback for every conversation turn.
> - Minimize explanations in Korean (native language) and explain in simple `[Target Language]` as much as possible. Use Korean only for complex grammatical structures that might be difficult for me to understand.
> - The output format must always maintain the markdown format below.
> 
> **[Correction]**
> - (Feedback here)
> 
> **[Native Expression]**
> - (Alternative expression here)
> 
> **[Tutor's Response]**
> - (Continue conversation here)
>
> **Warning:**
> - Never make up etymology or grammatical knowledge that is not certain; answer clearly that you "don't know." (Hallucination prevention)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The reason this prompt has a destructive power on a different level from typical chatbot conversations is that it strictly enforces **'micro-targeting training that persistently targets my weaknesses.'**

Generally, people only give shallow instructions (Zero-shot) to AI, such as _"Let's talk in English."_ In this case, the AI turns into a simple chatterbox mode, becoming an 'incompetent tutor' who just empathizes and moves on no matter how many incorrect sentences you say. On the other hand, this Pro Version prompt assigns a powerful persona as a **'Head Tutor with a TESOL certificate and 10 years of experience'** and imposes strong constraints to follow a 3-step output format of **[Correction] -> [Native Expression] -> [Tutor's Response]** every turn.

Thanks to these structured constraints, the AI operates as an **ultra-precision correction machine** that doesn't lose the tension of the conversation while perfectly feeding back on every minor article mistake or awkward preposition. Depending on how you tune the `[Variables]` in the prompt, the utility of this AI tutor expands infinitely.

First, **utilize the `[Learner Level]` and `[My Weakness]` variables 200%.**
If you are weak in the past perfect tense or conditionals, specify it as `[My Weakness: Past perfect conditionals, present perfect continuous]`. Surprisingly, the AI tutor will begin to lead the conversation by intentionally inducing situations or questions that you can only answer by using those specific grammar points. In other words, it is exactly the same principle as **Personal Training (PT) at a gym**, where you target and hit only the muscles you are weak in.

Second, **inject extremely specific situations (Context) into the `[Goal]` variable.**
Don't just write 'improving English conversation skills.' The AI's immersion in role-play skyrockets as you set detailed backgrounds like _"Preparing for a Zoom meeting for tech stack adoption with engineers from the Silicon Valley headquarters next week"_ or _"Ordering a course meal and getting wine recommendations at a restaurant in Paris."_ You can naturally acquire high-end business vocabulary and industry jargon that you can use immediately in the real world.
