>
> **Context:**
>

>
> **Task:**

> 2. The core plot should revolve around `[Story Theme/Conflict, e.g., leaving to find a friend on melting ice due to global warming]`.
> 3. Naturally integrate at least 3 factual ecological habits of the `[Target Animal]` (e.g., swimming techniques, diet, how they stay warm) into the narrative.
> 4. `[Variables]` are to be filled in by the user, but you must provide the story based on the examples provided if left blank.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Maintain a gentle, hopeful, and comforting tone suitable for bedtime. Avoid overly sad, scary, or tragic elements.
> - Length: Keep it under 500 words.
> - Style: Use abundant onomatopoeia and sensory words to bring the story to life for a young child.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not invent false scientific facts. If you are unsure about an animal's specific trait, rely on universally accepted zoological data.

## ğŸ™‹ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I use this for very young toddlers (ages 2-3)?**
  - A: Absolutely! Just add a constraint to the prompt like: "Use very simple vocabulary and short, rhythmic sentences suitable for a 3-year-old."

- **Q: What if I want to include my child's name in the story?**
  - A: You can easily modify the prompt by adding: "Make the main character's human sidekick named [Your Child's Name]." The AI will seamlessly integrate them into the adventure.

- **Q: Will the AI accidentally include graphic animal hunting scenes?**
  - A: The "Constraints" section specifically instructs the AI to maintain a gentle and comforting tone. However, if you are choosing an apex predator, you can explicitly add: "Describe feeding habits without mentioning hunting or violence (e.g., 'enjoying a big fish dinner')."
