## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Won't drinking coffee right before a nap wake up my brain and make it hard to sleep?**
  - A: Not at all! It takes exactly <b>about 20 minutes</b> for caffeine to be absorbed into the body and reach the brain's fatigue receptors. If you close your eyes immediately after drinking coffee, the caffeine slowly moves to your brain while your body rests. Then, at the exact moment the alarm goes off 20 minutes later, the caffeine begins to exert its explosive alertness effect, allowing you to feel dramatically refreshed.

- **Q: What if I can't fall asleep in such a short time? Does just closing my eyes really work?**
  - A: Yes, it is perfectly effective! You don't have to fall into a deep, snoring sleep for your fatigue to lift. Just the <b>act of closing your eyes</b> and remaining still while blocking out visual stimuli causes the brain to immediately start generating 'Alpha waves,' which is the relaxation mode. The key secret to success is to let go of the obsession that "I must fall asleep in 20 minutes!" and instead feel like you are just putting a cover on your brain.

- **Q: Can I get the same effect with decaf coffee, green tea, or sweet instant coffee?**
  - A: Unfortunately, no. The core science of the Coffee Nap is for a sufficient amount of caffeine to preemptively block 'adenosine' receptors, which cause fatigue. Therefore, it's hard to expect a clear brain reboot effect with decaf drinks or teas that have significantly lower caffeine content. Also, instant coffee with a lot of sugar causes a blood sugar spike, which can actually increase fatigue immediately after sleeping. We recommend the most reliable and powerful method: drinking a hot <b>espresso shot</b> or strong <b>black Americano</b>.
