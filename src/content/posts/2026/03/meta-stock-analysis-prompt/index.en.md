> _"I know Meta is making good money with AI ads, but they're burning $15 billion a year in Reality Labs... is this really okay?"_

Meta (formerly Facebook) has made a spectacular comeback since its **"Year of Negative Growth"** in 2022. After plummeting to $90, the stock surged past $500, showing one of the most dramatic turnarounds on Wall Street. The core driver is the **AI-based advertising optimization engine**. AI ad tools like Advantage+ have drastically improved advertisers' ROAS (Return on Ad Spend), creating a virtuous cycle where both ad prices and volume grow simultaneously.

However, investors remain concerned. Reality Labs (VR/AR/Metaverse) is recording an **operating loss of over $15B annually**. Zuckerberg calls it a "10-year long-term investment," but nearly $15 billion (approximately 20 trillion KRW) is being poured every year into a <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">business with no visible returns</span>. Depending on how you evaluate this, Meta can look either "cheap" or "expensive."

This prompt completely separates Meta into **"Family of Apps (Ad Business)" and "Reality Labs (Metaverse)"** to value them independently. It clearly distinguishes what the P/E ratio is for the ad business alone and what the value is if Reality Labs is viewed as a "loss-making venture investment."

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The most powerful insight of this prompt is calculating the **"Standalone P/E of FoA."** While Meta's combined P/E is 25x, excluding the $16B loss from Reality Labs drops the FoA P/E to 18x. This is the lowest level among Big Tech (AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN). In other words, **even if you value Reality Labs at $0**, the current stock price is fully supported by the earning power of FoA. Reality Labs is essentially a "free option."

The reason I included `[Advantage+ AI Ad Effect]` is that this is the core engine behind Meta's earnings recovery from 2023 to 2026. As AI automatically handles ad targeting and creative optimization, advertisers' <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) has improved by an average of 32%</span>. When advertisers are convinced they can "make more money by pouring more into Meta," they increase their ad spend. As long as this virtuous cycle holds, ad revenue growth will continue.
