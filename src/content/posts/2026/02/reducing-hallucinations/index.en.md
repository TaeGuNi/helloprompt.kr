We have perfectly compressed these three innovative principles into a single prompt.

Move beyond one-dimensional instructions like "Don't lie." Now, your AI will be equipped with a rational judgment system that constantly doubts its own logic, seeks evidence ONLY within the documents you provide, and confidently declares "I don't know" when it isn't sure. ## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Enforce Grounding:** Strictly limit all AI responses to be based only on the provided documents and clear sources.
2. **Introduce Chain of Verification (CoVe):** Force the AI to go through a stage of critically questioning and correcting factual relationships itself before outputting the final result.
3. **Utilize Uncertainty Markers:** Force the explicit use of expressions like "Not verified" or "Uncertain" so the AI doesn't pretend to know what it doesn't.

## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this lightly when you need to quickly grasp the truth of information or require a concise summary based only on facts.

> **Role:** You are a `[Professional Researcher]` who prioritizes fact-checking above all else.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Analyze the following `[Text/Topic]`.
> 2. However, never make up content that is not in the given text.
> 3. For anything you don't know, you must clearly answer "Unknown."

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert Type)

This is the complete prompt deployed when 100% flawless reliability is required, such as for critical business reports or academic data analysis.

> **Role:** You are a `[Senior Data Verification Officer]` who prioritizes the accuracy and integrity of information above all else.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: `[Writing a competitor trend analysis report]`
> - Goal: `[Derive summaries and insights based 100% on facts from the provided materials]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Write the analysis results based ONLY on the provided `[Reference Materials]`.
> 2. **Grounding:** At the end of every claim, you must specify the source (e.g., [Paragraph 2]) from which part of the `[Reference Materials]` it was extracted.
> 3. **Chain of Verification (CoVe):** Before outputting the final answer, go through a process of creating and verifying 3 questions that ask about factual relationships yourself. If contradictions or content without sources are found during the verification process, revise the answer immediately.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Do not arbitrarily mix in external knowledge or pre-trained data.
> - For parts where information is insufficient or you are not certain, you must use an Uncertainty Marker in the form of "This part is not certain based on the provided materials alone, but..."
> - Use Markdown bullet points (List) for the output format.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Hallucination is absolutely unacceptable. Decisively discard even slightly uncertain information and process it as "Not verified."

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

This prompt is the **pinnacle of sophisticated prompt engineering**, completely moving away from traditional one-dimensional instructions that simply yell "Don't lie" or "Tell only the truth." It is the result of meticulously implementing the **CoVe (Chain of Verification)** methodology and **Grounding** techniques—which have established themselves as the standards for fact verification in the 2026 AI academic community—so that general professionals can copy and deploy them into their work in just one second.

In fierce business environments and rigorous academic research fields, an AI's random 'creativity' or plausible storytelling ability can actually be poisonous. What is overwhelmingly more important is **'extremely conservative fact-checking'**—the kind that taps on a stone bridge hundreds of times before crossing. The most powerful weapon of this prompt is the **Grounding mechanism**, which strictly limits the answer's context to the `[Reference Materials]` provided by the user and forces explicit source citations (e.g., [Paragraph 2]) at the end of every sentence. This fundamentally blocks the AI from subconsciously mixing in vast (but irrelevant to the current context) fictional information it learned in the past and "writing a novel." The surest way to prevent hallucinations is to remove the space for imagination itself.

Added to this, **Chain of Verification (CoVe)** forces a kind of **'Internal Audit'** process where the AI critically questions and corrects factual relationships behind the scenes before spitting out the final result to the user. It is a self-purifying system that makes the AI ask itself, "Wait, was this figure really in the document?" as soon as it completes a sentence, and immediately delete or revise that content if it wasn't.

The key to **Constraint Control** to utilize this prompt 200% in practice lies in the quality and volume of the `[Reference Materials]` and the specificity of the `[Role]` variable. First, if the materials provided are too vast or the logical flow is jumbled, the probability of the AI losing its way and hallucinating increases. When analyzing PDF papers spanning hundreds of pages or contracts with complex intertwined clauses, it is a safer and better strategy to **input them by breaking them down into meaningful chapter units** rather than putting the whole document in at once. Second, instead of simply writing 'Researcher' in the `[Role]` variable, try giving it an extremely specific and sharp persona like 'Senior Data Verification Officer who has been analyzing global mobility market trends for 10 years.' You will feel the AI's attitude toward the document and the professional depth of the output change completely.

Personally, I use this prompt as a fixed template whenever I need to summarize complex legal advice or financial market analysis reports. In the past, I couldn't trust the summaries the AI provided, so I had to keep the original document on a dual monitor and cross-verify by highlighting it all night. However, since introducing this technique, I have **drastically reduced the information cross-verification time that humans had to perform by more than 95%.** I hope you also experience this powerful control firsthand. Simply put, AI becomes as honest as you delicately and strictly control it.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Doesn't the Chain of Verification (CoVe) process make the response output too slow?**
  - A: Because it goes through a fact-verification stage internally one more time, token generation may take slightly longer than a typical prompt. However, considering the painful resources required for a human to later find and fix incorrect information generated, this amount of waiting time is a much more economical and safe investment.

- **Q: The prompt results are still unstable and it keeps saying different things. How should I tune it?**
  - A: If the AI keeps pulling in learned external knowledge, try adding the phrase `Strictly prohibit access to external web searches and pre-trained data other than the provided text` even more strongly to the Constraints. Also, if the `[Reference Materials]` themselves are too vast or the logical structure is ambiguous, the AI might lose context and hallucinate. In such cases, breaking the material into chapters for input is an excellent strategy.

- **Q: Can I use this prompt on other language models (Claude, Gemini, etc.) instead of a specific model?**
  - A: Yes, this is a universal prompt that works smoothly on most modern commercial models. Especially when applied to models with large context windows and excellent instruction-following capabilities, such as Claude 3.5 Sonnet or Gemini 2.5 Pro, you can experience the most perfect and reliable results that do not allow for lies.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1.  **Forced Grounding:** All grounds for AI responses are strictly limited to the interior of the `[Reference Materials]`, and explicit source citations are required. This perfectly blocks fatal errors where the AI subconsciously mixes in fictional information it already knows.
2.  **Chain of Verification (CoVe) Design:** The AI is made to go through a critical thinking process regarding its own answer one more time. A powerful safety device was built so that the AI internally censors and corrects logical leaps or factual contradictions before the user even checks them.
3.  **Allowing Uncertainty Markers:** Paradoxically, the key is to open a legitimate 'exit' for the AI where it can confidently declare "I don't know." This prevented the worst-case hallucination scenario where the AI feels forced to make up a plausible answer and deceive the user.
