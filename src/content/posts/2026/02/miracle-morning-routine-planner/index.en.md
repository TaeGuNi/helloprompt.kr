## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

Here are two versions: the **Basic** version for quickly setting a foundation based on your core goals, and the **Pro** version for full control over variables like biological rhythms and oversleeping. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variables]` in brackets to match your situation.

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Productivity and Time Management Expert]`.
> 
> **Context:** I wake up at `[6:00 AM]` and have `[1 hour 30 minutes]` before I leave for work. My morning goals are `[stretching, reading 10 pages, and having a light breakfast]`.
> 
> **Task:** Create a realistic and efficient morning routine timeline on a minute-by-minute basis to achieve these goals.

### 🥇 Pro Version (The Professional)

> **Role:** You are a `[Lead Behavioral Psychologist and Productivity Coach helping top 1% CEOs manage their time]`.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Wake-up Time: `[6:30 AM]`
> - Available Morning Time: `[Approx. 1 hour]`
> - Energy Level after waking: `[Very low, takes at least 15 minutes to fully wake up]`
> - Core Goals: `[1. Drink warm water, 2. 10-minute yoga, 3. Write down top 3 tasks for today]`
> - Obstacles: `[Habit of checking social media on smartphone immediately upon waking]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Propose a minute-by-minute morning routine timeline tailored to my available time and energy levels.
> 2. Since my energy level is low immediately after waking, place 'warm-up actions' in the first step to gently wake me up.
> 3. Include at least one specific 'environment setup tip (nudge)' to prevent the habit of checking my smartphone.
> 4. Provide a 'Minimal Emergency Routine (Plan B)' for cases where I oversleep and my available time is reduced to `[20 minutes]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - All time allocations must be extremely realistic and include 1-2 minutes of 'Buffer time' between actions.
> - Do NOT use tables; use clear bulleted lists and a timeline format for mobile readability.
> - **Bold** important keywords and times.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Exclude generic or abstract advice (e.g., "Have a positive mind," "You can do it"). Provide only specific, physical instructions that can be acted upon immediately.

## 💡 Writer's Comments (Insight & How to use)

The true power of this prompt lies in two pillars: **'Objectifying Energy Levels'** and **'Systemic Plan B Construction.'** The most common mistake people make when designing a routine is unrealistically assuming their morning condition will always be at 100%. But we aren't machines; there are days when we work late, have unavoidable dinners, or simply sleep poorly. On those days, our energy is at zero.

The **`[Energy Level after waking]`** variable is the key that makes the AI recognize your reality. If you honestly input "Very low, body feels heavy," the AI will immediately push high-intensity exercise or deep-focus reading to the end of the timeline. Instead, it prioritizes **'Zero-Friction Warm-ups'** like drinking water or light stretching. This behavioral psychology approach lowers psychological resistance and triggers the brain to start moving subconsciously.

Furthermore, we need a mechanism to prevent the psychological collapse that happens when we oversleep. In psychology, the feeling of "I've already ruined my morning, I might as well give up on the whole week" is called the **'What-the-hell effect.'** To defend against this, you must have a pre-built **'Minimal Defense Line (Plan B).'** The Plan B requirement in the prompt prevents your routine from crumbling entirely. It protects your self-efficacy by allowing you to say, "At least I did my core routine today." These small wins accumulate to drive the rest of your day toward success.

Also, the more honest you are about your "obstacles"—like mindless scrolling or hitting the snooze button—the more precise the AI's prescription becomes. In my own experience, the AI's nudge to **"place your phone charger outside the bedroom and use a smartwatch vibration for your alarm"** reclaimed 30 minutes of my morning that I had lost for years. Fill the variables with your honest weaknesses and constraints. The AI won't judge; it will only provide the most scientific and effective solution for your success.

Focusing solely on "waking up early" is like pouring water into a leaking bucket. The core is how you wake up your brain and which healthy way you choose to release your first dose of dopamine. This prompt isn't just a schedule generator; it is a precision psychological tool designed to start your day successfully even when your willpower is depleted. ## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if even the AI-designed timeline feels too burdensome to practice?**
  - A: Don't hesitate to give the AI immediate feedback. Tell the AI, *"The schedule is too tight. Add 20% more buffer time to each item and remove the lowest priority goal,"* and ask it to reconfigure. The AI will instantly adjust the schedule to a more realistic version that fits your pace.

- **Q: My wake-up times for weekdays and weekends are very different. Can this be reflected?**
  - A: Absolutely. In the **Context** section, state, *"Weekday wake-up is `[6:00 AM]`, and weekend wake-up is `[8:30 AM]`."* Then, in the **Task** section, explicitly instruct it to provide separate timelines for weekdays and weekends.

- **Q: My morning goals change frequently. How should I set them?**
  - A: Instead of specific single actions (e.g., 'Read 3 trend articles'), ask the AI to group goals into flexible 'High-level Categories' (e.g., '20 mins for self-development and input'). This allows you to swap activities within that category based on your mood, significantly increasing the sustainability of the routine.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Hyper-Personalization of Context:** By forcing the input of **'Real energy levels'** and **'Chronic obstacles'** beyond just wake-up times, we block the AI from providing soulless, generic self-help advice.
2. **Systematizing Emergency Plans (Plan B):** By pre-controlling for the most fatal variable—oversleeping—we prevent the "all-or-nothing" collapse of the routine and build a sturdy psychological safety net.
3. **Behavioral Psychology Nudge:** By explicitly requesting physical environment tips and gradual warm-ups, we force the AI to design a system that leads the user into action without exhausting their willpower.
