## 🚀 The Solution: "AI Morning News Anchor"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Deploy this lightweight version when you have a handful of specific articles or raw text dumps and need an instantaneous, high-level summary.

> **Role:** You are an expert news editor.
> **Task:** Summarize the following news articles into exactly 3 key bullet points each, strictly highlighting the most critical facts.
>
> `[Insert News Articles or URLs here]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Implement this robust, comprehensive prompt to engineer a seamless daily intelligence routine. Feed it raw text blocks, RSS feeds, or a batch of URLs to generate a fully bespoke, executive-grade briefing.

> **Role:** You are a veteran executive news anchor and chief editor serving a top-tier industry professional.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I must stay aggressively updated on `[Your Industry/Topic, e.g., AI technology, global finance, SaaS marketing]` without wasting a single second on fluff, noise, or clickbait.
> - Goal: Deliver a concise, highly scannable morning briefing engineered to be read in exactly 3 minutes.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Deeply analyze the provided `[News Sources/Articles/Text]`.
> 2. Select the top 3-5 most critical stories that carry a direct, actionable impact for a `[Your Job Title, e.g., Product Manager, Investor]`.
> 3. For every selected story, provide a compelling headline, a razor-sharp 2-sentence summary of the core facts, and exactly one sentence explaining _why this matters_ specifically to my industry and role.
> 4. Conclude the briefing with a "Quote of the Day" directly related to elite productivity or the overarching theme of today's news.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Format the output using pristine Markdown (utilizing headers, bullet points, and bold text for strategic emphasis).
> - Absolutely no personal opinions; adhere strictly to the verifiable facts presented in the source material.
> - Maintain a tone that is exceptionally professional, ruthlessly objective, and appropriately urgent.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - If the ingested text yields zero highly relevant news for my specific sector, you must explicitly state: "No significant updates in this sector today." Under no circumstances should you hallucinate data or force-fit irrelevant news stories.
>
> **News Input:**
> `[Paste your daily news text, RSS feed content, or raw article dumps here]`

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Am I required to manually paste article text every single morning?**
  - A: If you are operating on a free-tier AI model lacking live web access, yes. However, if you leverage premium tools like ChatGPT Plus, Microsoft Copilot, or Perplexity, you can entirely automate this step. Simply replace the input section with: "Search the live web for today's most critical news regarding `[Topic]`."

- **Q: Can this exact framework be repurposed for personal interests, like sports or gaming?**
  - A: Absolutely. The underlying architecture is universally applicable. Just swap out the variables in the "Context" section to target your specific hobby (e.g., "Premier League Football" or "Indie Game Releases"), and the AI will curate your personal passions with the same rigorous efficiency.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works)

1. **Role & Context (`Role`, `Context`):** By explicitly casting the AI as a "veteran executive news anchor," we immediately enforce an uncompromisingly high standard for professional tone, brevity, and elite curation.
2. **Actionable Deliverables (`Task`):** The mandate to explain "why this matters" forces the model to synthesize analytical, forward-looking value, preventing it from merely regurgitating the source text.
3. **Strict Formatting (`Constraints`):** Mandating a clean Markdown structure ensures the final output is highly scannable visually—a non-negotiable requirement for hitting that strict "3-minute" readability constraint.
4. **Anti-Hallucination (`Warning`):** The hardcoded instruction to transparently admit when there is a lack of relevant news acts as a critical safety rail, actively preventing the AI from fabricating facts just to satisfy the prompt's structural demands.
