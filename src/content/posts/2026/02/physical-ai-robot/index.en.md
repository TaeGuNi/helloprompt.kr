## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in parentheses to match your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you quickly need Physical AI application ideas for a specific industry.

> **Role:** You are a Lead Physical AI and Robotics Planner with peerless expertise at the level of `[LG AI Research]`.
>
> **Task:** Propose 3 innovative service scenarios that can be created when introducing Physical AI (robots with autonomous decision-making capabilities) to the `[specific industry, e.g., Logistics/Retail/Healthcare]` field. Be sure to include specific expected benefits and anticipated technical limitations for each scenario.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a complete proposal that reflects the characteristics of advanced AI models, including detailed behavioral control and user sentiment.

> **Role:** You are a 'Physical AI New Business Director' who is an authority in robotics and well-versed in the latest deep learning technologies (especially large vision-language models like EXAONE).
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: As of 2026, the adoption of Physical AI is spreading rapidly across all industries. Beyond simple process efficiency, improving the quality of Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) is emerging as a core business competitive advantage.
> - Goal: To establish a specific execution plan and service scenarios for introducing Physical AI robots to our company's `[Target Industry and Service Name]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Derive 2 core Physical AI Use Cases perfectly specialized for the `[Target Industry]`.
> 2. For each use case, describe the steps of the 'Physical Action' the robot must perform in detail.
> 3. Provide an in-depth analysis of the expected 'User Sentiment' (emotional reactions, resistance, anxiety, etc.) upon service introduction, and propose psychological/technical solutions to convert this into a positive experience.
> 4. Realistically propose an initial Pilot Test (PoC) operation plan tailored to the `[Implementation Budget Scale]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - All analysis must be written based on the premise of a **'Physical Robot'** that interacts with the real world through sensors and actuators, not just software AI operating on a screen.
> - Use Markdown format to maximize readability, and organize the use case comparisons cleanly using bullet points.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Strictly exclude far-fetched scenarios assuming excessive science fiction (SF) level technology that is not currently commercialized (e.g., humanoids with perfect free will). Reflect only realistic technical levels that can be implemented within the next 1-2 years. (Hallucination prevention)

## 💡 Writer's Commentary (Insight & How to Use)

The most powerful weapon of this prompt is that it strongly forces the AI's persona to recognize itself not as a 'chatbot spitting out text on a screen,' but as an **'Agent acting directly in physical space.'** By providing the clear and trendy keyword "Physical AI" while simultaneously instructing it to consider the physical action procedures of the robot and the resulting User Sentiment, the quality and depth of the planning proposal change completely.

Particularly in professional environments where vague curiosity about new technology coexists with anxiety that 'it might take my job,' **how meticulously you manage user sentiment** determines the success or failure of the service. Try using this prompt actively in the early stages of constructing robot control scenarios or HRI (Human-Robot Interaction) planning. You can instantly turn the vague blueprint of a robotics business that was only in your head into a concrete execution plan that both management and development teams can agree on.

**Know-how for Controlling and Utilizing Prompt Variables (`[Variables]`):**
The most important factor when using this prompt in practice is how **sharply and specifically** you define the `[Target Industry and Service Name]` variable. If you simply enter `[Retail Store]`, the AI is likely to give somewhat plain and general answers. However, try entering this variable with specific spatial constraints and characteristics, such as `[Urban 24-hour Unmanned Ice Cream Shop]` or `[Meat Counter in a Traditional Market]`. The AI will then derive a surprising level of physical action scenarios that consider the narrow traffic flow of the space, the location of freezers, and even the age group of the primary customers.

Additionally, the `[Implementation Budget Scale]` variable is a key mechanism for triggering the AI's **Reality Check**. Setting the budget to `[50 million KRW]` might lead to a proposal for a small-scale PoC using a single robotic arm, while setting it to `[1 billion KRW]` will result in a scenario for warehouse logistics automation with a swarm of autonomous mobile robots (AMRs). By clearly identifying budget constraints, you get an **executable business plan** that can be put on an executive's desk tomorrow, rather than a fantasy novel that is technically over-engineered.

Finally, never overlook the AI's response regarding **'User Sentiment'** analysis. A common mistake made by roboticists is chasing mechanical efficiency while ignoring the discomfort or threat humans feel. This prompt forces the AI to preemptively identify the psychological pressure people might feel when a robot approaches and proposes specific UX/UI solutions such as 'gentle deceleration,' 'visual UI feedback,' and 'friendly voice guidance.' Including such a human-centric approach from the beginning of the planning stage will allow you to successfully launch a **robot service loved by customers**, rather than just introducing a machine.

The hardest part for planners is the 'integrated thinking' that breaks down the boundary between hardware and software. Emphasizing only hardware specs lacks an IT service perspective, while emphasizing only software AI ignores physical laws (gravity, friction, sensor blind spots, etc.). This prompt perfectly bridges the gap between these two worlds by requesting a **'Detailed Description of Physical Action Procedures.'** Since the AI logically reasons through and writes about the angle of the robot's arm, movement speed, and obstacle avoidance mechanisms, you can draft a high-quality Product Requirements Document (PRD) that even non-robotics majors can understand. This is the ultimate secret to utilizing generative AI not just for text, but as a true **Business Partner and Senior Robotics Consultant**.

- **Q: Can this prompt only be used by engineers with professional knowledge of robotics?**
  - A: Not at all. In fact, it is a much more powerful tool for service planners, marketers, and new business managers who may not know hardware specs or complex coding deeply. It helps them quickly review the business feasibility of Physical AI adoption and establish the backbone of a Business Requirements Document (BRD) for smooth communication with development teams later.

- **Q: Does this prompt only work with the EXAONE model?**
  - A: It shows particularly excellent context understanding in high-performance multimodal models specialized for Korean industrial domains like EXAONE, but it also derives excellent and logically consistent scenarios in top-tier global models like GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet.

- **Q: What if the AI's output is too abstract or generic?**
  - A: Rather than just writing 'Retail' or 'Logistics' when entering the `[Target Industry]` variable, try making the spatial characteristics and constraints extremely specific, such as '24-hour Unmanned Ice Cream Shop' or 'Cold Chain Fresh Food Warehouse (1,500 square meters)'. The resolution and practical applicability of the results provided by the AI will increase dramatically.
