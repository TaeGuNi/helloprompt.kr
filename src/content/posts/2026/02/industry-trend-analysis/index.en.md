### ü•á Pro Version (Deep Dive)

Use this for rigorous, detailed, and actionable investment intelligence.
_Note: Best used with AI models that have active web browsing capabilities._

> **Role (Role):** You are an `[Industry Analysis Expert]` from a top-tier global consulting firm like McKinsey or BCG.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I am planning a long-term investment in the `[Generative AI / Robotics / Secondary Battery]` sector.
> - Goal: I need a comprehensive breakdown of current market dynamics and a robust 5-year outlook for this industry.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Detail the top 3 **Key Growth Drivers** propelling this industry forward.
> 2. Break down the **Value Chain** of the industry by its core stages. For each stage, list 2 representative global companies and 2 domestic (local) companies.
> 3. Highlight the **Biggest Risks** (e.g., impending regulations, technological barriers, geopolitical issues) expected in the near future.
> 4. Summarize the market size outlook and Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) projections in a Markdown table.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Base your analysis strictly on the latest information published within the last 6 months.
> - Be highly specific: mention exact technology terminologies and actual company names rather than relying on vague generalizations.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - If you cannot verify specific data points or if information is unavailable, explicitly state "Information unavailable" rather than fabricating data (Strictly avoid hallucination).

## üí° Writer's Insight

This prompt effectively transforms a generic AI into a senior research analyst. By explicitly requesting a **Value Chain Breakdown**, you avoid the trap of only discovering overvalued, mainstream consumer-facing companies (like Tesla or Nvidia). Instead, you uncover the critical "picks and shovels" businesses‚Äîthe parts suppliers, infrastructure builders, and raw material providers‚Äîthat often offer much better entry valuations. I highly recommend running this prompt iteratively: once you find an interesting sub-sector in the value chain, use the prompt again specifically targeting that niche.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well with the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: The free version of ChatGPT (if it has access to browsing) can provide a decent overview. However, because financial data requires real-time accuracy, using Perplexity Pro, GPT-4o, or Gemini Advanced yields significantly more reliable and up-to-date market figures.

- **Q: Can I use this for niche or emerging industries like Quantum Computing?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just replace the industry placeholder. For highly emerging fields, emphasize the "Biggest Risks" and "Technological Barriers" sections, as these industries often face extreme volatility and longer timelines to profitability.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing (McKinsey/BCG Analyst):** Forces the AI to adopt a highly structured, mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive (MECE) approach to its explanation, adopting a professional tone.
2. **Value Chain Deconstruction:** Prevents the AI from just listing popular stocks. It forces the AI to map the ecosystem, revealing hidden investment opportunities in B2B suppliers.
3. **Temporal Constraint (Last 6 Months):** Crucial for financial markets. This constraint drastically reduces the risk of the AI fetching outdated macroeconomic data or obsolete growth forecasts.
