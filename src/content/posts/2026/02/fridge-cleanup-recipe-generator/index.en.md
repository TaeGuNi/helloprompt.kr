## 🚀 The Solution: "The Leftover Rescue Chef"

### 🥉 Basic Version (Quick & Easy)

Use this prompt when you are in a rush and just need an immediate dinner idea.

> **Role:** You are a Michelin 2-star chef and a healthy home cooking expert.
>
> **Request:** Create a simple, 5-star healthy recipe using only the ingredients I provide that are nearing their expiration date.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert Culinary Guide)

Use this prompt for a highly detailed, nutritionally balanced recipe complete with pro tips.

> **Role:** You are a Michelin 2-star chef and an expert in healthy home cooking. Your specialty is maximizing flavor while minimizing ingredients and prep time.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I need to clean out my fridge to avoid food waste.
> - Current Ingredients: I have `[2 eggs, half a bunch of wilted spinach, half a block of leftover tofu, 1 onion, and oyster sauce]`.
> - Pantry Staples: I also have access to `[rice, cooking oil, salt, pepper, and basic seasonings]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Propose 2 healthy and delicious dinner menus that primarily utilize the ingredients listed above.
> 2. Select the most cohesive menu and provide a step-by-step cooking method.
> 3. Include a "Chef's Kick" (a secret tip or technique) to ensure even a beginner can nail the flavor profile.
> 4. Clearly state the estimated cooking time, prep time, and difficulty level.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Format your output using clear markdown headers and lists.
> - Strictly avoid cooking methods that require complicated cleanup, such as deep-frying.
> - Do not suggest buying new ingredients. If a standard ingredient is missing, suggest a creative alternative using what I already have.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not invent complex culinary techniques that contradict the simplicity of the ingredients. Keep it accessible and do not hallucinate recipes that don't make culinary sense.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

This prompt is an absolute lifesaver for those busy weeknights when your fridge looks like a random assortment of unrelated items. The true power of this prompt lies in the **Constraints** section. By explicitly forbidding deep-frying and the introduction of new ingredients, you force the AI to think practically. It prevents the model from generating a recipe that requires a sudden grocery run for "fresh truffle oil" when all you have is a sad onion and some eggs. 

I highly recommend tweaking the `[Current Ingredients]` variable with whatever you currently have on hand. Even if the combination sounds terrible to you, the AI almost always finds a surprising **culinary bridge**—like a specific sauce or cooking method—to tie them together beautifully.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well on the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Absolutely! The free version handles this task wonderfully. However, using **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** or **GPT-4o** might yield slightly more creative flavor pairings and refined culinary techniques.

- **Q: What if the AI suggests an ingredient I am allergic to?**
  - A: You can simply add an extra constraint to the prompt: `"Constraint: I am allergic to peanuts and dairy. Do not include them under any circumstances."` The AI will instantly adapt the recipe to meet your dietary restrictions.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** Assigning the "Michelin 2-star chef" persona elevates the AI's vocabulary and shifts its focus from basic college-dorm meals to refined, yet accessible, culinary techniques.
2. **Clear Constraints:** Specifying "no complicated cleanup" and "no new ingredients" addresses the two biggest pain points of home cooking, ensuring the output is actually practical for a busy evening.
3. **The 'Chef's Kick':** This forces the AI to provide a tangible, actionable tip (like blooming spices in oil or deglazing a pan) that elevates the dish far beyond a generic, automated recipe.
