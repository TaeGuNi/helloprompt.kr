## 🚀 This is How Real Experts Write

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trial-and-error iterations. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[variables]` in brackets to fit your situation, and deploy it immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Useful when you just want to quickly grasp approximate calories and core nutrients before a meal. Attaching a photo maximizes analysis accuracy.

> **Role:** You are a precision nutritional analyst. You can accurately estimate weights and nutritional content from just a photo or a simple description.
> 
> **Request:** Calculate the total calories and approximate macro (g) ratios for the food in the attached photo (or the text I've written).

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you want to systematically track your entire day's diet and receive comprehensive recommendations for your next meal based on remaining macros.
_(※ Use of GPT-4o or Gemini 1.5 Pro models is highly recommended for their superior image recognition.)_

> **Role:** You are a professional nutritionist and fitness trainer with 10 years of experience. You accurately and conservatively (estimating calories slightly high) estimate weights and nutrients from food photos and simple descriptions.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - My daily target intake: `[2500]` kcal
> - Target Carb:Protein:Fat ratio: `[5:3:2]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Analyze the attached photo or `[Food List: 1 serving of spicy pork bowl for lunch, an Americano, and 3 pieces of rolled omelet]` and calculate the total calories and Carbs/Protein/Fat (g).
> 2. Accurately calculate the **'Remaining Nutrients (Remaining calories and macros)'** by subtracting current intake from my daily goal.
> 3. Recommend 2 realistic dinner options to perfectly fill the remaining nutrients and explain why.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Calculate conservatively based on standard restaurant serving recipes.
> - Organize the diet analysis and remaining nutrients into an easy-to-read Markdown Table.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up absurd ingredient weights or calories. If an ingredient is difficult to estimate, add a comment explaining the basis of your calculation.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The true destructive power and practical value of this prompt lie in perfectly combining **'Automatic Remaining Macro Calculation Logic'** and **'Custom Next Meal Recommendation'** into one. General meal apps are nothing more than 'passive logs' that simply sum up the data entered by the user. However, the moment you use this prompt, the AI transforms into an **'active coach'** that dynamically analyzes data and proposes future solutions.

The point where dieters and meal managers experience the most frustration and stress in daily life is the question: _'What on earth should I eat for dinner tonight to exactly meet my daily goal?'_ When you've unexpectedly over-consumed fat and carbs at a lunch meeting or outing, calculating the optimal meal you can have within the remaining calories and macro limits is a grueling task that requires significant nutritional knowledge and mental effort. The <b>`[Daily target intake]`</b> and <b>`[Target Carb:Protein:Fat ratio]`</b> variables in this prompt are core triggers meticulously designed to delegate this complex nutritional equation entirely to the AI.

<b>💡 Constraint Control for Overwhelming Accuracy</b>

To utilize this prompt at 200% in real life, it is crucial to inject **additional context to supplement the AI's vision recognition limits**. No matter how excellent its visual intelligence, an AI cannot see through the broth of a stew to the amount of ingredients hidden underneath or the exact amount of oil used in a stir-fry. Therefore, adding short text (variables) like the following when sending a food photo will drastically increase the accuracy of the result:

1. **Intake Control:** If you didn't eat the entire serving provided, specify the actual consumption ratio, such as _"I left about a third of the rice,"_ _"I only ate the tofu and meat from the stew without drinking the broth,"_ or _"I only used half the sauce for dipping."_ The AI will immediately reflect this and adjust the calories downward.
2. **Specifying Hidden Ingredients:** Provide hints for high-calorie ingredients that aren't clearly visible in the photo. Entering _"This salad has two tablespoons of olive oil dressing,"_ or _"There is a thick layer of cream cheese inside the bread,"_ allows the AI to reverse-calculate fat and sugar levels much more conservatively and accurately.
3. **Adding Nuance to Cooking Methods:** Describing the degree of grilling or frying, or a specific cooking method, is also a great way. Adding context like _"It's pork belly grilled crisp in butter,"_ or _"It's a chicken breast sausage cooked in an air fryer without oil,"_ will extremely narrow the margin of error for macro estimation.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can it analyze liquid dishes like stews or soups?**
  - A: Yes, it can! The AI identifies visible ingredients and the type of broth to estimate approximate calories. In this case, providing additional context like _"I didn't drink the broth and only ate the solid ingredients"_ alongside the prompt will greatly increase accuracy.

- **Q: Can I use this with the free version of standard ChatGPT?**
  - A: You can certainly use it with text input. However, since the free version may have limited photo recognition (Vision) features, you must write very detailed text descriptions of the type and approximate amount of food you ate. For overwhelming convenience, using latest AI models that support image attachments is recommended.

- **Q: Is the nutritional content calculated by AI 100% accurate?**
  - A: It's not a perfect laboratory-level answer, but a 'very close estimate' based on universal restaurant recipe data. However, for general dieting, maintenance, or body profile preparation, it serves as a sufficiently reliable and excellent tracking baseline.
