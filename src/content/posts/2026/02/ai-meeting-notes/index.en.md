## üöÄ The Solution: "The Executive Scribe Prompt"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, no-frills summary of a brief discussion.

> **Role:** You are an expert executive assistant.
>
> **Task:** Summarize the provided meeting transcript into key bullet points and list the action items.
>
> **Transcript:** `[Paste transcript here]`

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this for high-stakes meetings, project kick-offs, or complex syncs where meticulous detail and structured formatting are non-negotiable.

> **Role (Persona):** You are an elite project manager and executive scribe known for your meticulous attention to detail and ability to synthesize complex discussions into clear, actionable corporate documentation.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: We just concluded a critical `[meeting type, e.g., weekly sync, project kickoff]`.
> - Objective: I need to distribute a comprehensive, structured meeting summary to all stakeholders that highlights decisions made, bottlenecks, and next steps.
>
> **Task:**
> Analyze the following meeting transcript and generate a structured summary including:
>
> 1. **Meeting Objective:** A 1-2 sentence summary of what the meeting was about.
> 2. **Key Decisions:** Bullet points of all final decisions made during the call.
> 3. **Action Items:** A clear list of tasks, identifying **who** is responsible and the **deadline** (if mentioned).
> 4. **Parking Lot:** Topics that were brought up but tabled for future discussion.
>
> **Transcript:**
> `[Paste the full meeting transcript or your raw notes here]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Format the output in clean, professional Markdown.
> - Do NOT include filler words or casual banter from the transcript.
> - If an action item lacks a clear owner, flag it as `[Unassigned]`.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not hallucinate or invent information. If a decision wasn't explicitly made, do not state that it was.

## üí° Writer's Insight

Automating your meeting notes is easily the highest-ROI habit you can adopt this year. However, the true secret isn't just feeding a transcript to ChatGPT; it's about **structuring the prompt to enforce accountability**. By explicitly asking the AI to flag `[Unassigned]` tasks, you instantly spot the gaps in your meeting's conclusion before they become blockers later in the week.

**Pro Tip:** If your company enforces strict NDAs, be sure to use enterprise-grade AI tools (which don't train on your data) or manually replace sensitive client names with "Client A" before running the prompt. Efficiency is great, but data security is paramount.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Do I need a paid AI subscription for this?**
  - A: The Basic version works flawlessly on free models like standard ChatGPT or Claude. However, for the Pro version handling hour-long transcripts, the larger context windows and superior reasoning of GPT-4, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, or Gemini Advanced are highly recommended.

- **Q: How do I get the meeting transcript in the first place?**
  - A: Most modern video conferencing tools (Zoom, Google Meet, Teams) have built-in transcription features. Alternatively, you can use dedicated tools like Otter.ai, Fathom, or Fireflies to capture the audio and generate the raw text.

- **Q: What about privacy and confidential company information?**
  - A: Never paste PII (Personally Identifiable Information) or trade secrets into public AI models unless your organization has an enterprise agreement guaranteeing data privacy. Always sanitize and anonymize data first if using a free tier.

## üß¨ Anatomy of the Prompt (Why it works)

1. **Role Assignment:** Giving the AI the persona of an "elite project manager" changes its tone from a generic bot to a corporate professional.
2. **Structured Outputs:** By clearly defining sections (Decisions, Action Items, Parking Lot), we force the AI to categorize the unstructured transcript logically, rather than just returning a wall of text.
3. **The `[Unassigned]` Constraint:** This simple rule prevents tasks from disappearing into the void, ensuring every action item has an owner or is visibly flagged for immediate follow-up.
