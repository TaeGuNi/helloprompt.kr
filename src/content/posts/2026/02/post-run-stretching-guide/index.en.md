> **Warning:**
>

## 💡 Author's Commentary (Insight & How to Use)

The true beauty of this prompt lies in **clearly letting the AI recognize your 'available tools' and 'current pain areas'**. Searching for "post-run stretches" on portals or video platforms yields generic results that don't even consider if you have a foam roller or a massage gun at home. However, using this prompt allows you to filter and construct **immediately executable custom movements tailored to the tools in your room**.

Additionally, entering your **running pace (e.g., 4-min/km vs. 7-min/km) and distance** is a crucial point. This is because the AI can determine if your run today was in the high-intensity anaerobic (interval) zone or the aerobic (jogging) zone, and accordingly recommend different movements—either focused on lactic acid breakdown or joint mobility recovery. Try repeating this routine for a few days and continue the conversation: "Thanks to the foam roller massage you suggested yesterday, the knee stiffness is much better. Today, add a new routine focused on opening up my hips and pelvis." You'll have a perfect personal physician by your side.

The core structure I poured the most effort into when designing this prompt is the **'Separation of actions by location'**. Often, runners finish an outdoor run, and with heavy breathing and wobbly legs, they trudge back home. If you're recommended static stretches that require lying down or kneeling at that moment, it's environmentally impossible and drains your will to act. That's why I explicitly instructed to separate **"Dynamic cooldowns that can be done immediately while standing outdoors"** and **"Static care using tools after returning home."** This design ensures you don't miss the golden time for recovery while your body temperature is still high, allowing you to immediately release muscle tension using a bench or wall, and naturally transition into deep muscle relaxation before your post-run shower.

The anatomical term filtering included in the **Constraints** at the bottom of the prompt is also a vital tip for maximizing readability. If you give the conversational AI a highly professional persona like 'Head Rehabilitation Trainer,' the AI tends to overuse complex medical and anatomical terms like "Perform myofascial release on the gastrocnemius and soleus" or "Apply strong pressure to the trigger point of the tensor fasciae latae" to show off its knowledge. Facing such unfamiliar words when exhausted from training only adds stress before you can even visualize the stretch. That's why I added the strong constraint: **"Explain using analogies easy for a layperson to understand (e.g., like wringing out a towel)."** The output becomes much friendlier and more intuitive.

Finally, for the `[Available Tools]` variable in this prompt, you don't necessarily have to list expensive foam rollers or massage guns. Feel free to enter whatever tools are around you: "doorframe pull-up bar, resistance band, baseball, thick book." You'll witness the AI's amazing ability to adapt in ways you never imagined. Break away from the boring, repetitive national gymnastic-style finishes and prepare for tomorrow's safe run with a **hyper-personalized recovery routine** that perfectly fits your body's condition and environment. The true completion of a run begins the moment you untie your laces.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: My pain is so severe I can't walk. Can I still use this prompt?**
  - A: Absolutely not. Stretching is for relieving light 'stiffness' or 'muscle fatigue.' If you have acute pain to the point where it's hard to bear weight, do not ask the AI; visit an orthopedic or rehabilitation specialist immediately.

- **Q: Can this be used after cycling or hiking?**
  - A: Of course! If you modify the running record in the Context to `[4-hour round-trip hike at Mt. Bukhan]` and the specific issues to `[sore quads and calves]`, it will design a perfect recovery routine for hikers.

- **Q: I'm afraid I'll get bored if it recommends the same movements every day.**
  - A: Just add one line to the Task section: "Include 2 new variation movements that don't overlap with yesterday." You can experience different types of stimulation every day.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Cross-Targeting of Situation and Constraints:** By using pace and distance to infer exercise intensity and tool availability to force only immediately feasible answers.
2. **Action Separation by Location:** By clearly dividing 'Outdoor (Standing)' and 'Indoor (Lying/Tools)', the flow is designed so a runner can practice the recovery routine without interruption from the moment they finish their exercise until they shower.
3. **Professional Term Filtering (Constraints):** Assigning a rehab trainer persona often causes the AI to overuse difficult muscle names. To prevent this, 'Easy explanation through analogies' is forced to maximize readability.
