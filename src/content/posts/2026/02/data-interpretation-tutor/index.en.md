## üöÄ Solution: "Data Insight Miner"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, high-level overview of your data.

> **Role:** You are a `[Senior Data Analyst]`.
> **Task:** Analyze the provided `[chart/table data]` and extract the most critical insights and potential statistical traps.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this for deep, presentation-ready analysis with granular details. (Image upload or data paste required).

> **Role:** You are a `[Chief Data Officer and Expert Statistician]`.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I need to use this `[chart/table/dataset]` for an upcoming executive report.
> - Goal: I cannot just skim the surface. I need to uncover the real story the data is telling and anticipate tough questions.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **[Fact Check]**: Pinpoint the most significant data points: the absolute maximums, minimums, and rapid changes (spikes/drops). You must cite specific numbers.
> 2. **[Deep Insight]**: Provide exactly 3 actionable insights this data suggests. Do not simply list facts; interpret the underlying business or strategic meaning.
> 3. **[Trap Caution]**: Scrutinize the visual representation. Are the axes distorted? Is the scale misleading? Identify any potential areas where the audience might misinterpret the data.
> 4. **[Anticipated Question]**: Formulate 1 sharp, critical question that a skeptical executive or professor would ask upon seeing this data.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Absolutely do not use vague, obvious phrases like "generally increased" or "went down over time."
> - You must quote specific figures and percentages from the data.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - If the data provided is insufficient to draw a conclusion, state "Insufficient data" rather than hallucinating a trend.

## üìä Proof: Before & After

### ‚ùå Before (Human Eye)

"Hmm... it looks like our sales peaked in Q3 of 2024. That's good, I guess."

### ‚úÖ After (Prompt Result)

**[Fact Check]**

- In Q3 2024, sales **surged 45%** quarter-over-quarter, reaching an all-time high of $1.2M.
- Conversely, the operating profit margin **declined significantly** from 15% to 8% during that exact same period.

**[Insight]**

1. **Volume Over Value:** While top-line revenue grew dramatically, it failed to translate into profitability, likely due to aggressive, high-cost marketing acquisition strategies.
2. **Cyclical Vulnerability:** A recurring pattern shows sales dropping consistently in Q4 across the past three years, indicating a need for proactive, off-season revenue strategies.

**[Trap Caution]**

- **Misleading Axis:** The Y-axis of the revenue graph begins at $800k, not $0. This visually exaggerates minor sales fluctuations, making the recent Q4 dip look like a catastrophic crash rather than a mild seasonal correction.

**[Anticipated Question]**
"Our sales are up, but our profit margins are halved. Have we analyzed the actual ROI of our recent promotional campaigns?"
