_"Write just 3 things you are thankful for today. ...You say nothing comes to mind?"_

We are sensitive to what we lack or don't have, but insensitive to what we already have. A gratitude journal is a training to turn the brain's attention from 'deficiency' to 'fulfillment'. It's hard at first, but it becomes much easier if AI finds materials next to you and asks questions.

> **Context:** I want to write a gratitude journal, but I don't know what to write because nothing special happened today. Things I did today are `[I almost was late because I woke up late in the morning, ate Kimchi stew for lunch, and came sitting on the bus on the way home]`.

>
> 1. Excavate **3 specific gratitude points** that can be found in my daily routine. (Re-illuminate things taken for granted.)
> 2. To feel this gratitude deeper, ask a **'What if this didn't exist?'** subjunctive question for each.
> 3. Give one 'small gratitude mission' I can practice for tomorrow.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Take out too instructive or religious feelings.
> - Write in a pleasant and light tone.
