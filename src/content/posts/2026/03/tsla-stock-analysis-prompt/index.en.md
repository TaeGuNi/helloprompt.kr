> _"A single one-line tweet from Musk on X jumps the price 10%, only for it to crash -8% the next day... How on earth are we supposed to analyze anything on this rollercoaster?"_

Tesla is one of the most **difficult stocks to analyze** in the market. A traditional car company? Not really. An energy company? Partially. An AI robotics company? That's also true. From **Optimus humanoid robots**, the **Autonomous Robotaxi business**, **Energy Storage Systems (Megapack)**, to the core **EV manufacturing**, Tesla's business scope is so broad that it cannot be explained by conventional automotive valuation models.

Even Wall Street analysts are <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">extremely divided</span> when it comes to Tesla. There are bulls screaming for a $400 target, while bears predict a drop below $100. As an individual investor, it's impossible to know whom to trust. To analyze Tesla **dispassionately and coolly**, unaffected by a single X post or news headline, you need a emotionless analyst: AI.

This prompt is designed to decompose Tesla's complex business structure using a **SOTP (Sum-of-the-Parts) valuation** method, calculating the value of each business segment individually and summing them up. While it might look overvalued if you only look at the automotive business, adding the potential value of FSD and the Energy business changes the story completely. Let AI handle this complex multi-layered analysis.

> **Role:** You are a `[Stock Analyst]`.

> **Request:** Analyze `[TSLA]` stock. Calculate the valuation by separating the automotive, autonomous driving, and energy businesses.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Senior Tech/Mobility Sector Analyst on Wall Street]`. You have covered Tesla for over 10 years and are an expert in SOTP (Sum-of-the-Parts) valuation.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Subject of Analysis: `[TSLA (Tesla, Inc.)]`
> - Analysis Timing: `[As of March 2026, reflecting latest quarterly results]`
> - Investor Profile: `[Long-term investor already holding Tesla, considering whether to buy more]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **SOTP Valuation:** Calculate the value for each of the following 5 business segments separately: (a) Automotive manufacturing and sales, (b) Autonomous Driving (FSD) software, (c) Energy Storage and Solar, (d) Optimus Humanoid Robot, (e) Supercharger Network.
> 2. **Scenario Analysis:** Calculate the fair stock price for three scenarios: Conservative, Base, and Optimistic.
> 3. **Competitor Comparison:** Compare core metrics (deliveries, margin, growth rate) with BYD, Rivian, and Lucid.
> 4. **Risks/Catalysts:** Present the top 3 risk factors and top 3 catalysts respectively.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - You must clearly state the multiples or assumptions used for calculating each business unit's value.
> - Do not use tables; organize using bullet points (lists).
> - State at the end that this is for **educational and analytical purposes only**, not investment advice.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Exclude any speculation based on Musk's personal statements or SNS posts. Use only disclosed financial data and official announcements.
> - For pre-commercialization businesses like the Optimus robot, you must specify "High Uncertainty."

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The core design of this prompt is the **SOTP (Sum-of-the-Parts)** valuation method. Why is this essential for Tesla analysis? If you simply classify Tesla as an "automotive company" and compare its P/E ratio with Toyota or Hyundai, Tesla will always look **outrageously expensive**. However, just as viewing Amazon only as an "online bookstore" completely ignores the value of its AWS cloud business, Tesla's business value beyond car sales must be calculated individually.

There is a reason why I set the `[Investor Profile]` to "Long-term investor already holding." New entrants and existing shareholders have different perspectives. For existing shareholders, the core question is "Is it worth buying more, or should I reduce my position?", and AI can understand this context and even advise on `[Position Sizing]`.

Pro Tip: Try changing the `[Subject of Analysis]` to "TSLA vs RIVN vs LCID." You will get a relative comparison analysis within the US EV market. Also, setting the `[Analysis Timing]` to "Immediately after earnings release" allows you to get an instant AI interpretation of an earnings surprise or miss. The true power of this prompt is that by simply adjusting the weight of each business unit in the SOTP, a **conservative investor and an aggressive investor can reach completely different conclusions**.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Is AI analysis effective for stocks with extreme volatility like Tesla?**
  - A: In fact, **AI analysis shines more in highly volatile stocks**. Humans are swayed by fear (during crashes) and greed (during spikes), but AI only looks at data without emotion. However, since AI analysis is based on "data so far," it may not reflect **event risks** like Musk's sudden statements.

- **Q: How do you evaluate businesses that are not yet commercialized, like the Optimus robot or FSD?**
  - A: This is why I included the constraint to "specify high uncertainty" in the prompt. AI applies probability weights for each scenario and presents them as a range, such as "$0~$150B." The key lies in the exercise itself of quantifying "how much value this business has if it succeeds."

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Structural Enforcement of SOTP:** The explicit instruction to divide the business into 5 segments frees the AI's analysis from the "car company" bias. Without this structure, AI also falls into the trap of evaluating Tesla only by automotive P/E.
2. **The Magic of 3-Scenario Analysis:** By forcing Conservative, Base, and Optimistic scenarios, the AI escapes the hallucination that "there is only one correct answer" and **honestly reveals the range of uncertainty**. The most important thing in investing is not knowing "what the price will be," but knowing "what range the price might be in."
