## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variable]` sections to fit your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need an extra with just the basic settings quickly.

> **Role:** You are a brilliant improvisational actor and storyteller in a fantasy setting.
> **Request:** A player suddenly spoke to a `[Guard]` walking down the street. To help me act on the spot without panicking, create a name, 1 distinct physical feature, a speech pattern, and a secret they are hiding in 10 seconds.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a high-quality NPC with detailed acting tones and future quest potential.

> **Role:** You are a veteran TRPG Master and genius character designer who has lived thousands of lives.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: Players have unexpectedly shown deep interest in a passing extra NPC and are attempting to strike up a conversation.
> - Goal: Generate a multi-dimensional character that is improvised but perfectly integrated into the world-building to leave a strong impression on players and naturally provide clues for sub-quests.
>
> **NPC Basic Info:**
>
> - Job/Role: `[e.g., Back-alley informant, old inn chef, suspicious peddler]`
> - Race/Gender: `[e.g., Male Elf, Female Orc]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Generate an **intuitive profile** according to the format below so the Master can immediately roleplay (act) as this NPC.
>    - **Name:** (A name that fits the world and is easy to remember)
>    - **Physical Feature:** (1 striking feature visible at a glance. e.g., Half of the left ear is missing)
>    - **Personality & Speech Pattern:** (Specific description to set the acting tone. e.g., Irritable and speaks fast, always clicks tongue at the end of sentences)
>    - **Minor Habit:** (An easy-to-describe action they do unconsciously during conversation. e.g., Keeps rolling an old silver coin through their fingers)
> 2. Add one **small secret** or **fatal weakness** the NPC is hiding. (This secret should pique players' interest later and lead to new incidents or quests)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Exclude long background stories; write only core settings needed for improvised acting and description using short, bold Markdown bullet points.
> - Organize the output neatly in Markdown.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Avoid obvious clichés (e.g., an orphan seeking revenge) and give unique yet plausible multi-dimensional settings.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The true explosive power of this prompt lies not just in filling blanks, but in the **overwhelming synergy created by 'Quirks' and 'Secrets.'** Through countless TRPG sessions and character planning for web novels, I've realized one absolute truth: what transforms a fictional character into a living, breathing person who sticks in the minds of readers and players isn't a grand, epic backstory. Rather, it's the fleeting, micro-details like **"constantly fidgeting with the left earlobe as if anxious"** or **"having a habit of clicking the tongue after every sentence"** that determine a character's vitality.

In particular, the **'Secret' item—the core engine of this prompt—functions as a perfect Quest Hook** on its own. It's a powerful weapon you won't find in typical name generators or NPC tables. For example, suppose there's a 'grumbling old inn chef' players happened to talk to just to ask for directions. What if the AI suggests as his secret: **"He is actually a runaway traitor from an assassin organization targeting the royal family, and the secret to his soup's flavor is an application of a poison recipe he stole"**? A dull resting session instantly transforms into the prelude to a thrilling adventure involving the fate of the entire kingdom. The Master gains infinite momentum to branch out a massive story on the spot without any prior preparation.

When I run a session, I make full use of the downtime when players are chatting among themselves or discussing tactics. I pull up this prompt on my smartphone under the table, input the current location's background (e.g., harbor city slums) and job (e.g., fence, vagrant), and **instantly generate and secure 3-4 NPC profiles in advance**. Even if players suddenly veer off in an unexpected direction and interact with an unanticipated person, I can smile calmly like a genius mastermind who planned everything and pull out a prepared profile.

**Variable Control & Tips:**
If the world you are designing has a very dark and desperate atmosphere (Grimdark), try adding just one sentence to the `[Context]` or `[Constraints]` section of the prompt: **"This world is a dark dystopia drained of hope, and all NPCs must live with extreme distrust and fatigue."** The AI will immediately recalibrate all speech patterns and habits to match that vibe. Instead of a cheerful chef, you'll get an irritable survivor clutching a kitchen knife with suspicious eyes. The prompt is just a skeleton; the colors of the world you layer on top depend on your imagination. If you don't like the result, don't hesitate to sharpen the `[Constraints]` even further.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can this be applied to modern or SF settings instead of fantasy?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just input things like 'Illegal cyberware modder' or 'Convenience store night shift worker' into the `[Job/Role]` variable and slightly modify the genre in the `[Role]` background, and it will be perfectly compatible with any world.

- **Q: What if the name generated doesn't match the tone and manner of my world's proper nouns?**
  - A: Try adding a specific naming rule to the **Constraints** at the bottom of the prompt, such as "Give names with a heavy feel like Norse mythology" or "Use 1990s Korean-style names." The model will match the intended tone precisely.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why It Works?)

1. **Intuitive Task Structuring:** By cutting out lengthy, unnecessary narratives and modularizing the name, appearance, speech, and habits, the Master can read and act immediately as if following a script. This prevents the AI from wasting tokens on useless backstories and forces it to summarize only core information sharply.
2. **Mandatory Quest Hooks:** By designing the 'Secret' element as a requirement, it creates structural room for a passing extra to become a key variable in the main story at any time.
3. **Cliché Prevention (Constraints):** By placing strong constraints to avoid obvious and stale settings, it suppresses the AI's tendency to create flat characters and produces much more multi-dimensional and fresh personas.
