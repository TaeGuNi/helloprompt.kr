## 🚀 How Real Experts Write Them

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variables]` to put it to work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version (Standard)

Use this without hesitation when you need a quick story to read tonight.

> **Role:** You are a child sleep therapist and meditation guide.
> 
> **Request:** Write a 5-minute sleep induction story for a 5-year-old child that naturally incorporates muscle relaxation and deep breathing techniques. The theme is 'A Trip on a Cloud Train.'

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you want to create a one-of-a-kind custom sleep story that perfectly reflects your child's age, personality, and favorite motifs.

> **Role (Role):** You are a child sleep therapist with 10 years of experience and a meditation guide with a gentle voice.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Target: A `[5]`-year-old child named `[Ji-hoon]` who is lying in bed but tossing and turning.
> - Background: On top of a warm and cozy bed in my room.
> - Goal: To have the child naturally release physical tension and fall into a deep sleep while listening to the story.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Write a sleep induction story based on the theme of '`[A Cloud Train to Cozy Cotton Candy Village]`.'
> 2. Throughout the story, naturally describe 'Progressive Muscle Relaxation' techniques (releasing tension from toes to head) at a child's eye level. (e.g., "Shall we let your toes go soft and loose? They're becoming light like a cloud.")
> 3. Actively use drowsy and soft words that stimulate sight, sound, and touch (e.g., cozy, softly, warm, fluffy, gentle).
> 4. Since this is a script for a parent to read, write it in a friendly, gentle colloquial style as if speaking.
> 5. Appropriately place ellipses (...) and commas (,) between sentences so that the reading speed can be adjusted.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Never include elements that could wake or alert the child, such as villains, conflict, loud noises, or scary descriptions.
> - Adjust the length so that the total reading time is about 5 to 7 minutes when read slowly (around 1,000 characters including spaces).
> - Do not use tables for mobile readability; organize it in a highly readable text format.
> - Be sure to **bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - State that this cannot replace medical advice and answer only in the form of a story.
> - Do not make up information that is not certain; answer "I don't know" if unsure. (Anti-hallucination)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

When reading commercial storybooks in a real parenting setting, it's common for a child to wake up fully because they are curious about what happens next. While colorful pictures and dynamic stories are excellent materials for daytime activities, they act as **one of the strongest sleep disruptors** at night when sleep should be induced. The true value of this prompt we designed does not lie in creating a grand **'Narrative'** with a clear beginning, middle, and end. Rather, its core purpose is to slow down the child's brainwaves and lead to physical **'Sensory Relaxation'** through strictly calculated language.

The first thing to note is the precision of **Variable Control.** Variables like the age `[5]` and name `[Ji-hoon]` in the Pro version prevent the AI from generating generic stories for an unspecified audience. When a child's name is repeated gently, they feel more deeply immersed and secure. Additionally, the theme variable `[A Cloud Train to Cozy Cotton Candy Village]` can be modified infinitely. If your child likes dinosaurs, try changing it to **"A Warm Hot Spring Bath with a Baby Brachiosaurus,"** or if they like cars, try **"A Fluffy Camper Van Trip Following the Starlight."** By cleverly bringing motifs your child is passionate about into the realm of sleep therapy, you can minimize resistance and lead them gently to dreamland.

Furthermore, I strongly recommend using top-tier reasoning models like **GPT-4o** or **Claude 3.5 Sonnet**, which have outstanding literary description and emotional vocabulary selection. These models are incredible at producing perfect **read-aloud scripts** that gently stimulate the imagination while soothing the mind and body, with phrases like "The clouds are tickling your cheeks" or "The smell of warm cocoa is wafting by." Synesthetic descriptions encompassing sight, sound, smell, and touch are highly effective in turning off the brain's tension switch and activating the parasympathetic nervous system.

Finally, when applying the generated results to the actual parenting scene, the parent's **'reading skill'** is more important than anything else. Even the most perfectly generated sleep induction script will lose its effectiveness if read quickly in a high pitch. Be sure to pause for about 1-2 seconds and take a deep breath at the **ellipses (`...`)** intentionally inserted into the script. You should lower your voice tone by one or two notches from your usual speaking voice and read slowly, whisper-like, and rhythmically. When your steady heartbeat and calm voice waves are transmitted to your child, the true magic of this AI prompt will activate, and your child's heavy eyelids will begin to close. To achieve successful sleep independence and a peaceful parenting clock-out, use this cheat code tonight!
