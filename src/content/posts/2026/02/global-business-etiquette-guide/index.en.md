## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this right before boarding to quickly check only the most important core points.

> **Role:** You are a 'Global Business Etiquette Expert' with 20 years of experience.
> 
> **Task:** I have a meeting with a client in `[Country Name]` tomorrow. Clearly summarize 3 core manners that determine the first impression and 1 fatal mistake that must never be committed.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a perfect simulation tailored to the local situation ahead of a multi-million dollar contract.

> **Role:** You are a 'Cross-Culture Business Consultant' with 20 years of experience coaching Fortune 500 executives for overseas business.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Visiting Country: `[e.g., United Arab Emirates]`
> - Meeting Purpose: `[e.g., Proposal for new IT solution implementation and partnership discussion]`
> - Counterparty Info: `[e.g., Conservative male executive in his 50s]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **First Impression Strategy:** Describe in detail the correct greeting (handshake strength, level of eye contact, angle of bow, etc.) and the business card exchange process.
> 2. **Ice-breaking:** Recommend 3 'safe and likable small talk topics' that can lower the other party's guard.
> 3. **Red Flags (Taboos):** Strongly warn of 3 'fatal mistakes (actions, gestures, words)' that must never be done in that country's business culture.
> 4. **Sense of Time & Negotiation Style:** Clearly explain the perception of appointment times (arriving exactly on time vs. 10 minutes early) and whether communication should be indirect or direct (High/Low Context).
> 5. **Gift & Dining Etiquette:** Suggest etiquette for meals after the meeting and appropriate gift items (including gifts to avoid).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Focus on 'practitioner-level tips' used in actual business rather than general Wikipedia-level information.
> - To maximize readability, emphasize each item using Markdown bullet points and **bold text**.
> - Do not make up cultural information that is uncertain; clearly state it as "may vary by individual."

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The true power of this prompt lies not just in telling you 'how to greet' or 'surface-level etiquette,' but in its ability to perfectly control the other party's **unique negotiation style (High/Low Context cultural characteristics)** and the **fatal taboos (Red Flags)** that must never be violated. I will share in detail the overwhelming advantages of using this prompt in practice and the know-how for controlling prompt variables.

**1. The Value of 'Red Flags (Taboos)' That Wikipedia Doesn't Tell You**
The scariest thing about overseas business trips is the fact that 'you don't even know what you don't know.' An action you took to be helpful can be the worst insult in the other person's culture. For example, in a meeting with a Middle Eastern client, casually handing over a business card with your left hand or warmly asking about the health of the client's wife is a fatal mistake that immediately shatters all deals and trust. The core of this prompt is in the `Red Flags (Taboos)` section. By going beyond asking AI "what to do" and forcing it to output **"what specifically never to do,"** you fundamentally block irreversible diplomatic and business catastrophes caused by cultural ignorance. Reading this result just once on a plane has the effect of hedging risks worth millions.

**2. The Magic of Variable Control: Detail in `[Counterparty Info]` is Key**
To utilize this prompt 100%, you must inject as much specific and detailed context as possible into the `[Counterparty Info]` variable. Entering 'American buyer' vs. '30-something Indian-American CTO of an IT startup located in Silicon Valley' results in a completely different dimension of insights from the AI.

- **Bad Example:** `[Counterparty Info]: Japanese company employee`
- **Good Example:** `[Counterparty Info]: 60-something procurement executive from a conservative manufacturing company with a 50-year tradition based in Osaka`

The more details you provide—such as the person's rank, age group, company orientation (conservative/innovative), and place of origin—the more the AI discards generalities and derives sharp, 'practically impactful' insights comparable to those of a top-tier consultant. Try filling this variable by referring to the person's LinkedIn profile or previous email history.

**3. Small Talk Dominates the Business**
Many Korean professionals tend to open their laptops and show PPTs to get straight to the point as soon as they sit down. However, in global business—especially in Latin America, the Middle East, and Southern Europe—it's no exaggeration to say that this ice-breaking time *is* the business. The requirement for `3 safe and likable small talk topics` included in the prompt allows you to perfectly dominate those awkward 5 minutes. If the AI recommends a topic that touches on recent positive issues or cultural pride that only locals would know, rather than obvious talk about the weather, the other party's firm guard will melt away, and control of the negotiation table will naturally shift to you.
