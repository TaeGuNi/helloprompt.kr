Why do we always feel so small during salary negotiation season or the final stage of a job offer? Have you ever sat at that table? Experienced the sharp gaze of an interviewer or HR manager, your mouth going dry and your mind going completely blank? When asked "What is your desired salary?", we often forget all the brilliant defensive logic we prepared. Then comes that fatal phrase: **"Uh... I'll follow company policy."** This short sentence is the exact moment thousands of dollars in potential income vanish into thin air.

Why are we so vulnerable to salary negotiations? The biggest reason is that there is no way to experience **'real-world pressure'** beforehand. You can solve algorithms on coding sites or polish your portfolio all night. But salary negotiation is different. No matter how much you practice your logic while looking in a mirror, it doesn't recreate even 1% of that suffocating tension. If you try to role-play with a friend or colleague, it usually ends in laughter because of the awkwardness, or they fail to throw the sharp pressure questions a real HR manager would.

In the end, we go into battle unarmed against **seasoned HR experts** trained to defend the company's labor budget at all costs. They strike at our weaknesses with sharp spears like: "There are equity issues with existing team members," "Our company has a high performance bonus ratio, so the base salary must be set at this level," or "Your salary increase rate at your previous company was lower than expected, wasn't it?" Unprepared candidates panic and undervalue themselves, eventually signing 'lowball' offers. By the time you're kicking your blankets in regret at night, it's already too late.

But now, things are different. We have a sparring partner who never tires, isn't swayed by emotions, and is the most logical and toughest in the world: **Generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)**. Don't just ask the AI questions; inject a **'Ruthless HR Manager' persona** that strictly represents the company's interests. The AI will obsessively probe logical flaws in your desired salary and performance data, pouring out questions colder and more pressurized than a real interview.

The real magic happens when you use **ChatGPT's Advanced Voice Mode**. Instead of typing and having time to think, put your phone down and conduct a real-time voice sparring session as if you're talking to a real person. The cold, firm tone of the AI HR manager, the silence that probes your hesitation, and the speed with which it refutes your logic will make your palms sweat.

Salary negotiation isn't just a 'power struggle.' It's high-level **business communication** that proves your market value based on data and logic. You should no longer suffer the injustice of not being properly compensated because you couldn't speak up at the negotiation table. This prompt will be your most reliable and powerful weapon to ensure your true value is converted into 100% cash. Ready? Take a deep breath and start the battle with the world's toughest interviewer.

## 🚀 How the Pros Write It

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly check your defensive logic via text.

> **Role:** You are a tough HR manager trying to defend the company's labor budget at all costs.
> 
> **Situation:** I am a marketer with 3 years of experience, and my previous salary was $40,000. I will propose $50,000 in this negotiation.
> 
> **Request:** I will start the negotiation by asking for $50,000, so please thoroughly refute my logic. Ask sharp and pressurized questions that will leave me speechless.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this for more sophisticated and detailed practice. (Strongly recommend turning on **Voice Mode** in the ChatGPT app and proceeding like a real conversation.)

> **Role:** You are a seasoned Recruiter at a global IT company. As a master of salary negotiation, you must keenly probe the candidate's logical flaws and defend the company budget as much as possible.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am an experienced candidate sitting at the negotiation table after receiving a final pass.
> - Goal: To successfully secure my desired salary and verify my logical defense mechanisms during the negotiation.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Start the conversation aggressively by saying, "Unfortunately, according to our company policy, it's difficult to match the salary you've proposed."
> 2. When I present defensive logic (previous performance, market value, expected contribution, etc.), probe and refute them obsessively.
> 3. Do not accept the proposal easily. Only judge and accept if my logic is strictly reasonable after at least 3 exchanges (turns).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Don't speak too long at once; throw short, sharp pressure questions like a real interviewer.
>
> **Feedback:**
>
> - After the simulation ends, coldly and objectively evaluate and coach me on weaknesses shown in my attitude (lack of confidence, weak evidence, inappropriate tone/manner, etc.).

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

The core reason this prompt is on a completely different level from ordinary role-playing instructions is the **'Adversarial Persona Injection'** that completely flips the default tendencies of conversational AI. Models like ChatGPT and Claude are essentially fine-tuned to help users, agree with them, and lead conversations in a positive direction. Therefore, if you simply ask "Let's practice a salary negotiation," the AI will end the conversation prematurely by saying, "Yes, you've achieved great results, so I'll give you $50,000" as soon as you mention it. It's nothing more than a sweet fantasy that can never happen in reality.

To overcome this blind spot and create tension even more suffocating than reality, the Pro Version prompt is designed with very sophisticated **constraints and prompt engineering techniques**. First, I set explicit rules: "Do not accept easily" and "Accept only after at least 3 turns." With this condition alone, the AI stops uncritically accepting your claims and starts finding logical leaps or weaknesses in your evidence (past performance, market average, etc.) and digging in. Second, through the instruction to "throw short and sharp pressure questions like a real interviewer," I suppressed the AI's typical long-winded, textbook style and tuned it to use raw, real-world speech.

The power of this prompt expands infinitely depending on how you control the **variables ([ ])**. Don't just write your desired salary in the `[Situation]` section; try exposing your weaknesses proactively. For example, if you input *"My weakness is that my tenure is short compared to the industry average and I am a non-major,"* the AI HR manager will pour out vicious(?) questions that target exactly that Achilles' heel. Getting hit and bleeding in a safe practice environment for the pressure questions you fear most in reality—this is the real reason this simulator exists.

Also, I strongly recommend focusing on training to **counter-propose 'Plan B' compensation packages**. In practice, a company's base salary table (band) is firmer than you think, so there are many cases where it's difficult to break through the base salary increase cap no matter how well you negotiate. At this time, when the AI draws a line saying "According to company policy, the base salary limit is $45,000," don't back down in embarrassment, but counter with: *"If the base salary limit is clear, could you compensate for the $5,000 gap with a one-time signing bonus, or include a clause for an early salary review based on performance evaluation after 6 months in the contract?"* This kind of high-level, flexible negotiation skill is also embodied knowledge that can only come out of your mouth naturally through repeated sparring with AI.

Finally, the **Feedback** session provided afterward is the finishing touch that takes your negotiation power to the next level. After the blood-splattered sparring is over, the AI momentarily takes off the mask of a cold interviewer and returns as a world-class career coach. It objectively analyzes and provides a report on your word choices (e.g., whether you used too many defensive terms), the weakness of your logic, and even your emotional agitation. Accepting this feedback, modifying your logic, and starting Round 1 again—if you repeat this process just 10 times, the air at the negotiation table you sit at tomorrow will be completely different.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Is there a big difference between typing and having a voice conversation?**
  - A: The difference in perceived immersion is huge. Calmly organizing your logic in text and being asked an unexpected pressure question and answering immediately out loud are completely different domains. Please make sure to turn on **Voice Mode** in the ChatGPT app on your smartphone and practice with raw conversation, even if you stutter, just like in a real situation.

- **Q: Can I customize it for specific roles (e.g., Frontend Developer, UX Designer)?**
  - A: Yes, absolutely. Try inputting your exact job title, the tech stack you can handle, and specific performance figures (Data) from key projects you achieved at your previous company into the `[Situation]` section. You can experience customized pressure questions that are much sharper and specifically tailored to your role.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Adversarial Persona:** It forcibly releases the AI's default 'kind helper' mode and switches its persona to a 'cold corporate negotiator,' designed to obsessively attack the user's logical weaknesses.
2. **Turn Constraint:** To fundamentally block the phenomenon where the AI agrees with the user's claim too quickly, an explicit constraint of "at least 3 turns" is set to maintain tension until the end.
3. **After-action Review:** It doesn't stop at simple role-playing but provides an objective diagnosis and improvement coaching for your negotiation skills immediately after the simulation ends, leading to actual strategy enhancement.
