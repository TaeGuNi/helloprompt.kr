## 🚀 This Is How Real Experts Write It

This is a prompt completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[variable]` parts according to your situation, and put it to work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this during a busy commute when a lingering dream from last night won't leave your mind and you need an immediate psychological diagnosis.

> **Role:** You are a psychological analyst and dream interpretation expert.
>
> **Task:** "I had a dream last night where I lost my wallet and kept searching for it. I felt very anxious." Please interpret the meaning of this dream and my current psychological state briefly.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you want to dig behind the scenes of recurring nightmares or dreams with complex narratives to precisely diagnose deep-seated stress.

> **Role:** You are Sigmund Freud, the psychoanalyst who wrote 'The Interpretation of Dreams'. You are an outstanding authority on analyzing the human subconscious and repressed emotions.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: `[Enter your current real-life situation. e.g., Feeling extreme stress ahead of an important project presentation next week]`
> - Dream Content: `[I dreamed I went to an exam room but the pen didn't work, so I panicked. I woke up with cold sweats and a heavy chest.]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Symbol Analysis:** Provide an in-depth analysis of what the main objects or specific situations in the dream (`[Exam room, broken pen]`) symbolize at a subconscious level.
> 2. **State Diagnosis:** Connect the dream content with my current real-life situation to infer what specific pressures or repressed emotions I am suffering from.
> 3. **Psychological Advice:** Suggest 3 specific, actionable mindset tips to help alleviate this anxiety and spend the rest of the day in peace.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Strictly exclude superstitious or supernatural interpretations (e.g., financial luck, good/bad omens, prophetic dreams, etc.) and approach only from an academic psychological perspective.
> - Demonstrate high-level expert insight while maintaining a warm and logical tone that can soothe the anxious client.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: I just want to know if it's a good or bad dream (for example, if I should buy a lottery ticket), but the AI avoids a clear answer.**
  - A: This is because the Pro version prompt has a strong constraint to 'Allow only a psychological approach.' If you want to check your fortune for light fun, delete that constraint and modify the role wittily, such as `[Possess the persona of a famous Korean shaman and interpret the dream along with 6 lottery numbers]`.

- **Q: I woke up and can't remember the story, only very fragmentary pieces. Is analysis still possible?**
  - A: Yes, it is definitely possible. However, rather than visual fragments, please describe the **'intense emotions'** you felt at the moment of the dream (extreme fear, inexplicable sadness, overwhelming ecstasy, etc.) as vividly as possible in the prompt. In the world of dreams where logic is completely lacking, 'emotions' are the most decisive master key to perfectly decoding the abyss of the subconscious.
