## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The true destructive power of this prompt lies in its **'Exquisite Control of Physical Attributes,'** which goes completely beyond simple and superficial style designation. Most average users stop at giving AI vague and result-oriented instructions like "draw it like paper" or "do it in a paper art style."

However, the AI image generation model itself only handles the statistical arrangement of pixels; it does not understand the rough texture of paper existing in the real world or the refraction caused by light passing through media. Therefore, as experts, we must forcibly inject **virtual laws of physics and optical camera settings** into the AI's algorithm through the prompt. This is the key difference that determines high-end quality.

**The First Secret: Mathematical Design of Fine Textures and Shadows**

When you insert **fine and realistic physical features** that can only be observed in actual manual crafts—such as `Textured craft paper` or `Soft shadows between layers`—vivid artwork that feels like it’s popping off the screen is born.

Going beyond just visually separating flat layers, you must explicitly specify the main lighting angle as `Soft directional lighting` to generate natural and consistent shadows on each stacked paper surface. This consistent shadow direction, sculpted by text, is the scientific principle that creates a powerful illusion in the human brain to form a perfect 3D volume. How you design the light accounts for 80% of this prompt.

**The Second Secret: Granting Optical Properties to the Camera Lens**

Additionally, the optical characteristics of the camera lens, such as **`Depth of field`** and **`Macro photography`**, are the hidden cheats and the finishing touch that elevate this prompt to irreplaceable high-end quality. The paper crafts we encounter in daily life are essentially small 'miniatures' that fit in the palm of your hand.

Even if the subject you are describing is a massive mountain range or a grand medieval wall, try forcibly applying the shallow depth of field (the phenomenon where the background outside the narrow range of focus becomes blurred) characteristic of close-up miniature photography. The human brain, upon seeing the blurred periphery, subconsciously thinks, "Ah, this is a very small paper model sitting on a table in the real world." Only when supported by such subtle and meticulous optical direction can you completely escape the uncanny valley of artificial 3D renderings that look like plastic toys.

**The Third Secret: Two Absolute Principles for Controlling Practical Variables**

When you modify this prompt in practice to freely control `[Variables]`, please keep these two absolute principles in mind.

First, **extreme restriction of the color palette**. Analog paper craft is inherently difficult to mix and use infinite colors like digital prints. Therefore, the more you strictly limit the total number of colors to around 3 or 4—using keywords like `Pastel color palette` or `Monochrome warm tones`—the more the refined beauty and luxury unique to analog crafts will come alive. Allowing too many colors will immediately degrade the texture into something that looks like cheap plastic.

Second, **the mix-and-match of paper thickness and material**. If you want a slightly more rustic and cute fairy-tale feel, use the `Thick cardboard` keyword to create a sense of weight. Conversely, if you want delicate and elegant curves where light passes through luxuriously, try mixing in the `Translucent tracing paper` keyword. Changing just one material can appeal to a completely different target audience.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: The result looks like an ordinary 2D illustration rather than a 3D volume. How do I fix this?**
  - **A:** There is a high probability that the keywords controlling lighting and shadows are too weak. Try moving camera and lighting control keywords like `Soft directional lighting` and `Depth of field` to the front of the prompt to explicitly imprint the flow of light and sense of space on the AI. Instructing side lighting (`Side light`) instead of flat lighting is also a great alternative.

- **Q: The details of the subject are too fragmented, making it hard to intuitively recognize the shape.**
  - **A:** This is a common phenomenon when the geometric and angular characteristics of the `Origami` keyword are over-reflected. If you want a cozy illustration based on soft curves, it is recommended to boldly exclude `Origami` and focus all power on the `Layered paper cutout` attribute.
