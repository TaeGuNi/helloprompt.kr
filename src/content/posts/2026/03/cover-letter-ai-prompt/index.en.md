> _"I've been staring at a blinking cursor on a blank screen for three hours. What can I write in the 'Motivation' section to stand out among hundreds of candidates?"_

A self-introduction letter is one of the most **stressful tasks** in the job application process. What should I write for "Growth Process"? What if my "Motivation" sounds too generic? "Future Goals" need to be realistic yet ambitious... After agonizing over these questions for hours, many people fall into the temptation of slightly modifying **someone else's letter** found on the internet.

The problem is that recruiters read **hundreds of applications** a day. Eight out of ten letters start with something like, "I have been interested in IT since I was young." To catch a recruiter's eye, you need a letter that naturally integrates the <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">keywords the company wants</span> while letting your specific experiences shine.

If you ask AI correctly, you can create a **customized draft** in just 5 minutes that analyzes job posting keywords and structures your experience accordingly. Of course, you shouldn't submit an AI draft as-is. However, there is no tool more powerful for breaking through **Blank Page Syndrome** and establishing the structure and direction of your writing.

## 🚀 This Is How the Real Pros Write

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[variables]` in brackets to match your situation, and put it to work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Career Coach]`.
>
> **Request:** Write a self-introduction letter for the `[Kakao Data Engineer]` position. Summarize the Motivation, Core Competencies, and Future Goals in about 300 characters each.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Career Coach with 10 years of experience in corporate recruiting consulting]`. You have edited over 1,000 self-introductions and deeply understand which keywords and structures recruiters score highly.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Target Company: `[Kakao]`, Target Position: `[Data Engineer]`
> - Key Job Requirements: `[Experience in designing/operating large-scale data pipelines, proficiency in Spark/Kafka, team collaboration skills]`
> - My Experience: `[2 years at an O2O startup, built Spark batch pipelines, processed 50k daily order records, performed Kafka PoC]`
> - My Strengths: `[Habit of breaking down and solving problems, experience leading PoCs, ability to communicate with non-technical departments]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Job Posting Keyword Analysis:** Extract 5 key keywords from the job posting above and connect them to matching points in my experience.
> 2. **Motivation (300 characters):** Explain why this company and this position, connecting it to my experience. Start the first sentence with a number or a specific fact.
> 3. **Core Competencies (400 characters):** Structure my strongest experience using the STAR (Situation-Task-Action-Result) technique.
> 4. **Future Goals (300 characters):** Provide a specific roadmap for 6 months, 1 year, and 3 years after joining.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Never use cliché expressions like "Since I was young" or "I have always dreamed of."
> - Include specific numbers, technology names, and results in every sentence.
> - Strictly adhere to the character count for each section.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - This draft is generated by AI and must be verified and edited by the applicant based on their actual experience.
> - Do not include false career history or exaggerated achievements.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The core of this prompt is **"Job Posting Keyword Matching."** Because recruiters read hundreds of letters a day, they scan for **key keywords** within the first 30 seconds. Instead of saying "I have experience with Spark," writing "Reduced latency by 95% for 50,000 daily records using Spark Structured Streaming" will <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">instantly grab attention</span>.

The key is to honestly input your history into `[My Experience]`. AI cannot invent experiences, but it can rearrange the experiences you provide into the **most attractive structure and expression**. Even "working part-time at a cafe," when explained properly to the AI, can be transformed into a narrative about "realizing the importance of data-driven decision-making in customer service."

Pro tip: The most important part of the STAR technique is the **Result**. Recruiters trust results that can be **proven with numbers**, like "95% reduction in latency." Without numbers, even an AI-generated letter will stay at a "generic" level.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I submit the AI-written letter immediately?**
  - A: Absolutely not. An AI draft is a **starting point for structure and direction**. You must verify the facts with your actual experience and edit it to include your own voice and emotion. Since many companies now use AI detection tools, you should also filter out overly "AI-like" writing styles.

- **Q: Can new graduates with no work experience use this?**
  - A: Yes. Simply put your college projects, volunteer work, or club experiences into `[My Experience]`. AI can organize any experience persuasively using the STAR structure.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why It Works?)

1. **Enforced Keyword Matching:** By explicitly telling the AI to "extract keywords from the job posting," the letter becomes a 1:1 match with the requirements. This is the key difference between a "customized" and a "generic" letter.
2. **Structural Power of the STAR Technique:** By separating experience into S-T-A-R, the AI creates a **narrative structure** that explains "what judgment was made and what actions were taken under what circumstances, and what the result was," rather than just "I did this."
