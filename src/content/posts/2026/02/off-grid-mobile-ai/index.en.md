## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

Small local models (1.5B to 8B parameters) running on smartphones naturally lack the ability to grasp subtle nuances or complex contexts compared to cloud models with hundreds of billions of parameters. Therefore, the only secret to extracting overwhelming quality is to **extremely simplify the AI's role and strictly control the output format using Markdown.** We have prepared the most practical 'Secure Document Summary Prompt' that you can copy and use immediately in the Off Grid app.

### 🥉 Basic Version (Standard)

Use this when you want to quickly scan text or documents while on the move and grasp the core points.

> **Role:** You are a fast and accurate 'Core Summarizer.'
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Summarize the key points of the following text in 3 lines.
> 2. List only the most important facts, excluding fillers or difficult language.
>
> **Content:** `[Enter text or document content here]`

### 🥇 Pro Version (Professional)

Use this for meeting minutes, secure contracts, or core project plans that require multi-layered and structured analysis. Its power is doubled when combined with Off Grid's file attachment feature.

> **Role:** You are a 'Confidential Document Analyst' with the highest level of security clearance.
>
> **Context:**
> - Background: I am in a completely offline environment and need to quickly understand this important document to make critical decisions.
> - Goal: To accurately extract the document's core arguments, potential risks, and future action items in a structured manner.
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Clearly define the main topic of the document in a single sentence.
> 2. Compress the main risks or opportunities found in the document to a maximum of two items.
> 3. Derive up to three 'Action Items (To-Do)' that must be implemented immediately.
> 4. Analyze the `[Document Content]` section to derive the results.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - The output format must strictly follow the Markdown syntax presented in the 'Output Format' below.
> - Do **not** arbitrarily infer or invent content not specified in the document (No Hallucination). Boldly omit uncertain information.
> - For mobile readability, never use tables; instead, organize information using high-readability bulleted lists.
> - Ensure important keywords are marked in **bold**.
>
> **Output Format:**
> - **Topic:** 
> - **Key Insights:** (Write concisely with bullet points)
> - **Action Items:** (Write in checklist format)
>
> **Document Content:** `[Paste the text of the confidential document to be analyzed here]`

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

Handling AI on a mobile device while offline requires a meticulous approach that is on a completely different level than using AI backed by massive cloud computing resources. After hardcore testing of the Off Grid app on the latest flagship device (equipped with Snapdragon 8 Gen 3), I found text generation speeds to be around 15-30 tokens per second—more than enough for professional use. What was most astonishing was the **overwhelming performance of offline image generation (taking only 5-10 seconds)** utilizing NPU acceleration and **Vision AI**, which instantly reads context from receipts or complex objects through the smartphone camera lens without internet. This was a shocking result that proved the infinite scalability of mobile devices, defying the inherent limitations and prejudices previously held against local AI.

However, there is one fatal pitfall to be wary of when dealing with mobile local LLMs: the **'Hallucination' phenomenon**, where the model completely forgets instructions midway or calmly reports non-existent facts. This stems from the fundamental structural limitations of ultra-lightweight models (sLLM) with 1.5B to 8B parameters. If you toss a vague request like "Summarize this nicely" as if you were talking to GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet, the local AI will almost certainly lose the context, get lost, and start writing a random fairy tale. Therefore, with smaller models, it is absolutely necessary to have **sophisticated prompting skills that use short, clear sentences and set dense, airtight constraints.**

If you look closely at the **Pro Version Prompt** provided earlier, the core know-how and philosophy for training these local models are woven throughout. The most important design point is the sharp separation of the 'Task' and 'Constraints,' which significantly reduces the model's cognitive load. In particular, the sentence **"Do not arbitrarily infer or invent content not specified in the document"** is not just a suggestion; it acts as a powerful system-level brake to stop a runaway train. Since small models tend not to recognize their own limitations, you must clearly provide an explicit exit strategy like "omit if uncertain" in the prompt to fundamentally block the generation of dangerous misinformation.

Furthermore, the magic key that explodes the quality of the final output is the **forced freezing of the output format.** In a mobile computing environment with strictly limited performance, giving the model aesthetic freedom in layout design is a luxury and a waste of resources. By building a solid Markdown-based structural skeleton in the prompt beforehand, the model doesn't waste precious NPU computing resources generating unnecessary greetings or introductions. It devotes 100% of its power to 'value-creation computation'—extracting meaning and summarizing the core. As a result, even within the limited hardware of a smartphone, you can effortlessly obtain a clean, perfectly structured senior-level report in just a few seconds, indistinguishable from one produced by a high-performance cloud server costing dozens of dollars.

When adapting this prompt for a real, high-pressure work environment, the key is to finely tune the `[Role]` and `[Context]` variables to your specific domain. For example, if you are in finance, change the role to a 'Security-Cleared Financial Analyst' and narrow the goal to 'Extracting hidden cost-reduction points.' Or, if you are in legal, set it as a 'Contract Risk Review Specialist' to target and extract only toxic clauses within a vast contract like a pair of tweezers. Depending on how sharply you sharpen the AI's persona according to the nature of the data you input, the offline AI dwelling in your smartphone will transform from a reliable junior assistant to a cold-headed senior consultant, performing its mission perfectly. If you internalize these prompt control techniques, an airplane cabin without internet will no longer be a space of isolation, but your own most perfect and productive private office.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Strict Format Enforcement:** Giving too much freedom to a local model with limited performance will cause the output quality to collapse. By strictly enforcing a Markdown structure for the 'Output Format,' the model's limited computational resources are focused entirely on 'maintaining format' and 'core summarization' instead of generating random sentences.
2. **Blocking the Source of Hallucinations:** I included a firm constraint: "Do not invent anything not in the document." This prevents the AI from making fatal errors like adding plausible lies (personal opinions) and forces it to stick 100% to extracting information based only on the source data.
3. **Injecting High-Density Context:** I explicitly injected the background that the current situation is an 'offline environment' into the prompt. With this one line of context, the model skips unnecessary introductions and elaborations, sharpening its output to the high-concentration essence (insights and action items) that a professional needs immediately.
