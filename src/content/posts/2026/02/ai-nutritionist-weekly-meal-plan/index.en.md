### ðŸ¥‡ Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need precise macro calculations, specific dietary constraints, and detailed shopping lists.

> **Role:** You are a clinical nutritionist and personal trainer with 10 years of experience. You are an expert at creating actionable, nutritionally balanced, and sustainable meal plans.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am a beginner starting a `[Diet Type, e.g., ketogenic, vegan, Mediterranean]` diet. My height is `[Height]`, and my weight is `[Weight]`.
> - Preferences: I love `[Favorite Foods]`. I want to strictly avoid `[Disliked Foods / Allergies]`.
> - Constraints: I have about `[Daily Cooking Time, e.g., 30 minutes]` per day to cook.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Calculate my estimated daily caloric needs and macronutrient split based on my goal of `[Goal: weight loss / muscle gain / maintenance]`.
> 2. Create a detailed 7-day meal plan (Monday to Sunday) including Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and one Snack.
> 3. Provide the estimated calories and protein content for every single meal.
> 4. Generate a comprehensive weekly grocery shopping list categorized by sections (Produce, Meat, Dairy, Pantry, etc.).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use common, accessible ingredients that are easy to find at a local grocery store.
> - Focus on realistic, sustainable mealsâ€”exclude overly complex recipes.
> - Output the daily meal plan strictly in a Markdown table format.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not recommend extreme caloric deficits or medically unsafe advice. If a goal seems unhealthy, provide a disclaimer and adjust to a safe baseline.

## ðŸ’¡ Writer's Insight

This prompt is a game-changer because it eliminates decision fatigue, which is the number one reason diets fail. By explicitly defining your **cooking time constraint** and **disliked foods**, the AI stops giving you unrealistic gourmet meals and instead acts like a practical meal-prep coach. Personally, I found that adding the "accessible ingredients" constraint prevented the AI from suggesting expensive, hard-to-find superfoods, keeping my grocery bills low while keeping me on track with my fitness goals. If the AI suggests portions that feel too small, you can easily follow up with: _"Increase the volume of the meals by adding more low-calorie dense vegetables."_

## ðŸ™‹ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well on the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it works perfectly. However, models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet or GPT-4o tend to format the markdown tables and grocery lists a bit more neatly and offer slightly more creative recipes.

- **Q: Can I ask the AI to plan meals based on what's currently in my fridge?**
  - A: Absolutely! You can add a line in the Context section saying: _"I currently have [List of ingredients] in my fridge. Prioritize using these."_

- **Q: How accurate are the calorie and macro calculations?**
  - A: The AI uses standard nutritional databases, so the estimates are generally quite accurate. However, for raw ingredients (like meats and veggies), it is always best to weigh your food and use a dedicated tracking app if you need absolute precision.
