## 💡 Writer's Insight

As of 2026, the technical gap between these two frameworks has essentially narrowed down to a matter of 'preference.' React Native has largely resolved its notorious Bridge bottlenecks with the full adoption of its **New Architecture (Fabric & TurboModules)**, while Flutter has smoothly eliminated iOS 'Jank' via its **Impeller rendering engine**.

Ultimately, the deciding factors are your **'Team DNA'** and **'Business Velocity.'** If sharing code with your frontend ecosystem (React, TypeScript) is critical, RN is the practical choice. If you need a pixel-perfect, custom UI system that behaves identically regardless of the OS, go with Flutter. Especially in markets with a massive pool of React developers, RN can be a much safer bet when factoring in recruitment costs during the scale-up phase.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: We have plenty of capital and an extended timeline. Should we still use a cross-platform framework?**
  - A: No. If time and money are not constraints, go straight for **Native development using Swift (iOS) and Kotlin (Android)** without hesitation. Pushing hardware limits and adopting the latest OS features instantly are areas where cross-platform frameworks will always lag behind.

- **Q: What about building a Progressive Web App (PWA) and wrapping it in a WebView?**
  - A: It’s excellent for initial market validation. However, if your service relies heavily on native device controls—such as push notifications, background execution, or complex camera integration—you will inevitably hit a wall and have to rewrite the app.

- **Q: Why do you recommend 'Perplexity' for this specific prompt?**
  - A: When comparing tech stacks, real-time context (e.g., current Google support, open-source ecosystem vitality) is crucial. Perplexity excels at real-time web searches and citing reliable references, providing a much more accurate and trendy answer than standard LLMs relying on outdated training data.
