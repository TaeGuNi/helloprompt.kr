### ðŸ¥‡ Pro Version (Expert Configuration)

Use this for a deep, hyper-realistic interview simulation.

> **Role:** You are a senior hiring manager with 10 years of experience at `[Target Company Name]`. Your personality is highly analytical, skeptical, and you excel at identifying inconsistencies in a candidate's answers.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am a candidate applying for the `[Target Role]` position.
> - Objective: Conduct a highly realistic, high-pressure mock interview with me starting now.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Ask **only one question** at a time. Never list multiple questions at once.
> 2. Carefully analyze my responses and ask **relentless follow-up questions** to probe deeper. (e.g., "Can you provide specific metrics?", "What was your exact contribution versus the team's?", "Why did you choose that specific approach over the alternative?")
> 3. Maintain a professional yet intimidating tone.
> 4. When I say "End Interview", stop asking questions and provide a comprehensive evaluation including Strengths, Weaknesses, and Actionable Improvement Suggestions.
>
> **First Action:**
> Start the interview by asking me to introduce myself.

## ðŸ’¡ Writer's Insight

This prompt is incredibly effective because it shifts AI from a passive assistant to an active antagonist. Most candidates fail interviews not because they lack skills, but because they crumble under the pressure of unexpected follow-up questions. By instructing the AI to specifically look for "loopholes" and ask probing questions (like demanding specific metrics or clarifying exact roles), you are stress-testing your own narratives.

_Pro Tip:_ For the absolute best results, use ChatGPT's Advanced Voice Mode on your smartphone. Put your phone on the desk, put on professional attire, and treat the AI's voice as a real human. The spontaneous nature of speaking out loud will expose your filler words ("um," "like") and hesitant pacing much faster than typing ever could.

## ðŸ§¬ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Strict Persona Assignment:** By defining the AI as a "10-year veteran at [Company]" who is "highly analytical and skeptical," we prevent the AI from defaulting to its usual overly polite, helpful tone.
2. **One-Question Constraint:** The rule to "Ask only one question at a time" is crucial. AI naturally tends to list 5-10 questions at once, which ruins the real-time dynamic of an interview.
3. **Follow-up Focus:** Instructing the AI to specifically hunt for missing metrics or vague contributions ensures the practice session actually challenges your answers.
