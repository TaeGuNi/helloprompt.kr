## 🚀 This Is How Real Experts Write

### 🥉 Basic Version (Standard)

Useful when you want to quickly grasp just the core of a specific trend. Copy the text below and paste it into Perplexity or ChatGPT Plus.

> **Role:** You are a sharp IT trend analyst.
>
> **Request:** Clearly summarize the latest trends regarding the recent `[Search engine competition between Google and OpenAI]` and organize the key impacts this will have on `[General Users/Enterprises]` into three points.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need a high-level deep report that goes beyond simple summaries to include clear sources and immediately executable Action Items.

> **Role:** You are a Senior Tech Strategy Analyst advising Fortune 500 companies.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: A global AI search engine war is intensifying, led by Google (Gemini) and OpenAI (SearchGPT).
> - Goal: You need to write an executive report that coolly analyzes the threats and opportunities this massive technological tectonic shift poses to our industry.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Conduct an in-depth investigation into the `[Latest trends in AI search technology and major strategies by Big Tech companies]`.
> 2. Analyze the ripple effects this technological change will have on the `[Marketing/Content Industry]`, divided into short-term (within 1 year) and long-term (after 3 years) perspectives.
> 3. Derive 3 specific response strategies (Action Items) that we can immediately put into practice in our work.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - All claims must be written based on reliable **sources within the last 3 months** (news articles, industry reports, research paper links, or clear source names).
> - Organize the output as a clean report with excellent readability on mobile, actively using Markdown headings and bullet points.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up content for which accurate data or reliable sources cannot be found; boldly omit it and clearly mark it as "Insufficient Evidence." (Hallucination strictly prohibited)

As the AI search war enters its full trajectory, the one-dimensional research battle of 'who can find vast information faster' is already completely over. The most noteworthy part is the variable control method. Try changing the text inside the **Variable** brackets `[Marketing/Content Industry]` in the prompt to the actual domain you are involved in. AI inherently has an instinct to fill in blanks with plausible sentences. Through this, we can exponentially reduce even the time spent on fact-checking.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this prompt work correctly in the free version of ChatGPT (GPT-3.5, etc.)?**
  - A: The prompt itself will work, but the quality of the results will be significantly lower. This prompt requires a 'Real-time Web Search' feature to scrape the latest trends. Therefore, I strongly recommend running it in environments like **Perplexity, SearchGPT (ChatGPT Plus), Gemini Advanced, or Microsoft Copilot**, where web search functionality is built-in.

- **Q: Is there no risk that the sources provided by the AI are fake links (hallucinations)?**
  - A: The risk definitely exists. It is an inherent limitation of generative AI. That is exactly why we set strong hurdles in the prompt constraints: "Required sources within the last 3 months" and "Boldly omit uncertain information." Do not blindly trust the results completed by AI; you must develop the professional habit of clicking the provided source links at least once to **Cross-check** the context of the original text.
