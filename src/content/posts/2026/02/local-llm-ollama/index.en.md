## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Do I absolutely need a high-end PC worth thousands of dollars or an expensive external GPU to run a local LLM smoothly?**
  - A: Not at all. While higher specs are always better, a MacBook with an Apple M1 chip or higher and at least 8GB of RAM (16GB+ is strongly recommended for smoother multitasking) or a standard Windows desktop with modern architecture is sufficient. In particular, highly optimized recent lightweight models under the 8B (8 billion parameter) class, such as Llama 3 8B, can run very smoothly on typical work PCs without inference latency, often faster than you can type.

- **Q: Is the Korean text generation or coding logic reasoning significantly inferior to paid commercial cloud AIs (like ChatGPT Plus)?**
  - A: It is true that due to the physical parameter limits of the models, it is realistically difficult to expect the level of perfection found in commercial models with trillions of parameters when it comes to literary writing requiring subtle nuances or creative tasks with vast world-building. However, if you download and use excellent open-source models fine-tuned specifically for Korean data—such as the 'EEVE-Korean' model released by Yanolja or the community's 'Llama-3-Open-Ko'—they perform exceptionally well for everyday engineering and office tasks like source code reviews, log file analysis, meeting summaries, and internal data preprocessing.
