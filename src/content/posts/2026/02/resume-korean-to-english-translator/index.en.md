### ü•á Pro Version (Expert Polish)

Use this for a flawless, interview-winning resume.

> **Role:** You are a senior recruiter at a top-tier US Silicon Valley company and a professional resume writer.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I need to translate my Korean career history into a professional English resume.
> - Goal: This is not a direct translation. The output must be stylishly refined and perfectly aligned with the **English-speaking recruitment culture**.
>
> **Input Text (Korean):**
> [Paste your Korean resume content here]
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Start every single bullet point with a **Powerful Action Verb** (e.g., Spearheaded, Orchestrated, Optimized).
> 2. Boldly omit the subject (never use 'I' or 'My').
> 3. Emphasize metrics, numbers, and key achievements using **Bold** text.
> 4. Paraphrase awkward or literal translations ("Konglish") into the sophisticated business jargon that native professionals actually use.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - The output must be 100% free of grammatical and spelling errors.
> - Maintain strict tense accuracy (Past tense for previous roles, Present tense for current responsibilities).

## üí° Writer's Insight

Direct translation tools like Google Translate or DeepL are great for general communication, but they fail spectacularly at resumes. Why? Because a resume isn't just about language; it's about _formatting conventions_. Western recruiters expect to see "Action-oriented bullet points."

By assigning the AI the persona of a "Silicon Valley Recruiter," you force it to look past the literal words and focus on the _impact_. This prompt is particularly effective because it explicitly instructs the AI to highlight numbers‚Äîwhich is the universal language of business success. If you find the AI's output is too "flowery" or complex, simply add a constraint: `"Keep the vocabulary concise and strictly business-professional."`

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well with the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, the Pro prompt provides enough context that even free models like GPT-3.5 or Claude 3 Haiku will produce excellent results. However, advanced models (GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet) will provide slightly more natural industry jargon.

- **Q: What if I don't have exact numbers for my achievements?**
  - A: You should try to estimate them reasonably. If you absolutely cannot provide numbers, modify the prompt to focus on the _scope_ of the project or the _efficiency_ gained, rather than hard metrics.

- **Q: Should I use this for my Cover Letter too?**
  - A: Resumes and Cover Letters have different tones. For a Cover Letter, you _do_ use "I" and "My," and you write in full sentences. You will need a different prompt tailored specifically for Cover Letters.
