## 🚀 How Real Professionals Write

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in parentheses to match your situation for immediate professional use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Useful when you need to hear multi-angle opinions quickly and kickstart a brainstorming session.

> **Task:** Conduct a multi-perspective analysis on the `[Subject]` I provide, in the form of a free-wheeling debate among 3 experts: an Optimist, a Pessimist, and a Realist.
>
> **Subject (Context):** `[Insert your proposal or idea here]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need high-level logical results and concrete action plans that are polished enough to be reported directly in a professional setting.

> **Role:** You are not just a single AI right now; you are a **'Task Force Swarm'** composed of 3 top-tier experts.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: We are currently in a situation regarding `[Background of the problem or project to be solved]`.
> - Goal: The final objective is to derive the most perfect and verified execution plan to overcome this challenge.
>
> **Agents:**
>
> - 🧠 **Agent A (Strategist):** Proposes creative and innovative ideas that break the mold and designs the big picture of the project.
> - 🕵️‍♂️ **Agent B (Critic):** Very sharply points out logical flaws, fatal risks, budget, and resource issues that may arise from Agent A's proposal.
> - 🛠️ **Agent C (Executor):** Synthesizes the fierce discussion between the previous two agents to establish a concrete checklist and action plan executable starting tomorrow morning.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Round 1:** Agent A first proposes a detailed and bold draft.
> 2. **Round 2:** Agent B logically attacks 3 fatal weaknesses of that draft.
> 3. **Round 3:** Agent C perfectly coordinates the two opinions to reach a final conclusion and derives an execution plan in the form of bullet points (List) for high mobile readability.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - For each round, clearly indicate the identity of the agent currently speaking. (e.g., `**[Agent B - Critic]**`)
> - Do not maintain unnecessary politeness; debate fiercely based strictly on logic and data.
> - For mobile readability, do NOT use tables; organize in high-readability bullet points (List).
> - **Bold** all important keywords.
> - Do not make up information that is not certain; answer "I don't know" if necessary. (Anti-hallucination)

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to use)

The most common and fatal mistake many people make when using AI is stopping at **'One-way Instruction'**. If you simply say "Plan A" or "Summarize B," the AI just parrots a flat, predictable model answer that converges on the average of its training data. However, as shown in the prompt I've introduced, if you force-assign an **'Adversarial Persona'** like 'Agent B (Critic)' and induce interaction, the quality of the output leaps to a completely different dimension. The AI itself uncovers and excavates potential risks—such as budget blind spots, legal risks, or fatal operational bottlenecks—that a human planner might not have even noticed. Furthermore, you can witness an amazing **Self-Correction** process where it builds a solid and sophisticated logical defense to protect against those attacks.

The moment this prompt shows its most powerful strength in the real world is just before reporting a high-stakes new service proposal or a strategy report involving a large budget to a CEO, C-level executive, or a demanding client. Once your report draft is complete, copy and paste that content into the `[Subject]` variable and make sure to run a **'Virtual Defense Simulation'**. You can experience and identify the bone-chillingly sharp attack questions and stress-test-level verification that might pour out in a real executive meeting or client pitch, all from the safety of your desk. By doing so, you will have a magical experience where your defensive logic is perfectly prepared in advance, the vulnerabilities of your report are supplemented, and the structural integrity, overall persuasiveness, and quality of the plan as a business document soar vertically.

Here are a few more practical tips for **Constraint Control** within the prompt variables. In the basic prompt provided, I set the agent personalities as 'Strategist', 'Critic', and 'Executor', but you can modify these **Roles** in great detail depending on your specific business environment or the nature of your project. For example, if you are planning a B2C marketing campaign where target customer response is most important, try setting Agent A as a **'Trend-sensitive Gen Z consumer'**, Agent B as a **'Conservative and demanding Finance Manager'**, and Agent C as the **'Poor performance marketer who must coordinate both sides'**. You will get an eerily realistic and sophisticated final campaign plan that encompasses both the raw psychology of the target customer and the realistic barriers of internal budgets, perfectly simulated through hyper-realistic role-playing and conflict situations.

Additionally, the key to preventing AI agents from falling into deep philosophical debates or going off on a tangent (hallucination control) is to tune the **Constraints** section at the bottom of the prompt to your liking. If you want to prevent the discussion from dragging on, add a condition like *"Each agent's speech must be within 300 characters, getting straight to the point without introductions."* Conversely, if you need a very deep technical analysis, you can force extremely sharp and professional outputs by giving a constraint like *"When making a claim, you must provide specific data figures, relevant law names, or latest technology trend papers as evidence."* The moment you start handling variables and constraints like Lego blocks, you will evolve beyond a simple AI user into a true prompt engineer and a maestro conducting an AI organization. ## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Since three AIs are debating, doesn't the response get too long and consume too many tokens?**
  - A: That's correct. Multi-agent prompts have much longer context lengths than general one-off questions. Therefore, I highly recommend controlling the output length by adding explicit conditions in the constraints section, such as *"Compress each round's speech to within 500 characters, focusing only on the core points."*
- **Q: Does it work correctly if I increase the number of agents to 3 or more, such as a marketer, developer, and designer?**
  - A: Systematically, it is possible to configure up to 5 people. However, if the number of participating personas increases excessively, it may exceed the model's context window limit, leading to an increase in 'hallucinations' where the AI forgets initial settings or previous statements. Therefore, compressing down to the core 3 operators for problem-solving is the most stable and efficient swarm stack.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Splitting:** A massive and complex single task is broken down into 3 clear stages—'Idea Generation (A)', 'Risk Verification (B)', and 'Execution Synthesis (C)'—and assigned to independent personas. This prevents LLM computational overload and maximizes the resolution and accuracy of the answers.
2. **Self-Correction & Handover:** The handover process itself, where the discussion passes from agent to agent, acts as a powerful structural verification device. It real-time self-corrects the 'plausible lies' and logical leaps—the most chronic weaknesses of generative AI—through internal checks and balances.
