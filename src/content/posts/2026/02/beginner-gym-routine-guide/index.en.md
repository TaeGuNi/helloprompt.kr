### ü•á Pro Prompt (Expert Level)

Use this to generate a highly detailed, personalized, and safe routine.

> **Role:** You are an elite personal trainer and bodybuilding coach with 20 years of experience specializing in beginner hypertrophy and injury prevention.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am a beginner who just registered at the gym `[1 week]` ago. I can commit to working out `[3 times]` a week.
> - Goal: Free weights (barbells/dumbbells) are intimidating and I struggle with form, so I want to build a foundation using strictly `[resistance machines]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Design a **'No-Split Full Body Routine'** suitable for a `[3-days-a-week]` schedule.
> 2. Structure each daily session in this specific order: Large Muscle Groups (Chest, Back, Lower Body) ‚ûî Small Muscle Groups (Shoulders, Arms) ‚ûî Core (Abs).
> 3. For each exercise, provide: the exact machine name, recommended sets (e.g., 3-4), and reps (e.g., 10-15).
> 4. Add a highly actionable, one-sentence "Pro Tip" for each machine focusing on the mind-muscle connection or crucial form cues.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Strictly exclude high-skill free weight movements (e.g., barbell squats, deadlifts, free bench press). Replace them entirely with their machine equivalents (e.g., Leg Press, Hack Squat, Chest Press Machine).
> - Output the final routine exclusively as a clean, easy-to-read Markdown Table.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not recommend exercises that require a spotter or significant balancing skills. Focus purely on guided machine movements.

## üí° Writer's Insight

As someone who used to spend 40 minutes on the elliptical just to avoid eye contact in the free-weight zone, this prompt is an absolute game-changer. The key here is the constraint: **"strictly exclude free weights."**

By forcing the AI to focus solely on machines, you eliminate the learning curve of balancing barbells. Machines have fixed paths of motion, making them inherently safer for beginners to push close to failure. Furthermore, asking the AI for a "one-sentence Pro Tip" gives you exactly what you need to focus on during the lift (e.g., "drive through your heels") without overwhelming you with biomechanical jargon. Save the output table as a screenshot on your phone, and you'll navigate the gym floor like you've been going for years.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if my gym doesn't have the specific machine the AI suggests?**
  - A: Simple! Just reply to the AI with: _"My gym doesn't have a [Machine Name]. What is the best machine alternative for this?"_ The AI will instantly provide a substitute targeting the same muscle group.

- **Q: Is a 3-day full-body routine really enough to see results?**
  - A: Absolutely. For beginners, stimulating all major muscle groups three times a week provides the optimal frequency for "newbie gains." Splitting body parts (like a "bro split") is often less effective for novices who need repetition to learn motor patterns.

- **Q: How long should I rest between these machine sets?**
  - A: A good rule of thumb for beginners is 60 to 90 seconds. You can even ask the AI to add a "Rest Time" column to your Markdown table to keep your pacing consistent!

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Clear Boundaries (Constraints):** Explicitly banning free weights ensures the output is 100% actionable and safe for a beginner's current skill level.
2. **Logical Sequencing (Task 2):** Forcing the AI to order exercises from large to small muscle groups prevents early fatigue, which is a common mistake beginners make when designing their own routines.
3. **Bite-Sized Coaching (Task 4):** Requesting a single, actionable tip per machine prevents cognitive overload while you are actually in the gym trying to execute the movement.
