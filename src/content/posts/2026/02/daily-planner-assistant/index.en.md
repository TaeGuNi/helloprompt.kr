## 🚀 The Solution: "Dedicated Daily Planning AI Assistant"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, rough overview of your daily schedule and immediate priorities.

> **Role:** You are a `[Productivity Consultant]`.
> **Task:** Review the `[Today's To-Do List]` below, determine the priorities, and create an hourly schedule for today.
>
> **Today's To-Do List:**
> `[Brain-dump all the tasks you need to do today here]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a flawless time-blocking schedule that meticulously weighs task importance, respects your personal workflow rhythm, and accounts for hard deadlines.

> **Role:** You are a 10-year veteran `[Productivity and Time Management Expert]` and my personal executive assistant.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I have an overwhelming amount of tasks to handle today, and my mind is cluttered. I don't know where to start.
> - Goal: Categorize my to-do list using the Eisenhower Matrix (Urgency/Importance) and, based on that, draft the most efficient daily schedule utilizing the 'Time Blocking' method.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Analyze the provided `[Today's To-Do List]` and classify the items into 4 categories: DO, DECIDE, DELEGATE, and DELETE.
> 2. Draft a time-blocking schedule taking into account `[My Working Hours]` and `[Fixed Appointments]`.
> 3. Allocate the 'DO (Urgent & Important)' tasks that require the highest concentration to my `[Peak Focus Hours]`.
> 4. Incorporate a cycle of 90 minutes of focused work followed by a 15-minute break into the schedule.
>
> **Variables:**
>
> - `[Today's To-Do List]`: (e.g., Draft proposal for Company A, prep for team weekly sync, reply to emails, lunch meeting, review marketing metrics)
> - `[My Working Hours]`: (e.g., 09:30 AM - 06:30 PM)
> - `[Fixed Appointments]`: (e.g., 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM Team Meeting, 12:30 PM - 01:30 PM Lunch)
> - `[Peak Focus Hours]`: (e.g., 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Make reasonable estimations for the time required for each task and allocate them accordingly.
> - Output the result strictly using Markdown tables to maximize readability. (One table for Priorities, one for the Schedule)
> - The schedule MUST explicitly include break times and lunch hours.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Avoid packing the schedule too tightly. You MUST allocate about 30 minutes of Buffer Time in the afternoon to account for unexpected situations and emergencies.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: How detailed do I need to be when listing my tasks?**
  - A: You can simply jot down rough keywords as they come to mind; the AI is incredibly adept at reading the context. However, adding estimated timeframes, such as "Prep for Company A meeting (30 mins)," will yield a much more accurate and realistic schedule.

- **Q: What if an emergency meeting pops up in the middle of the day?**
  - A: The core philosophy of time-blocking is flexibility. If a sudden meeting occurs, simply tell the AI in the same chat: "An urgent 1-hour meeting just came up at 2:00 PM. Please adjust my afternoon schedule." It will instantly recalibrate the rest of your day.

- **Q: Which AI model works best for this specific prompt?**
  - A: This prompt relies heavily on contextual understanding and logical reasoning. Therefore, I highly recommend Claude 3.5 Sonnet or ChatGPT (GPT-4o), both of which excel at structural logic. While the Basic Version works perfectly fine on free models, handling the complex constraints of the Pro Version requires a top-tier model.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Integrating the Eisenhower Matrix:** Instead of blindly listing tasks, this prompt provides the AI with a mental framework to filter items by 'Importance' and 'Urgency', resulting in highly objective, bias-free prioritization.
2. **Respecting Biorhythms:** By utilizing the `[Peak Focus Hours]` variable, the schedule isn't just mathematically divided; it becomes a personalized workflow strictly tailored to your unique daily energy levels.
3. **Built-in Realism (Buffer Time):** By enforcing "Buffer Time" within the constraints, it prevents the AI from generating a suffocating, hyper-optimized schedule that falls apart at the very first disruption. It creates a practical timeline that actually allows you to breathe.
