### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need deep, nuanced names that reflect complex backstories and immersive world-building.

> **Role (Role):** You are a master novelist and cultural anthropologist with an exceptional sense for naming characters.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I am writing a novel and need the perfect names for my characters. The names must seamlessly fit their personalities, origins, and specific roles in the narrative.
> - Goal: To generate highly symbolic, memorable, and culturally appropriate character names.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> - **Cultural Background/Era:** `[e.g., Medieval European High Fantasy, 1920s Noir New York, Cyberpunk Neo-Tokyo]`
> - **Gender:** `[e.g., Male, Female, Non-binary, Neutral]`
> - **Core Traits & Backstory:** `[e.g., Cold on the outside but fiercely loyal, a master of dual-wielding, carries the guilt of a fallen kingdom]`
> - **Narrative Role:** `[e.g., A tragic mentor who sacrifices themselves for the protagonist]`
>
> **Instructions:**
>
> 1. Recommend **5 Full Names (First Name + Surname)** that perfectly align with the profile above.
> 2. For each name, provide the **Origin and Etymological Meaning** (Feel free to pull from flower language, ancient languages, mythology, or historical roots).
> 3. Describe the **Phonetic Nuance and Image** the name evokes when spoken out loud.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Absolutely exclude generic or overused names (e.g., James, Alex, Smith).
> - Ensure the names have a natural rhythm and are relatively easy for readers to pronounce.
> - Format the output as a clear Markdown list.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Do not invent fake linguistic roots. If you are blending languages or creating a fantasy name, clearly state the real-world linguistic inspirations you used to prevent hallucinations.

## üí° Writer's Insight

Naming is often the biggest bottleneck for writers. The brilliance of this prompt lies in the **"Phonetic Nuance and Image"** instruction. A name doesn't just mean something on paper; it has a specific 'mouthfeel.' Words with hard consonants (K, T, R) sound aggressive or sturdy (like 'Kalian'), while soft vowels and consonants (L, S, M) sound elegant or elusive (like 'Silas'). By forcing the AI to act as a cultural anthropologist, you avoid historically inaccurate naming conventions and get names that carry the exact narrative weight your character deserves.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well for Sci-Fi or completely alien fantasy worlds?**
  - A: Yes! Just change the `[Cultural Background/Era]` to something like "Alien civilization based on Arabic phonetics" or "Dystopian corporate future." The AI will adapt the linguistic rules accordingly to create cohesive naming conventions.

- **Q: The AI keeps giving me names that are too hard to pronounce. How do I fix this?**
  - A: Add a specific constraint to the prompt like: _"Limit the first name to a maximum of 3 syllables and ensure it is easily readable and pronounceable for native English speakers."_

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Dual Persona (Novelist + Anthropologist):** By assigning these two roles, the AI doesn't just generate "cool sounding" names. It grounds the names in real-world linguistics, culture, and narrative purpose.
2. **Origin & Etymology:** Demanding the historical or mythological roots ensures the names have depth, allowing you to hide narrative Easter eggs in your character names.
3. **Phonetic Rhythm:** Forcing the AI to consider how the name _sounds_ ensures it will read well in prose and dialogue, enhancing the reader's immersion.
