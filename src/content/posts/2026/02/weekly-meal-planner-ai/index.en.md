## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` to fit your situation, and put it to work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version (Standard)

Use this when you need a rough weekly guide starting today without complex conditions.

> **Role:** You are a professional clinical nutritionist with 10 years of experience.
>
> **Task:**
> Plan a one-week dinner menu for an office worker. I cannot eat seafood, and a diet of only chicken breast every day is boring. Focus on easy-to-cook, delicious recipes and provide a clean grocery list of necessary ingredients in a table format.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need high-quality details like exact calorie counts, macronutrient ratios, and budget limits.

> **Role:** You are a former head chef of a Michelin 3-star restaurant and an elite diet and nutrition consultant with 1 million subscribers.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Target: `[30s Female, Office Worker, Moderate Activity]`
> - Goal: `[Fat loss, Limit total daily intake to 1,500kcal]`
> - Preferred Diet: `[High Protein Low Carb, Carbs 30%, Protein 40%, Fat 30%]`
> - Budget: `[Weekly grocery bill under $50]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Plan a 5-day **`[Lunch Box, Dinner]`** meal plan from Monday to Friday based on the above situation.
> 2. Include **`[Simple Recipe Instructions]`** that even a beginner can finish within 15 minutes.
> 3. Below the meal plan, write a **`[Grocery Shopping List]`** categorized by section (Produce, Meat/Seafood, Dairy, Processed/Sauces) that can be used immediately at a supermarket.
> 4. Recognize the Target, Goal, Preferred Diet, and Budget items as variables that I can change at any time, and ask me for these values before our next conversation.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - The output must be visually organized using Markdown tables. (1 table for the meal plan, 1 table for the grocery list)
> - Allergies or Disliked Foods: **`[Nuts, Shellfish]`** (Never include these in the plan)
> - Use only cost-effective ingredients that are easily available at common local supermarkets or big-box stores.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not create bizarre or awkward ingredient combinations just to force the calorie and macronutrient ratios. (Prevent Hallucinations)
> - If it is impossible to meet the above conditions within the realistically set budget, do not force the plan; instead, advise from an expert's perspective on how much the budget needs to increase or what ingredients would be good substitutes.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Grocery prices change all the time. Can it really hit the $50 budget exactly?**
  - A: It’s realistically difficult for AI to reflect 100% of real-time prices or sale info. However, it works by prioritizing **cost-effective ingredients** with low price volatility like cabbage, tofu, pork, and eggs to keep you within your defensive budget line as much as possible.

- **Q: What if I need to solve all 3 meals a day at home, including weekends?**
  - A: The solution is simple! Just intuitively modify Task #1 of the prompt to: "Plan a **`[Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and Snacks for 7 days from Monday to Sunday]`** meal plan" to fit your lifestyle.

- **Q: I used the same prompt repeatedly, and the menus are getting repetitive.**
  - A: Just add one line to the constraints: _"However, please provide a diverse range of completely new recipes that do not overlap with last week's meals (e.g., Chicken Breast Fried Rice, Tofu Salad, etc.)."_ This immediately solves the monotony and gives you a much richer table every time.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Ultra-precise Targeting (Context):** It gives clear instructions on age, gender, activity level, and preferred diet type, plus a specific **budget ceiling**. This produces **hyper-personalized results** at an overwhelming level compared to generic internet search results.
2. **Execution Constraints:** It prevents the meal plan from being a mere list of menus. By excluding allergies and **forcing the physical availability of ingredients** based on common supermarkets, it ensures powerful practicality that can be acted upon tonight.
3. **Anti-Hallucination Safety Valve (Warning):** It prevents the AI's tendency to suggest extreme fasting or awkward ingredient combinations just to hit a budget. If constraints conflict, it’s designed to provide **honest feedback and alternatives** from an expert's perspective, maximizing the reliability of the output.
