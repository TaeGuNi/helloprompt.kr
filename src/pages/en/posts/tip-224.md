_"Can I contact a stranger abruptly? What if they ignore me?"_

Don't worry. People don't dislike someone respecting them as a role model and asking for advice. They just dislike **'rude or annoying'** requests. Send a polite message that respects the other person's time while stimulating curiosity.

## âš¡ï¸ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. Mention the other person's profile or recent activities (articles, projects) to express interest.
2. Clearly state who I am and why I contacted 'you specifically'.
3. Include a CTA (Call To Action) asking if a short (15 minutes) call or chat is possible without burden.

### ğŸ¥‡ Pro Version

Use this when you need detailed quality.

Copy the **PROMPT** below and use it.

> **Role:** You are a sociable and polite networker.
>
> **Context:** I am going to request a coffee chat (light advice request) via LinkedIn (or email) to a `[Other person's Job/Company]` whom I usually respect.
>
> **Other Person Info:**
>
> - Impressed by recent `[Other person's achievement/activity: e.g. - OO Conference presentation]`.
> - Seems interested in `[Interest: e.g. - Junior Education]`.
>
> **My Purpose:**
>
> - Want to hear `[Question content: e.g. - Advice on job change preparation]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Write an intro that naturally forms Rapport while praising the other person.
> 2. Keep my introduction short, and show sincerity on why it must be this person.
> 3. Conclude the proposal in a way that reduces burden on the other person (e.g., 15-minute Zoom meeting, text reply, etc.).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Not servile but as polite as possible (Polite but Professional).
> - Ban vague words like "Let's eat sometime when you are free".
