## 🚀 The Solution: The "Master PPT Architect"

### 🥉 Basic Version
Use this version when you need instant results.
> **Role:** You are an `[expert presenter with 10 years of experience]`.
> **Task:** Plan a 10-slide PowerPoint outline for a `[new product launch proposal]`.
### 🥇 Pro Version
Use this version for maximum precision and depth.
> **Role:** You are a Senior Director at a top-tier strategy consulting firm and an absolute master at crafting persuasive presentations.
> 
> **Context:**
> 
> - Background: `[Internal management report to increase next year's marketing budget]`
> - Audience: `[C-Level executives holding the budget decision-making power]`
> - Presentation Time: `[15 minutes]`
> 
> **Task:**
> 
> 1. Develop the overarching logical structure (storyline) for a presentation totaling `[10]` slides.
> 2. For each slide, provide the "Title," "Core Message," a "Visualization Suggestion (Tables/Charts, etc.)," and a "Speaker Script Summary."
> 3. Optimize and fill in the `[Variables]` to perfectly match my provided context.
> 
> **Constraints:**
> 
> - Keep the core message of each slide extremely concise, not exceeding 20 characters.
> - Maintain a highly professional, authoritative business tone throughout.
> - Structure the output cleanly using Markdown bullet points and numbering. (Do not use tables under any circumstances).
> 
> **Warning:**
> 
> - Never invent uncertain data or statistics; leave empty brackets for me to fill in manually.
> - Skip any introductory remarks or greetings and start immediately with Slide 1.

## 💡 Writer's Insight
The true value of this prompt lies in its ability to generate both concrete "Visualization Suggestions" and precise "Script Summaries" simultaneously. The trick is forcing the AI—which would normally just spit out dry text—to provide tangible design recommendations (like "A pie chart works best here").
After hundreds of pitches and proposals, one truth consistently proves itself: neither bosses nor clients are blinded by wild animations or fancy fonts. What matters is a watertight, logical structure that immediately answers the question: "What is your point?" So, stop wasting your precious time hunting for the perfect template. Once the content foundation is solid, even black text on a white background is enough to seal the deal. Erect this framework in just one minute with our prompt—and invest the time you save into your core tasks or a well-deserved break. That's how true professionals work.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
- **Q: What should I do if the AI's outline doesn't align with my actual intention?**
  - A: Simply add another line to the context section of the prompt, such as "Mandatory key data to integrate" or "Desired conclusion." Since the AI cannot read minds, you must define your goal as precisely as possible.
- **Q: Don't tools like Gamma handle all the design work for me anyway?**
  - A: In theory, yes. In the harsh reality of corporate life, however, you will 99% of the time be forced to use your company's rigid corporate design template (PPTX). Rapidly structuring the content via an LLM and then dropping that text into your company template is, at the end of the day, by far the fastest and safest approach.

## 🧬 Anatomy of the Prompt (Why it works?)
1. **Laser-focused Definition of Context and Audience:** Precise instructions like "for the C-Level" or "15-minute report" force the AI to independently optimize the presentation's pacing and the weighting of arguments.
2. **Mandatory Visualization Suggestions:** This hack lifts the heaviest burden of slide creation and proactively answers the agonizing question: "What's the best way to visualize this data?"
3. **Hard Character Limits for Core Messages:** To ruthlessly prevent walls of text on your slides, the 20-character limit forces the AI to mercilessly distill the content down to its absolute core essence.
