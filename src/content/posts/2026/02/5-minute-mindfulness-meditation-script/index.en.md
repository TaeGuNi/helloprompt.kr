## 🚀 This Is How Real Experts Write It

This is the prompt completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variable]` parts according to your situation to put it into practice immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly calm your emotions without complex settings.

> **Role:** You are a warm and calm mindfulness meditation leader with 10 years of experience.
> 
> **Task:** I am currently `[my heart is racing so much before an important interview]`. Write a short 3-minute meditation script in a conversational tone to help calm my mind.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This is a powerful prompt to use when you need detailed breathing guides and a deep level of psychological stability.

> **Role:** You are a professional mindfulness meditation leader and psychological counselor with a soft, calm voice.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Current State: I am currently `[facing an important presentation tomorrow]` and feel `[my heart pounding and extremely nervous]`.
> - Available Time: Exactly `[5 minutes]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Write a **`[5-minute]` mindfulness meditation script** tailored to my current situation that can lower tension and fill me with confidence.
> 2. You must follow this 4-stage structure:
>    - Stage 1: Introduction and focus on breathing
>    - Stage 2: Body scan and physical relaxation
>    - Stage 3: Situation-specific positive affirmation
>    - Stage 4: Gentle closing and return to reality
> 3. Include instructional phrases like "Slowly breathe in...", "Pause for a moment..." and reading timing guides like `[3-second silence]`, `[Deep breath]` inside parentheses.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - For mobile readability, never use tables; organize in a highly readable list format.
> - Be sure to **bold** important keywords.
> - Write in short, concise colloquial sentences.
> - Include sensory descriptions that stimulate sight and touch (e.g., "Cool air brushes the tip of your nose").
> - Exclude religious or overly spiritual words; focus strictly on psychological stability.
> - **[Important]** Once the script output is complete, wait until I say "Now read it."
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up information that is not certain; answer "I don't know." (Hallucination prevention)
