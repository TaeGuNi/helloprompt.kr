## üöÄ Solution: "The Nuance Polisher"

### ü•â Basic Version (The Quick Fix)

Use this when you need to convey urgency without coming across as demanding or offensive.

> **Role:** You are an expert in US corporate communication and business etiquette.
> **Original Sentence:** `[Give me the report by tomorrow.]`
> **Request:** Rewrite this sentence to be **polite, professional, and diplomatic**, suitable for a senior executive or an important client. Please provide 3 different variations ranging from casual to highly formal.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (The Diplomat)

Use this for high-stakes, delicate situations such as rejecting a proposal, issuing an apology, or navigating a tough negotiation.

> **Role (Role):** You are a Senior Global Communication Consultant holding an MBA from Harvard Business School, specializing in high-stakes corporate negotiations.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - **Recipient:** `[An overseas partner we have a friendly, ongoing relationship with]`
> - **Objective:** `[Their proposal is solid, but the pricing exceeds our budget. We need to negotiate a 10% discount without damaging the relationship.]`
> - **My Rough Draft:** `[Your proposal is good. But the price is too expensive. Discount 10% please.]`
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Completely rewrite my rough draft into a natural, highly persuasive Business English email.
> 2. Ensure the tone is **gentle yet firm**, maintaining our leverage while preserving the partnership.
> 3. Employ the "Sandwich Technique": Acknowledge the value of their proposal first, state our budget constraints clearly as the reason for the price cut, and end on a collaborative note.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Provide a compelling subject line and the full email body.
> - Avoid overly aggressive or culturally insensitive phrasing. Provide 2 distinct options: one slightly more direct, and one highly diplomatic.

## üí° Writer's Insight

This prompt is an absolute lifesaver when dealing with cross-border communication. Conventional translators operate on a 1:1 word-matching basis, completely stripping away the socio-cultural context. When I used to send emails translated directly from my native language, I often received cold or defensive replies. Why? Because direct, literal requests in many languages translate to aggressive demands in English.

By defining the AI's role as a "Harvard MBA Communication Consultant" and explicitly requesting the "Sandwich Technique," you force the AI to process the psychological impact of the words. It doesn't just translate your text; it translates your _intent_. This approach has personally saved me from several potential misunderstandings with overseas vendors and has noticeably improved the response rate and warmth of the replies I receive.

- **Q: I'm not negotiating. Can I use this for everyday internal team emails?**
  - A: Absolutely! Just adjust the "Context" and "Recipient" sections. Tell the AI the recipient is a "close team member," and it will adjust the tone to be casual, friendly, and collaborative rather than strictly formal.

## üß¨ Anatomy of the Prompt (Why it works)

1.  **Strategic Role-Playing:** Assigning the persona of a "Harvard MBA Consultant" forces the AI to access its training data related to high-level corporate communications and negotiation tactics.
2.  **The "Sandwich Technique":** Explicitly instructing the AI to use this psychological framework ensures that bad news or demands are cushioned with positive reinforcement, drastically increasing the likelihood of a favorable response.
3.  **Contextual Guardrails:** Providing the exact relationship dynamics (e.g., "friendly, ongoing relationship") prevents the AI from generating an email that is inappropriately formal or overly aggressive.
