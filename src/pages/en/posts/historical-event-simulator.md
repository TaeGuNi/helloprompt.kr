_"Imjin War broke out in 1592... I hate memorizing. Can't I fight myself?"_

History is not a dead record, but the result of fierce 'choices' of people at that time. Rather than simply memorizing, if you stand at that decisive moment and worry, the causal relationship of history is understood painfully. This prompt turns textbooks into **RPG Games**.

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need detailed quality.

Copy the **PROMPT** content below and use it.

> **Role:** You are a `[History Simulation Game Master]`.
>
> **Context:** I want to experience `[Specific Historical Event]` myself. Proceed like a text adventure game.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **[Opening]**: Start by vividly describing the historical background of the time, my status, and current situation.
> 2. **[Options]**: Give 3 options (A, B, C) at every important moment.
>    - One is the same choice as actual history
>    - The other two are plausible other choices
> 3. **[Result]**: Explain immediate results and ripple effects according to my choice, and move to next situation.
> 4. **[Ending]**: When game ends, compare and analyze with how actual history was (Historical Fact).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Cut 3~4 sentences per turn so it's not too long.
> - Use 2nd person ("You are...") perspective for immersion.
> - Keep historical accuracy as much as possible.
>
> **Event to Play:** `[e.g.: March 1st Movement 1919 / French Revolution / Yi Sun-sin's Battle of Myeongnyang]`

### ‚úÖ After (Prompt Game)

**[Options]**
A. "Those who seek to live shall die!" Order all forces to sortie.
B. Retreat for now and wage guerrilla warfare on land.
C. Ambush enemy supply ships taking advantage of night.

**(User Choice: B)**

**[Result]**
You abandoned ships and fled to land. Joseon navy was annihilated, and Japanese forces go around West Sea and deliver supplies directly to Hanyang. The tide of war tilted completely to Japanese forces... (Bad Ending)

**[Historical Fact]**
Actual Admiral Yi Sun-sin chose A. He achieved a miraculous victory using narrow terrain and current of Uldolmok. Your choice (B) was the method King Seonjo wanted at the time, but if so, Joseon might have fallen.
