## üöÄ The Solution: "The Executive Inbox Assistant"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this for a quick, straightforward reply when you're in a rush.

> **Role:** You are a professional executive assistant.
> **Task:** Write a polite business email replying to the following message. My goal is to `[Accept / Decline / Reschedule]`.
> **Message to reply to:** `[Paste the received email here]`

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this when the stakes are high and you need precise control over the tone, context, and desired outcome.

> **Role:** You are a senior communications director renowned for your impeccable business etiquette, clarity, and persuasive empathy.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Situation: I received an email from `[Sender's Name/Role]`. They are asking for `[Briefly describe their request]`.
> - My Goal: I need to `[State your exact goal, e.g., politely decline the offer, ask for an extension until Friday, propose a different time]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Draft a professional, concise email response.
> 2. Ensure the tone is `[Warm / Firm / Appreciative / Urgent]`.
> 3. Use clear, native-level business English.
> 4. Keep any placeholders I need to fill out in `[brackets]`.
> 5. **Message I received:**
>    `[Paste the email here]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Keep it under 150 words. Executives value brevity.
> - Do not be overly apologetic unless instructed. Maintain a confident, professional posture.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not invent details or commitments (like specific dates or reasons) that I did not provide in the context. Leave them as placeholders.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I use this for internal emails to my boss or team?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just change the Role to something like "Collaborative Team Player" and adjust the Tone to "Casual yet Professional."

- **Q: Does this work well in other languages besides English?**
  - A: Yes! Simply add "Please write the response in [Target Language]" to the Constraints section. AI models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet and GPT-4o are exceptionally good at maintaining formal business nuances across multiple languages.

## üß¨ Anatomy of the Prompt (Why it works?)

1. **Role Designation:** Assigning the "Senior Communications Director" persona ensures the AI avoids robotic phrasing and uses sophisticated, emotionally intelligent vocabulary.
2. **Context vs. Task Separation:** By clearly separating _what happened_ (Context) from _what needs to be done_ (Task), the AI stays focused on your specific goal without hallucinating.
3. **Tone Control:** Explicitly defining the tone prevents the default "overly enthusiastic AI" voice that often sounds unnatural in serious business contexts.
