## 🚀 The Solution: "Social Media Planner Automation Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a rapid burst of fresh ideas to simply get the ball rolling.

> **Role:** You are a `[social media content director with 10 years of experience]`.
> **Task:** Outline 30 days of Instagram Reels topics to promote a `[wealth management app for corporate employees in their 20s]`. I will only post from Monday to Friday.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Deploy this when you need to extract detailed daily themes, scroll-stopping hooks, and ready-to-publish captions all in a single sweep.

> **Role:** 
> You are a global social media marketing director with 10 years of experience who is obsessed with conversion rate optimization. You perfectly understand the algorithms of TikTok and Instagram Reels, and you are a master at writing hooking scripts that prevent drop-offs in the first 3 seconds.
>
> **Context:**
> - Business/Service: `[B2B SaaS workflow automation tool]`
> - Main Target: `[Junior marketers and planners with under 3 years of experience who are drowning in daily overtime]`
> - Channel: `[Instagram Reels, TikTok]`
> - Upload Frequency: `[3 times a week - Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays]`
> - Tone & Manner: `[Professional yet approachable, featuring a refreshingly blunt communication style with a touch of dry humor]`
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Based on the criteria above, map out a short-form content calendar of exactly 12 posts to be published over the next 4 weeks (one month).
> 2. Each content piece MUST include the following elements:
>    - 🗓️ Upload week and day
>    - 🎯 Content Topic (directly agitating the target's pain points)
>    - 🎣 3-Second Hook Script (the very first spoken sentence of the video)
>    - 📝 Visual Direction Guide (the specific visual scene or action on screen)
>    - 💬 Caption Draft and 3 highly relevant hashtags
> 3. The values inside `[ ]` are variables tailored to my specific service.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - NEVER output the format as a Markdown table. 
> - Instead, generate a highly readable list format utilizing bullet points and emojis.
> - Hooks must never be abstract; use specific numbers and high-impact phrasing. (e.g., "If you aren't doing this, you'll be stuck doing overtime forever", etc.)
> - Output ONLY the calendar content immediately. Do not include any conversational filler, intros, or conclusions.
>
> **Warning:**
> - Strictly avoid corporate clichés that the target audience cannot relate to. Pinpoint the visceral, real-world struggles experienced by actual employees (e.g., manual Excel grunt work, unreasonable demands from management, etc.).

## 💡 Insight (Author's Comment)

The true power of this prompt hinges on two critical factors: **obsessively targeting pain points** and **strictly banning table formats.**

Typically, if you ask an AI to create a content calendar, it defaults to a massive, sprawling table. Sure, it looks fine on a wide desktop monitor, but the moment you try to review it on the Slack or Notion mobile app during your morning commute, the layout shatters completely. You end up wasting precious time just reformatting the text. That’s exactly why I hardcoded the constraint to force a bulleted list format. In the real world of content management, your actual productivity is dictated by whether you can directly copy, paste, and execute the output without friction.

Furthermore, in the realm of short-form video, the first 3 seconds are literally life or death. If you vaguely ask an AI for "10 video topics," you'll receive sterile, uninspired garbage like "5 tips for workplace efficiency." However, by assigning a hyper-specific, ruthless persona—a director "obsessed with conversion rate optimization" who uses a "refreshingly blunt communication style"—the AI is forced to generate highly compressed, aggressive hooks. Instead of boring tips, you get scroll-stoppers like, "Who else ruined their Friday night copy-pasting cells in Excel?" Don't overthink the planning phase; let this prompt build the structural skeleton, sprinkle in your unique domain expertise, and jump straight into filming.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Does this work on the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it absolutely does. However, top-tier models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet or GPT-4o hold a massive advantage when it comes to capturing subtle conversational nuances—especially dry humor or a deliberately blunt tone. Free models tend to default to overly polite, sterile, and corporate-sounding text.

- **Q: Can I adapt this for YouTube Shorts or long-form blog posts?**
  - A: Absolutely. Simply swap out the `[Channel]` variable for 'YouTube Shorts' or 'WordPress Blog', adjust the tone parameters to match the platform, and the prompt will seamlessly adapt.

- **Q: What if the generated content feels too predictable or generic?**
  - A: Push your `[Main Target]` variable to the absolute extreme. The output quality skyrockets when you abandon generic labels like "women in their 20s" in favor of something hyper-specific, such as "female college students in their early 20s who hunt down exclusive pop-up stores every weekend and are obsessed with aesthetic cafe culture."

## 🧬 Why it works? (Prompt Anatomy)

1. **Role (Conversion Optimization Master):** By assigning the persona of a ruthless expert obsessed with algorithms and the 3-second drop-off rate, the resulting content immediately becomes punchier, visceral, and significantly more engaging.
2. **Context (Parameterization):** By perfectly modularizing the business model, target audience, platform, frequency, and tone, this prompt transforms into a universal framework. Simply swap out the variables to dominate any industry.
3. **Constraints (Format Control):** To entirely eliminate the friction of secondary formatting, the prompt strictly forbids the dreaded "broken mobile table" format, forcing the AI to generate a clean, instantly copy-pasteable list.
