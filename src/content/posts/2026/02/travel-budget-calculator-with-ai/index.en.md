>

>
> - Travel Destination: `[Country and City]`

> - Companions: `[Family/Couple/Friends/Solo]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Find all meaningful festivals or events happening in that region (or nearby) during the above period. (Include not just world-class festivals, but also food truck events, night markets, and local concerts that locals truly enjoy.)

> 3. Present realistic tips to smoothly avoid fatal risks that may occur during the festival period (accommodation price gouging, traffic controls, pickpocketing, restaurant waits, etc.).
> 4. If it is a paid event, include the official ticket booking link and a strategy for successful booking.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Actively use bullet points (-) and Markdown syntax to maximize readability.
> - Strictly exclude events that have already ended or whose occurrence is uncertain, and provide the most up-to-date and accurate information as of the search time.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up uncertain festival dates or events that do not overlap with my travel period. Instead, recommend other small-scale alternative events that fit the schedule. (Anti-hallucination)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

When you search portals like Google or Naver for "Places to visit in Sapporo in October" or "New York December events," you usually get flooded with the same obvious large tourist attractions or commercial tour products. Even if you dig through personal blogs, the info is often years old or skewed by the writer's subjective experience, making it hard to apply to your specific situation. However, using this prompt allows you to instantly fish out **'real local gems'** that are hidden in the corners of official tourism websites or quietly posted only in local communities (Reddit, local Facebook groups, etc.).

The core of the prompt lies in the **precise control of variables**. Specifically, the `[Companion]` variable is the master key that determines the quality of this prompt. Instead of just writing 'Family', try giving specific context like 'two elementary school children' or '70-year-old parents in wheelchairs'. The AI will then filter for face-painting events or safe parade spots perfect for kids, or cultural performances on flat ground that aren't too taxing for elderly parents. It will automatically exclude loud, crowded beer festivals or EDM festivals that end late at night, giving you the same effect as hiring a **professional-grade 1:1 customized local guide**.

Furthermore, this prompt holds overwhelming value in terms of **Risk Management**. During festival periods, "peak season premiums" cause the prices of accommodations and restaurants to skyrocket. The instruction "fatal risks and realistic tips" included in the prompt blocks such misfortunes at the source. Instead of overpriced lodging near the festival grounds, the AI suggests cost-effective hotels in satellite cities that are only 30 minutes away by public transport but at half the price. It briefs you on routes to bypass main roads paralyzed by crowds, the location of restrooms known only to locals, and hidden restaurant streets without waits.

Personally, thanks to this prompt, I was able to attend a modest 'Summer Matsuri' at a neighborhood shrine that happened to overlap with my trip to Osaka. It was a simple festival with not a single tourist, where neighborhood residents wore yukatas and shared shaved ice and takoyaki, but it left a much deeper and more moving impression than any flashy, famous theme park. I hope you don't settle for landmark verification shots that everyone else takes, but instead enjoy the thrilling experience of jumping into the real life of the destination through this prompt. **One small variable will change the entire saturation of your trip.**

After running the prompt, if you are not satisfied with the results, a great strategy is to throw **Follow-up Prompts** at the AI. For example, narrow it down by saying, "Among the recommended festivals, pick only the cost-effective events that are free or under $20," or "Find two more events held indoors in case it rains." If you are a foodie, try, "Tell me the top 3 limited-edition street foods I must try at the festival site, along with their local pronunciations." The AI will take care of details you hadn't even thought of, providing a perfect gastronomic experience. In this way, a prompt is not just a one-time copy-paste, but a powerful tool that can constantly evolve by reflecting your tastes.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if the festival dates recommended by the AI are different from the actual schedule?**
  - A: AI models may occasionally provide schedules from past years due to data training cutoffs or hallucinations. Therefore, we strongly recommend that you obtain the exact 'original language name' of the festival through the AI and then **cross-check once more** through Google or official websites for this year's latest dates.

- **Q: Can I get more accurate information by asking in English rather than Korean?**
  - A: That's correct. For example, if you want to find a festival in a small town on the outskirts of Spain, include the Spanish name in the `[Country and City]` variable. Or, add a line to the final constraints: _"Search thoroughly based on local language (Spanish) web documents, but output the final result translated into English."_ This allows you to obtain surprisingly deep, real local information.
