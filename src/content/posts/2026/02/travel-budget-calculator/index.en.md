## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight)

Before you stare at a blank Excel sheet with a blinking cursor, I strongly recommend running this prompt first. The most important key in calculating a travel budget is the **'real-time reflection of the latest data.'** Especially when run in environments like **Perplexity** or **ChatGPT Plus (GPT-4o)**, which have overwhelming real-time web search performance, it pinpointed the ever-changing average flight prices and exchange rate volatility with haunting accuracy. Since the AI scrapes flight booking sites or exchange rate information itself to find the most realistic median value for the current moment, we no longer need to manually toggle between Skyscanner and exchange rate windows with a calculator.

Once you fully understand the **variable (`[ ]`) control method** designed in the prompt, it becomes an all-purpose cheat key that can respond immediately to any city in the world. For example, the `[Travel Style]` variable is the most critical element determining the detail of the output. Instead of simply writing 'I want to go cheap,' try specifying specific lifestyles and extreme consumption patterns like **'Accommodation is a budget capsule hotel for sleeping only, but one meal a day must be a 100,000 KRW omaskase from the Michelin Guide.'** The AI catches these subtle differences in preference and presents a very intelligent budget distribution—drastically lowering the accommodation budget while setting aside separate funds for high-end restaurants in the food budget.

Furthermore, you need application know-how to freely modify **Constraints** according to the regional characteristics of the destination. If the destination is in Southeast Asia like Vietnam, Thailand, or the Philippines, try adding detailed instructions at the bottom of the constraints like _"Be sure to include local massage tip culture and the average daily estimated cost for ride-sharing services like Grab or Bolt as separate items."_ For a European trip, you can modify it to say _"Reflect the cost of Eurail passes for intercity travel and the advance booking entrance fees for major cathedrals and museums."_ By giving region-specific conditions like this, you can 99% perfectly prevent unfortunate incidents where your budget leaks away locally due to unexpected tips, taxi scams, or hidden entrance fees.

Lastly, what I want to emphasize above all else is the **magic of 'Emergency Fund' forced allocation**. Many people make the fatal mistake of planning their budget down to the last cent. However, a trip never goes according to our Excel plan for even a single day. There are always unexpected variables, such as having to cancel plans due to rain and go to a pretty cafe, or having to buy something new because you suddenly lost an item. This is why a strong instruction to _'allocate 10% of the total budget as an emergency fund'_ is included in Task 3 of the prompt I designed. This 10% buffer is not just a surplus number; it will serve as the most reliable psychological safety net, preventing you from having a mental breakdown in an unfamiliar land. Don't blindly trust the total amount derived by the AI; get into the habit of finally checking if this emergency fund item is properly intact. This one detail is the most decisive difference between a pro traveler who protects their bank balance and a beginner.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: It's a long-term trip departing in a year; will the budget be accurate?**
  - A: AI models basically calculate costs based on average data for the 'current moment.' Therefore, it's difficult for them to predict inflation or peak season premiums a year from now. In this case, the most reliable way is to specify the specific departure time in the context area, such as `[Based on the peak summer season in July next year]`, to prompt the AI to account for the variable.

- **Q: Can I include rental car fees or estimated fuel costs in the quote?**
  - A: Of course! Add a condition like `[Planning to use a rental car]` to the travel style item, and give specific instructions in the task section like _"Include local fuel costs and rental car fees based on an estimated travel distance of 300km."_ It will neatly distribute even the vehicle-related budget, which is the most difficult to calculate.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Forced Real-time Web Search:** By assigning a clear action verb—"calculate estimated costs by searching the web"—it fundamentally blocks the AI's hallucination problem of creating false information based only on past training data.
2. **Safety Net (Emergency Fund) Construction:** It forces the allocation of 10% of the total budget as an emergency fund to prepare for 'hidden costs' that travelers most commonly overlook in the planning stage, preventing painful budget overruns in advance.
3. **Unit Price-based Precision Targeting:** By setting realistic standard unit prices such as '15,000 KRW for lunch, 50,000 KRW for dinner' instead of grouping total food costs vaguely, the effectiveness of the final estimate is maximized.
