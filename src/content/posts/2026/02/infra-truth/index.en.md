> _"When everyone is shouting to dig for gold, the only one making real money is the merchant selling shovels."_

Currently, the IT industry is engulfed in blind overheating and uncontrollable noise. Every morning, news of new AI model releases floods in, and social media is filled with fear marketing claiming that if you don't adopt AI right now, you will fall behind the times and perish. Countless companies are scrambling to allocate massive budgets and are desperate to label their services with "AI-powered" to avoid being left behind. But what is the reality? Actual users experiencing these services are complaining of fatigue and getting tired of similar, hollow features that lack any real differentiation.

While management pops champagne, intoxicated by rosy fantasies and vanity metrics, the atmosphere at the practical level, where results must be proven by numbers, is turning cold. Looking at the astronomical cloud server costs billed every month and the API call costs flowing out to Large Language Model (LLM) providers, one can't help but feel a fundamental skepticism about whether this business is sustainable. A massive, unbridgeable gap has already opened between the brilliant "AI Utopia" promised to the public and the harsh profitability of the business field.

In this chaos, the first question we should ask is not "Which AI model is smarter?" but **"Who is actually making the real money in this massive gold rush?"** In the 19th-century California Gold Rush, the people who actually amassed great wealth were not the miners who rushed to dig for gold, but the merchants who sold them shovels and jeans. Today's AI revolution is following exactly the same trajectory. While numerous <b>"AI Wrappers"</b> bleed out in cutthroat competition, the entities quietly raking in astronomical amounts of cash behind the scenes are the companies that dominate GPUs and data center infrastructure.

Promising AI startups that received tens of billions of won in funding are shutting down due to capital depletion in just one or two years. What is their common failure? It's that they tried to compete with services that were merely thin shells over someone else's open-source models, without any overwhelming "Economic Moat." On the other hand, the <b>Infrastructure Layer</b>, which provides the essential computing resources (Compute) for training and inferencing AI models, has entered an unprecedented super cycle as demand explodes.

Ultimately, the truth we face is clear. We must throw the blind "AI adoption" slogan into the trash and think about how to convert our company's unique domain data and business logic into value, free from infrastructure dependency. Hollow services will eventually be reduced to subcontractors paying the server bills for infrastructure companies. Do not waste your time and capital on meaningless endeavors. Through the professional analysis prompts provided below, objectively diagnose the true face of business that will be revealed once the bubble clears, and establish a clear strategy for survival.
