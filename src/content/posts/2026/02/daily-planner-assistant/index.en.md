## 🚀 The Solution: "Dedicated Daily Planning AI Assistant"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, rough overview of today's schedule and immediate priorities.

> **Role:** You are a `[Productivity Consultant]`.
> **Task:** Review the `[Today's To-Do List]` below, determine the priorities, and create an hourly schedule for today.
>
> **Today's To-Do List:**
> `[Brain-dump all the tasks you need to do today here]`

<br>

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a flawless time-blocking schedule that precisely weighs task importance, respects your personal work rhythm, and factors in hard deadlines.

> **Role:** You are a 10-year veteran `[Productivity and Time Management Expert]` and my personal executive assistant.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I have an overwhelming amount of tasks to handle today, and my mind is cluttered. I don't know where to start.
> - Goal: Categorize my to-do list using the Eisenhower Matrix (Urgency/Importance) and, based on that, draft the most efficient daily schedule using the 'Time Blocking' method.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Analyze the provided `[Today's To-Do List]` and classify the items into 4 categories: DO, DECIDE, DELEGATE, and DELETE.
> 2. Draft a time-blocking schedule taking into account `[My Working Hours]` and `[Fixed Appointments]`.
> 3. Allocate the 'DO (Urgent & Important)' tasks that require the highest concentration to my `[Peak Focus Hours]`.
> 4. Incorporate a cycle of 90 minutes of focused work followed by a 15-minute break into the schedule.
>
> **Variables:**
>
> - `[Today's To-Do List]`: (e.g., Draft proposal for Company A, prep for team weekly sync, reply to emails, lunch meeting, review marketing metrics)
> - `[My Working Hours]`: (e.g., 09:30 AM - 06:30 PM)
> - `[Fixed Appointments]`: (e.g., 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM Team Meeting, 12:30 PM - 01:30 PM Lunch)
> - `[Peak Focus Hours]`: (e.g., 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Make reasonable estimations for the time required for each task and allocate them accordingly.
> - Output the result strictly using Markdown tables to maximize readability. (One table for Priorities, one for the Schedule)
> - The schedule MUST explicitly include break times and lunch hours.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Avoid packing the schedule too tightly. You MUST allocate about 30 minutes of Buffer Time in the afternoon to account for unexpected situations and emergencies.

The true value of this prompt isn't just in "making a timetable"—it lies entirely in **eliminating Decision Fatigue**. Every morning, agonizing over "Should I do this first or that?" consumes a massive amount of cognitive energy. 

I keep this exact prompt saved as a Notion template. During my morning subway commute, I brain-dump every task that comes to mind on my smartphone and feed it to the AI. By the time I arrive at the office, a perfectly structured priority list and timeline are waiting for me.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: How detailed do I need to be when listing my tasks?**
  - A: You can just jot down keywords as they come to mind; the AI is incredibly good at reading the context. However, adding rough time estimates, like "Prep for Company A meeting (30 mins)," will yield a much more accurate and realistic schedule.

- **Q: What if an emergency meeting pops up in the middle of the day?**
  - A: The core philosophy of time blocking is flexibility. If a sudden meeting occurs, simply tell the AI in the same chat: "An urgent 1-hour meeting just came up at 2:00 PM. Please adjust my afternoon schedule." It will instantly recalibrate your day.

- **Q: Which AI model works best for this?**
  - A: This prompt heavily relies on contextual understanding and logical reasoning. Therefore, I highly recommend Claude 3.5 Sonnet or ChatGPT (GPT-4o), which excel at structural logic. While the Basic Version works fine on free models, handling the complex constraints of the Pro Version requires a top-tier model.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1.  **Integrating the Eisenhower Matrix:** Instead of just listing tasks, this teaches the AI a mental framework to filter by 'Importance' and 'Urgency', resulting in highly objective, bias-free prioritization.
2.  **Respecting Biorhythms:** By utilizing the `[Peak Focus Hours]` variable, the schedule isn't just mathematically divided; it's a personalized workflow tailored to your unique daily energy levels.
3.  **Built-in Realism (Buffer Time):** By enforcing "Buffer Time" in the constraints, it prevents the AI from generating a suffocating, hyper-optimized schedule that falls apart at the first disruption. It creates a schedule that actually lets you breathe.
