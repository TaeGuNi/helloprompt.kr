> _"The service development is finished, but what on earth should I name it? Are you tearing your hair out, trapped in naming hell?"_

![Startup/Brand Naming: Creating a Memorable Name](/images/hooks/startup-brand-naming-ideas.jpg)

Toss, Danggeun, Yanolja, Baedal Minjok. The common thread among successful brands that dominate the market is that they are easy to call, intuitive, and unforgettable once heard. Many people spend months sweating over service development, only to find themselves **endlessly delaying the launch because they can't decide on the most important thing: the 'name.'** No matter how much the team brainstorms in front of a whiteboard in a cramped meeting room, they often end up circling around the same cliché and childish words.

"We must decide by this weekend!" they resolve, but the names brought to the Monday morning meeting are all somehow clumsy or lacking. They are either too similar to competitors to be differentiated, too difficult to pronounce to be remembered by customers, or, upon **searching domains and trademarks**, found to be already taken. When this frustrating process repeats for two or three weeks, team morale hits rock bottom.

In desperation, you look into professional naming agencies or brand consulting firms, only to be met with **staggering quotes** ranging from thousands to tens of thousands of dollars. For early-stage startups, project teams, or solo entrepreneurs struggling with a limited budget, this is not an easy cost to bear. Eventually, pressured by launch deadlines, they go to market with a hastily chosen name, only to experience the painful failure of being quietly buried without leaving any impression on the target audience. Even if you pour thousands into marketing, **if customers cannot remember your brand name**, all efforts become like pouring water into a leaking bucket.

But you no longer need to waste precious time tearing your hair out in 'naming hell.' With a single, precisely engineered prompt, **AI can brilliantly replace the role of a heavy-hitting planner from an expensive agency.** It has moved far beyond mechanically combining words to allow for strategic naming that deeply analyzes your brand's philosophy and reflects current market trends and linguistic play.

All you need to do is give the AI a clear persona as a world-class brand director and input specific details about your target audience and the core values of your service. Clever and sophisticated ideas that wouldn't emerge after days of all-nighters will flood the screen in just 5 minutes. And these aren't just lists of names; they come complete with detailed planning intent explaining **'why this name can move the target customer's heart'** and even hypothetical slogans.

For those of you facing a wall in naming as you prepare for a startup or a new project launch, we present the **ultimate brand naming prompt that strikes a chord with customers**. With a single copy-paste and execution, experience the magical moment where the abstract ideas floating in your head materialize into a powerful brand name worth thousands of dollars!

## 🚀 This is How Real Experts Write

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variable]` sections in parentheses to suit your situation and deploy it immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this for a quick sketch of ideas.

> **Role:** You are a naming expert who has birthed innovative brands like 'Baedal Minjok' and 'Toss'.
> 
> **Task:** Create 5 names for a new service that provides `[Core Service Function]`. They must be easy to call and memorable once heard.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need overwhelming results at the level of a naming proposal worth thousands of dollars. Copy the prompt below and modify only the content inside `[` `]` to fit your actual service situation.

> **Role:** You are a top-tier industry brand naming director who creates names that are intensely etched into the public's mind, like 'Baedal Minjok', 'Toss', and 'Danggeun'.
>
> **Context:**
> I am about to launch a new mobile app service. I need an overwhelming brand name that can occupy a unique position in a highly competitive market.
>
> **Service Information:**
> - **Main Function:** `[e.g., An app that recommends custom recipes when you input leftover ingredients]`
> - **Core Values:** `[e.g., Saving food costs, reducing food waste, the joy of cooking]`
> - **Target Audience:** `[e.g., Budget-conscious students living alone, single-person households new to cooking]`
>
> **Task:**
> Utilize the following 3 naming techniques to suggest **a total of 10 elite name candidates**.
>
> 1. **Intuitive Naming (4 names):** Simple and clear names where the function or benefit is associated within a second (e.g., Zigbang, Yogiyo)
> 2. **Portmanteau Naming (3 names):** Sensible combinations of two or more words to pique curiosity (e.g., Netflix, Musinsa)
> 3. **Symbolic/Abstract Naming (3 names):** Unique and emotional names that contain the service's vision or story (e.g., Apple, Zigzag)
>
> For each name candidate, provide the **"Logical reason for recommending this name (persuasion points)"** and an **"Expected slogan."**
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Must be short, between 2 to 4 characters (for Korean) or syllables.
> - Must be easy to pronounce and resistant to typos when typed on a smartphone.
> - Korean, English, or clever neologisms are all welcome.
>
> **Warning:**
> - Strictly exclude trademarks already famously in use by other brands or cheesy names. Do not make up uncertain information; state only facts.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The true power of this prompt lies in **'making the AI explain the logical validity itself.'** If you are just handed a name without context, you are bound to have endless doubts like "What does this even mean?" or "Does the name look too light?" However, the moment you read the **'planning intent'** and the attractive **'slogan'** derived by the AI, you will find yourself slapping your knee and saying, "Ah, that's why it's perfect!" This is not just a task of mixing words; it is the very process of **building a Brand Identity** that breathes soul into the brand. Beginners only ask for a name, but professionals ask for the reason and story why that name must exist. This prompt is the result of transplanting that 'professional mindset' directly into the AI's brain.

**💡 Variable Control for Practice (Constraint Control):**
The most important variables in this prompt are `[Target Audience]` and `[Core Values]`. Many people make the mistake of vaguely entering something like 'office workers in their 20s and 30s' for the target audience. However, to maximize the AI's creativity, you must **sharpen the persona.** For example, try specifying the target as 'a 3rd-year junior marketer for whom an Americano is essential every morning on the way to work.' The AI will accurately catch their lifestyle, frequently used slang, and needs, combining words that are much more sensible and relatable. The same goes for `[Core Values]`. Instead of the one-dimensional word 'convenience,' write 'the ultimate resolution of laziness where everything is solved with a single flick of a finger while lying on the sofa after work.' Surprisingly witty and memorable names will be born. The more specific the situation you provide, the sharper and more attractive the results the AI will return.

**⚠️ Caution and Verification Step (Troubleshooting):**
Even if the AI suggests a chillingly perfect name, you must **immediately cross-verify the following two things** before cheering. No matter how good a name is, it is useless if it cannot be yours.

1. **Trademark Search (KIPRIS Cross-Verification):** Access the Korea Intellectual Property Rights Information Service (KIPRIS) and first check if an identical or similar trademark is already registered. If targeting the Korean market, a trademark is your lifeline. No matter how clever a name is, if someone else has claimed it first, you cannot legally use it and may have to bear massive litigation costs later. Even if you change the spacing or cleverly alter the English spelling, it can still be flagged as a similar trademark, so search thoroughly.
2. **Global Domain Availability:** Check on sites like Gabia, Hosting KR, or Namecheap to see if `.com` or `.co.kr` domains can be registered. If someone has already taken the desired domain, I recommend considering intuitive variations of the spelling (e.g., reflecting trends like Flickr or Tumblr by omitting vowels) or actively considering extensions like `.io`, `.ai`, or `.co` that fit recent IT and AI startup trends.

After narrowing down the 10 candidates obtained through this prompt to the 2 or 3 you like best, it is also an excellent strategy to conduct **A/B testing** with team members or potential customers. If you collect intuitive feedback while showing the 'expected slogans' provided by the AI, you will be able to complete a perfect naming that reduces the failure rate to near 0%.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: The AI keeps suggesting cheesy or cliché names. What should I do?**
  - A: It is highly likely that the target audience and core values you entered in the prompt are too plain and vague. Instead of a one-dimensional setting like 'women in their 20s,' try describing the persona specifically and sharply, such as **'women in their 20s who value their own firm tastes and enjoy exhibiting them on Instagram.'** The magnitude of creativity the AI exerts is perfectly proportional to the depth of detail you provide.

- **Q: Is there a specific AI model you recommend for Korean naming?**
  - A: For tasks that require capturing subtle **'wordplay'** or a unique **'linguistic flavor'**, like naming or copywriting, the **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** model shows overwhelming performance. On the other hand, ChatGPT (GPT-4o) tends to derive somewhat stiff and textbook-honest names, so I recommend alternating between the two models to fit the character of your brand.

- **Q: I've narrowed it down to 2 or 3 names I like, but I can't make a final decision.**
  - A: It's good to show those names to acquaintances who fit the target customer group and ask for their intuitive feelings, such as "What image first comes to mind when you hear this name?" Alternatively, you can ask the AI back, presenting `[Candidate A]` and `[Candidate B]`, and say, **"Objectively compare and analyze the pros and cons and market feasibility of each name"** to undergo verification based on data.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **The Magic of Persona Maximization:** By assigning a very specific and authoritative role as a "top brand naming director," we induce the AI to maximize its own response standards and exclude mediocre results.
2. **Providing a Framework (Technique Categorization):** Instead of leaving the AI in a blank state, we expand the spectrum of ideas and secure a systematic structure by forcibly designating practical naming techniques such as **'Intuitive/Portmanteau/Symbolic.'**
3. **Value Proof Constraint:** By making it mandatory to describe the "logical reason for recommendation" at the bottom of the prompt, we derive branding ideas backed by **'planning that actually sells'** rather than just a list of words that look cool.
