## 📝 Job Offer Comparison & Counter Offer Strategy Prompt

- **🎯 Recommended for:** Professionals, job seekers, and mid-level managers navigating multiple offers
- **⏱️ Time Required:** 5 minutes
- **🤖 Recommended AI:** Any conversational model (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 🚀 **Utility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

> _"Company A is dangling a massive salary, Company B promises unbeatable work-life balance... and my current employer just matched their offer. What’s my next move?"_

Juggling multiple job offers is the ultimate "good problem to have," but it can quickly trigger analysis paralysis. This is not the moment to rely on gut feelings or emotional impulses—it’s the time for ruthless, calculated strategy. By deploying AI as your personal negotiation agent, you can objectively dismantle competing offers and execute a flawless counter-offer playbook, maximizing both your financial compensation and long-term career trajectory.

---

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. Objectively weigh competing job offers by evaluating base salary, equity, benefits, and long-term growth potential.
2. Draft a persuasive, risk-free counter-offer email to your top-choice employer, leveraging alternative offers to your advantage.
3. Strategically dissect the hidden risks and rewards of accepting a retention counter-offer from your current company.

## 🚀 Solution: "The Offer Negotiator"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Deploy this prompt when you need a rapid, high-level comparative overview.

> **Role:** You are an expert career strategist and executive negotiation coach.
> **Task:** I have received job offers from multiple companies. Help me objectively compare their terms and draft a compelling counter-offer strategy to negotiate a higher base salary.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Deploy this prompt for a granular, in-depth analysis and a custom-tailored negotiation script.

> **Role:** You are an elite career strategist and executive negotiation coach.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I have received the following job offers and need to make a strategic decision.
>   - **Company A:** Salary `[$60,000]`, includes stock options, high-pressure environment (frequent overtime), highly prestigious brand name.
>   - **Company B:** Salary `[$55,000]`, excellent work-life balance, fast-growing startup trajectory.
> - Goal: My primary objective is to join Company A, but I need to negotiate a higher base salary using Company B's offer as competitive leverage.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Comparative Analysis:** Formulate a pros and cons matrix directly comparing both offers. Recommend the optimal choice based on distinct career priorities (e.g., Stability vs. Aggressive Growth).
> 2. **Counter-Offer Strategy:** Draft a polite, professional, yet resolute email to Company A. The email must state that while I am highly motivated to join their team, I possess another competitive offer. Clearly indicate that I will sign immediately if they can match a base salary of `[$65,000]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Maintain an exceptionally professional and appreciative tone; absolutely do not sound arrogant, demanding, or entitled.
> - Ensure the email conveys genuine enthusiasm for the role while firmly anchoring the financial requirements.
> - Provide strategic counsel on whether to explicitly name Company B or keep the competing offer anonymous.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

Negotiating a job offer often feels like walking a tightrope, but in reality, it is standard corporate protocol. HR departments and hiring managers fully expect top-tier candidates to negotiate. The underlying anatomy of a successful counter-offer relies entirely on **leverage** and **tone**. 

By utilizing AI to architect your communication, you effectively strip the anxiety and emotional volatility out of the equation. This prompt ensures your response strikes the elusive balance between expressing genuine enthusiasm for the opportunity and holding a firm boundary on your market value. Remember, the absolute worst-case scenario is a polite "No, this is our final offer"—at which point, you still have a fantastic job waiting for you!

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Is it risky to negotiate? Will they rescind the offer entirely?**
  - A: It is incredibly rare for a reputable company to pull an offer simply because a candidate negotiated professionally. As long as your tone remains respectful and your request is grounded in reality (typically 5–10% above the initial baseline), the worst outcome is usually just a polite refusal.

- **Q: Should I disclose my external offers to my current employer?**
  - A: Only do this if you are genuinely prepared to walk away. Weaponizing an external offer to force a raise can severely damage long-term trust. Use the AI to meticulously weigh the political risks of accepting a retention counter-offer from your current boss.

- **Q: Do I need a paid subscription to ChatGPT or Claude to run this?**
  - A: Not at all. The free tiers of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are more than capable of processing these variables and generating elite-level negotiation emails.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** Assigning the AI the persona of an "executive negotiation coach" forces the model to generate highly professional, persuasive, and tactful corporate vernacular.
2. **Contextual Clarity:** By explicitly defining the pros and cons of each competing offer, the AI can prescribe bespoke, tailored advice rather than regurgitating generic negotiation clichés.
3. **Behavioral Constraints:** The strict directive to "not sound arrogant" is the linchpin of this prompt. It ensures the drafted email preserves goodwill, which is critical when negotiating with your future colleagues and managers.

### ❌ Before (Just Accepting)

```text

```

_(Result: You leave money on the table and accept the baseline terms without a fight.)_

### ✅ After (Attempting Counter Offer)

```text
"Thank you so much for the generous offer. Company A remains my top choice, and I am incredibly excited about the prospect of joining the team.

However, I am currently weighing another competitive offer that provides a slightly different compensation structure. If Company A is able to adjust the base salary to $65,000, I would be thrilled to accept and sign the offer immediately."
```

_(Result: A highly professional pushback. Success could yield a significant bump in base salary—and even if they decline, you lose absolutely nothing.)_
