### 🥉 Basic Version (Basic)

Useful when you need to quickly summarize core points and grasp overall technology trends.

> **Role:** You are a senior IT technology analyst.
>
> **Task:** Technically analyze the impact of the latest `[Apple M6 chip]` on `[mobile local LLM execution]` and summarize it into 3 key points.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need a dense and high-quality in-depth report, such as for an official tech blog post or sharing at an internal technical seminar.

> **Role:**
> You are a senior hardware engineer in Silicon Valley and a professional tech writer. You have an exceptional ability to explain complex Deep Tech knowledge clearly and insightfully so that both developers and non-developers can intuitively understand it.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Analysis Target: `[Apple M6 chip and next-generation Neural Engine]`
> - Core Theme: `[Optimizing local LLM (13B~30B parameter class) execution in mobile environments]`
> - Purpose: To share the latest on-device AI trends and hardware technical innovations with the internal development and planning teams.
>
> **Task:**
> Write an in-depth technical analysis report according to the following structure.
>
> 1. Introduction: Limitations of cloud AI and the background of the rise of on-device AI
> 2. Architecture Analysis: Explain how `[M6's Neural Engine and Unified Memory Bandwidth]` are optimized for LLM inference operations (matrix multiplication, etc.)
> 3. Performance and Efficiency: Substantial changes in inference speed (tokens/sec) and power efficiency (heat control)
> 4. Security and UX: Business value brought by `[local data hardware encryption through Secure Enclave]`
> 5. Developer Ecosystem: Changes in framework compatibility and fine-tuning, such as `[CoreML, Metal, mlx library]`
> 6. Conclusion: The future change in the mobile AI paradigm that this technology will bring
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - To ensure mobile readability, never use tables; instead, organize in the form of high-readability bullet points (List).
> - When technical terms (e.g., quantization, parameter, memory bottleneck) appear, provide a brief explanation to aid understanding in context.
> - Maintain the tone and manner of an objective and concise technical document.
> - Be sure to **bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Exclude uncertain hardware benchmark figures or rumors and focus on fact-based architectural structural advantages. (Strictly prevent hallucinations)

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

This prompt goes far beyond a simple IT news summary or translator level; it is meticulously designed to answer the fundamental question of technology: **"How does a minute change in hardware specs specifically impact the vast software ecosystem?"** Anyone can say 'performance has improved,' but explaining that the performance improvement is **due to the expansion of 'Unified Memory Bandwidth,' which plays a decisive role in reducing quantization loss for 13B parameter class models**, is a domain possible only for experts. We have forcibly injected this 'expert's eye' into the AI through the prompt.

The true value of this tool can be seen by looking closely at the **Constraint Control** within the prompt. While `[Apple M6 chip]` was used as a basic example, the variable area enclosed in brackets `[ ]` has infinite scalability. If you need to analyze **NVIDIA's next-generation Blackwell GPU**, you just need to change the analysis target and modify the variable in the second heading to `[impact of HBM3e memory mounting on NVLink bandwidth]`. Or if you want to analyze the entry of the **Qualcomm Snapdragon X Elite** chipset into the Windows ecosystem, a brilliant insight report with a completely different context will unfold before your eyes in 3 minutes as soon as you substitute the variables with the relevant keywords.

Particularly, the part most effort was put into in this prompt is the **setting of constraints for reader targeting**. The biggest cause of failure in tech reports is the writer getting absorbed and using difficult technical jargon alone. To fundamentally block this, I specified the condition: _"When technical terms appear, provide a brief explanation to aid understanding in context."_ Thanks to this one line of constraint, every time the AI uses words like 'quantization' or 'parameter,' it automatically adds a kind explanation in parentheses like _(model compression technology)_. This is a top-tier tech writing technique that allows readers to naturally acquire deep technical knowledge without breaking the flow of the text.

If the final approver of your report is a C-level executive from a non-developer background, you can see a huge effect just by slightly tweaking the tone and manner of the constraints. Try throwing a follow-up question prompt: <b>"Rewrite focusing on the business impact of the technology on our company's productivity improvement, server operation cost reduction, and on-premise class strong security rather than technical details."</b> The difficult architecture analysis report will instantly transform into a sharp strategic proposal that helps management decision-making. For practitioners who run latest tech blogs or need to regularly brief management and related departments on technology trends, this prompt will be **the most powerful, reliable, and best partner that will revolutionary advance your clock-out time.**
