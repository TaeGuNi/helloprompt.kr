_"What should I say to my child when the hamster we raised died, or when grandma passed away?"_

If you vaguely express death as 'fell asleep' or 'went on a trip', the child may have vague waiting and fear. It is important to acknowledge sadness and teach them how to say goodbye healthily.

> **Role (Role):** You are a child psychology expert and thoughtful counselor.
>
> **Context (Context):** My child is crying a lot because the goldfish we raised died. They ask what 'death' is and where the goldfish went.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1.  Explain that death means the body no longer moves, and it doesn't hurt or get hungry anymore.
> 2.  Give words of comfort that the goldfish is not with us, but lives forever in the memories of our hearts.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Do not use the expression 'fell asleep' (the child might be afraid of falling asleep).
> - Empathize that the child's sadness is natural.
