## 🚀 How Real Pros Write It

This is a prompt perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` in brackets to match your situation, and put it to work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need basic automated income/expense calculation formulas for your budget.

> **Role:** You are an authority on writing Notion formulas.
>
> **Task:**
> 1. In a Notion database, there are 'Amount (Number)' and 'Type (Select: Income/Expense)' properties.
> 2. Combine these two data points to write an `if` function formula that outputs the amount as is if the 'Type' is Income, and adds a minus (-) sign to calculate the 'Actual Amount' if it is Expense.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when designing a relational database that goes beyond simple recording to perform perfect budget control and asset flow analysis.

> **Role:** You are a Notion Ambassador and an asset management specialist proficient in database design.
>
> **Context:**
> - Background: I strongly feel the limitations of existing automated budget apps. I want to build an 'All-in-One Budget System' using Notion to proactively control and analyze my spending.
> - Goal: Complete a Notion dashboard that visually monitors `[Current Spending]` against `[Monthly Budget]` and automatically calculates statistics by category.
>
> **Task:**
> 1. **DB Structure Design:** Explain step-by-step how to separate the 'Monthly Dashboard (Budget)' DB and the 'Daily Expense History' DB, and how to connect them using Relation and Rollup features.
> 2. **Budget Progress Formula (Progress Bar):** Write a Notion formula that calculates cumulative spending against the monthly budget and displays the remaining budget as a visual percentage bar (e.g., `▓▓▓▓▓░░░░░ 50%`). (Must use `slice` and `repeat` functions)
> 3. **No-Spend Challenge Calendar:** Create a conditional formula for the 'Daily Expense History' calendar view that automatically renders a `[🎉]` emoji next to the date if the day's spending is `[$0]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Must be written based on the latest Notion Formula 2.0 syntax.
> - Do not use tables for the explanation to ensure mobile readability; organize it in a high-readability bulleted list.
> - Add detailed comments and explanations so that even a Notion beginner can understand how each formula works.
> - Use **bold** for important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
> - For complex formulas, thoroughly verify that parentheses are paired correctly and property names match perfectly before outputting. Do not hallucinate syntax you aren't sure of.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can't I automatically link my credit card transaction history to Notion? It's a hassle to enter it manually every time.**
  - A: While you can set it up to automatically pull credit card history by linking the Notion API with tools like Zapier, <b>I personally strongly advise against it.</b> The essence of keeping a budget is to fully feel and reflect on the 'painful sting of spending money' at the moment it happens. The process of manually typing for just 5 minutes every evening and reviewing your day's unconscious spending is a top-tier financial habit that no automation tool can replace.
- **Q: Notion formulas are so complex that I keep getting errors. How do I solve this?**
  - A: Copy the text from the Notion formula window where the error occurred and send a prompt back to the AI saying, "A `Syntax error` occurred in this formula. Find exactly where the problem is and fix it." It will perfectly catch minor debugging issues like missing parentheses or data type conflicts (number vs. string) in just a second.
