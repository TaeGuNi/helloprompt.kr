## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Break it down and connect:** Designing a sequential prompt chain that follows a trail is overwhelmingly superior to a single, vast, and vague instruction.
2. **Assign multiple personas (Roles):** Strictly separate roles into Planner, Executor, and Critic to make the AI debate itself and compensate for its own flaws.
3. **Run the feedback loop:** Boldly discard the AI's first result. You must force a self-improvement process where it criticizes itself, identifies fatal weaknesses, and revises them.

## 🚀 How the Real Experts Write

Use these when you need to set the overall framework of a project and divide roles clearly, or to derive the best results within a single conversation session. Copy the prompts below and fill in the `[Variables]` inside the brackets to fit your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this lightly when you need to set the overall framework of a project and divide roles clearly.

> **Role:** From now on, you are a senior `[Project Manager]` with 10 years of experience.
>
> **Task:** Plan 3 key milestones necessary to successfully achieve the `[Project Goal]`. For each stage, list the specific staff (roles) that should be involved and their concrete action items in a table format.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This is a powerful and disruptive prompt that makes the AI manifest as a team of three top-tier experts (Strategic Planner, Professional Copywriter, Senior Critic) within a single conversation session, completing everything from planning to rigorous cross-examination on its own.

> **Role:** From now on, you are a **'Super Agent'** acting out an entire team of three top experts in the industry (Strategic Planner, Professional Copywriter, Senior Critic).
>
> **Context:**
> 
> - Background: I currently need a high-resolution `[Type of Output, e.g., Sales Landing Page Copy]` for `[Subject/Product]`.
> - Goal: To derive a compelling result that stole the reader's heart and possesses logic that overwhelms competitors.
>
> **Task:**
> You must perform the following 3 steps sequentially. (Never pour everything out at once; think deeply for each step.)
>
> 1. **Phase 1 (Strategic Planner):** Logically plan a clear target persona, key selling points (USP), and a full outline for `[Subject]`.
> 2. **Phase 2 (Professional Copywriter):** Write an actual draft based on the plan from Phase 1. Maintain a `[Desired Tone, e.g., Professional and Persuasive]` tone and manner.
> 3. **Phase 3 (Senior Critic):** Mercilessly and critically deconstruct the draft from Phase 2. Point out logical leaps, cliché/boring expressions, and lack of evidence. Then, **incorporate all of this criticism to output a dramatically improved 'Final Version' again.**
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Clearly mark each phase with a **"### Phase X: [Phase Name]"** Markdown header as it begins.
> - The Senior Critic must never give meaningless praise like "Well written." Only dig into weaknesses.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Between Phase 2 and Phase 3, you must first show a **"Summary of Criticism and Improvement Direction"** pointed out by the critic. I need to be able to verify the thought process.

## 💡 Writer's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

Those new to Agentic Workflow prompts often ask, "Can't I just ask it to write well once?" But the real destructive power of this prompt lies not just in outputting a long piece of text, but in the operation of the <b>'Self-Refinement'</b> system hidden in the final third step. If you apply this workflow directly to real business tasks, you will be shocked by the chilling process where the AI goes beyond simple polishing and actually finds its own logical loopholes and builds a solid defensive logic against them.

The biggest mistake we often make is trying to trust and use the 'first draft' the AI produces. Just as the human brain doesn't pull out a perfect masterpiece on the first try, LLMs (Large Language Models) also tend to output answers by combining the most statistically common and safe (predictable) tokens in the initial generation stage. By forcing a very demanding and hostile persona called the <b>'Senior Critic'</b> into the system, you can perfectly block edge cases, potential backlash from target customers, and fatal logical leaps that a human planner might have missed. The Senior Critic mercilessly hacks away at the draft, saying, "This copy is too cliché," or "I see no differentiation compared to competitors." And it is only when the <b>re-write</b> is performed while accepting that painful feedback that the AI's true reasoning power and creativity explode.

This is the most core and immediate form of practical application of the <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">"Agentic Workflow"</span>, which world-class AI scholars like Professor Andrew Ng of Stanford University have praised as a key task for next-generation AI. You don't have to code and physically weave multiple AI agents together to create a workflow. If you design the separation of roles and the feedback loop within a single prompt like this, it becomes a superb system.

Of course, it may take a little more time to sophisticatedly design the initial prompt and carefully enter the `[Variables]` than simply typing "Write a blog post." But keep this in mind: the mere 5 minutes invested in writing the prompt will perfectly protect your precious overtime hours that would otherwise be spent sighing and tearing apart a sloppy result for 3 hours. The Phase 3 result finally generated through this process 100% guarantees <b>overwhelming high-end quality</b> that can be put to work immediately without further large-scale modifications or polishing. With a single sophisticated prompt execution, your ordinary work will be reborn as a flawless masterpiece completed after a night of fierce brainstorming by experts with decades of experience. Now, the quality of the result depends not on the limits of AI, but on how excellent a team you set up.

One important tip for applying the prompt in practice is to sharpen the `[Type of Output]` and `[Subject/Product]` variables inside the `[Context]` block as specifically as possible. Instead of simply writing 'diet food,' try adding detail like 'Instagram sponsored ad copy for a plant-based protein shake targeting professional women in their 30s.' As the resolution of the input variables increases, the Strategic Planner's targeting becomes sharper, and the Critic's standards become even harsher. Just by observing the AI's reasoning process and seeing this happen with your own eyes, you will feel your own planning skills and critical thinking abilities grow exponentially.

To truly make this prompt chain your own, you need an attitude that isn't afraid of failure in the first few attempts. Sometimes the Critic's standards may be too strict, causing the draft to be overturned in an unexpected direction, or conversely, the persona settings may be loose, resulting in weak feedback. In such cases, don't panic and try fine-tuning the constraints of each Phase. Much like building teamwork with real employees, as you repeat the interaction feedback loop with the AI, your virtual Avengers team will evolve into an increasingly invincible organization. Now that you hold this powerful weapon, there are no obstacles left to block your potential.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does it work well on free models (e.g., ChatGPT free version)?**
  - A: It can function, but there are clear limits to the quality of the output. This prompt strongly requires high-level 'complex reasoning' and 'context maintenance' to remember a long conversation. To perform three different expert roles without confusion, it is strongly recommended to use top-tier models like GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet.

- **Q: What if the AI ignores the instructions and outputs all 3 steps at once?**
  - A: This occasionally happens when the context gets long. In such cases, try adding a strong control sentence in the Task section: _"Wait for my approval after each Phase ends before moving to the next Phase."_ This can stop the AI from rushing and significantly increase your control.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Multi-Persona:** It simultaneously assigns three complementary roles to the AI rather than a single role. This is a powerful device that forces a context switch within a single model to minimize confirmation bias.
2. **Chain-of-Thought:** It forces the AI to follow the exact working method of top human experts: [Planning → Execution → Harsh Verification]. This process dramatically reduces hallucinations and maximizes logical density.
3. **Iterative Refinement:** An AI's first answer almost always stays at a 'good enough' level. The core secret that leads to a vertical rise in quality is this rigorous process of introducing a merciless Senior Critic to shatter the draft and build it back up.
