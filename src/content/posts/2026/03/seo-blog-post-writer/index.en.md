## 🚀 The Solution: "SEO Blog Post Master"
### 🥉 Basic Version
Use this when you quickly need idea development and a basic outline.
> **Role:** You are a 10-year experienced `[Content Marketer and SEO Expert]`.
> **Task:** Write a blog post draft optimized for high Google rankings based on the `[Main Keyword]`.
### 🥇 Pro Version
Use this when you need detailed SEO optimization and a perfect Markdown structure.
> **Role:** You are a `[Technical SEO Expert and Lead Copywriter]` with 15 years of experience.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: We are planning an informative post to increase traffic to our company blog.
> - Goal: Rank on the first page of Google search results and maximize reader retention time.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Write a flawless blog post using the provided `[Main Keyword]` and `[Sub Keywords]`.
> 2. Include a compelling hook and a 3-line summary at the beginning of the post to encourage the reader to scroll.
> 3. Establish the structure of the article by logically using H1, H2, and H3 tags.
> 4. Ensure the main keyword is included in the title (H1), introduction, and conclusion. Distribute the sub-keywords naturally throughout the body text.
> 5. Include a clear CTA (Call to Action) in the conclusion that prompts the reader to take action.
>
> **Variables:**
>
> - `[Main Keyword]`: (e.g., How to use Notion)
> - `[Sub Keywords]`: (e.g., Notion templates, Notion shortcuts, Work efficiency)
> - `[Target Audience]`: (e.g., Junior employees using Notion for the first time)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - The output format MUST be in Markdown.
> - Use a friendly yet professional tone.
> - Strictly avoid keyword stuffing (awkward repetition) and blend them naturally into the context.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not fabricate facts.
> - Deliver the core information concisely without unnecessary fluff.

## 💡 Author's Insight
The true value of this prompt lies in its 'forced structure'. If you simply tell the AI to "write an article," it spits out a wall of text, which Google bots absolutely hate. Google loves clean, structured documents utilizing heading tags (H1, H2, H3).
From my actual experience in the field, using this prompt drastically reduces my workload to just 'trimming a few soulless sentences from the AI's draft and tossing in one or two real-world examples from our company'. A word of caution: do not publish the AI-generated text 100% as is. You need to sprinkle in a line or two of specific 'experiences' or 'metrics' that a real person has encountered. This adds a human touch and helps avoid being flagged as duplicate content. It's a weapon that will get you off work early, so go ahead and pump out a blog post with it today.

## 🙋 FAQ
- **Q: Which model is better for writing, ChatGPT or Claude?**
  - A: For blog writing, especially natural copywriting, Claude 3.5 Sonnet is overwhelmingly superior. ChatGPT can sometimes feel a bit stiff and translated.
- **Q: What is the optimal keyword density?**
  - A: Generally, around 1-2% of the total body text is recommended. Rather than forcefully stuffing them in, the key is to place them naturally in the introduction, conclusion, and one or two H2 subheadings.
- **Q: Do I really need to set the Target Audience (Variables)?**
  - A: It is essential. The vocabulary and examples chosen by the AI will be completely different when the target audience is an 'elementary schooler' versus a 'developer with 10 years of experience'.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)
- 🎯 **Clear Role and Context:** By assigning the identity of an 'SEO Expert' and setting a goal, we guide the AI to write a thoroughly search-engine-targeted article rather than a generic essay.
- 🏗️ **Markdown Structure Directive:** Forcing the use of H1~H3 tags results in a well-structured document.
- 🚫 **Constraints:** We actively prevented penalties by prohibiting keyword stuffing.
