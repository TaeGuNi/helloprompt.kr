### 🥇 Pro Version (Multi-Platform Multi-Use Master)

This is a professional-grade prompt used when you want to simultaneously target blog search traffic, Instagram's visual virality, and Twitter's (X) explosive retweet spread with a single source.

> **Role:** You are a trend-leading `[Content Marketing Expert and Tech Copywriter with 10 years of experience]`.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I just uploaded a YouTube video that provides useful insights to viewers. Now, I want to use the original script of this video to explosively distribute traffic across various platforms.
> - Goal: Maintain the core message of the original script without damage while generating three types of derivative content perfectly tailored to each platform's algorithm and user consumption tendencies.
>
> **Source:**
>
> - Original Script: `[Paste the entire YouTube script here]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **SEO-Optimized Blog Post:** Structure this into a professional informational post using `<H2>` and `<H3>` tags, targeting high search engine rankings. Maximize readability by actively using bullet points so readers can scan quickly.
> 2. **Instagram Carousel Text:** Extract the 5 most fundamental core insights from the original. Summarize them into short, impactful phrases that can be inserted into 5 image slides. Write the text for the first slide as a powerful hooking line that will stop users from scrolling.
> 3. **Twitter (X) Viral Thread:** Start with an interesting first sentence that stimulates dopamine, and write a thread consisting of 5-7 short, intense sentences under 140 characters.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Never make up facts that do not exist in the original script. (Strictly forbid hallucinations)
> - Present the final output in Markdown format, clearly divided into three sections: Blog, Instagram, and Twitter.
> - Be sure to include appropriate emojis that match the atmosphere of each medium.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The true value of this prompt, and the decisive difference between a generic summary bot and an expert-level AI assistant, lies in the **'Power of Structuring.'** Scripts for video platforms like YouTube are characterized by long, continuous 'flow' and 'storytelling' to prevent viewer drop-off and induce immersion. However, readers on text-based platforms are different. They lack patience and want to quickly scan for the core information they want.

Therefore, simply telling the AI to *"change this script into a blog post"* is the worst approach. The AI will just change long spoken language into long written language, still outputting a tedious 'Wall of Text.' Readers will hit the 'back' button within 3 seconds of seeing such a post. For successful content repurposing, you must explicitly demand **Accurate Formatting**, such as applying `<H2>` and `<H3>` tags, summarizing keys using **Bullet Points**, and turning text into slide-by-slide carousel copy. The key is to force the form of the output to match the ecosystem of each platform by subdividing the `[Task]` section within the prompt.

Also, one must not overlook the importance of **Constraint Control**. The biggest chronic problem with generative AI is 'Hallucination.' If the AI determines the original script's content is insufficient, it will try to fill the length by making up plausible but false information. To prevent this, I've placed a strong constraint in the `[Constraints]` section at the bottom: *"Never make up facts that do not exist in the original script."* This is more than just a rule; it's a lifeline that protects the credibility of the content you publish.

Additionally, another realistic barrier faced during text conversion is the 'vastness of the original data.' A script for a YouTube video longer than 10 minutes contains a surprising amount of text. Trying to process it all at once can cause the AI to lose context or omit key points. In such cases, combining the **Chain-of-Thought** technique—dividing the script into meaningful segments (e.g., Introduction, Body 1, Body 2, Conclusion) and instructing conversion step-by-step—will yield much denser and more accurate results. For example, you can first instruct, *"Extract a hooking line for Instagram from the first half of this script,"* and then build on that output by saying, *"Now expand the specific solutions from the second half into the body of a blog post."*

In fact, as a field marketer, I experienced a tremendous leverage effect after introducing this automation pipeline into my work. In the past, I wasted more than half a day writing a new blog post and planning an Instagram feed after uploading a YouTube video. Now, once a single video script is finished, it takes less than 10 minutes to fill a week's worth of social media content calendars without any gaps. I've fully delegated the emotional labor of worrying about the tone and manner of each medium to the AI, allowing me to focus my energy solely on the essential planning of 'what message to deliver.'

As such, a prompt is not a fixed magic spell but a flexible tool that can be constantly modified and tuned to your intentions and situation. Use the skeleton of this prompt as a base, but try adding your own unique writing style, frequently used greetings, or specific channel hashtag rules to the `[Constraints]`. Time is money, and the more you use it, the more it will evolve into 'your own senior editor' that perfectly clones your brain.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Which tool is most efficient for extracting YouTube scripts (subtitles)?**
  - A: I highly recommend the Chrome extension 'YouTube Summary with ChatGPT & Claude.' With one click, you can neatly copy just the raw script text without annoying timestamps. If it's a video you uploaded yourself, downloading it directly in `.txt` format from the 'Subtitles' tab in YouTube Studio is also an excellent method.

- **Q: The original script is so long that the AI cuts off its answer in the middle or causes an error. What should I do?**
  - A: If the volume is vast, I recommend inputting the script in halves sequentially. Instruct "Summarize the Part 1 script first," and then continue with "Now summarize the Part 2 script using the same criteria" to get a perfect result without losing context.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Assigning an Expert Persona (Role):** Instead of treating the AI as a simple text summary bot, I gave it a specific identity as a 'Content Marketing Expert.' This ensures that professional marketing perspectives—such as hooking, virality, and readability—are naturally embedded in the final output.
2. **Multi-dimensional Format Instructions (Task):** I avoided vague instructions like "Summarize this nicely." By explicitly defining the output form and grammar for each target platform—such as SEO-optimized blogs, Instagram carousels, and Twitter threads—it produces high-quality results that can be put to work immediately upon execution.
3. **Ensuring Reliability through Strict Constraints (Constraints):** By strongly specifying 'not to make up facts not in the original,' I fundamentally blocked hallucinations, the fatal flaw of generative AI. This is because the life of all content stems from unwavering 'information reliability.'
