1. **Decode Cryptic Abbreviations:** Automatically translates complex knitting shorthand (like K, P, YO, ssk) into intuitive, plain-English instructions.

## 🚀 The Solution: "AI Knitting Translator Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick text-to-text translation of a complex pattern into plain English.

> **Role:** You are a friendly `[Knitting Master]` with 20 years of experience in both knitting and crochet.
>
> **Task:**
> Translate the following heavily abbreviated knitting pattern into a beginner-friendly, plain-English, step-by-step guide.
> Do not just give a literal translation; convert it into complete, easy-to-read sentences using standard, clear terminology (e.g., explain exactly what to do for 'yo' or 'ssk').
>
> **Input Pattern:**
> `[Paste the copied text of the knitting pattern here]`
>
> **Format:**
> Break it down with clear line breaks for each Row/Round so it is completely foolproof to read while knitting.


### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need to decode a visual chart or completely resize a pattern (recalculate stitches) to fit the exact yarn thickness you have.

> **Role:** You are a `[Head Knitting Designer]` who is an expert in gauge math and pattern drafting.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Project: `[e.g., Women's M size top-down sweater]`
> - Original Pattern Gauge: `[e.g., 20 sts and 28 rows = 4" (10cm) in stockinette stitch]`
> - My Swatch Gauge: `[e.g., 22 sts and 30 rows = 4" (10cm) (My yarn is thinner than the original)]`
> - Original Cast-On Stitches: `[e.g., 100 sts]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Gauge Math:** Recalculate the starting cast-on stitches and overall proportions to perfectly match my swatch gauge. Briefly explain your calculation process.
> 2. **Chart Reading:** `[Upload the image of the symbol chart]` Translate this visual motif chart into row-by-row, plain-English text instructions, starting from Row 1.
> 3. **Pro Tip:** Advise me on how the fit or drape might change since I am using a different yarn weight than the original, and give me a tension tip to make this specific motif pop.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Round all stitch calculation results to exact whole numbers.
> - If any chart symbol is blurry or ambiguous, do not guess. Explicitly state that "verification is needed." (To prevent hallucinations)

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can it also translate Crochet patterns?**
  - A: Yes, perfectly! Crochet is notorious for having different terminologies in US and UK English (for example, a 'dc' is a double crochet in the US, but a treble crochet in the UK). To avoid a sizing disaster, just add *"Please determine whether this pattern uses US or UK crochet terms first"* at the beginning of the prompt, and the AI will read the context and translate accurately.

- **Q: What if my pattern is locked in a PDF file?**
  - A: You can simply copy and paste the text into the 'Basic Version'. Alternatively, if you are using advanced models (like GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet), you can upload the entire PDF document at once and use the 'Pro Version' prompt to have the AI process the whole pattern in one go.

- **Q: Does this work for 3D projects like Amigurumi (stuffed toys)?**
  - A: Absolutely. The AI accurately calculates and explains complex increase/decrease formulas starting from a "Magic ring 6sc", completely eliminating the headache of losing count of your stitches in the round.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works)

1. **Term Mapping:** The AI has been trained on vast amounts of data from global crafting communities. It doesn't just use a dictionary; it maps complex abbreviations to the exact plain-English phrasing real knitters use.
2. **Mathematical Reasoning:** Moving beyond simple translation, the prompt forces the AI to apply the mathematical rules of gauge proportions, effectively acting as a technical editor to Redesign a custom pattern for your specific yarn.
3. **Hallucination Control:** By explicitly stating "do not guess if unsure" in the constraints, we prevent the catastrophic scenario where a single hallucinated stitch count forces you to "frog" (unravel) hours of hard work.
