- **🎯 Recommended for:** Planners, tech investors, and marketers feeling AI trend fatigue

> _"Tech companies scream 'innovation,' but practitioners complain of 'fatigue.' Is the 'Singularity' truly arriving, or is it just clever fear marketing to sell more GPUs?"_

AI innovation news is pouring in every single day. We find ourselves defenselessly exposed to a relentless storm of information noise. We wake up to sensationalist headlines claiming "a new AI model has finally completely surpassed human intelligence," and by lunchtime, we are bombarded with warning columns from consulting firms stating, "companies that don't invest astronomical sums in the latest AI infrastructure right now will go bankrupt within three years." Practitioners and executives on the ground, already struggling to focus on creating core business value, are suffering from extreme **'AI Fatigue'** as they chase after mysterious new technologies and technical jargon every day. Are those articles we read this morning really the innovations that will determine our survival?

Executives of Big Tech companies and venture capitalists praise the rosy future and the explosive growth of computing power, raising their voices to claim that the **'Technological Singularity'**—where technology escapes human control—is just around the corner. However, the reality felt by practitioners struggling in the actual business field is quite different from their presentations. A massive gap still exists between the disruptive innovations promised by the media and tech companies and the value that directly translates into increased revenue or productivity for an actual company. This phenomenon is no accident. Deeply embedded across the industry is a sophisticated commercial strategy: **'Fear Marketing.'** It intentionally inflates the pace of AI development far beyond what the public experiences, fostering vague fear to justify astronomical investments in servers, GPUs, and cloud infrastructure.

This marketing tactic cleverly manipulates the public's and corporations' FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out), forcing companies to push through uncritical capital expenditures without cool-headed ROI analysis or clear use-case verification. Blindly deciding, "everyone else is doing it, so let's just buy expensive servers first," eventually becomes a poison that erodes a company's competitiveness, leaving behind only massive sunken costs. In this critical crisis, if we fail to gain the insight to see through to the essence of technology, we are bound to become eternal victims of the endless infrastructure expansion race orchestrated by Big Tech.

However, the romantic era of blindly worshipping the latest AI technology like a religion is over. What we need most right now is cold reason that strips away the flashy packaging and thoroughly verifies practical utility. The ability to sharply separate technologically proven **'Facts'** from commercial **'Rhetoric'** aimed at short-term profit—**Critical Thinking**—is the core competitiveness of the coming era. We no longer need to be swayed by the vague fears they throw at us. We simply need a powerful filtering tool to identify the true intentions behind their messages and selectively take only the information that is practically helpful for our business.

In this post, we reveal the **"AI Hype Detector Practical Prompt,"** designed to sharply extract the core essence of real information while completely filtering out unnecessary noise from the mudflats of overwhelming hype. By applying this prompt to your daily workflow, you can transparently deconstruct the hidden commercial intent behind even the longest, most abstruse articles or complex corporate presentations in just one minute. Break free from blind fear and fatigue, and transform into a smart planner or investor who proactively evaluates and controls the real business impact of technology. It will be your most reliable weapon for safeguarding your precious time and massive capital amidst the flood of information.

## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

Use AI to coolly verify whether the flood of articles and corporate presentations are true innovations or typical FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) marketing. This powerful prompt was perfected after dozens of trials. Copy the prompt below and fill in the brackets for **[Variables]** to fit your situation for immediate use in your work.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly grasp the tone of an article and the hidden marketing intentions behind it.

> **Role:** You are a **[Tech Critic and Fact Checker]**.
>
> **Task:**
> 
> Analyze the **[Article or Corporate Announcement]** below and provide 3 clear reasons to determine whether this is a genuine technological innovation or simple hype designed for investment attraction and infrastructure sales.
>
> **Input Data:**
>
> - Content: **[Insert text to analyze]**

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this for a deep deconstruction of hidden intentions and technical limitations in corporate white papers, new product announcements, trend reports, etc.

> **Role:** You are a Silicon Valley veteran **[Tech Critic]** and a data-driven **[Investment Analyst]**.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: Recently, tech companies have been engaging in fear marketing by exaggerating the arrival of 'Singularity' or 'AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)' to induce astronomical infrastructure investments.
> - Goal: To thoroughly separate marketing rhetoric from technical reality in the input text and coolly verify the validity of the claims.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Extract all core keywords from the provided text that 'foster fear' or induce 'FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)'.
> 2. Evaluate whether the claims are technologically proven facts or merely hypothetical inferences about the future.
> 3. Closely analyze which entity (e.g., chipset manufacturer, cloud provider, etc.) stands to gain the greatest economic benefit from this announcement.
> 4. Write a detailed and critical report based on the content of the **[Text to Analyze]**.
>
> **Input Data:**
>
> - Text to Analyze: **[Insert corporate announcement, news article, or executive statement]**
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Strictly exclude emotional criticism and describe objectively based only on logic and business structures.
> - For mobile readability, never use tables; organize clearly in a highly readable bulleted list format.
> - Ensure important keywords are **bolded**.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never invent technical principles or financial data that are not certain; clearly state them as "unverifiable claims." (To prevent hallucination)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The word 'Singularity' has been one of the most enchantingly consumed words in the tech industry over the past few years, yet paradoxically, it is the most deceptive marketing keyword. If you carefully dissect the countless IT news stories and new product announcement keynotes from Big Tech companies we encounter daily, you'll easily find that the logic always boils down to: "Therefore, you must use our company's most expensive and massive model, and to do so, you must pay enormous GPU server costs."

This prompt is designed to firmly shatter our passive inertia of uncritically accepting AI news and growing vague anxiety, and instead, relentlessly dig into the most core and capitalistic question: **"Ultimately, who is the real entity reaping massive financial benefits through this grand and flashy message?"**

If you are a planner developing new business models and technology adoption strategies, or a venture investor moving tens of billions in capital, I strongly recommend using this prompt as your first verification tool every time a grandly titled AI trend report or a provocative interview with a global CEO is released. It will go beyond simply obtaining a summary of an article and serve as your best and most powerful weapon to instantly strip away flashy rhetorical noise and sharply extract only the **actual business impact and revenue creation structure** that the technology will bring.

In particular, the most important point when using this prompt is the **Role** assignment. Conversational AI models are fundamentally trained to take a friendly and receptive attitude toward the text entered by the user. Therefore, if you simply instruct it to "analyze this," there's a high probability it will parrot what the article claims and summarize only the positive outlooks. However, by forcibly injecting the picky and suspicious persona of a **'Silicon Valley veteran tech critic'** and a **'data-driven, cool-headed investment analyst'**, you force the AI to look at the loopholes and logical leaps in the text as if through a microscope.

Furthermore, the conditions included in the **Constraints**—"Never use tables" and "Specify uncertain information as unverifiable"—are brilliant moves that maximize practical utility. They push readability to the limit for executives who quickly skim reports on mobile, while also fundamentally blocking the AI's inherent fatal flaw of **Hallucination**, ensuring the report's reliability is close to 100%.

In today's harsh market environment, where the bubble of indiscriminate AI blind faith is slowly fading and the wheat is being separated from the chaff, what we truly need is not the recklessness of buying multi-billion dollar GPU clusters faster than others. It is **'Cool-headed Critical Thinking'** that sees through the flashy side of technology to find real profit models. What this prompt points out is that we should not make wrong decisions by being swayed by agitational and blind fear marketing, such as "AI will replace all human jobs tomorrow" or "unless you execute massive infrastructure investments immediately, you will be ousted from the market right now."

- **Q: Won't the AI's analysis results come out excessively biased toward criticism?**
  - A: This prompt is designed somewhat extremely to force the AI to relentlessly dig into the loopholes of the text by mandating the strict persona of a 'Tech Critic.' If you need insights from a more balanced perspective for an internal executive report, flexibly adjust the tone and manner by adding an instruction at the bottom of the Task area: *"In addition to the critical analysis, also describe one realistic and positive effectiveness that the technology can bring to our business."*

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Overwhelming Persona (Role) Injection:** By forcing the identity of a 'picky tech critic' and 'cool-headed investment analyst' onto the AI, it prevents the AI from accepting the input text at face value and being sycophantic. It shifts the AI's thinking circuit 180 degrees to track business intentions and logical errors hidden behind the scenes.
2. **Structural Fact Deconstruction Technique:** It is designed to forcibly separate emotional marketing rhetoric (exaggeration) from objective technical facts, and ultimately identify the biggest **'economic beneficiary'** of this massive agenda. This completes a sophisticated, multi-dimensional business analysis report that goes beyond a simple summary of an article.
3. **Flawless Constraint Control:** By completely banning Table output, it maximizes text readability for mobile environments during commutes. Furthermore, by forcing uncertain financial data or technical principles to be specified as "unverifiable," it sets up the safest firewall to fundamentally block fatal **Hallucination**.
