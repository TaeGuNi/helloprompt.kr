# 📝 Get Your Overnight News Briefing in Just 1 Minute with AI

- **🎯 Target Audience:** Busy professionals, marketers, and stock investors who can't afford to waste their morning commute.
- **⏱️ Time Saved:** 20 mins → 1 min
- **🤖 Recommended AI:** Perplexity, Bing Chat (Copilot), Gemini (Real-time web search capability is mandatory)

> _"Are you still scrolling mindlessly through portal front pages every morning? You might be missing the 'real signals' that directly impact your wallet and career, buried under a mountain of gossip."_

The US stock market fluctuates overnight, new IT trends emerge that could affect your projects today, and domestic economic indicators shift constantly. We don't have the luxury of time to manually search and filter all this noise. By feeding one single, perfectly engineered prompt into an AI equipped with real-time web search, you can have your own dedicated, 20-year veteran news anchor deliver a hard-hitting 1-minute briefing tailored exactly to your needs.

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. You must use an **AI with real-time web access** (like Perplexity, Copilot, or Gemini) to retrieve the latest overnight news.
2. Instead of a vague "Tell me the news," **strictly narrow down your focus** to specific industries and interests (e.g., US stocks, IT trends).
3. Always demand **source links** to verify the credibility of the information and avoid AI hallucinations.

---

## 🚀 The Solution: "My Personal Morning News Briefing Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you just need a quick, high-level overview of the night's biggest events.

> **Role:** You are a news anchor responsible for delivering fast and accurate morning briefings.
> **Task:** Summarize the top 5 most important news stories that occurred between last night and this morning.

\

### 🥇 Pro Version

Highly recommended for investors, marketers, and product managers whose survival depends on catching market trends early. Customize the bracketed `[ ]` areas to fit your specific needs.

> **Role:** You are a 20-year veteran economic/IT journalist and my personal news curator.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - I am a professional 30-something `[office worker/investor]` currently commuting to work.
> - I am particularly interested in `[US stock market closing summaries]` and `[major trends in the domestic IT industry]`.
> - I need highly actionable insights that directly impact my daily work and investment portfolio.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Summarize the overnight `[US stock market (S&P 500, NASDAQ) closing conditions]`, including key figures and the primary drivers behind the gains or losses.
> 2. Select 3 core business/economic issues from today that are likely to impact `[the local stock market or my specific industry]`.
> 3. Provide a deep dive into 1 newly trending topic or product launch within the `[IT/Tech sector]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - For every news item, you must explicitly mention the **[Related Companies / Stocks / Industries]** that will be affected.
> - To ensure reliability, you must append the **Source (Article Link)** at the end of each news item.
> - Format the entire briefing in concise bullet points so it is easy to read on a smartphone while walking.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Strictly exclude any tabloids, community rumors, or unverified speculations. Base your briefing purely on verified facts.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

The true power of this prompt lies not in merely summarizing information, but in forcing the AI to extract **Actionable Insights**. You are essentially making the AI answer the question: "So what does this news mean for my stock portfolio and my company?" That single constraint—demanding the AI to identify `[Related Companies / Stocks / Industries]`—transforms a generic news recap into a potent, institutional-grade intelligence report. I personally start every single day by opening the Perplexity app on the subway and firing off this exact prompt. It guarantees I walk into the office fully equipped and ahead of the curve.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work on the free version of ChatGPT (GPT-3.5 or GPT-4o mini)?**
  - A: It can summarize general topics, but to fetch the exact news from "last night to this morning," real-time web search capabilities are non-negotiable. Therefore, I strongly recommend using **Perplexity** or **Microsoft Copilot (Bing Chat)**, which offer powerful live search features for free.

- **Q: What if I only want to read news from specific, trusted publishers?**
  - A: Simply add a sentence to the Constraints section: "Base your summary exclusively on articles published by `[Bloomberg, The Wall Street Journal, Reuters]`."

- **Q: Can it translate foreign news into my native language for the briefing?**
  - A: Absolutely. An AI with web access can instantly read breaking news from foreign outlets (like Reuters or WSJ) and translate the briefing into your preferred language in real-time. It's the ultimate tool for breaking down global language barriers and catching global trends first.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Persona Optimization (Role):** By assigning the persona of a "20-year veteran journalist," we elevate the AI's output from a superficial summary to an analytical briefing rich with context and foresight.
2. **Hyper-Focus (Context & Task):** Narrowing the scope to specific domains like 'US stocks' or 'IT trends' maximizes the density and relevance of the information, cutting out the noise.
3. **Actionability (Constraints):** Forcing the AI to map news directly to 'related companies' ensures the output isn't just readable, but immediately usable for trading decisions or strategic planning.
