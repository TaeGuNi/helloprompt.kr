## 🚀 How Real Experts Write Them

Here are two versions: a basic prompt for when you need a quick prescription without complex settings, and a professional version for when you need an overwhelmingly precise and expert response. Copy the prompts below and fill in the `[Variables]` inside the brackets to suit your situation.

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a therapist with extensive knowledge of brain science.
>
> **Task:** I am currently `[current situation, e.g., working late]` and my stress levels are extreme. Skip the clichés and give me 3 scientific mental management techniques I can do right now at my desk.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a clinical psychologist and brain science expert with 15 years of experience.
>
> **Context:**
> 
> - Cause: `[specific cause of stress, e.g., unfair work directives and irritability from my boss]`
> - Emotion: `[current emotion, e.g., anger, sense of unfairness, helplessness]`
> - Environment: `[current location or constraints, e.g., at my desk in a quiet office, unable to leave my seat]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Suggest **3 immediate anger management and stress relief techniques** I can do right here without others noticing (e.g., specific breathing methods, acupressure, eye movement).
> 2. Recommend **2 dopamine-detox-based rest activities** to let my exhausted brain recover after work. (Strictly forbid alcohol, binge eating, or SNS scrolling)
> 3. Provide one piece of **psychological metacognitive advice** that helps me view this situation and my emotions objectively and separately.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Never use empty, soulless platitudes like "Time will heal everything" or "Just think positively."
> - Briefly state the neuroscientific principle (e.g., parasympathetic activation, cortisol reduction, etc.) for every solution to increase persuasiveness.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - For cases of severe depression or panic disorder that require medical diagnosis, add a mandatory disclaimer at the end advising a consultation with a specialist.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

Beyond simply complaining to the AI that "I'm having a hard time," the **core mechanism** of this prompt is accurately targeting **'in what space,' 'because of what,' and 'what emotion'** you are experiencing. Artificial intelligence is designed to output the most generic and safe (i.e., useless) answers from its training data if you don't provide specific context.

I often pull out this prompt when my mental state is shattered while sitting in a bathroom stall. Following specific breathing techniques like the **'4-7-8 method'** or **'Box Breathing'** suggested by the AI with my eyes closed, I've experienced my racing heart rate calming down in less than 5 minutes.

When caught in a whirlpool of emotion, **cold neuroscientific facts and intuitive action guidelines** are far more powerful at holding a collapsing mental state together than someone else's awkward attempt at comfort.

In particular, controlling the `[Environment]` variable in the prompt is crucial. By specifying **physical constraints** like 'unable to leave my seat' or 'at my desk in a quiet office,' you prevent the AI from giving impractical advice (e.g., go for a walk, scream out loud) and force it to produce high-impact solutions you can apply immediately. If you are working from home or in a private space, try changing this variable to `[In my room alone, can make noise]`. The AI will suggest a completely different dimension of stress relief (e.g., active muscle relaxation techniques).

As your finish time approaches after surviving the crisis, try re-entering the prompt with the `[Specific cause of stress]` variable detailed with the most painful moment of your day. By inputting the raw cause, like "the boss's unreasonable demand to shorten the schedule," the AI will provide insightful directions for a healthy cooldown so you don't end up eating away at yourself.

- **Q: Can AI truly replace professional psychological counseling?**
  - A: Absolutely not. AI is merely a **'first-aid kit'** for temporary emotional turbulence. If your stress has become chronic and is severely affecting your daily life or sleep, you must seek a psychiatrist or professional therapist immediately.

- **Q: What if I'm too angry to even type?**
  - A: Turn on the **'Voice' feature** of ChatGPT or Claude on your smartphone and just vent the core details. Even if you just blurt out, "I'm at work and I'm about to explode because of my boss. Tell me how to catch my breath right now," the AI will recognize the urgency and immediately guide your breathing with a calm voice.
