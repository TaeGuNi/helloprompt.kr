## üöÄ The Solution: "Personal Study Planner"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you just need a quick, no-frills study schedule.

> **Role:** You are a strict and thorough `[Exam/Certification Coordinator]`.
> **Task:** Create a realistic study plan based on my specific goal and available time, and take full responsibility for keeping me motivated.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need a highly detailed, actionable, and personalized study curriculum.

> **Role (Role):** You are a strict and highly analytical `[Exam/Certification Coordinator]`.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I want to achieve `[Goal]`. I have exactly `[Period]` left, and I can dedicate `[Time]` hours a day to studying.
> - Objective: Create a highly realistic, customized study plan perfectly optimized for my current situation.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. **[Weekly Roadmap]**: Divide the entire study period into weekly segments and establish a clear milestone for each week.
> 2. **[Daily Routine]**: Design a detailed daily timetable that distributes my study hours efficiently, including strategic break times.
> 3. **[Checkpoint]**: Establish intermediate checkpoints (e.g., mock exams, pop quizzes) so I can objectively measure my progress.
> 4. **[Mental Conditioning]**: Provide a stinging, brutally honest piece of advice I can read whenever I feel lazy or want to give up.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - The plan must be challenging but 100% achievable. Do not set unreasonable expectations.
> - Designate exactly one day a week as a complete rest day ("Cheat Day").
> - Format the output using clean Markdown.
>
> **My Information:**
>
> - Goal: `[e.g., Pass the Information Processing Engineer Written Exam in 3 months]`
> - Available Time: `[e.g., 2 hours after work on weekdays, 5 hours on weekends]`
> - Current Level: `[e.g., Non-major, absolute beginner]`

## üí° Writer's Insight

This prompt is incredibly effective because it removes the cognitive load of _planning_ how to study, allowing you to focus purely on the _execution_. When we plan our own schedules, we suffer from the "Planning Fallacy"‚Äîwe assume we will always operate at 100% efficiency. By outsourcing the scheduling to an AI with strict constraints (like mandatory breaks and cheat days), you get a routine that acknowledges human limits. Whenever I prepare for a certification, I print out the "Mental Conditioning" quote the AI generates and tape it to my monitor. It's a surprisingly effective wake-up call when the mid-study slump hits.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well with the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it works perfectly fine. However, more advanced models like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet tend to provide more logically sound daily breakdowns and much sharper "Mental Conditioning" advice.

- **Q: What if I miss a few days of studying? Will the plan be ruined?**
  - A: Not at all! You can simply feed your progress back into the AI. For example, reply with: "I completely missed Week 2. Please adjust the remaining schedule to accommodate this setback without removing my cheat days."

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning the persona of a "Strict Coordinator," the AI shifts from a generic assistant to an authoritative guide, providing structured and firm output.
2. **Contextual Variables:** By explicitly stating the `[Goal]`, `[Period]`, and `[Time]`, the prompt prevents the AI from generating generic, one-size-fits-all advice.
3. **Structured Tasking:** Breaking down the request into specific outputs (Weekly Roadmap, Daily Routine, Checkpoint, Mental Conditioning) ensures the final response is immediately actionable rather than a wall of text.
