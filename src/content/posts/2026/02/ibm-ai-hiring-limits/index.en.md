## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

In an age of information overflow, this prompt is meticulously designed to fish out **'truly valuable business intelligence'** beyond simple facts. The core working principle is narrowing the context and assigning a specific expert persona to control the AI's broad creativity into the sharp analytical domain we precisely want.

While recent Large Language Models (LLMs) possess vast knowledge, they tend to pour out overly comprehensive or Wikipedia-level generalities if not given proper direction. If you simply tell an AI to "summarize this article," it will list obvious facts anyone could know. However, the moment you force a clear Role and Task, such as **"Analyze the hidden intent from the perspective of a 15-year strategist,"** the AI transforms from a simple summarizer into a brilliant analyst deep-diving between the lines of the article.

Let's look back at the IBM case. Superficially, it might just be a simple hiring news story about "expanding entry-level recruitment." But the moment it passes through this prompt, it makes the AI derive the company's true strategic intent: "building a future leadership pipeline and cultivating Gen Z orchestrators." The resulting output possesses a high level of insight that can be used immediately in team meetings or reports.

Here are a few more "cheat code" tips for modifying the `[Variables]` in this prompt for practical use. First is **segmenting the target**. Try adding your specific job role to the Context section, such as "From the perspective of a practitioner in charge of performance marketing in the current B2B SaaS market." The AI will extract the immediate impact of that news on your job and response strategies with surprising precision. Second is **customizing the output format**. If you add a condition to the Constraints like "This will be used for report writing, so write the first sentence in a head-heavy style, presenting the conclusion first," you can get a clean summary in the form your boss likes most in just one second.

From a prompt engineering perspective, the parts that exert the strongest control are the **Constraints and Warning** sections. The constraint to "thoroughly exclude obvious clichés or generalities" prevents the AI from giving lazy answers. Also, the warning to "express speculative content conservatively" dramatically reduces hallucinations—a fatal inherent weakness of AI. Since false information in business insights can lead to dangerous results, setting these guardrails is a necessity, not an option.

Lastly, as an empirical tip from testing many articles, this prompt proves its true worth especially when applied to major tech news such as 'crisis overcome cases,' 'organizational restructuring,' or 'radical M&A.' If you consistently practice reading the flow of capital and the agony of management behind corporate movements with this prompt, you will eventually discover your own amazing intuition to see through the lines of an article even without the prompt.
