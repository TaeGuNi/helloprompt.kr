> _"Company A offers a high salary, Company B guarantees work-life balance... and my current employer just matched their offer. What should I do?"_

Receiving multiple job offers is a fantastic problem to have, but it can quickly become overwhelming. This is not the time to make emotional decisions; it is the time to be fiercely strategic. By leveraging AI as your personal negotiation agent, you can objectively evaluate competing offers and execute a flawless counter-offer strategy to maximize your compensation and career trajectory.

## üöÄ Solution: "The Offer Negotiator"

### ü•â Basic Version

> **Role:** You are an expert career strategist and negotiation coach.
> **Task:** I have received job offers from multiple companies. Help me compare their terms and draft a counter-offer strategy to negotiate a better salary.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this for in-depth analysis and customized email drafting.

> **Role:** You are an elite career strategist and executive negotiation coach.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I have received the following job offers and need to make a decision.
>   - **Company A:** Salary $60,000, includes stock options, high-pressure environment (frequent overtime), prestigious brand name.
>   - **Company B:** Salary $55,000, excellent work-life balance, fast-growing startup.
> - Goal: I want to join Company A but need to negotiate a higher base salary using Company B's offer as leverage.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Comparative Analysis:** Create a pros and cons matrix comparing both offers. Recommend the best choice based on different career priorities (e.g., Stability vs. Aggressive Growth).
> 2. **Counter-Offer Strategy:** Draft a polite, professional, yet firm email to Company A. The email should state that while I am highly motivated to join them, I have another competitive offer. Indicate that I will sign immediately if they can match a base salary of `[$65,000]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Maintain a professional and appreciative tone; do not sound arrogant or demanding.
> - Ensure the email conveys that I am seriously considering their offer but need the financial aspect to align.
> - Provide strategic advice on whether to explicitly mention Company B by name or keep it anonymous.

## üí° Writer's Insight

Negotiating a job offer can feel intimidating, but it is standard business practice. HR departments and hiring managers actually expect candidates to negotiate. The key to a successful counter-offer is _leverage_ and _tone_. By using AI to draft your communication, you remove the emotion and anxiety from the process. This prompt ensures your email strikes the perfect balance between expressing genuine enthusiasm for the role and firmly holding your ground on compensation. Remember, the worst they can say is "no, this is our final offer," at which point you still have a great job lined up!

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Is it risky to negotiate? Will they rescind the offer?**
  - A: It is incredibly rare for a company to pull an offer simply because you tried to negotiate professionally. As long as your tone is respectful and your request is reasonable (typically 5-10% above the initial offer), the worst outcome is usually just a polite decline.

- **Q: Should I tell my current employer about the external offers?**
  - A: Only if you are genuinely willing to walk away. Using an external offer to force a raise at your current job can damage trust. This prompt can help you weigh the risks of accepting a counter-offer from your current employer.

- **Q: Do I need the paid version of ChatGPT or Claude for this?**
  - A: No, the free versions of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are perfectly capable of analyzing these conditions and writing excellent negotiation emails.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** Assigning the AI the role of an "executive negotiation coach" forces it to use professional, persuasive, and tactful language.
2. **Contextual Clarity:** By clearly defining the pros and cons of each company, the AI can tailor its advice rather than giving generic negotiation tips.
3. **Behavioral Constraints:** The instruction to "not sound arrogant" is critical. It ensures the drafted email maintains goodwill, which is essential when negotiating with future colleagues.
