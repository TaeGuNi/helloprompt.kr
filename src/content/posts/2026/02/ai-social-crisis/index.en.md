## 📝 The Social AI Crisis: Destroying Human Relationships and the Direction of Service Planning

- **🎯 Recommended for:** Service Planners, Product Managers (PMs), Marketers
- **⏱️ Time Required:** 2 hours of research → 5 minutes saved using prompts
- **🤖 Best Performance:** Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o (Strong in deep analysis)

_"We call it innovation, but users cry out in fatigue. As AI replaces human roles, real human bonds are being severed."_

The tech industry is currently engulfed in a glamorous noise. Every morning, executives cheer at the upward-trending **Time Spent** metrics on their dashboards, but the reality on the ground is starkly different. We are witnessing a massive chasm between the rosy illusions promised by AI and the deep sense of alienation actually felt by users. Every app is rushing to feature "AI friends just for you" or "24-hour interactive chatbots." Soulless automated responses and endless hyper-personalized recommendation algorithms have certainly succeeded in increasing user screen time. However, behind this success, a fatal and heavy side effect known as the **‘AI-Social-Crisis’** is festering. Within a perfectly curated digital environment, users click incessantly, but as technology replaces human conversation, the sturdy **Human Bond** between people is being shattered to pieces.

The bigger problem is that this isolation doesn't just end with simple fatigue; it is shaking the very foundations of business. Under blind top-down directives from executives—"Our competitors are doing it, so we must implement innovative AI features too!"—countless planners are forcibly cramming in AI that doesn't align with the essence of their service. The results are disastrous. Even when users open a community app, they don't feel like they are interacting with living, breathing people; instead, they feel like they are **shadowboxing with cold, mechanical algorithms**. Initially intrigued by an AI that never conflicts and perfectly caters to their words, users eventually feel crushed by a hollow emptiness disconnected from reality and silently delete the app. The cutting-edge technology we introduced at great cost to defend against churn has, paradoxically, become the **culprit that makes users flee our service most quickly and coldly**. How long can we ignore the psychological burnout of users quietly burning beneath the wrapping paper of performative innovation?

Now is the time to break this destructive cycle of a death spiral. A completely new planning paradigm is desperate. We must discard the narrow view of AI as a mere 'automated substitute that reduces human communication costs.' Instead, we must reposition the core value of our products so that AI acts as a **powerful bridge and facilitator**, reconnecting the broken bridges between people and expanding real-world social networks. To achieve this, a **Guardrail** is essential from the very first stage of planning to thoroughly simulate and verify the impact a feature will have on a user's offline relationships. "When this new AI feature is introduced, will the user be isolated in their room staring only at a screen? Or will they connect more deeply and richly with real people?" If a planning proposal cannot provide a clear business answer to this sharp and heavy question, it deserves to be thrown into the trash immediately.

That is why we have prepared this: the **essential 'Human-Centric' planning verification prompt** to rescue service planners and PMs drowning in metric pressure and indiscriminate AI implementation. By deploying this prompt at the early stages of new feature planning, you can perfectly preempt potential social risks and user fatigue. Moving beyond simple defense, you will derive innovative ideas that seamlessly transition in-app activities into positive relationship building offline. Consequently, you will move past the trap of superficial short-term time spent and witness an overwhelming increase in **Long-term Retention** and **LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)** as your product integrates deeply into users' lives. Use this prompt right now to verify whether your product is losing people or gaining them.

```text
💡 [Analysis Report] Repositioning AI Chatbots for Humanity Recovery

   - 🤝 Community Pathfinder: After engaging in deep conversations on a specific topic with the AI three or more times, the user is rewarded with a ticket to a local community meetup on that topic to encourage real-world participation.
```

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **The Fatal Trap of Metrics:** An increase in time spent does not guarantee user satisfaction; it is often merely 'isolated immersion.'
2. **AI Fatigue at Its Peak:** Blindly mounting AI features is actually causing extreme fatigue and churn among users.
3. **Human-Centric Repositioning:** AI must be a complement, not a substitute. An 'Ethical Planning Verification Prompt' is essential to check this in advance.

## 🚀 How the Real Pros Write Them

This is a core prompt used during the planning stage of a new AI feature or service to verify in advance whether the product severs a user's offline relationships and to derive practical improvements. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variable]` sections in brackets to fit your situation for immediate use in your work.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly check for potential side effects of a service during the early planning stages.

> **Role:** You are an `[IT Ethics and UX/UI Psychology Expert]`.
> 
> **Task:** Predict 3 negative impacts that the `[Service/Feature Name]` we are planning might have on a user's offline human relationships or mental health, and suggest Guardrail ideas to defend against them.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need an in-depth analysis report to drive decision-making among executives before a service launch.

> **Role:** You are a Silicon Valley `[Senior Product Manager]` and a `[Digital Sociologist]`.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: The tech industry is currently experiencing a 'Social AI Crisis' due to over-dependence on AI. Users are feeling fatigue from indiscriminate AI features and desire real human connections.
> - Goal: Derive planning revisions to position our new `[AI-based Social/Matching/Content Feature]` launch not as something that destroys human relationships, but as a Facilitator that helps connect people.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Conduct an in-depth analysis of the risks of 'Isolation' and 'Human Relationship Substitution' that this feature might cause.
> 2. Suggest 2 ideas for **'On-Offline Bridge Features'** that can offset these risks.
> 3. To persuade executives, explain using business logic how this ethical approach will positively impact long-term Retention and LTV (Customer Lifetime Value).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - The output format should be a highly readable report using Markdown bullet points and lists.
> - It must not be cliché ethical preaching, but practical advice directly linked to actual Business Metrics (KPIs).
> 
> **Warning:**
>
> - Skip general pros and cons of AI; analyze sharply only from the perspectives of 'Human Bonds' and 'User Fatigue.'

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight)

This prompt is not just a tool for generating text. It will be the **most powerful business shield and practical weapon for service planners, PMs, and Product Owners (POs)** over the next five years. We habitually open Excel and Figma to write feature specifications and draw UIs. However, before that, the process of asking the most fundamental question of a service—**"Does this feature actually make the user less lonely?"**—is often tragically skipped. Amidst the waves of indiscriminately pouring technology, this prompt acts as a compass to help planning proposals from losing their way.

Recently, many large social platforms and dating apps have rushed to introduce 'hyper-personalized AI chatbots.' What was the result? They may have succeeded in a temporary MAU (Monthly Active Users) pump. However, users soon felt extreme **Fatigue** from soulless conversations with machines and carried out large-scale exits. This is because what users truly wanted was not a perfect AI, but an **Authentic Connection** with a real person, however imperfect. If you deploy this prompt in your work to identify fatal risks at the early planning stage, you can save millions in sunk costs and immediately tune your product's trajectory from "technology for the sake of showing off technology" to **"technology that connects people."**

The key tuning point to watch in the prompt is the **Variable Control of the `[AI-based Social/Matching/Content Feature]`**. The more specific the situation provided to the model, the more exponentially sharp its insights become. Don't just write vaguely like "chat feature." Instead, specify the chillingly detailed operation of the feature you are planning, such as "An AI bot that learns a user's past text data to offer emotional comfort at 2 AM." The more specific the context, the more sharply the AI points out the danger of 'conversational isolation' and suggests radical **On-Offline Bridge ideas** that can pull the user back into the real world.

Furthermore, the requirement for **"explanations directly linked to actual Business Metrics (KPIs)"** placed in the Constraints block is the true killer part of this prompt. We often fail to persuade C-level executives who apply immediate metric pressure, even when we know an ethical approach is better. However, instead of offering moral lectures, this prompt constructs defensive logic by translating it into the **Business Language** of executives—Long-term Retention and LTV. When backed by a solid data framework like "We must modify this feature this way to prevent 40% of user churn in six months," your human-centric planning proposal can finally receive the approval stamp on the conference table.

Clearly, in an era of technological parity, the point of differentiation is no longer 'how well did you use AI.' It is determined by **'how cleverly did you hide the AI and highlight human warmth.'** Try substituting the core features of your current planning documents into the prompt variables today. With just a 5-minute investment, you will experience the amazing transformation from a planner who traps users in isolated prisons behind screens to a true product leader who guides them into warm, real-world communities. Never forget that sincere concern for the user, rather than perfect code, is your unique, irreplaceable weapon that no algorithm can substitute.
