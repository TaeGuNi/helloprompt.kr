## 🚀 This is How Real Experts Write

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in brackets to match your situation for immediate results.

### 🥉 Basic Version (Standard)

Use this when you want to quickly establish a solid foundation for your profile.

> **Role:** You are a senior recruitment consultant at a global IT firm.
>
> **Task:** My job title is `[Current Role]` and my core skills are `[3 Core Skills]`. Please write 3 LinkedIn headlines that are optimized for headhunter searches and a 300-character "About" section that attractively showcases me in both Korean and English.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

A sophisticated master prompt for when you want to establish an overwhelming presence among many competitive candidates.

> **Role:** You are a senior recruitment consultant for a Silicon Valley global IT firm and a LinkedIn SEO optimization expert.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Goal: To attractively renew my LinkedIn profile to receive high-quality position offers (InMail) from headhunters at least 3 times a week.
> - My Information:
>   - Current Role: `[e.g., 5-year B2B SaaS Product Manager]`
>   - Core Skills: `[e.g., Data-driven decision making, Agile Scrum Lead, Customer Journey Optimization]`
>   - Key Achievements: `[e.g., 30% reduction in initial churn rate by improving onboarding process, 15% increase in MRR through new feature launch]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Search Engine Optimization (SEO) Keywords:** Extract 5 core keywords that recruiters use most frequently when searching for someone in my role.
> 2. **Headline:** Propose 3 headline concepts (under 120 characters) that are highly searchable and click-worthy: ① Performance-oriented (proven by numbers), ② Expertise-focused, ③ Keyword-optimized list.
> 3. **About:** Write a first-person storytelling introduction that makes me stand out.
>
> - Start with a powerful hook in the first two sentences that forces the reader to continue.
> - Show my work philosophy and problem-solving methods rather than just listing my history.
> - Use clear paragraph divisions, appropriate bullet points, and emojis for readability.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Avoid exaggerated or artificial expressions; maintain a professional yet confident tone and manner.
> - Output the results clearly separated into Korean and English versions.

## 💡 Writer's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

The two most critical and high-impact areas for LinkedIn profile optimization are the **Headline** and the **About** section. Recruiters and search firm headhunters around the world scroll through hundreds or thousands of candidate listings every day. They decide whether to click on your specific profile page in **just one second**. And what dominates that split second is the short text under your name—the headline (within 120 characters).

The most important tip when using the Pro version is to fill the **[My Information]** variables with specific, vivid **'Data' and 'Action'**. Most job seekers habitually write vague expressions that anyone could use, such as "Diligently performed performance marketing tasks" or "Led smooth communication within the team," which fail to leave an impression.

To extract 200% of the capability from AI models (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet), you must feed them **result-oriented hard facts**. For example, specify quantitative data like "Achieved 300% ROAS and managed an annual performance marketing budget of $1M" or "Defended customer churn rate by 25% by introducing a new onboarding process." AI will use these dry numbers as raw materials to weave **a much more multi-dimensional, credible, and professional storytelling** that shows how you define and solve business problems.

Additionally, this prompt includes a **strong constraint to generate both Korean and English versions simultaneously**. In today's globalized job market, having an English profile exponentially expands the pie of opportunities available to you. For those who felt burdened by writing an English resume because it's not their native language, or those seriously preparing to move to global foreign firms or remote roles at Silicon Valley startups, this feature will be the most powerful weapon—**perfectly replacing English resume editing services that can cost hundreds of dollars.**

Finally, rather than just copying and pasting the generated results, adopt an **A/B testing mindset**. Try switching your headline every two weeks among the three concepts proposed by the AI (Performance, Expertise, Keyword List) and track the data yourself to see which keywords and phrases lead to a surge in profile views and InMail frequency. Personal branding is not a static document you set and forget; it's a **dynamic product** that you constantly optimize based on market response. I hope you find the optimal language through this prompt that makes your value shine the brightest.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: I'm a fresh graduate and don't have impressive numerical achievements. What should I do?**
  - A: Instead of grand business figures, write about your 'problem-solving experiences' or 'hard-won insights from projects.' For example: `[e.g., As a team leader in a major project, coordinated schedule delay issues and achieved an A+ grade]`. AI will use this to complete an attractive introduction that emphasizes your potential and tenacious problem-solving attitude.

- **Q: Won't my current colleagues or boss notice I'm looking for a job if I update my profile too often?**
  - A: If you turn off the 'Share profile updates with your network' option in the LinkedIn settings menu, your connections will not receive any notifications. You can quietly refine your profile like a ninja and then change the setting back once your job search preparation is complete.

- **Q: What if the English version generated by AI feels a bit awkward or stiff?**
  - A: This prompt strongly recommends using Claude 3.5 Sonnet or GPT-4o, which have overwhelming quality in translation and composition. If the expression feels a bit stiff, simply ask again: `> Please revise the English version to be a bit more casual and use a natural Silicon Valley tone commonly used by native speakers in the workplace.`

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1.  **Goal-Oriented Context:** Instead of vaguely saying "Write a profile," it feeds the AI a very specific goal: "Receiving headhunter contacts at least 3 times a week." This perfectly aligns the direction of the output with real-world sales pitching rather than a simple self-introduction.
2.  **Three Structured Concept Proposals:** By asking for three distinct headline concepts (Performance, Expertise, Keyword), it provides the user with options and allows for profile A/B testing. This is a sophisticated design intended for optimization.
3.  **Strong Constraints:** It strictly suppresses exaggerated expressions and forces a professional tone. This removes the typical "AI-like" artificial phrasing and guarantees a level of credibility that can be immediately copied and pasted into a professional environment.
