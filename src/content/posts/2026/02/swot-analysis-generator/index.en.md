## 🚀 The Solution: "McKinsey Strategy Consultant Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version (Quick & Easy)

Use this when you need a high-level overview in seconds.

> **Role:** You are a Senior Strategy Consultant at McKinsey & Company.
>
> **Request:** Please conduct a comprehensive SWOT analysis for a `[insert your business type or idea]`.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert Level)

Use this when you need detailed, investor-ready strategic insights.

Copy the **PROMPT** content below and paste it into ChatGPT or Claude.

> **Role (Role):** You are a Senior Strategy Consultant at McKinsey & Company with 15 years of experience in market analysis.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: We are preparing to launch a `[insert specific product or service]` in the `[insert target market]` industry.
> - Goal: To identify our competitive edge and mitigate potential risks before entering the market.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Conduct a deep-dive SWOT analysis identifying 3 to 5 highly specific points for each category: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats for our business model.
> 2. Provide a one-sentence, data-backed rationale for every single point you make.
> 3. Based on the analysis, develop 1 key strategic initiative for each cross-section: SO (Maxi-Maxi), WO (Mini-Maxi), ST (Maxi-Mini), and WT (Mini-Mini).
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Avoid abstract, generic corporate jargon. Use highly specific, industry-relevant language.
> - Present the final output in a clear, well-structured Markdown table format.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Do not hallucinate market trends. If you lack specific data about this niche, state your limitations clearly before proceeding.

## 💡 Writer's Insight (Insight)

This prompt completely flips the traditional strategic planning process on its head. Normally, teams waste 80% of their energy just brainstorming the basic SWOT bullet points, leaving only 20% of their time to figure out what to actually do with that information.

By running this **Pro Prompt** first, you feed the AI your basic premise, and it acts as an ultra-fast, objective brainstorming partner. It consistently highlights crucial blind spots—especially within the **Threats** and **Weaknesses** quadrants—that internal teams are often too biased to see. **Pro Tip:** Do not just blindly accept the AI's first output. Use it as a robust first draft. Bring it to your team meeting and spend your valuable time debating and refining the actual **strategies** (the SO, WO, ST, and WT initiatives) rather than arguing over the raw data.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well on the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: It works adequately on the free version. However, for a task requiring deep industry knowledge and nuanced strategic reasoning, premium models like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet will provide significantly sharper, less generic results.
- **Q: What if the AI gives me really obvious, generic answers?**
  - A: This typically happens if your `[Context]` is too vague. The more specific you are about your target audience, current budget, and unique value proposition in the Background section, the more penetrating the AI's analysis will be.

---

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing (McKinsey Consultant):** By assigning a top-tier consulting persona, we force the AI to access its training data related to professional business frameworks and rigorous analytical standards.
2. **Cross-Sectional Strategy (SO, WO, ST, WT):** A standard SWOT is often just a passive list. By explicitly demanding the TOWS matrix strategies, we immediately turn passive observations into highly actionable business initiatives.
3. **Strict Formatting Constraints:** Forcing a Markdown table output ensures the final result is immediately readable and ready to be pasted directly into a presentation slide or executive report.
