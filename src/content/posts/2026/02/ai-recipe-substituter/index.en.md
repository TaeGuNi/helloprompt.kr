## 🚀 Solution: "The AI Recipe Substituter"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you're in a rush and just need a quick, simple alternative.

> **Role:** You are a 3-Michelin-star chef.
> **Task:** I am making `[cream pasta]` right now, but I am out of `[heavy cream]`. Give me 3 common ingredient substitutes I can use immediately. Keep it short.

<br>

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need absolute precision, proper ratios, and a deep understanding of how the swap will affect the final flavor profile.

> **Role:** You are a head chef and culinary researcher with 20 years of experience, possessing a flawless understanding of ingredient chemistry and flavor balance.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am currently cooking `[Dish you are making, e.g., Cream Pasta]`.
> - Problem: I just ran out of `[Missing ingredient, e.g., Heavy cream]`, which is essential for this recipe. I cannot go to the grocery store right now.
> - My Pantry/Fridge Status: I currently have `[Available ingredients, e.g., Milk, sliced cheddar cheese, butter, eggs, Greek yogurt]` in my kitchen.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Propose 3 optimal recipe substitutes for the `[Missing ingredient]` using ONLY the ingredients I currently have available.
> 2. For each alternative, provide the exact **mixing ratio** and **cooking method** (e.g., "Melt 10g of butter into 100ml of milk over low heat").
> 3. Explain how using this substitute will alter the taste, texture, and aroma compared to the original dish.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Format the output beautifully in Markdown for high readability.
> - Strictly exclude any rare or specialty ingredients; only utilize what is commonly found in a standard home kitchen based on my list.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - If a specific combination carries a high risk of separating, splitting, or ruining the dish's texture, you MUST provide a clear warning beforehand.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

The true brilliance of this prompt lies far beyond a simple "use B instead of A." **It provides the exact scientific ratios and explains the nuanced differences in taste and texture.** 

For instance, when baking, substituting butter with cooking oil alters the moisture content, often ruining the bread. By using this prompt, the AI calculates the chemical properties and advises, "Use only 80% oil and monitor the texture." It highlights the exact pitfalls that trip up beginners, drastically lowering your chances of failure. You can even adapt this prompt for "fridge foraging" (clearing out leftovers) to transform random scraps into a gourmet meal.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Will the food taste weird? I'm worried about creating a culinary disaster.**
  - A: AI operates on a massive global database of verified culinary knowledge, making the chances of it recommending a chemically incompatible or gross combination very low. However, taste is subjective, so always taste as you go and adjust seasonings gradually!

- **Q: Can I use this for baking, where precise measurements are a matter of life and death?**
  - A: Absolutely. Because baking relies heavily on chemical reactions (leavening, coagulation, etc.), just make sure to explicitly state in the `[Dish you are making]` variable that you are "baking in an oven." The AI will then provide highly precise substitution ratios that account for moisture and fat content.

## 🧬 Anatomy of the Prompt (Why it works)

1. **Resource Constraints (Context):** By feeding the AI your exact `My Pantry/Fridge Status` upfront, you completely block "hallucinations" where it might suggest expensive or inaccessible ingredients you don't own.
2. **Action-Oriented Details (Task):** Requesting exact ratios, cooking methods, and flavor differences extracts a multi-dimensional culinary lesson that a simple Google search could never provide.
3. **Risk Management (Warning):** Forcing the AI to warn you about potential texture disasters (like a cream sauce splitting) acts as a safety net for amateur cooks.

Here are 3 perfect substitute recipes for heavy cream using the ingredients in your kitchen:

1. Milk + Butter (The Classic Swap)
- Ratio: 3 parts Milk to 1 part Butter (e.g., 150ml milk, 50g butter)
- Instructions: Melt the butter over low heat, slowly pour in the milk, and whisk thoroughly. The key is to warm it gently—do not let it boil.
- Flavor Profile: Yields the closest rich, nutty flavor to actual heavy cream. It will be slightly thinner, so reduce the amount of pasta water you add to the sauce.

2. Milk + Sliced Cheddar Cheese (For Extra Thickness)
- Ratio: 2 slices of cheese per 200ml of milk
- Instructions: Once the milk is at a gentle simmer, tear the cheese into pieces, drop them in, and stir constantly until the sauce thickens.
- Flavor Profile: Delivers an explosive umami kick with a signature cheesy saltiness. Because the cheese is already salty, hold off on adding extra salt until the very end.
