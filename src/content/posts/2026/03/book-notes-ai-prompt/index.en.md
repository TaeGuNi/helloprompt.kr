## 📝 Reading Notes: AI Extracts the Core for Lifelong Retention

- **🎯 Recommended For:** Those who forget books right after reading, and those who want to turn reading into a habit.
- **⏱️ Time Required:** 2 hours writing notes → 10 minutes AI structuring
- **🤖 Top Performance:** GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.5 Pro

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
- 🚀 **Utility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

> _"I read 30 books last year, but I only remember three of them now. I was moved while reading, but a month later, even the title is fuzzy."_

The biggest problem with reading is that **reading and remembering are two completely different acts.** Research shows that if you don't organize anything after reading a book, you forget 80% of the content within a week. We know we should write reading notes, but we often don't know "how to organize" them, so we end up just copying a few underlined sentences.

By providing the core content of a book to AI, it systematically organizes it into a <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">3-line summary, 5 key insights, and an action plan</span> to apply to your life. In particular, asking it to "connect the core ideas of this book with other books or concepts" goes beyond simple summarization and creates a **knowledge network.**

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Structured Memory:** Organizing book content hierarchically into "One-sentence core → 3-line summary → 5 insights → Action plan" dramatically increases retention.
2. **Knowledge Network:** Connecting concepts from this book to others transforms fragmented knowledge into three-dimensional understanding.
3. **Action-Oriented:** The ultimate goal of reading is to convert "what you learned" into "what you will do tomorrow."

## 🚀 How Real Pros Write

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Reading Coach]`.
>
> **Request:** Write a reading note after reading `[Atomic Habits]`. Organize 5 key points and an action plan.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Knowledge management expert well-versed in the Second Brain (Zettelkasten) methodology]`. You are a master of note-taking systems that extract the essence of books and convert them into permanent memory.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Book read: `[Atomic Habits — James Clear]`
> - Key passages I underlined: `["Habits are a vote for your identity", "Start with the 2-minute rule", "If you design your environment, you don't need willpower"]`
> - My areas of interest: `[Productivity, self-management, software development]`
> - Other books read recently: `[Deep Work, The System, Nudge]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Core in One Sentence:** Compress the entire content of this book into a single sentence.
> 2. **3-Line Summary:** Summarize the core argument of the book within 3 lines.
> 3. **5 Key Insights:** Organize each insight in the format of Title (Keyword) + Description (2 lines) + Specific Example.
> 4. **Knowledge Connection:** Connect concepts from this book with concepts from other books I've recently read (at least 3 connections).
> 5. **Action Plan:** Create 3 action items that I can implement immediately starting tomorrow.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Instead of just listing the book's content, organize it from a perspective applied to my areas of interest.
> - Write the action plan as specific actions that can be "started within 2 minutes."

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The core of this prompt is **"Knowledge Connection."** Book summaries are everywhere on the internet. A truly valuable reading note creates a <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">conceptual link</span> like "Concept A in this book connects to Concept B in that book." The more these connections grow, the more solid your knowledge becomes and the more applicable it is in practice.

Pro Tip: Including `[Key passages I underlined]` determines the quality. If you only ask to "summarize" without underlines, the AI generates a generic summary. You must include passages that resonated with you to complete a **reading note from your own perspective.**

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can't I just get a summary from AI without reading the book?**
  - A: You can get a summary, but the **quality of insights and action plans** will be significantly different. By giving AI the passages you personally read and underlined, you get a personalized note "connected to your own experience." A summary of something you haven't read is just someone else's thought.

- **Q: Is it possible to organize reading notes for multiple books at once?**
  - A: Yes. You can also create comparative analysis notes by asking, "Extract common themes from these 3 books and compare any conflicting arguments."

---

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Hierarchical Compression:** The hierarchy of one line → three lines → five insights matches the **human memory system (Chunking Theory).** A range of 7±2 information units is the optimal range for immediate recall.
2. **Enforcing Action Plans:** Converting "what you learned" into "what you will do" turns reading from simple consumption into behavioral change. This is the difference between "reading and forgetting" and "reading that changes your life."
