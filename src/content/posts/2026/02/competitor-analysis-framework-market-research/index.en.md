## 🚀 How the Real Pros Write It

This is a prompt perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the brackets `[variable]` to match your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this lightly when you want to quickly grasp the overall competitive landscape and flow of the market.

> **Role:** You are a senior market research analyst with 10 years of experience.
>
> **Task:**
> Analyze the strengths and weaknesses of `[Competitor Name]`, a major competitor of our service `[Our Service Name]`, and summarize 3 key points we should benchmark.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This is a high-end prompt to pull out when you need precisely structured business insights sophisticated enough to be used immediately in executive reports or project proposals.

> **Role:** You are a senior market researcher at a global top-tier strategy consulting firm.
>
> **Context:**
> - Our Service: `[Our Service/Product Name and Core Features]`
> - Major Competitors: `[Competitor A]`, `[Competitor B]`
> - Analysis Purpose: Deriving competitive advantages for new feature planning and marketing strategy formulation.
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Conduct an in-depth analysis of each competitor's Core Value Proposition and main target audience.
> 2. Backtrack and analyze the competitors' major marketing channels and Acquisition Strategy.
> 3. Based on app store reviews, community reactions, and latest market trends, uncover Niche Markets the competitors are missing and key Customer Pain Points.
> 4. Based on the above analysis, propose 2 specific points we should benchmark and 2 points where we must differentiate ourselves.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - You MUST activate the latest web search function to reflect market trends within the last 6 months.
> - Output the analysis results visualized in a Markdown Comparison Table format for easy viewing.
>
> **Warning:**
> - Never hallucinate or make up figures or information from unclear sources; clearly state "Data unavailable" if information is not confirmed. (Anti-hallucination)

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

Many marketers and planners introduce ChatGPT into their work but often get disappointed quickly because they don't see the expected results. The reason is very simple. They only assigned AI the role of a **'Searcher'** and didn't demand the role of an **'Analyst.'** If you simply ask it to wrap up information floating on the internet nicely, you'll only get a shallow summary at that exact level. However, what we truly need in the field isn't the simple fact that "Competitor A has 1 million users," but a clear conclusion on **"So, what should we as the working team do right now?"**—in other words, **Actionable Insight**.

The reason this prompt has overwhelming power is that it is structured strictly from a practitioner's perspective. In my experience, this prompt works true magic when used in environments like **Perplexity Pro** or **ChatGPT (GPT-4o)** where real-time web search is supported. The part to especially note is the third instruction: **'Uncover Niche Markets the competitors are missing and key Customer Pain Points.'**

In the past, you had to capture every single review of a competitor's app, move reactions from communities like Blind or Reddit into Excel, and perform sentiment analysis yourself. But with this prompt, the AI scans the vast web ecosystem on its own and immediately pulls out raw insights like **"Users love the competitor's fast delivery, but are leaving because the refund process is too complex."** When planning new features or writing ad copy that pierces the customer's heart, these insights provide truly irreplaceable inspiration. Knowing the enemy's weakness clearly gives you the answer on how to position our strengths.

I'd also like to mention the **control of `[variables]`** within the prompt. What if your company is a newly launched startup or a strictly B2B solution where external web search data is significantly lacking? Don't panic; just fill the `[Context]` area with richer information. Before running the prompt, scrape the text of the competitor's investment news, their sales brochure text, or interview articles of their CEO and paste them all at the bottom of the prompt. Just add one sentence: "Prioritize the attached text materials to perform the previous instructions." Based on the Context you fed it, the AI will analyze the competitor's vulnerabilities as if it were a whistleblower.

Furthermore, the constraint to **'output in Markdown Comparison Table format'** is not just a design requirement. By forcing output in a table format, the AI naturally filters out unnecessary flowery language and keeps only the most core words and concepts from the vast text it generated. In other words, the table format itself acts as a **powerful summary and optimization filter**. The resulting table is high-density and polished enough to be copied and pasted directly into your Notion project plan or executive report slides.

Ultimately, the core of prompt engineering lies in how well you handle AI as a **'Thinking Partner.'** This prompt is not just a tool to reduce research time. it is a powerful weapon that expands your business thinking and allows you to discover gaps in the market that others miss. Apply this prompt to the project you are working on today. With just a 5-minute investment, your proposal will gain solid logic and sharp insights that no one can argue with.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I move the table output by the AI directly to Excel or Google Sheets?**
  - A: Yes, it is perfectly compatible! Simply drag and copy the entire Markdown table generated by the AI, and paste it (`Ctrl+V` or `Cmd+V`) into the first cell (A1) of Excel or Google Sheets. The formatting will be cleanly separated according to the cell specifications, so you can paste it into professional reports immediately without additional processing.

- **Q: Is it possible to analyze competitors in B2B SaaS models or extremely niche markets?**
  - A: Absolutely. However, for B2B businesses or very limited niche markets, popular web search data may be lacking. In this case, try directly attaching 2-3 links to the competitor's official landing page URLs or related PR articles in the `[Context]` area. The AI will focus its learning on the given context to derive much sharper and denser insights.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Assignment:** Instead of a generic AI chatbot, we assigned a very specific and authoritative persona: **'Senior Researcher at a global top-tier strategy consulting firm.'** This pushes the depth of analysis and the professionalism of business vocabulary to the limit.
2. **Structured Task:** Vague instructions like 'compare competitors' were strictly excluded. By forcing **Chain-of-Thought** reasoning—moving from value proposition identification to target audience analysis, marketing channel tracking, pain point discovery, and benchmarking derivation—it completes a report with no logical gaps.
3. **Hallucination Defense Control:** We embedded controls that mandate latest web searches and block the generation of uncertain data at the source. This is a powerful shield that ensures only **fact-based, highly reliable data** is output for immediate use in actual business decision-making.
