> _"Right after a long hour-long meeting, is the only thing left in your head the overwhelming question, 'So, what am I supposed to do now?'"_

You’ve probably had the experience of frantically typing away to record every single word of a meeting, only to realize you’ve completely missed the actual flow and critical context of the discussion.

Often in the meeting room, we are so focused on increasing our typing speed that we fail to grasp the speaker's facial expressions or subtle nuances. When you return to your desk after an intense hour of discussion and look at your monitor, your text editor is often filled with nothing but scattered fragments of meaningless words. You find yourself unable to answer fundamental questions like, **"Was the final deadline for Plan B, which Team Leader Kim emphasized so strongly, exactly this Friday or next Wednesday?"** or **"So, in the end, was it decided that our team or the marketing team would handle this complex additional research?"** Eventually, you end up in the embarrassing and frustrating situation of having to message participants again on Slack or Teams to double-check.

This **simple recording and summarization task** is one of the primary culprits that quickly drains an office worker's precious energy. Spending another full hour refining a document just to share the contents of a one-hour weekly meeting with team members is the definition of extreme inefficiency in the modern business environment. Furthermore, relying solely on imperfect human memory to write meeting minutes creates critical communication risks where differing recollections between departments can lead to unclear project accountability. While writing meeting minutes is often seen as a tedious chore for the youngest or most junior members of a department, it is actually a high-level communication task that requires both sharp judgment and a deep understanding of the overall business context.

But now, it’s time to completely escape that painful and exhausting typing hell. What really matters in a practical business meeting is not every single word spoken by the participants. You only need to clearly retain two core elements: **'What was decided (Key Decisions)'** and **'Who needs to do what, by when (Action Items).'**

The solution I strongly propose to you today is <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">the combination of STT (Speech-to-Text) tools</span>—such as your smartphone recorder, Clova Note, or Daglo—<span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">and highly engineered AI prompts</span>. Once you build this perfect automated pipeline, you will never need to hide behind your laptop monitor during meetings again. You can simply look the other person in the eye and focus entirely on productive conversation and idea generation.

I can tell you with certainty that this well-structured prompt can transform a disorganized, scattered raw voice transcript into a **perfectly structured business directive** in just one minute. No more being fooled by vague, responsibility-shirking phrases like "I'll look into the schedule sometime this week." AI excels at sharply converting hazy everyday conversation into specific, measurable action goals.

The moment you apply this prompt to your work, your daily routine and performance will shift to an entirely different dimension. As soon as you leave the meeting room, clean meeting minutes in Markdown format will be shared in real-time on Slack and team channels. All team members will clearly understand their roles and deadlines without a hint of misunderstanding and can dive into their work immediately. The precious time previously wasted on organizing meeting minutes will be converted into productive time for tackling the core business logic of the project. Your colleagues and superiors will praise your overwhelming processing speed and your ability to produce clean documentation that allows no room for ambiguity. ## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and simply fill in the `[Variables]` in brackets to fit your situation and put it to work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this lightweight version when you want to quickly identify key decisions and your own tasks.

> **Role:** You are a highly competent and sensible executive assistant.
>
> **Request:** Read the following meeting transcript text and briefly summarize the 3 most important decisions and the Action Items that team members need to do right now.
>
> **Input:** `[Meeting transcript text]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need to immediately share official and structured meeting minutes with team members or related departments.

> **Role:** You are a meticulous Project Manager (PM) with 10 years of experience in an IT company.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Meeting Purpose: `[e.g., Q3 Marketing Campaign Kick-off]`
> - Meeting Date: `[e.g., 2026-02-12]`
> - Participant List: `[e.g., Team Leader Kim, Assistant Manager Park, Associate Lee]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> Based on the provided meeting transcript text, write official meeting minutes according to the defined Markdown template below.
>
> **[Meeting Minutes Template]**
>
> - **📅 Date/Time:** `[Meeting Date/Time]`
> - **👥 Participants:** `[Participant List]`
> - **🗣️ Agenda:** `[Summarize the core topics discussed in 3 items or less]`
> - **✅ Decisions:** `[Items clearly agreed upon in the meeting]`
> - **📝 Action Items:**
>   - `[Assignee Name]` : `[Specific action instruction]` (~`[Estimated Deadline]`)
> - **⏭️ Next Steps:** `[Next scheduled date, such as a follow-up meeting]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Completely remove jokes, weather talk, and small talk unrelated to decision-making.
> - Avoid vague expressions for 'Action Items' (e.g., "Review," "Look into") and rewrite them focused on specific deliverables (e.g., "Create competitor price comparison table and share on Slack").
> - If it is unclear who should do the task, mark it as "(Assignee TBD)".
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never hallucinate or make up dates, figures, or information not mentioned in the transcript. Mark uncertain parts as "[Confirmation Required]".
>
> **Input Data:**
> `[Paste the meeting transcript text extracted from Clova Note, etc., here]`

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The true value of this prompt lies in the **'perfect removal of ambiguity'** and the **'extreme optimization of the business communication process.'** When you conduct numerous project meetings in actual practice, the biggest problem that eats away at team efficiency is not the poor quality of the discussion itself, but the confusion that arises during the 'Execution' phase following the discussion. Record your meetings without fail using AI-based STT (Speech-to-Text) apps like Clova Note, Daglo, or Apple's built-in voice memo summary feature, then copy the raw extracted text and apply it immediately with this prompt. The dramatic reduction of your grueling meeting minutes writing time from 30 minutes to just 1 minute is actually just a basic side effect.

Immediately after an intense meeting, confidently share the clean Markdown summary generated by this prompt in your team's official Slack or internal messenger channel. You can earn overwhelming positive evaluations and firm trust from your colleagues and the evaluating superiors, who will think, **"This person works so cleanly and efficiently"** or **"They've grasped the complex context of the project instantly."** This goes beyond simply shortening document work time; it becomes a powerful and definitive means of personal branding that proves your work capability and professionalism within the organization.

A part you should particularly notice and apply is the meticulous instruction design in the Constraints section of the prompt: **"Convert verbs into specific deliverable-oriented actions."** This single line of prompt engineering completely changes the quality of the entire meeting minutes and the execution power of the team. In everyday business conversations, vague and defensive evasive expressions like "I'll look into it sometime tomorrow" or "Let's review it with the relevant department when we have time" are bound to be rampant. However, when this prompt intervenes powerfully, these hazy words are forcibly converted into very clear, action-oriented tasks like **"Finalize Price List for Company A and share via email"** or **"Submit Slack report analyzing 3 competitor design references."** Through this rigorous process, the entire team's unnecessary communication costs (follow-up costs) and the wasteful back-and-forth of asking "Assistant Manager, is this what you meant yesterday?" are drastically reduced.

Furthermore, strictly controlling core variables like `[Participant List]` and `[Meeting Purpose]` in advance is a very reliable safety device to prevent the fatal 'Hallucination' phenomenon of AI. Even the most outstanding AI often finds it difficult to accurately perceive 'who the final decision-maker is' or 'who the lead person in charge is' through only the flow of text-based conversation. However, if we provide clear role boundaries like 'Team Leader Kim, Assistant Manager Park, Associate Lee' at the top of the prompt, the AI cleverly utilizes this context to accurately infer the speaker's rank and work distribution. This allows it to perfectly assign Action Items to each person in charge much more naturally and accurately without any awkwardness.

Finally, when processing long meeting transcript texts exceeding an hour, the capacity of the AI model you choose is also a very important variable. As the physical context of the conversation grows longer, lower-performance AI models may completely forget the core content from the beginning or arbitrarily omit important conclusions. Therefore, I most strongly recommend using the **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** model, which has a very wide context window that can digest tens of thousands of characters at once and boasts overwhelming performance in summarizing complex business documents and logical structuring. If your organization actively uses Google Workspace, Google's **Gemini 2.5 Pro** is also an excellent alternative that can show great results in processing long documents. The moment you understand the underlying principle of how this prompt works and freely customize the provided variables to fit your unique work environment, you will be reborn as a true process manager who leads team productivity, rather than a mere practitioner following instructions.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: The 1-hour meeting transcript text is too long. Does it all fit in the prompt window?**
  - A: The free version of ChatGPT (like GPT-3.5) might cut off the text in the middle. For long meetings lasting over 30 minutes, I strongly recommend using **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** or **Gemini 2.5 Pro**, which are overwhelmingly strong at processing long context.
- **Q: The speaker separation in the recording file isn't perfect, so it shows up as 'Participant 1', 'Participant 2'.**
  - A: If you put the actual participant names in the `[Participant List]` at the top of the prompt, the AI will grasp the context of the conversation (e.g., titles used for ranks, work distribution content, etc.) and map the speakers fairly accurately. However, please make sure to meticulously double-check the `[Assignee Name]` part in the final output.
- **Q: Our team uses Notion. Can it be summarized in a table format?**
  - A: Yes, of course! If you just add one line to the Constraints section—_"Create all output results in a Markdown Table format so I can copy/paste them directly into Notion"_—you can get the results organized in a perfect table format.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Template Enforcement:** It prevents the AI from explaining at length in prose and fixes the output to a business document format, pulling readability to the extreme.
2. **Action-Oriented Restructuring:** By instructing it to replace vague and everyday language in the conversation with a clear To-Do list structure, practical utility is maximized.
3. **Hallucination Control:** To fundamentally block the most fatal error in meeting summaries—'making up facts that don't exist'—strict warnings are placed at the end of the prompt.
