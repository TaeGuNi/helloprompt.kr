## 🚀 Solution: "T20 Points Table Data Analyst Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a rapid, high-level overview of the current standings.

> **Role:** You are an expert sports data analyst.
> **Task:** Analyze the `[T20 World Cup Points Table Data]` provided below. Summarize the current standings and highlight the teams most likely to advance to the next round in exactly three lines. Do NOT use a Markdown table; cleanly format the output as an emoji bulleted list.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Deploy this when crafting a professional-grade newsletter or requiring an in-depth, broadcast-ready analysis report.

> **Role:** You are a veteran sports journalist and elite data analyst with over 10 years of experience. You possess an absolute mastery of Cricket T20 World Cup rules, especially the intricacies of the NRR (Net Run Rate) system.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: You have just received the latest raw T20 World Cup points table data, reflecting all recent match results.
> - Goal: Based strictly on this data, draft a rapid, highly accurate, and engaging situational analysis report designed to captivate cricket fans and sports newsletter subscribers.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Analyze the provided `[Points Table Data]` and cleanly structure the current group standings into an intuitive emoji bulleted list. (You must include rank, team name, matches played, wins, losses, total points, and NRR).
> 2. Logically evaluate the probability of the top two teams advancing to the semi-finals based on the current math.
> 3. Provide a crystal-clear, reader-friendly explanation of how the NRR (Net Run Rate) is impacting this specific, fierce ranking competition.
> 4. The raw data to be analyzed will be provided in the brackets below.
>    - Data: `[Paste the points table text copied from the website here]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - UNDER NO CIRCUMSTANCES should you output a Markdown table. To maximize readability on mobile devices, you must strictly utilize bulleted lists (-) and relevant emojis.
> - If technical jargon (e.g., NRR) is mentioned, you must append a concise, one-sentence annotation to ensure absolute clarity for casual readers.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not hallucinate or invent teams that do not exist in the provided text. Do not mix in historical data or past tournament results. Base your entire analysis strictly on the provided data after conducting a rigorous fact-check.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I apply this to analyze standings for the Premier League or the LoL World Championship instead of cricket?**
  - A: Absolutely! Just swap out specific keywords like "T20 World Cup" and "NRR" in the prompt with the exact terminology relevant to your target sport (e.g., "goal difference," "set score differential"). It will adapt flawlessly to any competitive format.

- **Q: Will the AI still process the data correctly even if the copied text formatting is completely ruined and jumbled?**
  - A: Yes, you have nothing to worry about. State-of-the-art AI models like GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet possess an incredible ability to accurately parse team names and statistical patterns, even when extracting them from a chaotic, single-line chunk of text with entirely broken line breaks.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role:** By injecting the strong persona of a "10-year veteran sports journalist," we force the AI to elevate its output from a dry, mechanical data dump into a highly engaging, broadcast-quality news report.
2. **Constraints:** We heavily prioritized the mobile reading experience. By strictly banning traditional tables—which notoriously break UI layouts on small screens—and forcing an emoji bulleted list, we guarantee a highly scannable, visually pleasing format.
3. **Warning:** To proactively neutralize "hallucinations"—the single most catastrophic error in sports journalism—we established rigid guardrails, forcing the model to infer insights exclusively from the provided dataset.
