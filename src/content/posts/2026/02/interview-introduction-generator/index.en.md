> _"Now, relax and please start with your 1-minute introduction."_

The moment you open the interview room door, your heart races and your palms get sweaty. As soon as you sit down, that first question inevitably comes flying at you. You’ve practiced and memorized it hundreds of times, but when you actually open your mouth, only cliché and stale phrases like "I am diligent in everything I do..." or "I value communication..." seem to float in the air. This is the **painful tragedy of the first minute** that most candidates experience. Interviewers are people suffering from chronic fatigue who are obligated to listen to dozens of introductions every day. Vague and abstract adjectives like "passionate" or "ready to learn" go straight to the mental trash bin without even grazing their minds. The fact that you passed the rigorous document screening means you’ve already objectively proven at least the minimum job competency. However, if you fail to land a **fatal hook** in that crucial first minute, the following 30 minutes of the interview will merely turn into a formal rite of passage. Isn't it incredibly unfair that your hard-earned experiences and shining achievements are undervalued just because the 'verbal packaging' is outdated?

The human brain is wired to feel bored by predictable information and immediately cut off focus. The moment you open your first sentence with "I was born in a happy family...", the interviewer's brain has already firmly shut its shutters. On the other hand, the moment a clear and specific figure like **"I have experience reducing customer acquisition costs by 30% through data"** hits their ears, the brain instinctively develops a strong curiosity about the story behind it. This is the **'Headline News' effect** that flips the interview game. Your 1-minute introduction shouldn't be a long-winded biography or a documentary; it should be the most provocative and attractive 15-second Super Bowl ad copy.

Now is the time to boldly discard the dry, stiff text written in your resume and prepare a 'Sales Pitch' fully armed with **specific numbers and overwhelming results**. But what if you're stuck on which part of your seemingly ordinary experience to emphasize as the core, or how to speak logically with a clear beginning, middle, and end?

This is exactly when you need an **AI Copywriter** to transform your scattered experiences into a perfect and smooth business story. Top-tier AI models like Claude or ChatGPT are world-class text experts that have deeply learned data from hundreds of thousands of successful cover letters and global business speeches. Just by tossing in your text fragments (resume and job description), the AI brilliantly identifies the **'core competencies'** that the interviewer wants to hear right now from your seemingly trivial experiences and weaves them into golden sentences.

Imagine this: the moment you utter your first sentence with a confident voice, the interviewers, who were staring only at their monitors, all look up at once and lock eyes with you. And exactly as you intended in your meticulously designed script, they start pouring out questions about **those core achievements and special experiences** that you can answer most confidently. An interview is not a place to passively defend against an interviewer's attacks, but a business meeting where you actively sell the best premium product called 'yourself.' With the **1-minute interview winning script** refined precisely and sharply by AI, take full control of the interview and lead it your way. ## 🚀 Real Experts Write Like This

This is a prompt completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` in brackets according to your situation, and deploy it immediately to your practice.

### 🥉 Basic Version (For Entry-level / Open Recruitment)

Use this when you want to convey your burning passion for the job and infinite potential with a heavy impact.

> **Role:** You are an HR manager at a large corporation and a senior interviewer with 10 years of experience.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Candidate Specs: `[Graduated with a Management degree, President of on-campus marketing academic club, 1 year of part-time experience at a convenience store]`
> - Target Position: `[Entry-level B2C Consumer Goods Marketing]`
>
> **Task:**
> Write a 1-minute interview introduction script that can instantly focus the interviewer's attention.
>
> 1. Emphasize "the experience of identifying customer needs directly in the field" as my core weapon.
> 2. Thoroughly exclude abstract expressions (diligence, effort, etc.) and explain persuasively with my own specific episodes.
> 3. Write in a natural spoken style as if speaking in an actual interview, and keep sentences short to improve readability.

### 🥇 Pro Version (For Experienced / Career Change)

Use this when you want to logically and confidently appeal that you are 'ready to perform' and can produce results immediately upon joining the organization.

> **Role:** You are a top-tier headhunter and career coach specializing in C-level executive recruitment.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Target Position: `[Performance Marketer / Growth Hacker for a Series B IT Startup]`
> - Core Competencies: `[1. Reduced CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) by 30% through A/B test optimization, 2. SQL-based cohort data analysis skills, 3. Fast hypothesis verification execution without fear of failure]`
>
> **Task:**
> Write an intense and overwhelming 1-minute introduction script that will be deeply engraved in the interviewer's mind.
>
> 1. **Opening (Hook):** Start with an intuitive and provocative core sentence like "I am [Name], a marketer who creates revenue with data."
> 2. **Body:** Logically develop the achievement of '30% CAC reduction' among the core competencies mentioned above, along with clear figures.
> 3. **Closing:** Finish by stating specific and confident ambitions about how my proven practical know-how can contribute to the explosive growth of this company.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Adjust the text length to fit exactly 50 seconds when spoken at a normal adult pace. (Approximately 350-400 characters including spaces)
> - Tone & Manner: Maintain a tone that highlights overwhelming professionalism and confidence, but is never arrogant (Confident but Humble).
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - To prevent hallucinations, never make up imaginary figures or experiences that I did not provide.

## 💡 Writer's Comment (Insight & How to use)

Keep in mind: the 1-minute introduction script meticulously written by AI is not a perfect 'final version' to be carried into the interview room and read like a parrot. It is merely the best and most powerful **Draft** that sharpens your tangled thoughts and scattered experiences. Reciting this flashy and perfect-looking script written by AI like a robot, without missing a single syllable, in the extremely tense state of an interview room will instead give the interviewer the worst impression of being an 'artificial and soulless candidate,' which is a dangerous strategy that leads to fatal point deductions.

The real core purpose of a 1-minute introduction is absolutely not to cram and brag about all 20-30 years of your life story in just 60 seconds. It is the process of accurately throwing an irresistible bait to the interviewer: **"Among my many competencies and experiences, this part is the most powerful and attractive, so please throw intensive and persistent pressure questions at me about this part from now on!"** In other words, the ultimate goal is to **completely take control of the following 30-minute interview in the direction I thoroughly intended**.

Therefore, do not struggle to perfectly memorize every single particle of the generated script. Instead, deeply engrave in your mind **3 core keywords** that you must, at all costs, drive into the interviewer's mind and **1 core achievement figure** that proves your worth. In an actual interview, using those engraved core keywords as reliable stepping stones and speaking to the interviewer as if in conversation with your usual natural tone and rhythm will have much greater sincerity and appeal.

It doesn't end here. I’m revealing one more **hidden cheat key know-how of Variable Control** that only the top 1% of experts use in practice.
Don't just be satisfied with getting a great draft and close the prompt window. You must fire off a more ruthless follow-up prompt to the AI based on the satisfying first result.

**"In this 1-minute introduction script you just perfectly wrote for me, accurately predict 3 risk points where a strict interviewer might sharply fire pressure questions (Tough Questions), digging into my weaknesses or logical flaws. And for each of those fatal attacks, write how I should defend calmly and logically, along with overwhelming defense logic and even a perfect model answer script."**

If you expand the horizon of your prompt one step further like this, you go far beyond simply getting a 1-minute presentation script and gaplessly acquire even an **'Expected follow-up question defense and crisis response scenario manual.'** The very moment the interviewer bites the attractive hook you threw and triumphantly asks a sharp question, you can inwardly cheer while outwardly appearing flustered, and calmly deploy the perfect defense logic you’ve already prepared.

Also, try actively injecting and manipulating the **'Tone and Manner of the target company's organizational culture'** variable into the `[Context]` or constraints within the prompt. Depending on whether the company you are applying for is a very conservative and hierarchical traditional large financial firm, or an agile Silicon Valley-style startup that pursues a horizontal and free atmosphere, the overall vocabulary selection of the introduction script result must change completely.

At the bottom of the prompt, add constraints like **"This company I'm applying for is a traditional financial firm with a very conservative culture. Therefore, adjust the tone of the overall script to use very refined and conservative vocabulary so that 'deep trust in the organization, a heavy sense of responsibility, and silent diligence' are conveyed rather than 'strong individuality'"**, or conversely, **"It's a trendy IT startup where people wear casual clothes and use English nicknames, so completely change it to a hip and provocative tone that fully emphasizes 'fast execution, proactive problem-solving, and explosive energy that is not afraid of failure.'"**

AI is honest to a fault. The more specific and dense the resolution of the context you throw, the more sharp and hauntingly accurate the customized results it spits out. How finely and cleverly you control the variables—this is the true secret of prompt engineering that transforms an average candidate into an overwhelming 'Priority Hire.' An interview is not a lottery left to luck, but a sophisticated theater stage where those who are thoroughly prepared win. Control everything from the subtle tone and manner of the script, the speed of speaking, and even the expected sharp follow-up questions to prove your irreplaceable value.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: The script doesn't stick to my tongue and feels somewhat robotic and awkward.**
  - A: Sentences written by AI as a draft are often closer to stiff written language. After getting the first script, try entering a follow-up prompt: **"Change the written script into a very natural and appealing spoken style as if briefing a close senior at work. Refine the tone by appropriately mixing endings like '~' and '~' to make it feel less tense."** The flavor of the sentences will change significantly.

- **Q: I'm a new grad and my job experience is so lacking that I have nothing to write about. What should I do?**
  - A: For entry-level candidates, they don't demand great and grand business results from the start. In the `[Core Competencies]` part of the prompt, write as specifically as possible **how you solved small problems in the past with what attitude and intense deliberation (e.g., overcoming fatal conflicts in a team project, deriving insights through all-night data research, etc.)**. AI will brilliantly extract the powerful 'job suitability' that the interviewer wants from those seemingly trivial experiences of yours.
