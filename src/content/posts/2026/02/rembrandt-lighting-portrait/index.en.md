## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this prompt work equally well in DALL-E 3 and Midjourney?**
  - A: Yes, both models understand the term "Rembrandt lighting" perfectly. However, Midjourney (especially v6.0) tends to handle the subtle skin textures and deep shadows (chiaroscuro) with a slightly more cinematic, photorealistic flair. DALL-E 3 will follow the prompt strictly but might lean slightly more illustrative unless you heavily emphasize "photorealistic."

- **Q: Sometimes the triangle of light doesn't appear on the cheek. How do I fix this?**
  - A: AI can sometimes struggle with exact spatial lighting. If the "Rembrandt patch" is missing, try adding directional cues to the prompt, such as "single light source from the upper left, casting a shadow across the right side of the face."

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1.  **"Rembrandt lighting" & "Chiaroscuro":** These aren't just descriptive words; they are heavily weighted artistic concepts in the AI's training data. They trigger a specific mathematical understanding of contrast.
2.  **"Triangle of light":** A micro-detail that forces the AI to specifically render the signature element of the Rembrandt style, ensuring the shadow isn't just a generic fade.
3.  **"--style raw" (Midjourney):** This parameter strips away Midjourney's default aesthetic "beautification," allowing the raw, dramatic shadows of your prompt to take full control of the image.
