## 💡 Author's Insights (How to use)

The most common mistake beginners make with music AI is <b>'entering only the lyrics'</b> into the prompt box. The AI audio engine doesn't automatically infer the literary meaning or hidden emotions of the text like a human. It is trained to react much more sensitively to the placement and logic of <b>'Structure Tags'</b>.

The first cheat code to explode your output quality is the <b>strategic placement of directing tags</b>. Go beyond just Verse 1 and Verse 2. Aggressively insert specific musical directing tags like `[Build-up]`, `[Drop]`, `[Guitar Solo]`, `[Beat Drop]`, and `[Acapella]` between your lyrics. This instantly adds dramatic progression and explosive dynamics to a flat song. For example, placing `[Pre-Chorus]` and `[Build-up]` in succession right before the highlight maximizes tension, delivering a thrilling catharsis to listeners when the `[Chorus]` finally hits.

The second core know-how is the <b>sharp segmentation of genres</b>. When controlling the `[Concept]` variable, never just say 'Pop' or 'Dance'. The audio generation algorithm reacts most strongly to <b>'Sub-genre'</b> keywords. Narrowing down the genre to 'Synth-pop', 'Dream Pop', 'Jersey Club', 'UK Garage', or 'Hyperpop' significantly increases the quality and sound consistency. Adding specific music attributes like <b>'135 BPM'</b>, <b>'dreamy female vocals'</b>, or <b>'heavy bass'</b> separated by commas allows you to accurately imprint your desired tone and manner onto the AI.

Thirdly, leverage the <b>linguistic mix-match (Konglish)</b> characteristic of K-pop. Trendy grooves often come from the natural intersection of English and Korean. When English words are mixed in, the AI vocal's flow and rhythm are maximized, erasing mechanical monotony and adding a sophisticated vibe. If the AI vocal's Korean pronunciation sounds awkward, try writing English words phonetically in Korean (e.g., writing 'Girl' as '') or using Latin transliteration for certain Korean lyrics. The vocal engine's intonation algorithm recognizes this and produces a much more natural and rhythmical singing style.

Lastly, the `[Background]` and `[Concept]` variables in the provided prompt are a blank canvas for your creativity. Whether it's 'Lo-fi for a rainy day in a cafe', 'Powerful Pop-punk to wake up to on the way to work', or 'Dark Synthwave in a Cyberpunk universe', any theme works. Also, controlling the song length is crucial. Since Suno has a limit on the length of audio generated at once, entering too many lyrics might result in the song cutting off before the highlight. It is better to create a <b>high-impact first verse</b> first. Once you have a verse you like, use the `Extend` feature to naturally add subsequent parts—this is the real secret used by pros to get high-quality audio. This prompt is the master key that translates your rough text into the <b>'musical language'</b> that the AI understands best. I highly recommend experimenting with these variables to complete your own signature sound.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Genre Deconstruction and Reconstruction:** It's designed to force the vague human request of "NewJeans style" into professional sub-genre keywords (UK Garage, Jersey Club, Y2K aesthetic) that the AI audio engine understands without error, maximizing output resolution.
2. **Linguistic Mix-match (Konglish):** Trendy K-pop grooves occur at the natural intersection of English and Korean. By explicitly instructing the mix of both languages, it erases the mechanical monotony of AI vocals and adds a sophisticated rhythm.
3. **Empowerment of Meta Tags:** The prompt is controlled to prioritize structural tags (like `[Chorus]`) over the literary content of the lyrics, enabling the AI to lead the dynamics of the arrangement and instrumentation beyond just generating a vocal line.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Who owns the copyright and ownership of songs generated by AI?**
  - A: If you are using a paid subscription plan (Pro or Premier) for Suno or Udio, the commercial usage rights and ownership of the generated audio generally belong to the user. However, if you use a free plan, it is strictly for non-commercial use and copyright belongs to the platform. Always cross-verify the latest license terms of each service before starting a project.

- **Q: My song cut off after the first verse. How do I finish it naturally?**
  - A: In Suno, you can use the `Extend` feature to naturally connect the back part of an existing song. During this process, entering lyrics with `[Outro]` or `[Fade Out]` tags in the additional prompt will create a smooth finish, as if mixed by a professional engineer.

- **Q: How can I fix awkward or unnatural Korean pronunciation in AI vocals?**
  - A: Try writing English words phonetically in Korean (e.g., 'Girl' -> '') or transliterating Korean lyrics into the Roman alphabet. The vocal engine's intonation algorithm often recognizes this and produces much more natural and rhythmical pronunciation.
