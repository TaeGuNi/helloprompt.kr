### ü•á Pro Version

Use this for precise, shot-by-shot production control, ensuring visual consistency across multiple generations.

> **Role (Role):** You are an Academy Award-winning Film Director and Technical VFX Supervisor.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: We are producing a short film in the `[Genre]` genre. The overall mood should be `[Mood/Vibe]`.
> - Goal: Generate a sequence of highly specific, technical prompts for a generative AI video model to create a cohesive scene.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Break down the following narrative sequence into `[Number]` distinct shots: `[Narrative Sequence Description]`.
> 2. For each shot, provide a highly engineered prompt that includes:
>    - Subject action and positioning.
>    - Camera movement (e.g., Dolly in, Pan left, Tracking shot).
>    - Lens type and focal length (e.g., 35mm, 85mm macro).
>    - Lighting setup (e.g., Chiaroscuro, Neon cyberpunk, Golden hour).
>    - Texture and film stock (e.g., 16mm grain, Anamorphic lens flare, 8k hyper-realistic).
> 3. Indicate variables using brackets like `[Variable]` so the user can fill them in later.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - The output must be formatted as a sequential shot list using bullet points or numbered lists.
> - Avoid vague adjectives; use precise cinematic terminology.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - If a specific camera movement is incompatible with current AI video generation limitations, suggest the closest viable alternative instead of making up impossible physics.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can AI video generators maintain character consistency across different shots?**
  - A: It is currently one of the biggest challenges. To mitigate this, keep your character descriptions extremely consistent in your prompts, or use image-to-video features where you feed the AI a reference image of your character for every shot.

- **Q: Will AI replace traditional filmmakers?**
  - A: Not entirely. AI is replacing the _logistics_ of filmmaking, not the _taste_ or _vision_. The directors who will thrive are those who master these tools to bring their unique visions to life faster and cheaper.

- **Q: Are there copyright issues with AI-generated movies?**
  - A: Yes, the legal landscape is still evolving. Currently, AI-generated content cannot be copyrighted in many jurisdictions unless there is significant human authorship involved in the curation and editing process. Always check the terms of service of the specific AI tool you are using.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Assignment:** Framing the AI as a "Technical VFX Supervisor" forces it to abandon generic descriptions and adopt industry-standard cinematic language.
2. **Structural Breakdown:** By forcing the AI to divide the narrative into a "sequential shot list," we prevent it from trying to cram an entire story into a single, chaotic 4-second generation.
3. **Cinematic Parameters:** Explicitly requesting lens types, camera movements, and lighting setups constrains the AI's latent space, resulting in much higher quality, photorealistic outputs.
