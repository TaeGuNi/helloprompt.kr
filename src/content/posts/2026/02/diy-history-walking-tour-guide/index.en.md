## 🚀 Real Experts Write Like This

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` to match your situation, and put it to work immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this for a quick, lightweight outline of a route and theme.

> **Role:** You are a local expert and 'Storytelling Tour Planner' who knows every corner of the back alleys in `[Paris]`.
>
> **Task:** Create a half-day walking tour course themed around the `[French Revolution]` that I can enjoy deeply on my own without an expensive guide.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this for a complete tour master plan that includes sophisticated route optimization, accurate time estimates, an on-site audio guide script, and even the placement of stylish rest areas.

> **Role:** You are a senior 'Storytelling Walking Tour Guide' with 10 years of experience, perfectly well-versed in the layered history and hidden alleyway stories of `[City Name]`.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Destination: `[City Name]`
> - Schedule: Half-day walking tour
> - Core Interests: `[Interests: e.g., Footsteps of Sherlock Holmes / 1920s Jazz Age / Gaudí's Unfinished Projects]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Plan an optimal walking route by organically connecting 4–5 meaningful locations that precisely align with my interests.
> 2. Design the route to be easy to walk, minimizing physical exertion, and write immersive storytelling scripts for each spot as if listening to a professional audio guide.
> 3. Include one rest spot, such as a scenic cafe or a peaceful park that matches the tour theme, at the exact timing when my legs will start to get tired.
> 4. To ensure I can search and find my way on Google Maps immediately, provide the 'Local Language Name' for all locations.
> 5. Clearly indicate the `[Variables]` so they are intuitively understood.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Strictly limit the total distance to under 3km and the total time to under 3 hours, including walking and sightseeing.
> - Specify a clear **[Starting Point]** and **[Ending Point]**.
> - For mobile readability, do NOT use tables; instead, organize the information in a clear bulleted list.
> - **Bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - To prevent AI hallucinations, do not invent non-existent cafes or fictional locations. Exclude any place with uncertain location information and recommend only verified landmarks. If you don't know something, suggest an alternative instead of making it up.

## 💡 Writer's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

The strongest weapon of this prompt is that it controls both **'physical constraints (3km, 3 hours)'** and **'storytelling commentary'** simultaneously. Traditional package tours where dozens of people rush around never consider an individual's stamina or walking speed. In a relentless march, we struggle to keep up with the guide's flag rather than enjoying the scenery. By using this prompt, you can precisely design **'golden rest timings'**—like sitting in a local cafe that fits your taste perfectly for an espresso just as your legs start to ache. The AI serves as both a great secretary and a meticulous tour manager.

The most important factor in putting this prompt into practice is the **specificity of the `[Variables]`**. If you simply write "historical places" in the `[Interests]` field, the AI will provide a generic answer like "the most famous old town." To pull 200% performance out of the prompt, you must **micro-target** this section. For example, give it a narrow and specific theme like **"The back alleys of Paris used as Resistance hideouts during WWII"** or **"The forgotten paths of Montmartre where 19th-century painters strolled for inspiration."** AI will dig through its vast database and present gem-like locations that ordinary tourists would never know.

Additionally, the physical limit of "under 3km" specified in the **Constraints** is a crucial safety measure. LLMs, especially those not perfectly integrated with Google Maps, can sometimes commit hallucinations, planning a 2-hour walk between point A and point B while ignoring the actual physical distance. To block this at the source, I set a clear limit of 3km. If you are traveling with children or the elderly, consider reducing this figure to **"under 1.5km."** The AI is trained to find the best solution within those constraints.

**Practical Tip:** Before you leave for your trip, copy the local language place names provided by the AI and pin them in advance using the 'Saved > Maps (My Maps)' feature in **Google Maps**. Copy the storytelling scripts into the notes section of each pin to complete your own perfect guidebook. Once on-site, just pull up the custom map and follow the blue GPS dot. As you look at the majestic architecture before you and read the script in the notes, you will experience an intellectual journey so dense it rivals premium private tours costing hundreds of dollars. I highly recommend enjoying this thrilling experience of regaining full sovereignty over your travel.

**Hallucination Handling Know-how:** Even if the AI plans a great course, real-world information changes daily. Many local restaurants or small cafes have closed or changed hours since the pandemic. While I’ve placed a strong **Warning** guardrail against inventing non-existent cafes, a final cross-check is essential. At the very least, check the **rest spot**—the highlight of the tour—on Google Maps for the latest reviews and opening status immediately after getting the AI's recommendation. If it's closed, you can direct the AI to make an immediate correction via re-prompting: "The cafe you recommended is closed, so replace it with another atmospheric cafe within a 500m radius." If you master this flexible approach, it's like having a perfect personal guide wherever you go in the world.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if a place recommended by AI is closed or under construction?**
  - A: AI models (especially free versions) may not reflect 100% real-time business status. While the prompt sets a great framework, I strongly recommend **cross-checking the latest reviews and opening hours on Google Maps** before you depart.

- **Q: What if the recommended route is too difficult to walk or requires public transit?**
  - A: Because the prompt includes a strict constraint of "under 3km," excessive forced marches are blocked in advance. If 3km is still too much, simply re-prompt: **"Re-plan the route by reducing the total walking distance to under 1.5km."**

- **Q: Can this be used for indoor tours, like museums or art galleries, rather than outdoor walks?**
  - A: Absolutely! Simply narrow the context of your interest to a specific landmark, like **[Renaissance Painting Tour inside the Louvre Museum]**. You can get a deep and excellent **personal indoor docent script** that's good enough to skip the line for the official audio guide.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Persona with Immersive Authority (Role):** I assigned the AI a specific profession—a **'Senior Storytelling Guide with 10 years of experience'**—rather than a simple informant. This moves the output away from dry Wikipedia-style data and into a lively narrative that stimulates the traveler's curiosity.
2. **Context-Aware Physical Constraints (Constraints):** To prevent exhaustion in unfamiliar places, I set a clear **upper limit on distance (3km) and time (3 hours)** and forced the inclusion of 'Local Language Names' to maximize practical utility on the street.
3. **Critical Hallucination Guardrail (Warning):** To prevent the AI from inventing fictional locations or leading travelers down the wrong alleys, I inserted a strict **Warning**, significantly increasing the reliability and on-site safety of the results.
