Reading that "data center investment is still in its early stages" makes you want to buy, but news that "revenue growth is slowing" makes you afraid. When you factor in the <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">cyclical risks</span> inherent to the semiconductor industry, things get complicated.

This prompt analyzes NVIDIA from the perspective of an "AI Infrastructure Platform Company." It quantitatively evaluates **penetration relative to TAM (Total Addressable Market)**, the **switching costs** of the CUDA ecosystem, and the threat levels from AMD and custom ASICs.

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to use)

The core of this prompt is the **"Quantification of the CUDA Moat."** Most NVIDIA analyses just say "the CUDA ecosystem is strong." However, if you cannot express "How strong?" with numbers, you cannot use it for investment decisions. If you explicitly instruct the AI to "quantitatively evaluate switching costs," it produces specific figures like "it takes an average of 6–18 months and $5M–$20M in engineer labor costs for a company to migrate from CUDA to another platform." These numbers are the key evidence for judging the threat level of competitors.

There is a reason I included the `[Cycle Risk]` item. Semiconductors have historically repeated a **boom-bust cycle**. Cisco did it during the 2000 Dot-com bubble, and the 2022 plunge followed the 2021 memory semiconductor boom. The claim that "This time is different" is the most dangerous phrase in the investment world. By forcing the AI to analyze the "downside in a peak scenario," you can <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">prepare for the worst-case scenario in advance</span>.

- **Q: Can AI accurately analyze the technical details of the semiconductor market?**

  - A: Try changing the `[Subject of Analysis]` in this prompt to a "NVDA vs. AMD" comparative analysis. Comparing the two companies with the same analytical framework allows you to clearly see market share trends and relative valuation gaps.
