Walking into an interview unprepared often leads to superficial, unstructured conversations. When you rely entirely on gut feelings, you risk missing the complete picture. ## 🚀 The Solution: "Interviewer AI"

### 🥉 Basic Version (Quick & Easy)

Use this when you simply need a fast, reliable baseline.

> **Role:** You are an Interviewer for the HR Team of a top-tier corporation.
> **Request:** Generate a list of sharp, insightful interview questions designed to uncover the applicant's true capabilities.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need detailed, high-quality output and a comprehensive evaluation rubric.

Copy the **PROMPT** below and paste it into ChatGPT or Claude.

> **Role:** You are a Senior HR Interviewer and Talent Acquisition Expert at a top-tier corporation.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am preparing to interview an applicant for the `[Job Position]` role.
> - Target: The key highlights of the applicant's resume include: `[Resume Summary or Key Achievements]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Formulate 3 in-depth, scenario-based questions to rigorously assess their core job competencies.
> 2. Create 2 behavioral questions designed to evaluate their Culture Fit and soft skills.
> 3. Provide 1-2 probing questions (tail questions) for each main question to dig deeper into their initial responses.
> 4. Outline an Evaluation Guide detailing what constitutes a 'Strong Answer' versus a 'Red Flag' for each specific question.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - All questions must be open-ended; strictly avoid any questions that can be answered with a simple "Yes" or "No".
> - Focus exclusively on the Behavioral Event Interview (BEI) framework, asking about past experiences rather than hypothetical pressure scenarios.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not invent arbitrary evaluation criteria. Base the evaluation guide strictly on established industry standards for the specified role to prevent hallucination.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I use this for entry-level positions or internships?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just add a constraint like *"Focus on foundational skills, learning agility, and academic projects rather than extensive industry experience."*

- **Q: Does this work well with the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it works flawlessly. However, advanced models like **GPT-4** or **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** will generally provide more nuanced probing questions and a much more sophisticated evaluation guide.

- **Q: What if the generated questions are too technical for me to evaluate properly?**
  - A: You can simply instruct the AI to *"Provide the evaluation guide in layman's terms"* or ask it to generate questions that focus primarily on problem-solving methodologies rather than highly specific syntax or tools.

---

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning the persona of a "Senior HR Interviewer," the AI naturally adopts a highly professional, discerning, and analytical tone.
2. **Contextual Anchoring:** Feeding the `[Resume Summary or Key Achievements]` ensures the questions are meticulously tailored to the candidate, completely preventing generic output.
3. **Behavioral Focus (BEI):** Constraining the AI to use the BEI framework forces candidates to prove their skills through actual past actions, not just theoretical knowledge.
4. **Evaluation Guide:** Asking for a rubric transforms the prompt from a simple question generator into a comprehensive interviewing tool, standardizing your entire assessment process.
