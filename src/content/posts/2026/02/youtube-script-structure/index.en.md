## 🚀 How True Experts Write

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variable]` parts in brackets to suit your situation.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly catch the overall framework and flow of the script.

> **Role:** You are a `[Main Producer for a 1M-Subscriber YouTuber]`.
>
> **Task:** For the `[Topic]` I provide, write a YouTube video script draft that prevents 5-second drop-offs and ensures viewers watch until the end.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a 'perfectly formed script' that considers viewer psychology and details ready for immediate shooting.

> **Role:** You are the `[Main Producer and Head Planner for a YouTube channel with 1 million subscribers]`. You are a top expert in writing scripts that penetrate viewer psychology and minimize drop-off rates.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Channel Target: `[20-30s Office Workers]`
> - Video Topic: `[3 Realistic Ways to Master English Conversation Alone at Home Without Spending a Dime]`
> - Video Length: Approximately `[8–10 minutes]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> Write a YouTube script that maximizes audience retention according to the following structure.
>
> 1. **Opening Hook (0–30s):** Accurately hit the viewer's pain point and present an overwhelming reason and expected benefit for watching this video until the end.
> 2. **Body Development (3 Parts):** Divide the topic into 3 sub-themes and explain them logically. Include appropriate examples or metaphors for each part so the viewer doesn't get bored.
> 3. **Closing & CTA:** Along with a summary of the content, write an emotional or logical script that makes viewers naturally want to 'Subscribe' and 'Like' without feeling pressured.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Write in a colloquial, conversational style as if talking 1:1 with the viewer in front of the camera, not a stiff explanatory tone.
> - Include production cues for parts requiring visual/audio elements in the format `[Screen: ~]`, `[Caption: ~]`.
> - Thoroughly exclude unnecessary introductions and deliver only the core points with speed.
> - For mobile readability, never use tables; organize in a highly readable bulleted list format.
> - Be sure to **bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never use cliché expressions ("Hello, I'm [Name]. Today...").
> - Do not make up unverified information; write based only on facts. (Prevent Hallucinations)

## 💡 Author’s Commentary (Insight & How to use)

The true destructive power of this prompt lies in the **'Specificity of Persona'** and **'Forced Production Cues'** beyond simple text generation. The biggest mistake many people make when asking AI to write a script is giving a one-dimensional command like "Write a YouTube script." If you enter it like that, the AI will simply list flat text in a common blog style found floating around the internet.

However, we assigned a very powerful and specific persona at the beginning of the prompt: `[Main Producer and Head Planner for a YouTube channel with 1 million subscribers]`. This single line of code completely changes the AI's "brain structure." Among the vast data it has learned, the AI prioritizes weights for **'script structures of successful channels, psychological tricks to lock-in viewers, and speech patterns that maintain tension.'** It starts writing not just as a writer, but from the perspective of a director responsible for the video's success.

Additionally, the **forced production cues in the format `[Screen: ~]`, `[Caption: ~]`** included in the constraints make a huge difference in practice. If you want a vivid 'script as a video production' rather than just an information delivery script, you must clearly ask the AI to point out screen transition timing, caption points, and even the creator's expressions and gestures. The resulting output can immediately replace a storyboard on set and serves as a perfect guideline providing ideas for cut editing points and caption design to the video editor.

In practice, I put a lot of effort into **Variable Control** to utilize this prompt 200%. The quality of the output varies significantly depending on how sophisticatedly you tune the target and topic variables in the `[Context]` section. For example, instead of just writing 'Office Worker,' try specifying it as `[An office worker in their early 30s who prepares for a career change and does 2 hours of self-development every day after work]`. The empathy of the opening hook becomes sharp enough to hit close to home. The reader feels 'Wait, is this my story?' the moment they see the video and naturally becomes immersed until the end.

The **'Human Touch'** stage after the draft is derived is also essential. I go through a rewriting process by overlaying about 10–20% of my own personal experiences, failure stories, or my unique witty tone on top of the solid framework and narrative structure provided by the AI. Through this process, not only is scriptwriting time dramatically reduced (at least by 90%) compared to starting from scratch, but you can also completely erase any suspicion that it was 100% AI-written. It is the moment an invincible script is born, combining logical density with human charm.

If the output feels a bit stiff, don't be satisfied with the first result and continue with follow-up prompts. The moment you give an additional instruction like, "The script structure you just wrote is great. But the tone is a bit stiff, so please rewrite the entire thing in a comfortable and direct way, like a close friend giving serious advice over a drink," you will be amazed by the AI's incredible text-massaging ability.

Remember, a prompt is not a finished product vending machine, but a **collaboration tool for brainstorming with a top expert.** When you get stuck while planning a script, exchange ideas with the AI about which hook would be better. I strongly recommend that you constantly modify variables to suit your channel's personality and evolve it into your own master prompt perfectly synchronized with your channel.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: How do I adjust if the script comes out too long or too short?**
  - A: Try adding specific numerical guidelines for length in the constraints, such as "Write around 2,000 characters total" or "Describe each part of the body in detail with at least 3 paragraphs." The more clear the numbers, the more accurately the AI matches the length.

- **Q: Can I remove that awkward 'AI Slop' feeling?**
  - A: To prevent this, I've included constraints against cliché expressions and for colloquial writing. If the result is still stiff, try a follow-up prompt like, "Refine the current script into a comfortable tone, as if telling a story to a close friend in a room." The context will become much more natural and engaging.

- **Q: Can this be applied to short-form scripts like Shorts or Reels?**
  - A: Of course. However, for short-form, you must increase the weight of the hook extremely. If you change the video length in the prompt to 'Shorts under 1 minute' and add the instruction "Compress the body into just 1 core point," a short-form framework that maximizes immersion within a minute will be completed instantly.
