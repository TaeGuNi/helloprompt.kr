## 💡 Author's Commentary (Insight & How to Use)

This prompt is the most reliable and powerful cheat code for extracting **overwhelming quality relative to time** when generating AI images in practice. The biggest mistake many people make while learning how to use Midjourney is relying on **'copy-pasting someone else's prompts.'** When you copy and use long, complex English texts floating around the internet, the atmosphere of your desired subject (`[Subject]`) often conflicts with the optical terms attached at the end, resulting in bizarre outputs. For example, if you're describing a warm family dinner scene but the copied prompt includes heavy `dramatic chiaroscuro` or cyberpunk-style `neon reflections`, the image will be completely ruined.

However, by using this method of granting ChatGPT the powerful persona of a **'Chief Cinematographer,'** the AI **calculates and adjusts the lighting and camera lens settings itself** to perfectly fit the text situation you've entered. You no longer need to flip through an English dictionary every time to memorize unfamiliar optical terms like 'Volumetric' (light ray phenomenon) or 'Rim light' (backlighting that highlights a character's outline).

**Prompt Variable Control Know-how:**
When using this prompt in practice, the most important thing is the detail of the `[Subject]` variable. Rather than just writing `[Spaceship]`, the AI Cinematographer will pull out more dramatic settings if you provide a specific description like `[An old, smoking spaceship that has crash-landed on a lonely planet]`. Also, if you want to force the tone and manner of the result to a specific director's style, try subtly adding sentences like **"Reflect the cold and grand direction characteristic of Director Christopher Nolan"** or **"Apply perfect symmetry and pastel tones like Wes Anderson"** to the prompt instructions. The AI brilliantly back-calculates the lens focal length (mm) and lighting methods primarily used by those directors and melts them into the prompt.

**Problem Solving & Troubleshooting Guide:**
If you generate an image in Midjourney and it turns out too dark overall against your intention, it's because keywords like `dramatic shadows` or `chiaroscuro` were applied excessively. In this case, give feedback to the AI Cinematographer: **"Maintain the overall atmosphere, but increase the intensity of the Key Light and add a Fill Light so the subject's face is clearly visible."** Conversely, if the image looks too artificial like a plastic 3D model, try requesting: **"Reduce the digital rendering feel and add film grain and subtle depth of field characteristic of film cameras to make it look much more photorealistic."** In this way, the moment you hold the weapons of lighting and lenses, you stop being a passive user who takes whatever the AI gives and become a true master who does not allow an error of even one pixel.

In particular, the **'Teal and Orange'** color grading keyword consistently included in this prompt is a visual directing technique favored as a signature by Hollywood masters like Michael Bay. By maximizing the complementary contrast between skin tones (orange) and the background (teal), it instantly turns a flat image into a three-dimensional cut that seems to pop out. Don't wander around looking for suitable sources in Google Image search anymore; hire your own exclusive cinematographer to produce perfectly controlled 'works of art.'
