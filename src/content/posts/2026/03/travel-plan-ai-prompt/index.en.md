## 📝 Travel Planning: Let an AI Travel Agent Design Your Local Itinerary

- **🎯 Recommended for:** Independent travelers, those who find planning tedious, and anyone seeking unique, non-cliché routes.
- **⏱️ Time Required:** 3 hours of reading 10 blogs → 10 minutes for an AI itinerary
- **🤖 Top Performance:** GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.5 Pro

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

> _"I'm planning a 4-day trip to Osaka, but every blog recommends something different. I can't decide when to visit Universal Studios or if I should do a day trip to Kyoto... I've been staring at Google Maps for 3 hours."_

The real challenge in travel planning is **information overload**. While blogs, YouTube, and Instagram are full of info, tailoring it to "your schedule, budget, and taste" is a different story. Factoring in travel time, operating hours, and route efficiency can be overwhelming.

If you provide the AI with your **"schedule, budget, preferences, and companions,"** you'll get an <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">hourly itinerary + travel routes + estimated costs</span> in just 10 minutes. By asking for a "local course instead of a tourist trap," it will even recommend hidden gems and local spots not found on typical blogs.

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Route Optimization:** Instead of just pinning locations on a map, it automatically designs the optimal route considering travel time and transportation costs.
2. **Hourly Schedule:** It provides a specific plan for "what to do at what time," so you don't have to worry once you're on the ground.
3. **Automatic Budgeting:** By aggregating accommodation, transportation, food, and admission fees, you can predict the total cost before the trip.

## 🚀 How the Pros Write It

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Travel Planner]`.
>
> **Request:** Create a travel itinerary for `[4 days in Osaka]`. Include a day trip to Kyoto.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a `[10-year veteran travel guide specializing in Japan]`. You have traveled the Osaka, Kyoto, and Nara regions hundreds of times and deeply understand the difference between tourist routes and local courses. You are an expert in route efficiency and budget optimization.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Schedule: `[4 days/3 nights, April 2026 (Cherry Blossom Season)]`
> - Companions: `[Couple (late 20s)]`
> - Budget: `[1 million KRW per person (excluding flights)]`
> - Interests: `[Food Exploration > Shopping > Photography > Sightseeing]`
> - Avoid: `[Extremely crowded tourist spots, group tour routes]`
> - Accommodation: `[Hotel near Namba]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Hourly Schedule:** Create an itinerary with a `[HH:MM]` timeline for each day.
> 2. **Travel Routes:** Include transportation methods, travel time, and fares.
> 3. **Food Recommendations:** Suggest restaurants for each meal, including estimated costs and recommended menu items.
> 4. **Hidden Spots:** Include one local recommendation per day that isn't commonly found on blogs.
> 5. **Budget Summary:** Create a daily and total budget summary table.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Limit to 5 or fewer locations per day (I want a relaxed trip).
> - Consider timing to avoid cherry blossom season crowds (popular spots early in the morning).
> - Do not exceed the budget of 1 million KRW per person.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The key to this prompt is **"Interest Priority."** By specifying "Food > Shopping > Sightseeing," the AI designs the route around dining spots and naturally places attractions in between. <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">Without priorities, you'll get a generic tourist course.</span>

Pro Tip: Adding constraints like "local recommendations" or "places not common on blogs" significantly improves the quality of the AI's suggestions. Since AI knows about lesser-known spots from its vast data, this one line can change the quality of your trip.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Do the restaurants recommended by AI actually exist?**
  - A: Since recommendations are based on AI training data, always verify their existence and operating hours on Google Maps or Tabelog (tabelog.com). It may occasionally suggest places that are closed or non-existent.

- **Q: Can I use this for other regions like Europe or Southeast Asia?**
  - A: Yes. Just change the `[Region]` and `[Interests]` to get an itinerary in the same structure for any destination.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Realistic Constraints:** By specifying real-world conditions like budget, companions, and season, the AI generates an "actually executable" itinerary rather than a "theoretically perfect" one.
2. **Time-Based Structuring:** The `[HH:MM]` timestamps act as a navigation guide that you can follow in real-time while traveling.
