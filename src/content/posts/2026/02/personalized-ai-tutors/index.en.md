## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

Use the prompts below when you need to quickly grasp complex, unfamiliar concepts or when you need an immersive learning experience with high levels of interaction. Copy and fill in the `[Variables]` to deploy immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to grasp the skeleton of a complex or unfamiliar concept as quickly and intuitively as possible.

> **Role:** You are the world's kindest and most brilliant 1:1 private tutor for `[Subject Name]`.
>
> **Task:** My current knowledge level is `[Current Level]`. Please explain step-by-step using intuitive everyday analogies so that I can perfectly achieve the `[Learning Goal]`.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert Type)

Use this when you need a solidly designed curriculum and an immersive learning experience accompanied by high-level interaction.

> **Role:**
> You are a world-class professional tutor with 15 years of experience and a Master’s degree in Education from an Ivy League university. You excel at accurately diagnosing a student's cognitive level and leading them to realize insights themselves through the Socratic Method.
>
> **Context:**
> - Subject: `[Topic or technology you want to learn, e.g., Python Data Analysis]`
> - Student's Current Level: `[Your current level, e.g., I can use Excel but am new to coding]`
> - Learning Goal: `[Specific goal, e.g., Pre-process a work CSV file using Python within 1 week]`
> - Preferred Learning Style: `[e.g., Practice-oriented rather than theory, prefer intuitive analogies]`
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Thoroughly analyze the provided level and goals to present an optimized **step-by-step learning curriculum** in a Markdown list format.
> 2. Immediately after briefing the curriculum, start the first session of the first step.
> 3. When explaining new concepts, you must include at least one **intuitive analogy** tailored to my level.
> 4. After explaining a concept, provide a **core check-up quiz or question** to verify my understanding. Do not move to the next topic until I provide the correct answer.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Do not pour out a massive amount of information in a single response. (Keep the pace short, within a maximum of 3 paragraphs).
> - Do not reveal the correct answer to questions immediately; provide step-by-step hints to induce me to reason it out myself.
>
> **Warning:**
> - If you must use technical jargon, always provide an easy-to-understand translation or explanation in parentheses.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The most important core principle driving this prompt is the meticulous design of high-density **'Interaction'** rather than simple text output. Many people consume high-performing AIs like ChatGPT or Claude like a one-way Wikipedia, getting information and letting it evaporate quickly. However, the moment you explicitly assign the **Socratic Method** and **check-up quiz** conditions within the prompt, an ordinary AI chatbot evolves dramatically into a real-time 1:1 personal tutor that provides a completely different level of immersion.

Above all, **Constraint Control** determines the quality of this prompt. By accurately declaring the learner's `[Current Level]` and `[Goal]` in the prompt, the AI receives clear guidelines on what depth to use and which vocabulary to choose for its explanations. There is a world of difference between simply commanding "Teach me Python" and providing a specific **Context** like "Teach Python for data analysis to a marketer who knows Excel but doesn't know coding." Furthermore, through the **strong Constraints** of "Do not reveal the answer immediately," we have fundamentally blocked the fatal side effect of the AI being "too kind" and feeding the answer, which hinders the development of the learner's metacognition.

This prompt's true power is especially evident when learning a completely new programming language or a complex, unfamiliar work tool (e.g., Figma, Tableau). Try copying and pasting an arcane error message or a piece of code you encountered during practice. Then, dig deep and ask the tutor: _"Why is this error happening? Can you explain with an analogy how this connects to the concept I just learned in the first session?"_ The AI tutor will 100% understand your question's context and scratch your cognitive blind spots much more agilely and accurately than a paid offline lecture or a famous academy instructor.

If the results don't go the way you want, don't panic and provide feedback immediately. Take control of the conversation and tune it actively: _"The analogy you just used is too hard for me. Explain it again using a soccer analogy,"_ or _"I perfectly understand this part, so let's move quickly to the next advanced stage."_ Unlike human instructors, generative AI never gets tired or annoyed even if you ask the same question hundreds of times. Therefore, without worrying about how you are perceived, be persistent and ask questions until you perfectly understand, utilizing 200% of this powerful AI tutor's infinite capacity. I am confident that this prompt will be your most reliable and perfect pacemaker in the process of conquering a new field.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Context & Leveling Synchronization:** By declaring `[Current Level]` and `[Preferred Learning Style]` as clear variables at the beginning of the prompt, it prevents the AI from arbitrarily adjusting the difficulty and pouring out unnecessarily professional or, conversely, overly childish answers.
2. **Socratic Method:** Instead of one-sidedly feeding the correct answer, it forces the AI to constantly ask reverse questions, powerfully activating the learner's metacognition to identify what they know and what they don't.
3. **Information Chunking & Interaction Constraints:** Through physical constraints like _"Do not give too much information at once"_ and _"Do not move on until I answer,"_ it prevents information overload and ensures the learner maintains control over the conversation.
