## 🚀 How Real Experts Write Prompts

### 🥉 Basic Version (Menu-Focused)

The most common everyday situation. Use this when the main dish is decided and you want to pick a drink quickly without failing.

> **Role:** You are the head `[Sommelier]` of a Michelin 3-star restaurant.
>
> **Context:**
> Tonight's menu is `[Spicy Tteokbokki and Assorted Tempura]`.
>
> **Task:**
> Recommend 3 wines that pair best with this food.
>
> **Constraints:**
> 1. Choose cost-effective wines in the `[20,000–50,000 KRW range]`.
> 2. Select popular varietals that are easy for wine beginners to drink.
> 3. Explain in one core sentence why each wine pairs well with this food.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Precision Taste Analysis)

A professional-grade prompt that captures even the subtle nuances of your taste to ensure a '0% failure' pairing. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` to use it immediately.

> **Role:** You are a **'Personal Wine Curator'** who perfectly understands my palate.
>
> **User Profile:**
> - **Preferences:** `[Full-bodied style with moderate tannins and rich fruit aromas.]`
> - **Dislikes:** `[Avoid wines with sharp acidity or sweet wines with noticeable residual sugar.]`
> - **Budget:** `[Under 30,000 KRW]`
> - **Purchasing Location:** `[E-mart, Costco, CU Convenience Store]`
>
> **Context:**
> I'm preparing a home party with 4 friends. The main menu consists of `[Costco Bulgogi Pizza and Fresh Salmon Salad]`. Since there's a mix of wine experts and beginners, I need choices that will satisfy everyone without being polarising.
>
> **Task:**
> Propose wine options categorized into the following 3 groups:
> 1. **Safe Pick:** A popular wine that anyone can enjoy (include specific product names).
> 2. **Adventurous:** An unexpected combination with an incredible pairing.
> 3. **Value:** A wine with overwhelming performance relative to its budget.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Organize the output format using Markdown bullet points (List).
> - Specify the wine name (English/Korean), expected price range, and the core reason for recommendation (pairing point) for each category.
>
> **Warning:**
> - Do not make up information; if you are unsure, say "I don't know." Exclude discontinued vintages or limited editions that are difficult to find in the market. (Hallucination prevention)

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

"Wine with spicy tteokbokki?" Many might tilt their heads at this combination. However, if you throw this challenge to an AI Sommelier, it provides surprising answers that break our prejudices. It might match it with a refreshing **'Sparkling Wine (Cava, Prosecco)'** to pleasantly wash away the intense heat of capsaicin, or a **'Moscato d'Asti'** that softly embraces the spiciness.

AI has already perfectly internalized the tasting notes of tens of thousands of wines and the pairing data of global gourmets. it scientifically calculates sophisticated harmonies that are hard to come by through human intuition alone. The point I admire most when using this prompt in daily life is the **Strong Constraint Control**. Try adding a constraint like `[Purchasing Location: Among the February promotion items at the CU convenience store in front of my house]`. Even if it can't track real-time inventory, it brilliantly identifies the 'Best Picks' among the core regular lineup of that distribution channel.

**Know-how for Controlling Prompt Variables:**
To utilize this prompt 200% in practice, the **detail of the variables** is key. Instead of vaguely writing `[Beef]`, specify the characteristics of the ingredients and cooking method, such as `[Thick Hanwoo sirloin grilled over charcoal]` or `[Australian chuck eye roll seared in butter]`. The AI calculates the smoky aroma of meat touching fire or the richness of butter to decide perfectly whether to recommend a bold, oak-aged Cabernet Sauvignon or a delicate Pinot Noir.

I also highly recommend including the **wine experience of the participants** as a variable. Just by changing the context from `[Friends in their 20s new to wine]` to `[Acquaintances who usually enjoy natural wine]`, the AI completely shifts the nature of the recommendation list—from popular, sweet Moscato styles to funky, acidic orange wines with strong personality.

The days of standing in the liquor aisle, scanning dozens of labels with a wine app open, are gone forever. Use the **'Safe Picks'** provided by AI to bring your failure rate to 0%, and occasionally enjoy the pleasure of an unexpected, perfect mariage through **'Adventurous'** choices. The moment you tune the prompt variables to your situation, your own perfect personal sommelier is born.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I take a photo of a specific wine label and ask?**
  - A: Absolutely! Make active use of the **Vision features** in GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet. Upload a photo of the wine label and ask, "Will this wine suit my taste (full body, low sweetness)? Will it go well with the tuna sashimi I'm eating today?" The AI will instantly read the text on the label and provide a sharp analysis of detailed tasting notes and pairing suitability.

- **Q: Can I do this for whisky, sake, or traditional liquors besides wine?**
  - A: Yes, it works perfectly. Just change the **Role** variable in the prompt to 'Whisky Master Bartender', 'Kikizakeshi (Sake Sommelier)', or 'Traditional Liquor Expert'. You can gain in-depth gourmet insights specialized for each field beyond just one type of alcohol.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Context & Constraints:** No matter how great a vintage wine is, it's useless if you can't get it right now. By clearly limiting the 'Budget' and 'Purchasing Location', you block vague recommendations and force realistic, immediately actionable results.
2. **User Profile:** It avoids the error of simply bifurcating wine into 'Red/White'. By injecting specific preferences and dislikes into the prompt, it precisely narrows the search spectrum and maximizes satisfaction.
3. **Task Options:** It presents three clear curation axes: Safe, Adventurous, and Value. This doesn't mean the AI unilaterally decides for you; instead, it gives the final decision-making power back to the user, allowing for the most flexible choice suited to the day's mood.
