### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you want a highly detailed recipe with substitutions and step-by-step guidance. Copy the prompt below and paste it into your favorite AI.

> **Role (Role):** You are a `[Creative Culinary Researcher and Expert Chef]`.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I need to use up leftover ingredients in my fridge before they go bad, but I want something more exciting than the usual thrown-together meal.
> - Goal: Create a delicious, cohesive dish using only what I currently have.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Recommend 2 distinct dishes I can make by combining the provided ingredients.
> 2. Explain the recipe step-by-step so even a complete beginner can follow along easily.
> 3. If standard seasonings are required but missing from my list, suggest creative alternatives that I might already have.
>
> **My Ingredients (Variables):**
>
> - Main Ingredients: `[3 pieces of leftover fried chicken, half an onion, a little sour kimchi, a bowl of cold rice]`
> - Seasonings Available: `[Soy sauce, sugar, red pepper flakes, mayonnaise, sesame oil]`
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Do not suggest recipes that require me to go to the grocery store.
> - Format the output using clean Markdown with clear headings and bullet points.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well with free AI models like the basic ChatGPT?**
  - A: Absolutely. Even the free versions of ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini are excellent at culinary combinations. However, more advanced models (like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet) tend to offer slightly better reasoning for _why_ certain flavors work together and provide more nuanced substitute suggestions.

- **Q: What if I only have completely mismatched ingredients, like peanut butter and tomatoes?**
  - A: Try it anyway! AI is trained on vast amounts of global cuisine data. It might introduce you to an African peanut stew concept or an Asian-inspired dressing that perfectly utilizes those seemingly incompatible items.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Specific Persona:** By assigning the role of "Creative Culinary Researcher," the AI shifts away from generic cookbook answers and focuses on innovative problem-solving.
2. **Strict Constraints:** Explicitly stating "Do not suggest recipes that require me to go to the grocery store" prevents the AI from frustrating you with missing ingredient requirements.
3. **Variable Segregation:** Separating "Main Ingredients" from "Seasonings" helps the AI understand the core bulk of the meal versus the flavor profile, leading to much better recipe construction.
