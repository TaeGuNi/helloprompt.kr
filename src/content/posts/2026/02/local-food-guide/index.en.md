>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Current Location: `[Le Marais, Paris, France]`
> - Craving: `[Authentic French Home Cooking]`

>
> **Request (Task):**
>
> 1. Recommend 3 hidden-gem restaurants near my current location that are highly rated by actual locals, strictly excluding any tourist-oriented establishments.
> 2. Provide the Google Maps rating and pinpoint the absolute **Signature Dish** for each restaurant.
> 3. Suggest a specific menu combination (e.g., starter, main, and drink) to try upon visiting.
> 4. Detail the logistical information: Is a reservation required? Are walk-ins accepted? What are their exact break times?
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Strictly filter out restaurants that are overly saturated with tourist blog reviews.
> - Exclude any establishments with mentions of poor hygiene or rude service in their recent reviews.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Do not hallucinate or make up restaurants. Only recommend real, currently operating businesses. If you cannot find a matching place, tell me you cannot find one.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work with the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it works well with free models. However, premium models like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet are generally better at parsing nuanced local reviews and avoiding hallucinations about restaurant operating hours.
- **Q: What if the AI recommends a place that is permanently closed?**
  - A: AI models have knowledge cutoffs. Always quickly verify the recommended restaurant's operating status on Google Maps before heading out!

- **Q: Can I use this for street food or cafes?**
  - A: Absolutely! Just change the 'Craving' variable from `[Authentic French Home Cooking]` to `[Local Street Food]` or `[Specialty Coffee Roaster]`.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** Assigning the "Michelin Guide inspector" persona forces the AI to elevate its standards and focus on culinary quality rather than just overall popularity.
2. **Hyper-Specific Context:** Providing exact budgets and neighborhoods narrows down the search space, preventing generic, city-wide lists.
3. **Negative Constraints:** Explicitly excluding tourist traps and hygiene issues dramatically improves the quality of the output, acting as a powerful filter against noisy data.
