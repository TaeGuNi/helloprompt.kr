_"Do you just wait after the interview is over?"_

Overseas (especially in the US), a **Thank You Email** after an interview is essential, but it is still unfamiliar in Korea. That's why it's more effective. A polite email sent when everyone else stays silent makes you remembered as a 'sensible and polite candidate'.

### ğŸ¥‡ Pro Version

Use this when you need detailed quality.

Copy and use the **PROMPT** content below.

> **Role:** You are a communication expert with excellent business manners.
>
> **Context:** I had an interview with `[Interviewer Name/Title]` at `[Company Name]` today. I want to write an email after the interview to express my gratitude and re-emphasize how suitable I am for this job.
>
> **Interview Atmosphere/Content:**
>
> - `[Positive points: e.g. - Complimented my portfolio]`
> - `[Regrettable points: e.g. - Did not answer the last question well]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Draft a thank you email with a polite and courteous tone.
> 2. Mention the conversation (positive points) shared during the interview so it doesn't feel like 'copy+paste'.
> 3. Add a comment that slightly supplements the regrettable points without being pathetic. (Omit if none)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Keep it not too long (visible at a glance on a PC screen).
> - Make the subject line eye-catching but polite.
