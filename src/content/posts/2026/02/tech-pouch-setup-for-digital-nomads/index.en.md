### ü•á Pro Version (Expert Detail)

>

>
> - Background: I am going on a workation to `[Destination]` for `[Duration]`.

> - Primary Device: The main device I use is `[Device Model: e.g., 16-inch MacBook Pro M3 Max / iPad Pro]`.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Recommend a lightweight yet powerful multi-port charger (e.g., GaN) and cable combination capable of charging my primary device and accessories simultaneously.
> 2. Explain how to easily set up a dual-monitor workspace in a cafe or Airbnb (e.g., using a tablet or portable monitor).
> 3. Provide the electrical voltage and plug type requirements for the destination, advising if a specialized travel adapter or power strip is necessary.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Prioritize solutions that aggressively minimize overall carry weight and bulk.
> - Include fail-safe tethering (eSIM/USIM/Portable Wi-Fi) strategies for when local internet drops.
> - Focus recommendations on technical specifications (e.g., 65W+ PD charging, Thunderbolt 4) rather than specific brand names.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - If you are unsure about the exact voltage or internet reliability of the specified destination, state "I don't know" rather than guessing. Do not hallucinate plug standards.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Do I really need to specify my exact laptop model?**
  - A: Yes. The power draw of a 16-inch high-performance laptop is vastly different from a lightweight ultrabook. Specifying the model ensures the AI recommends a charger with sufficient wattage (e.g., 100W vs. 45W) so your battery doesn't drain while plugged in.

- **Q: Can this prompt help me find local co-working spaces?**
  - A: This specific prompt focuses on your personal hardware setup (the "Tech Pouch"). However, you can easily add a line in the Task section asking: "Recommend 3 reliable co-working spaces or laptop-friendly cafes in `[Destination]`."

## üß¨ Prompt Dissection (Why it works?)

1. **Environmental Context:** By specifying the destination, the AI automatically retrieves critical local data (plug types, voltage, typical internet stability), which is a common blind spot for digital nomads.
2. **Spec-Driven Recommendations:** Asking for specs (like "65W+ PD") rather than specific brands prevents the AI from suggesting outdated or locally unavailable products, focusing instead on the actual utility of the gear.
3. **Fail-Safe Planning:** The explicit instruction to include backup internet solutions addresses the #1 productivity killer for remote workers: dropped connections.
