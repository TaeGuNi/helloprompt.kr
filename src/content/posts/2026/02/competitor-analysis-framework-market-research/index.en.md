## üöÄ The Solution: "Market Researcher"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, high-level overview of a competitor.

> **Role:** You are a Senior Market Research Analyst.
> **Request:** Analyze the core strengths, weaknesses, and key benchmarking points of `[Competitor Name]`.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need an in-depth, report-ready analysis with strategic recommendations.

> **Role (Role):** You are an elite Market Research Analyst with 10 years of experience in competitive intelligence.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: Our product is `[Your Product/Service]`, and our primary competitors are `[Competitor A]` and `[Competitor B]`.
> - Goal: To uncover strategic gaps in the market and identify actionable differentiation strategies.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Analyze the core Value Proposition and primary target audience of each competitor.
> 2. Estimate their primary marketing channels and go-to-market strategies.
> 3. Identify Niche Markets or common customer pain points that these competitors are currently failing to address.
> 4. Summarize specific, actionable points we need to benchmark and how we can differentiate our offering.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Format the output using a clear Markdown Comparison Table for easy readability.
> - If the AI model supports it, utilize the web search function to incorporate the most recent market data and news.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Do not hallucinate data. If specific metrics or strategies are unknown, explicitly state "Insufficient Data" rather than guessing.

## üí° Writer's Insight

This framework completely transforms how I approach quarterly strategy reviews. Instead of drowning in endless tabs of competitor landing pages and customer reviews, I use this prompt to instantly generate a structural baseline.

The real magic here is the "Context" section. By explicitly stating your own product (`[Your Product/Service]`), the AI shifts from giving generic summaries to providing highly tailored, relative comparisons. It acts as an objective third party, often pointing out differentiation opportunities (like underserved niches or pricing gaps) that internal teams might overlook due to bias. I highly recommend running this prompt through Claude 3.5 Sonnet or GPT-4o with web browsing enabled to capture recent pricing changes or feature updates!

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well with free versions of AI models?**
  - A: Yes, but the Pro version shines brightest when using models connected to the live internet (like ChatGPT Plus, Copilot, or Gemini Advanced). Free models might rely on outdated training data, which can be fatal for competitive analysis.

- **Q: How can I ensure the AI's analysis is accurate and not hallucinated?**
  - A: Always cross-reference the generated table with your own quick search, especially for numerical data like pricing or user counts. The `Warning` section in the Pro prompt significantly reduces hallucinations, but human verification remains essential for final strategic decisions.

- **Q: Can I use this for B2B SaaS competitors?**
  - A: Absolutely! For B2B, I recommend tweaking the `Task` to include "Analyze their enterprise pricing strategy, integration ecosystem, and compliance certifications."

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** Assigning the "elite Market Research Analyst" persona forces the AI to use professional business terminology and structure its output like a formal consulting report.
2. **Contextual Anchoring:** Providing your own product name ensures the analysis is strictly comparative and relevant to your specific market position, rather than existing in a vacuum.
3. **Structured Output (Constraints):** Demanding a Markdown Comparison Table ensures the data is immediately usable for internal presentations or Excel exports, drastically reducing formatting time.
