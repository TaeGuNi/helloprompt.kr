Waking up early is only half the battle. Staring at a blank screen at 5:00 AM without a clear plan often leads to mindless scrolling or tackling low-value tasks like organizing emails. ## ‚ö°Ô∏è 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. Define your Single Most Important Task (MIT) the night before.

## üöÄ The Solution: "Sunrise Deep Work Planner" Prompt

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, no-nonsense schedule right before bed.

> **Role:** You are an elite productivity coach.
> **Task:** I have 2 hours tomorrow morning (5:00 AM to 7:00 AM). My main goal is to `[insert your main task, e.g., write a blog post]`. Break this down into four highly actionable 25-minute work blocks with 5-minute breaks.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this when you need absolute precision and neurobiology-backed flow triggers.

> **Role (Role):** You are a world-class productivity expert and neurobiology-based performance coach (similar to Cal Newport or Andrew Huberman).
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I am waking up at 5:00 AM to engage in "Deep Work" before the daily chaos begins. I have exactly 120 minutes of uninterrupted time.
> - Goal: My Single Most Important Task (MIT) is `[Your Goal, e.g., Drafting the Q3 Marketing Strategy Presentation]`.
> - Energy Level: High focus, but prone to getting stuck on minor details if not strictly timed.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Deconstruct my MIT into a precise, step-by-step schedule using the Pomodoro technique (25 mins work, 5 mins rest).
> 2. For each 25-minute block, define a hyper-specific, achievable micro-goal.
> 3. Provide a 5-minute pre-work ritual to trigger my flow state immediately upon waking.
> 4. Suggest what I should do during the 5-minute breaks to avoid cognitive fatigue.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Format the schedule as a clear Markdown table.
> - Do not include vague motivational advice; focus strictly on actionable execution steps.
> - The output must be realistic to achieve within the 120-minute timeframe.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Do not suggest checking email, social media, or news during the breaks. Keep the focus entirely on preserving dopamine and cognitive momentum.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well with free AI models?**
  - A: Absolutely. Basic models like the free tier of ChatGPT or Claude 3 Haiku handle this perfectly. However, Pro models tend to provide slightly better, more realistic micro-goals based on the context you provide.

- **Q: What if my MIT is too big for a 2-hour block?**
  - A: Tell the AI! Update the context to state: "This is a massive 10-hour project. Focus this 2-hour block ONLY on completing the first foundational phase." The AI will adjust the scope accordingly.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning a "neurobiology-based performance coach" persona, the AI incorporates scientifically proven focus techniques (like proper break activities) rather than just a generic to-do list.
2. **Decision Fatigue Elimination:** It shifts the cognitive load of planning to the AI the night before, allowing your brain to wake up and immediately enter a state of execution.
3. **Micro-Stepping:** Large tasks cause procrastination. Breaking them into 25-minute micro-goals triggers immediate action and frequent dopamine hits upon completion.
