> **Context (Context):**
>

> 3. Provide a rough calculation of when the highest-priority loan will be fully paid off (in months), assuming I consistently repay `[1,500 USD]` every month.
>
> **My Debt Profile (Variables):**
>
> 1. Loan A: Balance `[3,000 USD]` / Rate `[18%]` `[(Credit Card Debt)]`

> 3. Loan C: Balance `[10,000 USD]` / Rate `[6%]` `[(Personal Loan)]`
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Prioritize the method that yields the highest interest savings overall. (However, if the psychological burden of the debt is excessively high, weigh the benefits of eliminating smaller debts quickly).
> - Present the calculation process in a simple, easy-to-understand format using markdown tables where appropriate.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Do not hallucinate financial formulas or guess interest accruements. If the math is too complex to estimate without exact daily compound rates, state the limitations clearly.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work for varying interest rates, like adjustable-rate mortgages?**
  - A: Yes, but you should explicitly prompt the AI to account for potential rate hikes. Simply add a constraint like: "Assume Loan B's interest rate increases by 0.5% every 6 months."

- **Q: Can the AI factor in my daily living expenses?**
  - A: Absolutely. Instead of providing a fixed repayment amount, you can list your monthly net income and total essential expenses. Then, ask the AI to calculate your optimal monthly repayment capacity first before running the strategy simulation.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Dual Strategy Evaluation:** By explicitly asking the AI to compare the Snowball and Avalanche methods, you force it to provide a balanced, objective analysis rather than defaulting to a generic "pay your debts" response.

3. **Actionable Milestones:** Requesting the estimated payoff date for the very first loan provides a short-term, achievable goal that acts as a powerful catalyst for psychological momentum.
