## 🚀 The Solution: "Local Gourmet Guide"

### 🥉 Basic Version (Quick Search)

Use this when you need a fast, reliable dining recommendation on the go.

> **Role:** You are a Michelin Guide inspector and a local culinary expert.
> 
> **Request:** I want to avoid tourist traps. Recommend 3 highly-rated, authentic local restaurants near `[Location]` that serve `[Cuisine]`.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Detailed & Expert)

Use this when you want a meticulously curated dining experience complete with all the necessary logistical details.

Copy the prompt below and paste it into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

> **Role:** You are a Michelin Guide inspector and an elite local culinary expert.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Current Location: `[Le Marais, Paris, France]`
> - Craving: `[Authentic French Home Cooking]`
> - Budget: Around `[30 Euros]` per person for lunch.
>
> **Request:**
>
> 1. Recommend 3 hidden-gem restaurants near my current location that are highly rated by actual locals, strictly excluding any tourist-oriented establishments.
> 2. Provide the Google Maps rating and pinpoint the absolute **Signature Dish** for each restaurant.
> 3. Suggest a specific menu combination (e.g., starter, main, and drink) to try upon visiting.
> 4. Detail the logistical information: Is a reservation required? Are walk-ins accepted? What are their exact break times?
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Strictly filter out restaurants that are overly saturated with tourist blog reviews.
> - Exclude any establishments with mentions of poor hygiene or rude service in their recent reviews.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not hallucinate or make up restaurants. Only recommend real, currently operating businesses. If you cannot find a matching place, tell me you cannot find one.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

This prompt acts as your personal local fixer. The true magic happens in the **Constraints** section—specifically the explicit instruction to filter out establishments dominated by tourist reviews. While standard searches on Google Maps or TripAdvisor often prioritize restaurants with massive marketing budgets, this AI-driven approach cross-references genuine local sentiment to find places where neighborhood residents actually dine. For the most accurate results, always input your exact district or neighborhood (e.g., "Le Marais" instead of just "Paris") to receive highly localized, walkable recommendations.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work with the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it works perfectly fine with free models. However, premium models like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet are generally much better at parsing nuanced local reviews and avoiding hallucinations regarding restaurant operating hours.
- **Q: What if the AI recommends a place that is permanently closed?**
  - A: AI models have specific knowledge cutoffs. Always perform a quick verification of the recommended restaurant's current operating status on Google Maps before heading out!
- **Q: Can I use this for street food or cafes?**
  - A: Absolutely! Simply change the 'Craving' variable from `[Authentic French Home Cooking]` to `[Local Street Food]` or `[Specialty Coffee Roaster]`.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** Assigning the "Michelin Guide inspector" persona forces the AI to elevate its standards and focus on culinary excellence rather than just overall popularity.
2. **Hyper-Specific Context:** Providing exact budgets and neighborhoods drastically narrows down the search space, preventing generic, city-wide lists.
3. **Negative Constraints:** Explicitly excluding tourist traps and hygiene issues dramatically improves output quality, serving as a powerful filter against noisy data.
