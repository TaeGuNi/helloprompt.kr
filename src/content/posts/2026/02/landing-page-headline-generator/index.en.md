- **🎯 Recommended For:** Marketers, Founders, UX Writers, Growth Hackers

- **🤖 Recommended Model:** All Conversational AIs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.)

> _"Whether a visitor becomes a customer or a bounce statistic is decided in the first 3 seconds by your Hero Copy."_

Your Hero Section is the digital storefront of your entire landing page. Fail to instantly pinpoint your customer's deepest pain point and offer a crystal-clear solution, and your bounce rate will inevitably skyrocket. Stop staring at a blank screen waiting for inspiration to strike. Leverage this AI framework to engineer intuitive, conversion-optimized headline and sub-headline combinations in mere seconds.

## 🚀 The Solution: "Hero Section Maker"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Deploy this when you need rapid inspiration or a quick initial draft.

> **Role:** You are a world-class `[Web Copywriter]` and `[UX Writer]` specializing in high-converting landing pages.
> **Request:** Write a landing page Hero Section copy for `[My Product/Service]` that instantly hooks the target audience within 3 seconds.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Deploy this for production-ready, highly tailored copy that directly addresses your specific market and drives tangible results.

> **Role:** You are an elite `[Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) Copywriter]` and `[UX Writer]`. Your absolute expertise lies in crafting irresistible hooks that capture user attention within 3 seconds.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I need to design the Hero Section for our newly launching landing page.
> - Goal: Maximize the click-through rate (CTR) on our primary Call-To-Action button.
>
> **Task:**
>
> Please generate 3 distinct versions of the Hero Section based on the following product details:
>
> 1. Product Name: `[Insert Product Name]`
> 2. Target Audience's Pain Point: `[Describe the primary problem your customer faces]`
> 3. Our Solution/Differentiator: `[Explain how your product uniquely solves the problem]`
>
> For each version, deliver the following 3 elements:
>
> - **Headline:** A bold, powerful hook. Summarize the core value proposition in a single, punchy sentence.
> - **Sub-headline:** A supporting sentence that adds vital context, builds immediate trust, and provides concrete details backing the headline.
> - **CTA Button:** A highly actionable, low-friction button text that compels the user to click right now.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Keep the Headline strictly under 15 words. Make absolutely every word count.
> - The Sub-headline must not exceed 2 lines of text.
> - Use concrete, quantifiable benefits (e.g., "Save 10 hours," "Start for $0") instead of vague, fluffy buzzwords like "Innovative," "Best," or "Synergy."
> - Present the final output clearly in a structured Markdown format.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not invent features, metrics, or statistics that are not explicitly provided in the prompt. Stick strictly to the provided context to prevent hallucinations.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

Creating landing page copy often falls into the fatal trap of "we-centric" writing—focusing obsessively on what the company does rather than what the user actually gets. This specific prompt is reverse-engineered to force the AI into an uncompromising "you-centric" mindset. By explicitly requiring the _Customer's Pain Point_ and demanding _concrete benefits_, the AI automatically bypasses generic marketing fluff.

_Pro Tip:_ I highly recommend deploying the "Problem-Agitation" version first. Agitating a specific pain point directly in the headline ("Still doing X manually?") almost always outperforms generic benefit statements in initial A/B split tests.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this framework perform well with the free versions of AI (GPT-3.5/GPT-4o-mini)?**
  - A: Yes, the basic structural logic will work fine. However, for highly nuanced, emotionally resonant copy that truly reads like a seasoned human UX writer, premium models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet or GPT-4o will yield significantly sharper, more natural-sounding results.

- **Q: What if the generated copy feels too aggressive or "salesy"?**
  - A: Simply inject an additional constraint into the prompt: `Use a conversational, empathetic tone. Avoid aggressive sales jargon.` You can also pinpoint the exact brand voice you desire (e.g., `Tone: Playful, witty, and approachable like Mailchimp`).

---

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing (CRO Expert):** Instructing the AI to act specifically as a Conversion Rate Optimization expert violently shifts its output from generic text generation to laser-focused, performance-driven copywriting.
2. **Structural Enforcement:** By forcing the output into an exact trio (Headline + Sub-headline + CTA), you guarantee the AI delivers a complete, structurally sound Hero Section rather than a sprawling, disjointed paragraph.
3. **Anti-Buzzword Constraint:** The strict mandate to avoid corporate fluff like "Innovative" and instead rely on concrete numbers is the ultimate secret sauce. AI models naturally default to fluffy adjectives; this constraint forcefully recalibrates them to be precise and undeniably persuasive.
