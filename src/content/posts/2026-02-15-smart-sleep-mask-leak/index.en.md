Recent security research has uncovered a severe vulnerability in a popular smart sleep mask. Designed to improve sleep quality by tracking brainwaves (EEG), the device was found to transmit this sensitive biometric data without encryption.

## The IoT Security Nightmare

As smart home devices and wearables advance, they bring convenience but often neglect security. The affected sleep mask collected users' EEG data and sent it to its companion app, completely bypassing basic security protocols.

## Why Brainwave Data Matters

This goes beyond just knowing "when you sleep." Brainwave data is unique biometric information that can reveal:

- **Sleep Stages & Quality**: Detailed patterns like deep sleep or REM cycles.
- **Health Conditions**: Sleep disorders, stress levels, and potential neurological issues.
- **Personal Identification**: Brainwave patterns are as unique as fingerprints, potentially allowing user identification.

## What Can You Do?

This incident highlights the need to prioritize security over features when choosing IoT devices.

1. **Check for Updates**: Always keep your device firmware up to date.
2. **Choose Carefully**: Verify if the manufacturer clearly states data encryption and privacy policies.
3. **Limit Data Sharing**: Review app permissions and disable unnecessary data collection.

Technology exists to aid our lives, but without security, it can become a nightmare.
