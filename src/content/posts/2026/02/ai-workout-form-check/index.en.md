## 🚀 How Real Experts Do It

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` inside the brackets to match your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Perfect for a quick 1-minute check of any fatal flaws in your current form.

> **Role:** You are a fitness trainer with 10 years of experience.
>
> **Task:** The attached video is of my `[squat]` form. Identify any incorrect parts that risk injury and rate my form on a scale of 1 to 10.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this for a deep-dive coaching session and customized routine recommendations to perfectly prevent joint injuries and drive explosive stimulation to target muscles.

> **Role:** You are a senior personal trainer with 15 years of experience, possessing perfect knowledge of sports medicine and rehabilitation therapy.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am working out alone at `[home]`, and I feel a dull pain in my `[knees]` when performing this exercise.
> - Goal: To master the correct form with proper stimulation and zero injuries.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Joint and Path Analysis:** Perform a detailed frame-by-frame analysis of whether spinal neutrality is maintained and if the angles of the Hip, Knee, and Ankle are mechanically safe.
> 2. **Problem Diagnosis:** Identify critical flaws in my current posture using professional terminology such as "Knee Valgus" or "Butt Wink."
> 3. **Solutions and Accessory Exercises:** Recommend two specific stretches or accessory exercises to perform before or after the main workout to correct these faulty movements.
> 4. **Cognitive Queuing:** Provide one intuitive sentence (e.g., "Imagine tearing the floor apart with your feet") that I can repeat in my head to immediately improve my form during the next set.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use Markdown bullet points and bold text for easy reading on mobile devices.
> - Highlight the single most critical mistake that poses the highest injury risk first.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - If any part is difficult to judge clearly due to video quality or angle issues, do not guess. State "Unable to verify due to video blur."

## 💡 Author Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The **Vision API**, AI's powerful visual processing feature, captures even the most minute changes in joint angles frame-by-frame that the human eye could never catch. This is an innovative technology that goes beyond simple image recognition; it uses a deep-learning-based **precision Pose Estimation algorithm** to provide expert-level analysis. The core principle involves recognizing key human landmarks (shoulders, hips, knees, ankles) as mathematical 3D coordinates to accurately calculate mechanical imbalances and load distribution. This essentially allows us to enjoy a national-athlete-level biomechanical analysis system for free at home.

However, after testing the limits of these prompts and AI's cognitive abilities by filming dozens of home workout videos in various environments, I found that the single most important factor for perfect feedback is not the prompt's content, but rather the **'filming method.'** No matter how sophisticated your prompt is, if the quality of the video data itself is poor or the human body isn't properly recognized, all coaching becomes useless. The limits of the input visual data directly dictate the limits of the AI's reasoning.

For example, when I performed squats wearing baggy oversized t-shirts or wide jogger pants that completely hid my silhouette, the results were disastrous. The AI mistook the fluttering of the fabric for joint bending or spinal curvature, leading to completely wrong feedback or even inappropriate corrections that could cause injury. This is a classic **Visual Hallucination** error where the AI incorrectly estimates the alignment of bones hidden by clothing. Therefore, to elicit the best coaching results, users must strictly control the quality of the visual data they provide to the AI.

**Be sure to remember and practice these 'three filming cheats' to instantly boost AI analysis accuracy by over 200%:**

1. <b>Dress Code:</b> Wear tight workout clothes (leggings, slim-fit functional tees, sports bras, etc.) that clearly show the contours of your body, bone alignment, and especially the bending of major joints. Maximizing visual hints so the AI can accurately pinpoint the key landmarks of your hips, knees, and ankles is the most important first key to successful analysis. Clothes should not flutter so that the trajectory of the joints can be clearly tracked.

2. <b>Camera Angle:</b> Place the camera at a <b>side view or a 45-degree diagonal</b> rather than a front-facing angle, which can cause severe perspective distortion and make it hard to gauge knee travel. Set the angle so your entire body, from head to toe, is captured within a single frame without being cut off. This view is biomechanically essential for capturing things like 'Butt Wink' (lumbar rounding) or 'Knee Over-travel' due to lack of ankle mobility at a pixel level.

3. <b>Lighting & Contrast:</b> It is absolutely advantageous to film in a bright space where there is a clear color contrast between your body (the subject) and the room's background. In backlit rooms, dark lighting, or cluttered spaces with junk on the floor, the AI's visual tracking performance will inevitably drop. I strongly recommend using a clean, solid-colored wall or a tidy floor as a background to make the subject stand out.

If you fully understand these physical **Constraint Controls** and apply them to your workout routine, you will be able to enjoy overwhelming AI coaching for various exercises like `[Barbell Bench Press]`, `[Romanian Deadlift]`, or even complex full-body movements like `[Kettlebell Swings]` simply by changing the `[variable]` in the brackets.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: What if the recorded video is too long?**
  - A: I strongly recommend editing the video down to a **short 10–15 second clip** that clearly includes 1 or 2 repetitions of the core movement. If the video is too long, server analysis time may be delayed, and the AI's cognitive focus might blur, increasing the chance of strange results.

- **Q: Can it analyze other exercises like deadlifts or golf swings besides squats?**
  - A: Of course. Try changing the `[squat]` variable in the prompt to `[Romanian Deadlift]` or `[Golf Driver Swing]`. You can instantly receive professional feedback tailored to the mechanical mechanisms of that specific sport. It boasts chillingly good performance, especially in analyzing orbit deviations in golf swings.

- **Q: Can I use the video upload feature with the free version of ChatGPT?**
  - A: Yes, it is perfectly possible. Currently, the image and video upload features of the latest **GPT-4o model** are open to free ChatGPT users on a limited basis. Just click the file attachment icon at the bottom of the chat window to upload your video.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Rehabilitation Medicine Persona (Role):** I narrowed the persona's scope from a generic local gym trainer focused only on muscle mass to a _"Senior Trainer with 15 years of experience and knowledge in sports medicine and rehabilitation."_ This leads to a higher level of professional diagnosis based on joint mechanics rather than just counting reps.
2. **Cognitive Queuing:** Instead of anatomical instructions that beginners find hard to understand (like _"strongly contract your quadriceps"_), I forced the use of intuitive metaphors like _"imagine burying your hips deep into an invisible chair,"_ a technique favored by top-tier trainers. This allows you to apply it to your body the moment you read it.
3. **Safety Mechanism (Warning):** To fundamentally block the AI's characteristic Hallucination errors that can occur when a video is blurry or the angle is restricted, I added a strong constraint: _"If you are not sure, clearly state that it is unable to be verified."_ This pushes the reliability of the feedback to its limit.
