_"Write down just 3 things you are grateful for today. ...You say nothing comes to mind?"_

We are sensitive to what we lack or don't have, but insensitive to what we already have. A gratitude journal is a training to turn the brain's Attention from 'lack' to 'fulfillment'. It's hard at first, but it becomes much easier if AI finds topics and asks questions by your side.

>
> 1.  Discover **3 specific gratitude points** that can be found in my daily routine. (Re-illuminate things I took for granted.)
> 2.  To feel this gratitude more deeply, ask a hypothetical question **'What if this didn't exist?'** for each.
> 3.  Give one 'small gratitude mission' to practice tomorrow.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Remove too instructive or religious feelings.
> - Write in a pleasant and light tone.
