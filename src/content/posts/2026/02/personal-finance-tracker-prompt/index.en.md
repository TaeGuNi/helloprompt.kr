## 🚀 This Is How the Real Pros Write It

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in brackets to suit your situation.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly categorize this month's expenses and see a light summary of the total amounts.

> **Role:** You are a meticulous `[Accountant]` who is very sharp with numbers.
>
> **Task:**
> 
> Based on the `[Payment History]` data provided below, accurately classify the transactions into intuitive categories such as food, transportation, shopping, and fixed expenses, and calculate the total amount for each.
> 
> `[Payment History]` : (Copy and paste your credit card payment history text here)

### 🥇 Pro Version

This powerful prompt is for when you need complete financial consulting feedback—going beyond simple addition to provide a sharp analysis of consumption habits and a concrete budget plan for the next month.

> **Role:** You are a highly skilled 10-year senior `[Wealth Manager]`.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am a `[3rd-year office worker]` who receives a stable monthly salary but has no control over spending and saves almost nothing.
> - Goal: To receive an objective and cold analysis of my consumption patterns and create a practical and specific budget plan to increase my savings rate by `[20%]`.
>
> **Task:**
>
> Thoroughly analyze my `[Monthly Payment History]` provided below and perform the following tasks:
>
> 1. **Expense Classification & Statistics:** Group the payment history into intuitive categories such as food, housing/telecom, transport, shopping, and culture/leisure, and summarize them in a Markdown table for an at-a-glance view.
> 2. **Spending Pattern Fact-Attack:** Identify the Top 3 most critical 'leaks (waste)' in this month's spending and provide a painful analysis of the causes.
> 3. **Action Plan:** Provide specific and realistic budget reduction guidelines that I should implement immediately starting next month to achieve my target savings rate.
>
> `[Monthly Payment History]` : (Copy and paste your credit card payment history text here)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use a highly readable Markdown format for the output, and ensure statistics are organized into a Table.
> - Exclude vague comfort or emotional empathy; write in an objective and firm tone based strictly on data and numbers.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up or guess numbers. All total figures must match the original data with 100% accuracy.

## 💡 Author’s Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The true destructive power and overwhelming value of this prompt lie in the fact that it doesn't leave the AI as just a soulless 'fast calculator.' The core of this technique is transforming the AI's persona into <b>'your own 1:1 Senior Wealth Manager'</b> who doesn't hesitate to give painful advice without leaving room for excuses. The most innovative and attractive point here is that you <b>don't need</b> to follow the old-fashioned process of logging into credit card websites with certificates and downloading Excel (CSV) files. Simply <b>drag and copy your payment history as text</b> from the apps you check every day, like Toss, BankSalad, or your banking app, and <b>paste it directly into the prompt</b> to start the magic. Large Language Models (LLMs) parse fragmented information like dates, merchant names, and amounts, infer the context, and convert them into organized structured data in just a second.

<b>💡 Cheat Key for Extreme Constraint Control in Practice:</b>
You don't need to worry if the payment history text is a chaotic mess containing items like `[10/01 Starbucks 4,500 KRW]`, `[Delivery Hero 21,000 KRW approved]`, or even unnecessary ad text and notification formats. Modern reasoning-specialized models like <b>Claude 3.5 Sonnet</b> or <b>GPT-4o</b> have context-understanding capabilities beyond human imagination; they will filter out the noise and categorize items perfectly into 'Food,' 'Cafe/Snacks,' or 'Online Shopping.' However, to prevent the AI from splitting items into too many tiny categories, you can perfectly control the variables by adding a constraint like: <b>"Strictly limit categories to only five: Food, Transport, Housing, Shopping, and Culture/Leisure."</b>

Furthermore, if you want to adjust the intensity of the AI's 'fact-attack,' try strategically tweaking the `[Role]` variable or the instructions in the `[Task]`. ## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if the card payment history contains sensitive personal information like names or addresses?**
  - A: Before entering payment data into the prompt, it is strongly recommended to delete or mask (\* treat) sensitive identifying information such as real names, full account numbers, or detailed addresses. Secure data protection is the first step in asset management.
- **Q: Can I directly upload an Excel file (CSV) downloaded from a bank or credit card company?**
  - A: Absolutely! If you are using ChatGPT Plus (Paid version) data analysis features or Claude, upload the CSV file directly and command, "Run the prompt above based on the data in this file." It analyzes data with even more overwhelming speed and seamless accuracy than text copying.
- **Q: Sometimes the total amount or category totals seem slightly wrong.**
  - A: Due to the structural limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs), minor numerical errors (hallucinations) can occasionally occur in complex or massive arithmetic calculations. To prevent this, I have included strong constraints on calculation accuracy within the prompt. Use your financial app for cross-verifying exact total payment amounts, and use the AI as the core brain for <b>'spending pattern analysis and strategic advice.'</b>
