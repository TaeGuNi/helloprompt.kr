_"Can I contact a stranger out of the blue? What if they read and ignore it?"_

Don't worry. People don't dislike someone respecting them as a role model and asking for advice. They just dislike **'rude or annoying'** requests. Try sending a polite message that respects the other person's time while stimulating curiosity.

3.  Include a CTA (Call To Action) asking if a short (15 mins) call or chat is possible without burden.

### ğŸ¥‡ Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need detailed quality.

Copy the **PROMPT** below.

> **Role (Role):** You are a sociable and polite networker.
>
> **Context (Context):** I'm going to apply for a coffee chat (light advice request) via LinkedIn (or email) to a `[Counterpart Job/Company]` person I've always respected.
>
> **Counterpart Information:**
>
> - Recently saw `[Counterpart's Achievement/Activity: e.g. - OO Conference Presentation]` impressively.
> - Seems interested in `[Interest: e.g. - Junior Education]`.
>
> **My Purpose:**
>
> - I want to hear `[Question Content: e.g. - Advice on job change preparation]`.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1.  Write an intro that naturally forms rapport while praising the other person.
> 2.  Briefly introduce myself and show sincerity about why it has to be this person.
> 3.  Finish the proposal in a way that relieves the other person's burden (e.g., 15-minute Zoom meeting, text reply, etc.).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Be as polite as possible but not servile (Polite but Professional).
> - Prohibit vague words like "Let's eat sometime when you have time".
