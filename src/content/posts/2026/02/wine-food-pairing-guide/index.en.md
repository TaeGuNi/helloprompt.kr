# 🍷 What's for Dinner? Your AI Sommelier's Guide to Wine Pairing

- **🎯 Target Audience:** Anyone who spends 10 minutes staring blankly at the wine aisle, those whose wine vocabulary ends at "not too sweet red," and home party hosts.
- **⏱️ Time Saved:** 10 minutes → 1 minute
- **🤖 Recommended Models:** GPT-4o (Vision feature is a game-changer), Claude 3.5 Sonnet

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
- 🚀 **Utility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

> _"Lost in a sea of identical wine labels? Let your AI sommelier craft the perfect pairing for tonight's dinner."_

Pairing soju with pork belly or beer with fried chicken feels like second nature. But wine? That’s a different story. The sheer volume of unfamiliar terminology—varietals, terroirs, body, tannins, and acidity—can be paralyzing. We’ve all felt the sting of splurging on an expensive bottle, only to discover it clashes horribly with our food. Stop gambling your evening away on generic Vivino ratings. Here is exactly how to prompt your AI Sommelier to find the absolute perfect wine that harmonizes beautifully with both your palate and tonight's menu.

---

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Contextual Curation:** Pinpoint the perfect bottle tailored to any specific occasion—whether it's a quiet solo night, a romantic dinner, or a bustling home party.
2. **Maximize Accessibility:** Demand hyper-realistic, actionable buying instructions, such as "Suggest a wine I can grab right now at the 7-Eleven down the street."
3. **The Science of Pairing:** Move beyond generic recommendations with scientific breakdowns of _why_ a specific wine elevates your food, enriching your overall culinary experience.

---

## 🚀 Solution: "The AI Personal Sommelier Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this quick prompt when your dinner menu is locked in, and you need to grab a complementary bottle on the fly.

> **Role:** You are the head `[Sommelier]` at a 3-star Michelin restaurant.
> **Context:** Tonight's dinner menu is `[spicy tteokbokki and an assortment of fried snacks]`.
> **Task:** Recommend 3 wines that pair flawlessly with this meal.
> **Constraints:**
>
> 1. Restrict choices to affordable wines in the `[$15 to $35]` price range.
> 2. Select widely available, crowd-pleasing varietals that are approachable for wine beginners.
> 3. Explain exactly _why_ each wine pairs beautifully with the food in one concise, compelling sentence.

\

### 🥇 Pro Version

This expert-level prompt factors in the subtle nuances of your personal taste profile, your budget, and your shopping constraints to deliver a foolproof, "zero-failure" pairing.

> **Role:** You are my **'Personal Wine Curator'** who intimately understands my palate.
>
> **User Profile:**
>
> - **Preferences:** Full-bodied, moderate tannins, and rich dark fruit flavors.
> - **Dislikes:** High acidity or wines with noticeable residual sugar (sweetness).
> - **Budget:** `[Under $25]`
> - **Where to Buy:** `[Costco, Trader Joe's, or local convenience stores]`
>
> **Context:**
> I'm hosting a home party for 4 friends. The main course is `[Costco Bulgogi Pizza and a fresh salmon salad]`. The group is a mix of seasoned wine enthusiasts and complete beginners, so I need versatile, crowd-pleasing options that everyone will enjoy.
>
> **Task:**
> Suggest wine options categorized strictly into the following 3 sections:
>
> 1. **Safe Pick:** A universally appealing, guaranteed crowd-pleaser (provide the specific product name).
> 2. **Adventurous:** An unexpected but mind-blowing, unconventional pairing.
> 3. **Value:** A hidden gem that vastly over-delivers for its price point.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Output the recommendations as a Markdown bulleted list.
> - For each category, explicitly state the wine name, the estimated retail price range, and the core scientific/flavor reason for the recommendation (the pairing point).
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not recommend discontinued vintages, rare allocations, or limited editions that are difficult to source in standard retail stores.

## 💡 Writer's Insight

"Wine with spicy tteokbokki?" It’s a pairing most traditionalists wouldn't dare to suggest. However, when you consult an AI Sommelier, you'll get surprisingly brilliant, data-backed answers. It might point you toward a crisp **'Sparkling Wine (like Cava or Prosecco)'** or a slightly sweet **'Moscato d'Asti'**, explaining how the effervescence and residual sugar act as a perfect foil to neutralize fiery capsaicin.

AI models are trained on vast global flavor profiles, tasting notes, and pairing principles. They can scientifically calculate the intricate harmonies of 'sweet and salty' or 'spicy and sweet' that humans often struggle to intuit. My absolute best tip for maximizing this prompt is leveraging the **Accessibility Constraint**. Try instructing the AI: "Pick exclusively from the February promotional lineup at my local convenience store." Even without real-time API inventory data, the AI will accurately single out a foolproof, widely distributed staple from their famous, always-stocked roster. Say goodbye to standing awkwardly in the aisle, scanning dozens of labels on your phone.

## 🙋 FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

- **Q: Can I just take a picture of a wine label at the store and ask the AI about it?**
  - A: Absolutely! This is where you should maximize the Vision capabilities of GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet. Simply snap a photo of the label and ask: "Does this wine align with my taste profile (full-bodied, dry)? Will it hold up against the rich tuna sashimi I'm having tonight?" The AI will instantly decode the label, providing detailed tasting notes and a tailored pairing analysis.

- **Q: Does this framework work for whiskey, sake, or craft beer instead of wine?**
  - A: Yes, it translates flawlessly. All you need to do is modify the 'Role' in your prompt. Change it to 'Master Whiskey Bartender', 'Kikizakeshi (Certified Sake Sommelier)', or 'Craft Beer Cicerone'. You will instantly receive highly specialized, expert-level recommendations tailored to that specific category of alcohol.

---

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1.  **Context & Accessibility Constraints:** The highest-rated wine in the world is entirely useless to you if you can't buy it before dinner. By strictly defining the 'Budget' and 'Where to Buy', we establish realistic, actionable boundaries for the AI's recommendations.
2.  **Multidimensional Taste Profiling:** We move far beyond a rudimentary 'Red vs. White' dichotomy. Explicitly defining both 'Preferences' and 'Dislikes' drastically narrows the search space, practically guaranteeing a much higher satisfaction rate.
3.  **Tripartite Choice Architecture:** By presenting three distinct stylistic options—Safe, Adventurous, and Value—we empower the user with the autonomy to make the final call based on the specific vibe and mood of the evening.
