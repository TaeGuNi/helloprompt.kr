> *"What if your face, or the faces of your users, were stolen for non-consensual pornography? It's time to build a robust 'system' to defend against the most horrifying ethical hellscape created by technology."*

The rapid advancement of generative AI has given us incredible tools for creation, but simultaneously, it has become a weapon that tramples on human dignity. The proliferation of non-consensual deepfake pornography, in particular, is not merely a technological side effect; it is an 'ethical hellscape' and a severe violation of personal rights.

In this 'technological gap' where laws and regulations struggle to keep pace with the speed of innovation, every organization and community must now equip themselves with guidelines to protect their own. This issue goes far beyond simply identifying fake videos—it is fundamentally a matter of universal human rights and **'Consent'**.

This post introduces a prompt that allows you to construct a comprehensive **'Anti-Deepfake and Ethics Guideline'** in just 3 minutes. This will help you proactively block the malicious use of deepfakes within your organization and respond immediately should an incident occur.

## 🚀 The Solution: "Deepfake Ethics & Defense Policy Generator"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need to quickly draft the core principles of an ethical policy or a community announcement.

> **Role:** You are an AI Ethics Expert at an IT company.
> **Task:** Draft the "5 Core Principles Prohibiting the Creation and Distribution of Non-Consensual Deepfakes" to protect the users of our service.

<br>

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a comprehensive, practical guideline that encompasses your organization's actual response manual and technical/legal defense mechanisms.

> **Role:** You are the AI Ethics Officer and Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: We are currently in a 'technological gap' where the malicious use of generative AI for non-consensual deepfake pornography and fake news is rapidly increasing.
> - Goal: To establish robust ethical guidelines and a crisis response manual that completely blocks the malicious use of deepfakes within `[Organization/Service Name]` and enables an immediate response if an incident occurs.
>
> **Task:**
>
> Write a comprehensive guideline that must include the following:
>
> 1. Core Principles: State the principles of 'Consent'-based content generation and the protection of human dignity.
> 2. Technical Response: Propose technical control measures such as watermarking, metadata insertion, and the adoption of detection algorithms.
> 3. Sanctions & Legal Action: Establish a zero-tolerance policy for violations (creation and distribution) and outline the disciplinary/reporting procedures.
> 4. Victim Relief Protocol: Create a 3-step crisis response scenario ranging from report reception to content blinding and connection to legal/psychological support.
> 5. Keep the `[Organization/Service Name]` placeholder in brackets `[ ]` so the user can fill in their own company name.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Format the output using Markdown headings (`###`) and bullet points (`-`) to ensure a highly readable manual structure.
> - Strictly avoid an academic or theoretical tone. Maintain a firm and clear Corporate Policy Tone that the operations team can immediately declare as an internal regulation.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Align the guidelines with universal human rights and global ethical standards that do not conflict with actual current laws in specific countries. Do not make definitive statements about specific national laws. (Prevent hallucination)

## 💡 Writer's Insight

The key to defending against deepfake crimes lies not in the AI technology itself, but in the **'rapid processes of the organization'**. In a technological vacuum, the presence or absence of clear guidelines determines the scale of the damage.

This prompt translates vague AI ethics into concrete 'actionable guidelines'. In particular, if you proactively systematize the **'Victim Relief Protocol (Report-Delete-Support)'**, your platform can drastically reduce risks and build immense trust with your users. Even if you don't currently have a perfect detection system, simply declaring a zero-tolerance policy serves as a powerful deterrent.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Do guidelines written by AI have legal validity?**
  - A: They can serve effectively as internal regulations and terms of service. In the event of a dispute, they provide strong evidence that the organization 'fulfilled its duty of care'. However, we strongly recommend having your internal legal team review them before final publication.

- **Q: Can't we just use AI to detect the deepfake videos themselves?**
  - A: Relying solely on Text-Based Large Language Model (LLM) prompts has limitations for this. For actual detection, it is far more accurate to adopt dedicated detection AI specialized in analyzing video frequencies and pixels, such as Intel's FakeCatcher or DeepBrain AI's detection solutions.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Assigning Complementary Personas:** By combining the roles of 'Ethics Officer (Value Guardian)' and 'CISO (System Defender)', the prompt ensures the output goes beyond moral declarations to include practical and technical defense mechanisms.
2. **Enforcing Zero-Tolerance & Relief Principles (Constraints):** By explicitly requiring a 3-step protocol for victim relief in the Task, the spectrum of ethical responsibility is extended to include post-incident management, rather than just sanctions.
3. **Controlling Tone & Manner:** The constraints to 'avoid academic tone' and 'maintain a firm policy tone' ensure the output is ready to be copied and pasted directly onto internal notice boards.
