## üöÄ The Solution: "The Essayist's Lens"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you just need a quick, polished result.

> **Role:** You are an expert essayist.
> **Request:** Turn this brief diary entry into a short, relatable essay: `[Insert your diary text here]`

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need nuanced, literary quality with genuine depth.

> **Role:** You are an observant and philosophical essayist, writing in the evocative, contemplative style of Haruki Murakami or Joan Didion.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I have a short, rough diary entry that I want to develop into a polished essay.
> - Goal: To create a relatable piece of writing that others can read and find emotional resonance in.
>
> **My Diary (Draft):**
> `[e.g., Took the subway today. It was incredibly crowded and suffocating. It made me realize how hard everyone is working just to survive.]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Based on the draft above, write **one literary essay (exactly 3 paragraphs)**.
> 2. **Observation:** Go beyond a simple description of the situation. Awaken the five senses and include vivid, granular details.
> 3. **Insight:** Connect this everyday experience to a broader philosophical contemplation on life, society, or human connection.
> 4. **Title:** Suggest 3 emotional, captivating titles that invite readers to click.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Do not be overly preachy or attempt to teach the reader a heavy-handed lesson. Keep the tone grounded and empathetic.
> - Write in a warm, conversational, yet refined tone.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not invent false memories or exaggerate the core facts of the draft. Keep the essence authentic.

## üôã FAQ

- **Q: Will the AI make up things that didn't actually happen to me?**
  - A: It might embellish sensory details to set the scene (like the sound of rain or the smell of coffee), but the core events remain yours. If it adds something too fictional, simply tweak your `[Draft]` to be more specific or remind the AI in the constraints not to invent events.

- **Q: Can I use this for professional LinkedIn articles?**
  - A: Absolutely! While designed for personal essays, changing the "Role" to a "thought-leadership columnist" and altering the tone constraints to be more professional can yield excellent reflections on your workday or career journey.
