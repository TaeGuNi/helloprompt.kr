> _"Your experience is stellar, but does your writing style scream 'rookie'?"_

The lifeblood of a resume is **'Professionalism'**. Colloquial phrases, excessive adverbs, and vague expressions chip away at your credibility. By entrusting your draft to an AI editor, you can instantly upgrade your writing into a crisp, authoritative business tone that commands respect.

## üöÄ The Solution: "Professional Tone Polisher"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, reliable grammar and tone check.

> **Role:** You are a senior editor at a top-tier publishing house and an expert in business document refinement.
> **Task:** Refine the following resume draft to fit strict business standards. Catch all typos and grammatical errors, and elevate the tone to increase professional credibility. [Insert your text here]

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this for a comprehensive, structural overhaul of your professional narrative.

> **Role (Role):** You are a senior corporate recruiter and an expert business copyeditor.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I have written a draft for my resume and cover letter, but the sentences feel clunky, colloquial, and may contain typos.
> - Goal: I need my writing perfectly polished to sound like a seasoned professional.
>
> **Task (Task):**
> Please process the following text based on the guidelines below:
>
> [Paste text to correct here]
>
> 1. **Typo/Spelling Correction:** Identify and fix all spacing, punctuation, and spelling errors.
> 2. **Tone & Manner Adjustment:**
>    - Standardize colloquial or conversational phrasing into a formal, professional literary style.
>    - Ruthlessly eliminate unnecessary adverbs and filler words (e.g., 'really', 'too', 'extremely', 'very').
>    - Convert passive voice into strong, action-oriented active voice.
> 3. **Grammatical Refinement:** Restructure sentences for clarity, ensuring perfect subject-predicate agreement.
>
> **Output Format (Format):**
>
> - Present a side-by-side or Before/After comparison for each major change.
> - Include a brief editor's note explaining _why_ the change was made to improve the business impact.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Do not alter the core facts, metrics, or the fundamental meaning of my experiences.
> - If any part of my original text is too vague to rewrite effectively, ask me for clarification rather than making assumptions.

## üí° Writer's Insight

As a hiring manager, I often see candidates with fantastic technical skills get rejected because their resumes read like casual text messages. This prompt acts as your ruthless, objective editor. It's particularly effective because it doesn't just fix typos; it fundamentally shifts the _vantage point_ of the writing from a passive observer ("I was tasked with...") to an active driver ("Spearheaded..."). When I used this to help a junior developer friend, removing words like "really hard" and replacing them with quantifiable action verbs entirely changed how recruiters responded to her profile. Always review the AI's output to ensure it hasn't accidentally changed your actual metrics!

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Will this make my resume sound like a robot wrote it?**
  - A: Not if you use the Pro Version. By explicitly instructing the AI to act as a "senior corporate recruiter" and retaining your core facts, it polishes your voice rather than replacing it. Always add your personal touch after the AI generates the draft.

- **Q: Which AI model is best for this specific prompt?**
  - A: Claude 3.5 Sonnet is currently the gold standard for nuanced, professional copywriting and maintaining a natural tone. ChatGPT (GPT-4o) is also excellent, but occasionally leans towards overly flowery vocabulary, so be sure to review it.

- **Q: Can I use this for cover letters as well?**
  - A: Absolutely. The "Tone & Manner Adjustment" rules are perfectly suited for cover letters, cold emails to recruiters, or even updating your LinkedIn 'About' section.

---

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Dual Persona (Role):** Assigning both "recruiter" and "copyeditor" roles ensures the output is not only grammatically perfect but also optimized for what hiring managers actually want to read.
2. **Actionable Constraints (Tone & Manner):** Instead of just saying "make it professional," we give specific mechanics: removing adverbs, forcing active voice, and standardizing formality. This prevents the AI from just swapping words with thesaurus synonyms.
3. **Rationale Requirement (Output Format):** Asking the AI to explain _why_ it made changes helps you learn business writing principles, meaning you'll naturally write better drafts in the future.
