## 📝 The 'LinkedIn Writing' Prompt to Build Your Own Brand

- **🎯 Recommended for:** Junior professionals, developers, marketers, job seekers
- **⏱️ Time Saved:** Reduced from 30 minutes → 3 minutes
- **🤖 Top Performance:** Claude 3.5 Sonnet (excellent for writing), ChatGPT (GPT-4o)

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 🚀 **Usability:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

> _"Everyone else seems to be doing so well... can my ordinary day really become a post on LinkedIn?"_

Do you sigh every time you see those flashy LinkedIn feeds? You might wonder, "I spent all day wrestling with error messages and just organizing meeting minutes—can these trivial daily tasks really become content?" Reading the **overwhelming insights** and **success stories** poured out by famous startup CEOs or high-earning senior developers can make your own everyday work experience feel incredibly humble. Self-censorship kicks in: "Can I really show my face among these amazing people?" You've probably had the experience of clicking the 'Start a post' button with high hopes, only to hit backspace after a few words and close the window. It's the moment you fall back into being a mere consumer, just reading others' posts.

However, if you remain a **spectator** who only hits 'Like' on other people's posts, the value of your career will remain buried forever. In today's job market and business environment, if you don't actively promote yourself, no one will recognize your skills and potential. We are in an era where **your own records of fierce contemplation and growth**, consistently documented every week, move the hearts of headhunters and HR managers much more powerfully than a single line of credentials on a flashy resume. LinkedIn is no longer just a place for successful people to brag. Rather, it is your most powerful **personal branding weapon** and career portfolio that can vividly prove your practical capabilities, attitude, and problem-solving process.

So, how can you break through this daunting wall of writing and create your own attractive content? The answer is surprisingly simple. Never try to invent a perfect, flawless success story. Instead, the **'small failures and painful realizations'** you gain from hitting walls and falling down in the field every day form a much deeper and more authentic bond with countless professional readers living with similar concerns. Start by casually tossing your raw emotions from a minor episode or a silly mistake you had today to the AI. Your smart AI partner will work its magic, transforming your rough, diary-like notes into the **professional business language** that the LinkedIn algorithm loves most.

The **'LinkedIn Writing (Post)' prompt** I am sharing with you today is the most powerful and practical tool to solve these writing worries at once. It doesn't matter if you have no knowledge of complex marketing theories or lack fancy writing skills. All you have to do is fill in the prompt's variables with what happened today and the frustrations or small realizations you felt. The AI will automatically apply the 'Hook - Story - Insight - CTA' structure, which is thoroughly verified by data, to stop busy readers from scrolling and turn your post into an **engaging insight post** that invites explosive comments. Stop being afraid of the blank screen. Your ordinary but hard-fought day will become your strongest branding asset, creating entirely new career opportunities for tomorrow!

## 🚀 How Real Professionals Write

This prompt was completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in parentheses to suit your situation.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you're commuting or want to quickly pull out a draft containing only the essentials.

> **Role:** You are a B2B marketing expert and a LinkedIn Top Voice influencer.
>
> **Request:** Something happened at work today: `[What happened or mistake made today]`. From this, I realized `[What I felt/learned]`. Based on this content, write a LinkedIn post that draws empathy from people.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This is a powerful weapon to pull out when you need overwhelming professional quality and an authentic tone.

> **Role:**
> You are a LinkedIn Content Director and Personal Branding Expert with 100,000 followers. You have an excellent ability to extract sharp insights from the experiences of ordinary professionals.
>
> **Context:**
> I am a junior `[Job title, e.g., Frontend Developer]`. I want to post an honest yet professional story on LinkedIn based on an episode I experienced at work today.
>
> **Material:**
> - **Event/Situation:** `[e.g., Got lectured by a senior developer today for sloppy variable names during code review]`
> - **Emotions/Thoughts:** `[e.g., Felt embarrassed at first, but realized that code others can read is the mark of true skill]`
> - **Key Message:** `[e.g., Importance of fundamentals, coding for collaboration]`
>
> **Task:**
> Based on the provided material, write a LinkedIn post according to the following steps (Chain-of-Thought):
> 1. **Stop the Scroll (Hook):** Start with a single sentence that stimulates the reader's curiosity or subverts common knowledge.
> 2. **Explain the Situation (Story):** Describe the situation I experienced plainly without exaggeration. (Honestly admit failures or mistakes)
> 3. **Insight:** Clearly point out the practical lessons or growth points gained through this experience.
> 4. **Call to Action (CTA):** End by asking for the readers' experiences or opinions to encourage comment participation.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Do not sound too "preachy" or overly self-promoting.
> - Never use cliché expressions like "These days," "In conclusion," or "Hello."
> - Limit one paragraph to a maximum of 2-3 lines for mobile readability, and be sure to put empty lines between paragraphs.
> - Add only 3-5 core hashtags directly related to the content at the bottom.
>
> **Warning:**
> - Do not make up unconfirmed information; answer "I don't know" if unsure. (Hallucination prevention)

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

The content that LinkedIn algorithms and readers are most enthusiastic about and 'like' is never 'the obvious bragging of a flawless person.' People are already feeling extreme fatigue from the countless success stories overflowing on social media, and they often close their hearts to stories that are just too perfect. The real weapon that captures busy readers' attention and draws deep empathy is **'the fierce struggle of an ordinary professional like me.'** The key secret to utilizing this powerful prompt to its full potential is to write the embarrassing mistakes or raw concerns you experienced in the `[Event/Situation]` variable section very specifically and honestly. Coolly admitting your shortcomings and showing the persistence to find new learning within that painful experience is the essence of the most human and attractive branding story that invites readers' support.

Let me give you an important tip for choosing an AI model as well. For writing where delicate human emotions and narrative flow are important, I strongly recommend the **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** model. While the existing ChatGPT shows strength in logical and stiff information-delivery writing, Claude shows overwhelming performance in naturally capturing the speaker's subtle emotional lines, meaning between the lines, and non-forced human nuances. AI will be a reliable and excellent 1:1 writing partner in delicately packaging your raw emotions and rough experiences into sophisticated and professional business language.

However, there is a critical warning you must keep in mind. No matter how great the writing the AI provides, **you must never copy and publish the generated result 100% as-is**. Even with a top-performing prompt, the AI's unique subtly exaggerated tone or overly smooth flow that gives off a mechanical feeling will inevitably be present. Therefore, while you borrow the strong power of the AI for the overall skeleton and logical structure, you must **go through the process of replacing words or tones with those you normally use when talking to colleagues** in the final polishing process. For example, if the AI wrote somewhat over-the-toply as "I gained a truly innovative realization," slightly tweaking it to something a real professional would say, like "It felt like I got hit over the head," will drastically increase the quality and authenticity of the writing.

Another point easily overlooked when handling prompts is the control over the `[Key Message]` variable. Depending on how you set this variable, the direction and conclusion of the entire post can change completely. If you greedily cram in various messages like "Collaboration is important, coding skills are important, and you should also do documentation well," there is a very high probability that the focus of the writing will be blurred and the content will go off track. Therefore, you need to train yourself to narrow it down to **just one clear and sharp keyword** per post, such as "Importance of basics," "Necessity of documentation," or "Communication with colleagues." No matter how long and good a post is, it can remain in the reader's mind for a long time and intensely only if it contains a single powerful key message.

Finally, I want to urge you to let go of perfectionism. Don't hesitate for too long, worrying for days to write a perfect post. A perfect first post that hits a home run from the start doesn't exist for anyone. Even if the context feels a bit awkward or the depth of the content seems lacking, the **courage to consistently leave your records and boldly press the publish button** is the true key to completing personal branding. Based on the draft created in 3 minutes with this prompt today, add a spoonful of your own unique voice and thoughts and post it right now. I am certain that only such small but consistent execution and accumulation is the only and surest shortcut to turning you, an ordinary professional, into a LinkedIn Top Voice.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Is it safe to enter company secrets or sensitive information into the AI prompt?**
  - **A:** **Absolutely not!** Specific sales figures, real names of clients, and undisclosed in-house core technologies can lead to critical security leaks. You must safely input information by using pseudonyms or generalizing the situation (e.g., 'Company A' ➡️ 'A large corporate client').

- **Q: The output from the AI feels too mechanical. Is there a solution?**
  - **A:** This is due to the AI's unique 'exaggerated' tone (e.g., "innovative," "it was a truly amazing experience"). Boldly remove unnecessary exclamations or overly descriptive adjectives from the result, and refine the sentences to be as short and dry as possible. You'll find it becomes surprisingly plain and starts to smell like a 'real person.'

- **Q: Is it advantageous for the algorithm to attach photos or images rather than just posting text?**
  - **A:** **Yes, overwhelmingly!** Even just adding a single photo containing traces of your daily life—such as whiteboard scribbles from a fierce meeting, a cup of coffee you drank while working late, or a densely organized Notion screen—will cause the reader's dwell time and the post's reach to skyrocket.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Maximize Persona (Role):** Instead of simply commanding the AI to 'write a post,' giving it a specific master persona like a 'Director with 100,000 followers' pushed the immersion and business professionalism of the result to the limit.
2. **Structural Approach (Chain-of-Thought):** By presenting the proven LinkedIn viral winning formula of 'Hook ➡️ Story ➡️ Insight ➡️ CTA,' the AI was strongly controlled to think and write systematically step-by-step.
3. **Readability Control (Constraints):** To block the AI's basic habit of spitting out dense blocks of text suited for desktop environments, strict constraints like "max 2-3 lines per paragraph" and "must add empty lines" were set to achieve mobile optimization.
