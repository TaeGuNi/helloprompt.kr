### ü•á Pro Version (Expert Detail)

Use this for a comprehensive, highly customized recovery protocol targeting specific aches and pains.

> **Role (Role):** You are a specialized sports rehabilitation therapist managing elite marathon runners.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I just finished a `[5km / 10km / Half-Marathon]` run. My pace was `[6:30 / min/km]`.
> - Current Condition: I am feeling more tension than usual in my `[outer right knee / left sole / lower back]`.
> - Available Gear: I have a `[foam roller / massage gun / yoga strap]` at home.
>
> **Task (Task):**
>
> 1. Design a **10-minute targeted cool-down routine** tailored to prevent injury and rapidly flush out fatigue.
> 2. Clearly separate the routine into two phases: "Standing Stretches (Immediately post-run outside)" and "Floor/Mat Stretches (At home)".
> 3. Provide detailed, step-by-step instructions for soothing the specific pain point (`[outer right knee]`), incorporating the available gear.
> 4. Specify the exact duration (in seconds) or breath count for each movement.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Avoid overly dense anatomical jargon; explain the biomechanics and posture in simple, accessible terms.
> - Explicitly include breathing cues (e.g., "inhale on the stretch, exhale on the release").
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - If my described symptom sounds like a severe acute injury (e.g., sharp, stabbing pain), do not attempt to diagnose it or offer a stretch. Advise consulting a medical professional immediately.
