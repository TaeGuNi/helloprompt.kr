_"Do you have any questions?"_

This isn't just a question, it's an **opportunity**. If you ask about salary or benefits here (while important), you drift further away from "hired". Throw a decisive punch that leaves the impression on the interviewer's brain: "This person is serious about our company."

### ğŸ¥‡ Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a strategic career coach.
>
> **Context:** I am going for an interview for the `[Job Title]` position at `[Company Name]`. There will be a **Reverse Interview** time at the end, and I need a list of questions to score points.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Pick 2 questions for each of the following categories.
>    - **Job Related:** In-depth questions about the work I will be doing after joining.
>    - **Company Vision/Culture:** Questions about the company's growth direction or team culture.
>    - **Interviewer Personal:** Questions asking about the interviewer's experience or thoughts (use when the atmosphere is good).
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Exclude questions like "Is there a lot of overtime?" or "How much is the salary?".
> - The intent of the questions should reflect 'I have passion'.
> - Add a buildup comment before the question like "According to my research...".
