> _"I saw news that ChatGPT is replacing Google Search... Is it okay to continue holding Google stock?"_

Google (Alphabet) has been the **absolute ruler** of the internet era. A 90% search market share, the video monopoly platform YouTube, and the rapidly growing Google Cloud. However, since the emergence of ChatGPT in 2024, there has been constant anxiety about the future of Google's core business, **Search Advertising**. "If AI replaces search, won't Google's ad revenue decrease?"

This anxiety is well-founded. Previously, when you searched for "best laptop recommendations" on Google, you scrolled through 10 search results containing ads. But if you ask the same question to an AI, it **gives you the answer immediately**. There is no room for ads in between. <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">The paradigm of search behavior itself is changing</span>, and can Google respond successfully to this shift?

However, the opposing logic is also strong. Google is actively integrating AI search with its **Gemini** models, and Google remains the platform where advertisers spend the most money. Considering the growth of YouTube Shorts, subscription services (Music, Premium), and AI workloads in Google Cloud, it might be possible to "compensate for a decline in search ads with growth elsewhere." Use this prompt to **quantitatively verify** both sides of the argument.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The core of this prompt is **"Quantifying the Net Effect of the AI Search Transition."** Most articles stop at **qualitative claims** like "AI threatens search" or "Google will do AI well." However, what is needed for investment decisions is **quantitative analysis** such as "ad impressions per query drop by -8%, but ad unit prices rise by +15%, resulting in a net effect of +6%."

If you ask the AI with this structure, you can see under what conditions it is good or bad for Google, rather than a simple "good/bad" binary. For example, <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">if AI Overviews increases search satisfaction and the number of queries itself increases</span>, it is a net positive; if users migrate to AI chatbots and the number of queries decreases, it is a net negative. With this framework, you can immediately determine whether the reason search revenue grew during each quarterly earnings release was query growth or price increases.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Why is Google's Forward P/E the lowest among Big Tech?**
  - A: Because the market is pricing in the "AI threat to search ads." You can judge whether this is an **excessive discount (AI Discount)** or a reasonable reflection of risk using this prompt. If the AI discount is excessive, the current price becomes an attractive entry point.

- **Q: How much will the DOJ antitrust lawsuit result affect the stock price?**
  - A: Ask the AI for the stock price impact in worst-case scenarios (forced sale of Chrome, ban on default search engine contracts). You can add "Analysis of DOJ lawsuit impacts by scenario" to the `[Core Question]` in the prompt.
