## üöÄ The Solution: "Click-Magnet Title"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need quick, catchy ideas without complex setup.

> **Role:** You are an elite `[Copywriter]` and SEO Expert.
> **Task:** Generate 10 highly clickable blog post titles for the topic `[Insert Topic]`. Make them engaging and optimized for search.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this for meticulously crafted titles tailored to your specific audience and content strategy.

> **Role:** You are an elite `[Copywriting Expert]` and a `[Power Blogger]` with over 1 million monthly visitors. You deeply understand consumer psychology and search intent.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I have drafted a blog post about `[Topic/Keyword]`.
> - Goal: I need titles that dramatically increase the Click-Through Rate (CTR) while remaining authentic to the content.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Analyze the core content: `[Brief summary of your article]`
> 2. Focus on the target reader: `[Who exactly is this for?]`
> 3. Generate 2 distinct titles for each of the following 5 psychological frameworks (10 titles total):
>    - Type A: Curiosity Gap (Provocative questions or unknown secrets)
>    - Type B: Specific Methodology (Numbered lists, step-by-step guides)
>    - Type C: Loss Aversion (Mistakes to avoid, warnings)
>    - Type D: Authority/Insider Secrets (What the experts aren't telling you)
>    - Type E: Quick Wins (Fast, easy solutions for immediate gratification)
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Keep titles under 60 characters to prevent truncation on mobile devices and search engines.
> - Front-load the primary keyword `[Keyword]` for better SEO visibility.
> - Ensure the tone is compelling but absolutely avoid deceptive clickbait. Output the results in a clear Markdown list.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not generate generic titles. They must be highly specific to the provided content summary.

## üí° Writer's Insight

This prompt isn't just about generating random catchy phrases; it systematically applies copywriting frameworks that have been A/B tested for decades. By forcing the AI to categorize the titles (Curiosity, Loss Aversion, etc.), you instantly get a diverse menu of angles for the exact same piece of content.

**Pro Tip:** I often run this prompt _before_ I even write the article. Choosing a strong "Type C" (Loss Aversion) title beforehand helps me structure the entire body of the text to deliver on that specific promise, making the writing process much smoother and more focused.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Will this make my blog look like cheap clickbait?**
  - A: Not if you use the Pro Version. The prompt explicitly instructs the AI to avoid deceptive clickbait and align the title with your actual content summary. It focuses on _framing_ the value, not faking it.

- **Q: Do I need the paid version of ChatGPT for this?**
  - A: The Basic Version works perfectly on free models. However, the Pro Version benefits greatly from the nuanced understanding of advanced models like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet, especially when applying specific psychological frameworks.

- **Q: Can I use this for YouTube video titles as well?**
  - A: Absolutely! The psychological triggers for clicking a blog post and a YouTube video are nearly identical. Just change the `[Topic]` to your video concept.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning the persona of a "Power Blogger" and "Copywriting Expert," the AI adopts a results-oriented, professional tone rather than generic AI speak.
2. **Psychological Frameworks:** Asking for specific types of titles (Curiosity, Loss Aversion) forces the AI to move beyond basic SEO phrasing and tap into deeply ingrained human behavioral triggers.
3. **Character Constraints:** The 60-character limit ensures the generated titles are immediately usable for actual SERPs (Search Engine Results Pages) without getting cut off midway.
