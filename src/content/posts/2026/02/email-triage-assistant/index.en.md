## 🚀 This is How Real Pros Write

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variable]` sections to deploy it immediately in your workflow.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly summarize the core points and set a simple reply tone.

> **Role:** You are my highly capable assistant.
>
> **Request:** Summarize the following email in 3 lines and draft a polite rejection reply stating, "I appreciate the proposal, but we cannot proceed at this time due to current budget constraints."
>
> **Content:** `[Paste email body here]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this for complex contexts that require precise understanding or high-level professional replies including specific Action Items.

> **Role:** You are a veteran business communication expert with 10 years of experience and my Chief of Staff.
>
> **Context:**
> - I am a Product Manager (PM) at an IT company.
> - The text below is an important business email that arrived this morning.
> - I need to politely decline the sender's proposal or reschedule.
>
> **Task:**
> 1. **Identify Core Points:** Extract the key 'Action Item' the sender is requesting from me in 1-2 lines.
> 2. **Draft Reply:** Draft a reply according to the following conditions:
>    - Accept the sender's meeting request (rescheduling).
>    - However, propose a new time of `[Next Monday at 2:00 PM]` instead of the original time.
>    - Explicitly state that pre-read materials for review will be sent by `[This Friday]`.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - To ensure mobile readability, never use Tables; organize using high-readability bulleted Lists.
> - Maintain a perfect business email format (Clear subject line, polite greeting, body text, clean closing).
> - Remove unnecessary fluff and maintain a concise, professional Tone & Manner focused on the business at hand.
> - Mark parts I need to edit myself with `[ ]` brackets.
> - Important keywords must be **bolded**.
>
> **Warning:**
> - Never invent unverified schedules or promises. (Hallucination prevention)
>
> **Input Data:**
> - Email Content: `[Paste full email body here]`

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: I'm hesitant to copy and paste work emails into AI because of company security policies.**
  - A: That is a very important and sharp point! Sensitive personal information (names, contact info, confidential project names, specific figures, etc.) must be pseudonymized or **masked** (e.g., `[Project A]`, `[Client]`) before being entered into the prompt. For the safest approach, I strongly recommend adopting enterprise AI models (e.g., ChatGPT Enterprise, Claude for Work) where data training is fundamentally blocked.

- **Q: Can I have it summarize an email in English into Korean, and then have it reply in English?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just add one line to the **Task** section of the prompt: "Summarize the email content in Korean, and write the reply draft in natural business English." You can instantly get excellent English emails that look like they were written by a native speaker, even in a global business environment.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **The Magic of Role Assignment:** By assigning a powerful persona like "10-year veteran communication expert," we guide the AI to use sophisticated and formal business terminology rather than awkward, entry-level sentences.
2. **Structured Tasks:** We didn't just give a vague instruction like "write a reply." By clearly separating Action Item extraction and draft writing, we structured it so the AI designs a logical response after perfectly understanding the core of the email.
3. **Sequence of Constraints:** We added constraints to exclude unnecessary fluff and maintain perfect business formatting. This ensures practical results that can be copied and pasted immediately into the workplace.
