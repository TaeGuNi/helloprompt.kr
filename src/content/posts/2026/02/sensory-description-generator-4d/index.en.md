### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need nuanced, high-quality prose with specific tonal shifts.

> **Role:** You are an elite literary writer and a master of atmospheric world-building.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: The sentences I write are too dry and rely too much on "telling" rather than "showing."
> - Goal: I need to rewrite them so readers feel completely immersed in the scene, experiencing it firsthand.

> **Input Sentence:** `[e.g., I entered an old abandoned house. It was very dusty and smelled bad.]`

> **Task:**
>
> 1. Rewrite the input sentence into 3 distinct versions, meticulously utilizing the **Five Senses (Visual, Auditory, Olfactory, Tactile, Gustatory)**.

>    - **Version A (Horror/Thriller):** Emphasize tension, discomfort, and creeping dread.
>    - **Version B (Emotional/Nostalgic):** Highlight the passage of time, lingering loneliness, and faded memories.
>    - **Version C (Mystery/Fantasy):** Focus on the bizarre, the arcane, and an ethereal, magical aura.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Absolutely NO abstract emotional words (e.g., "scary," "sad," "creepy"). Instead, _induce_ these emotions purely through sensory details.
> - Utilize sophisticated metaphors and similes to elevate the literary quality without being overly verbose.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Banning Abstract Emotions:** By explicitly forbidding words like "scary," the AI is forced to find physical, sensory equivalents (e.g., "a sudden drop in temperature," "the scent of copper and old rot").
2. **Multi-Atmosphere Generation:** Asking for 3 distinct emotional tones from a single input gives you immediate narrative options and highlights how sensory details shift based on context.
3. **Targeted Sensory Engagement:** Explicitly listing the five senses ensures the AI doesn't just focus on visuals, bypassing a common bias in AI text generation.
