## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was finalized after dozens of trials. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variables]` to put it into action immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you're hungry and just want one quick, intuitive recipe.

> **Role:** You are a `[Creative Food Researcher]` with 3 Michelin stars.
>
> **Task:**
> Provide one delicious and easy recipe using the `[List of leftover ingredients]` currently in my fridge.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert Level)

Use this when you want to escape repetitive fried rice or stews, or when you need a chef's tips for substituting missing seasonings.

> **Role:** You are a `[Creative Food Researcher]` who helps students and beginners save money while perfectly satisfying their palates.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: Payday is far away, and my fridge is full of awkward scraps. I want to avoid the usual boring meals (e.g., Kimchi fried rice, Ramen).
> - Goal: To fully utilize leftovers and create a meal impressive enough to post on social media.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Recommend **2 of the most unique and delicious dishes** that can be made by combining my main ingredients and seasonings.
> 2. Explain the cooking process in detail from step 1 so a total beginner cannot fail.
> 3. If I am missing an essential seasoning or ingredient, you must include a **chef's tip for a substitute** using other ingredients I have.
>
> **Variables:**
>
> - Main Ingredients: `[3 pieces of leftover seasoned fried chicken, 1/2 withered onion, a small amount of sour kimchi, a bowl of cold rice]`
> - Available Seasonings: `[Soy sauce, white sugar, red pepper flakes, mayonnaise, sesame oil, ketchup]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Summarize the dish names, brief introductions, cooking time, and difficulty first using highly readable bullet points. (Do not use tables)
> - Use intuitive measurements like 'tablespoons' or 'cups' instead of complex units (g, ml).
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never recommend food combinations that pose a risk of food poisoning.
> - Strictly exclude recipes that require fancy spices I didn't list (e.g., truffle oil, oyster sauce). Do not hallucinate uncertain cooking methods.

## 💡 Author's Insight

The true value of this prompt lies in its <b>Ingredient Substitution</b> and <b>Intuitive Measurement</b> capabilities. Why do beginners get frustrated watching YouTube or blog recipes? It's the gap between the screen and reality. <b>"I don't have chicken stock," "What is basil powder?" or "How do I measure 15g without a scale?"</b> These realistic hurdles make cooking feel like an insurmountable wall.

To solve this, we set a deliberate trap in the **[Constraints]** and **[Available Seasonings]** sections of the prompt. We <b>strictly force the AI to solve the problem using only the seasonings I listed</b>, preventing it from randomly pulling recipes from the internet. Additionally, considering the kitchen environment of single households without scales, we provided everyday measurement units like <b>'cups' or 'tablespoons.'</b> By strictly controlling these variables, the AI issues a <b>practical, customized recipe book</b> synced perfectly to your kitchen, rather than just reciting unreachable Michelin-level instructions.

Specifically, asking for <b>flexible substitution tips</b> when an essential seasoning is missing is a masterstroke. It provides clear guidance, such as what ratio of soy sauce and sugar can mimic the umami of oyster sauce, or how to use leftover soju or flat soda if you don't have cooking wine. This goes beyond a mere list of information; it’s true chef mentoring that helps beginners understand the characteristics of ingredients and build their culinary adaptability.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: My ingredients are very close to their expiration date. Can I ask the AI if they are still safe to eat?**
  - A: Absolutely not! The AI cannot check the actual storage condition, decay, or smell of your ingredients. Crucial judgments regarding hygiene and health—like "My milk tastes slightly sour; can I still boil it?"—are entirely your responsibility. Safety and health must always come first.

- **Q: I'm currently on a diet. Can I get recommendations for low-calorie, high-protein meals only?**
  - A: Of course. Just add a line to the **Context** section like: "I'm currently on a strict Keto diet" or "Limit the recipes to high-protein diet meals under 400kcal per serving." The AI will instantly transform into a diet specialist chef who meticulously considers nutritional content.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Clear Definition of Situation and Goal:** By setting specific goals and constraints like "avoiding boring meals" and "fully utilizing leftovers," we maximize the AI's creativity. This leads to unique results like 'Chicken Karaage-don' instead of just basic fried rice.
2. **Precise Control of Constraints:** We proactively blocked the main pain points for beginners—difficult measurements and missing seasonings—at the prompt stage. This perfectly controls 'Cooking Hallucinations' where the AI might ask for unrealistic high-end ingredients.
3. **Modular Separation of Variables:** By splitting main ingredients and seasonings into separate variables, the user doesn't need to edit the entire complex prompt. They can simply swap out the text inside the brackets `[ ]` to match their fridge, allowing for infinite reuse.
