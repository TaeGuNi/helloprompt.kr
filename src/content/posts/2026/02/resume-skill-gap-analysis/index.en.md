## üöÄ The Solution: "Skill Gap Analyzer"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, high-level overview of your fit for a role.

> **Role:** You are a pragmatic career coach and senior technical recruiter.
> **Request:** Compare my resume against the provided Job Description (JD). Identify my missing competencies (skill gaps) and suggest a strategy to overcome them in my application.

> **Context:**

>
> **Input Material:**
>

>    [Paste the entire job description here]

>    [Paste your complete resume content here]

> **Task:**
>

> 2. **Weakness Mitigation Strategy:** Provide actionable advice on how to reinterpret my existing experiences to bridge the identified gaps. If a gap cannot be bridged by past experience, suggest how I should frame my short-term learning plan in my cover letter or interview.
> 3. **Pass Probability:** Give me a brutally honest evaluation out of 100 points for my current pass probability, along with a detailed rationale.

## üí° Writer's Insight

When applying for jobs, we often disqualify ourselves prematurely because we don't meet 100% of the JD requirements. This prompt is incredibly powerful because it shifts your mindset from "I'm not qualified" to "How can I prove I can learn this?"

As a hiring manager, I rarely see candidates who perfectly match every bullet point. What truly stands out is a candidate who acknowledges a missing skill but immediately follows up with a relevant transferable skill or a proactive learning plan. Use the output of this prompt to write a highly targeted cover letter. Instead of hiding your weaknesses, address them strategically and turn them into a narrative of growth and adaptability.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I use this for completely different industries (e.g., changing careers)?**
  - A: Absolutely. In fact, it's even more useful for career pivoters. Just add a line to the `Context` section stating: _"I am transitioning from [Current Industry] to [Target Industry]. Please focus heavily on identifying transferable skills."_

- **Q: Should I include the company name and background in the JD input?**
  - A: Yes! Including the company overview helps the AI understand the corporate culture and the implicit soft skills the employer might value, leading to a more nuanced analysis.

- **Q: The AI gave me a very low pass probability (under 40 points). Should I still apply?**
  - A: A low score usually means your resume doesn't use the same terminology as the JD. First, try rewriting your resume to incorporate the keywords the AI highlighted in the 'Gap' section, and run the prompt again. If the core experience is genuinely missing, it might be a sign to aim for a slightly more junior role or invest time in upskilling before applying.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing (Senior Recruiter):** By setting the persona as a "pragmatic career coach and senior recruiter," the AI avoids overly generic advice and adopts an evaluator's perspective.
2. **Structured Inputs:** Separating the JD and Resume clearly prevents the AI from mixing up what you _have_ done with what you _need_ to do.
3. **Tough Love Constraint:** The instruction to provide "fact violence" (brutal honesty) forces the AI to highlight fatal flaws rather than offering false hope, giving you a chance to prepare real defenses.
