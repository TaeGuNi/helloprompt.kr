## 🚀 How the Pros Write It

I have prepared a basic prompt to get you started quickly and an expert-level prompt that perfectly reflects your lifestyle. Copy the prompts below and fill in the `[variables]` to put them to use immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly grab the basic framework of a diet plan without complex settings.

> **Role:** You are a clinical nutritionist and diet coach with 10 years of experience.
>
> **Request:** My target weight is `[Target Weight]`kg. Please create a realistic weekly diet meal plan and a grocery shopping list to help me achieve this goal.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this for a sophisticated, high-quality meal plan that thoroughly reflects your busy lifestyle, tastes, and fridge inventory.

> **Role:** You are a veteran clinical nutritionist and personal trainer. You are a top-tier expert at creating nutritionally balanced meal plans that are realistic for busy modern individuals.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Current Weight: `[Current Weight]`kg
> - Target Weight: `[Target Weight]`kg (Target Duration: `[Duration, e.g., 3 months]`)
> - Preferred Foods: `[Preferred Foods, e.g., chicken breast, salmon, salad, tofu]`
> - Disliked Foods/Allergies: `[Disliked Foods/Allergies, e.g., cucumbers, nuts]`
> - Daily Cooking Time: Max `[Available Cooking Time, e.g., 30 minutes]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Estimate my BMR and activity level to calculate the recommended daily calorie intake for weight loss.
> 2. Based on this, create a meal plan for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks from Monday to Sunday.
> 3. Clearly display the approximate calories and key nutrient (protein) content for each meal.
> 4. Finally, organize a Weekly Shopping List categorized by Vegetables/Fruits, Meat/Seafood, and Others.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use only common ingredients that are easy to find at a local grocery store.
> - Strictly exclude menus with complex cooking processes; focus on simple recipes or those suitable for meal prep.
> - Use a Markdown Table format to make the daily meal plan easy to read at a glance.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not hallucinate calorie information.
> - Never suggest extreme fasting or "one-food" diets that require medical supervision.

## 💡 Author's Commentary (Insight)

The reason this prompt is so much more powerful than generic diet bot prompts is that it strictly controls two extremely realistic variables: **'Available Cooking Time'** and **'Preferred/Disliked Ingredients.'** The biggest reason why many dieters give up after a few days—even with a nutritionally perfect plan—is because they are recommended "Instagrammable" menus that are too complex to prepare given their energy levels after a long commute.

If you often work late and have very little energy for cooking, I strongly recommend going beyond just writing "30 minutes" in the `[Available Cooking Time]` variable. Instead, try adding extreme constraints like **"Only include recipes under 10 minutes that primarily use a microwave and don't require a cutting board"** or **"Only include no-cook salads and meal prep recipes."** When you confine the AI's imagination within the framework of your reality, the practical execution of the result jumps by over 200%. This is because the plan is filled only with manageable tasks you can actually do even on your most exhausted days.

What if you have leftover vegetables or ingredients near their expiration date after the weekend? Don't throw this prompt away—recycle it. In the `[Context]` section, add a detail like: **"Additional Condition: I have half a cabbage, one block of tofu expiring tomorrow, and a pack of enoki mushrooms in my fridge. Prioritize using these ingredients in the meals for the beginning of this week."** This allows you to create a smart "fridge clearing" plan that prevents food waste while maintaining nutritional balance with a single tap of the Enter key.

Another advanced tip I love is controlling exceptions within the prompt variables. As an office worker, you might have unavoidable social plans or need a "cheat day" on Friday nights or weekends. Instead of trying to resist, declare it in the constraints: **"On Friday night, I have a social dinner planned (e.g., pork belly or sashimi) within 800kcal. Adjust the Friday lunch and Saturday morning meals to be low-carb detox meals to compensate."** The AI can handle these complex calorie carry-over calculations and macro adjustments in seconds. Dieting isn't about starving yourself or being trapped in perfectionism; it's a technical process of regaining control over your life through flexible and precise variable management.

Additionally, feeding the feedback loop from your first week back into the prompt is a vital step toward becoming a pro. After following the plan for a week, you might find you were hungrier than expected or that certain ingredients didn't sit well with you. Don't open a new chat; instead, in the same window, say: **"In last week's plan, I felt a sharp drop in energy and hunger around 3 PM. For next week, increase the ratio of complex carbs at lunch by 15% or add a light, low-glycemic protein snack for 4 PM."** By continuously training the AI with your failure data and physical feedback, you will eventually secure a "fail-proof golden meal plan" tailored specifically to your body—something even expensive trainers can't easily replicate. This is the essence of prompt engineering: turning AI into a true professional partner.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I include ingredient prices or a weekly budget in the plan?**
  - A: Yes, absolutely. Add a sentence to the Constraints section like: "Keep the weekly grocery budget under $50 and focus on cost-effective protein sources like tofu and eggs." You'll get an optimized **'budget-friendly' meal plan** that protects both your nutrition and your wallet.

- **Q: I already have a standard 'Chicken/Sweet Potato/Veggie' diet from my gym trainer. Can I adapt that?**
  - A: Of course. In the Context section, state: "My current base diet is: [Diet Details]. Keep the original nutritional macros strictly, but provide 7 different recipe variations using different sauces or cooking methods." You'll experience the magic of maintaining nutritional integrity while enjoying your diet every day without getting bored.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **The Power of Role:** By assigning a specific and authoritative persona—a 'clinical nutritionist and personal trainer with 10 years of experience'—rather than a vague assistant, we guide the AI to output highly systematic plans based on medical and nutritional evidence.
2. **Micro-details in Context:** By meticulously designing variables like current weight, disliked foods, and cooking time, we ensure the result is an ultra-personalized plan synced with your lifestyle and limitations, rather than generic advice.
3. **Realism in Constraints:** Adding strict behavioral constraints like "exclude complex menus" and "use common ingredients" prevents the **chronic failure pattern of dieters** who give up when faced with practical barriers after receiving an overly ambitious meal plan.
