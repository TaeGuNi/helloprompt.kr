> _"I want to buy Bitcoin, but I'm not sure if I can trust the exchanges... What if I invest indirectly through Coinbase stock?"_

Coinbase is the only <b>listed crypto exchange</b> in the U.S. and the largest custodian for spot Bitcoin ETFs. With major Bitcoin ETFs, including BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT), choosing Coinbase as their custodian, a new business model has emerged that generates **stable custody fee revenue**, regardless of Bitcoin's price.

The challenge, however, is Coinbase's <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">extreme revenue volatility</span>. Transaction fees surge during crypto bull markets but can drop by half during bear markets. After recording losses in 2022, they hit record performance during the 2024–2025 Bitcoin boom. To judge if "the current price is appropriate," one must separate cyclical revenue (transaction fees) from recurring revenue (custody, subscriptions, and interest).

This prompt splits Coinbase's revenue into **"Cyclical" and "Recurring"** categories and calculates revenue and profit based on various Bitcoin price scenarios (Bear/Neutral/Bull). The key question is whether Coinbase can remain profitable even if Bitcoin drops to $50K.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The core insight of this prompt is the **"Recurring Revenue Ratio."** In 2023, recurring revenue was only 22%, but thanks to ETF custody and USDC interest income, it has reached 37% by 2026. If this ratio exceeds 50%, Coinbase can be re-evaluated as a **"financial infrastructure company"** rather than just a "bet on crypto market direction."

The most important part of the Bitcoin scenario analysis is **"Is it profitable even in a bear scenario?"** If it is <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">profitable even at BTC $50K</span>, buying now becomes an asymmetric bet: a bonus if Bitcoin goes up, and a solid foundation even if it drops. This is the core investment framework this prompt pursues.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Is buying Coinbase stock better than buying Bitcoin directly?**
  - A: Direct Bitcoin investment is a bet solely on price action. Coinbase stock is a bet on the "trading volume and infrastructure demand of the entire crypto ecosystem." With diverse revenue streams like custody, staking, and Base L2, it serves a different purpose in a diversified portfolio.

- **Q: Can the SEC lawsuit have a fatal impact on Coinbase?**
  - A: We included regulatory risk in the prompt. Analyze both the worst-case (delisting of specific tokens, fines) and best-case (increased institutional investment due to regulatory clarity) scenarios.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Core of Cyclical/Recurring Split:** Without this structure, an AI might calculate valuation based on bull market revenue, leading to the dangerous conclusion that it's "extremely cheap." This split prevents that trap.
2. **Independence of Bitcoin Scenarios:** Instead of predicting "will Bitcoin go up or down," it calculates "what is Coinbase's enterprise value at each price," enabling investment decisions that don't rely on price predictions.
