## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

### 🥉 Basic Version (Simple Distribution)

Use this lightly when you quickly need a rough time plan.

> **Role:** You are a `[Time Management Expert]`.
>
> **Request:** I'm going to do `[English Conversation Shadowing]` and `[Work-related Reading]` for 2 hours from 5 AM to 7 AM tomorrow morning. Please create an efficient schedule.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Neuroscience-based Expert)

Use this when you need detailed and systematic behavioral guidelines for peak immersion.

> **Role:** You are a neuroscientific learning strategist and Deep Work expert.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Available Time: 05:00 ~ 07:00 (Total 2 hours)
> - Goal Task: `[Write first draft of new service proposal]`
> - My State: Not fully awake, groggy, and finding it difficult to achieve immediate high concentration.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **05:00~05:15 (Brain Warm-up Phase):** Recommend a specific light first activity that can gently wake up the sleeping brain and stimulate dopamine secretion.
> 2. **05:15~06:45 (Deep Work Phase):** Apply the Pomodoro technique (e.g., 50 min focus / 10 min break) to break down and arrange the main task into stages. Provide specific milestones to be achieved in each session.
> 3. **06:45~07:00 (Reflection and Closing Phase):** Construct a closing routine to visualize today's achievements and start the main workday feeling good.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Format the output as a bulleted list instead of a table for mobile readability.
> - Provide clear, action-oriented sentences for 'what' and 'how' I should do things in each time slot.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not assign an unrealistic workload; ensure realistic break times are included considering the limits of human concentration.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: I'm a night owl, so waking up early is impossible. Can evening people use this too?**
  - A: Of course. If you change the available time in the 'Context' of the prompt to your peak concentration hours at night, such as `[22:00 ~ 24:00]`, you can achieve the exact same effect.

- **Q: I want to focus for the full 2 hours. Do I really need to include break times?**
  - A: Yes, it's essential. Neuroscientifically, there is a clear limit to how long the human prefrontal cortex can maintain peak concentration. Short breaks (staring blankly, stretching, etc.) are a necessary recharging process to prevent brain overload and fuel the next concentration session.

- **Q: What if I find it annoying to run the prompt every morning?**
  - A: You don't have to do it every day. I highly recommend putting a week's worth of core tasks (e.g., Monday-Proposal draft, Tuesday-Industry trend reading, etc.) into the `[Goal Task]` variable on the weekend and generating 5 days' worth of cue sheets at once.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Explicit Neuroscientific Approach:** Instead of just mechanically dividing time, it forces a scientific cycle of 'Brain Warm-up - Deep Work - Reflection' into the Task, maximizing the success rate of actual behavior.
2. **Action-oriented Constraints:** Instead of a vague instruction like "Write proposal," constraints are set to output clearly "what and how" things will be done, significantly increasing the practicality of the AI's response.
3. **Reflecting State (Context):** By providing the specific constraint of being in a 'groggy, not fully awake state,' it fundamentally blocks the AI from proposing unrealistic high-intensity tasks immediately after waking (Hallucination-free scheduling).
