## 🚀 How the Pros Do It

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` to fit your situation, and deploy it immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly grasp the overall flow and general meaning without a deep analysis.

> **Role:** You are a Professor of History and a Classical Philologist.
>
> **Task:** Please translate the historical document (or source) below into a conversational style that is easy for modern people to understand, and summarize the core theme of this text.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you want to analyze the deep details of the historical background and the author's cleverly hidden intentions. Copy the prompt below and modify the variables inside the `[brackets]` to suit your purpose.

> **Role:** You are a `[Classical Philologist and Historical Narrator]` who has studied primary sources for decades. You have an exceptional ability to explain complex and difficult classical texts to the public in an easy and interesting way.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am currently reading `[Title of the classical text or source to be analyzed]`.
> - Problem: The sentence structure is difficult, and I lack historical background knowledge, making it hard to grasp the true meaning the author intended to convey.
> - Goal: I want to perfectly understand this text and grasp the historical context of the time in a multi-dimensional way.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **[Modern Translation]:** Translate this text into a smooth, modern conversational style that even a high school student today could easily understand.
> 2. **[Reading Between the Lines]:** Provide a sharp analysis of the author's true intent in writing this or the political/social background hidden behind the text.
> 3. **[Keyword Explanation]:** Select the 3 most important core terms from the text and explain them accurately according to the 'context of that era' rather than their modern meanings.
> 4. **[Historical Significance]:** Summarize why this source (text) is historically important in one powerful line.
>
> **Input Text:**
> `[Paste the classical text or primary source original here]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - For mobile readability, do not use tables; organize the information using high-readability bullet points (lists).
> - Avoid mechanical literal translation; use natural **paraphrasing** focused entirely on conveying meaning.
> - Distinguish each section (Modern Translation, Reading Between the Lines, Keyword Explanation, Historical Significance) with Markdown headings (`###`).
> - Use **bold** for important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up information for parts where the historical background or facts are uncertain; clearly state "Multiple theories exist" or "Clear verification is difficult." (Strictly prevent historical distortion and hallucinations.)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The difference between simply telling an AI to "Translate this sentence" and using this prompt lies in **lifting 'flat knowledge' into 'three dimensions.'** This prompt is not just a one-dimensional machine translator. It is meticulously designed to function as a **'Context Decipherer'** that delicately reads the layers of time. While general translation prompts stop at mechanically matching modern words 1:1, the core of true classical and primary source analysis lies in piercing through the fundamental question: **"Who wrote this, when, and why did they risk their life to write it?"**

The parts I spent the most effort on while designing this prompt are the **[Reading Between the Lines]** and **[Keyword Explanation for the Era]** instructions. These two core constraints force the AI to move beyond the limits of a simple text converter or language model and actively exercise the **persona of a seasoned historian** who has spent decades digging into historical sources. Language is a mirror of its time. Even the same word carries vastly different nuances when used in 18th-century Joseon versus 21st-century modern Korea. This prompt assigns the AI the high-level mission of capturing those 'temporal nuances' and replacing them with modern equivalents.

During the verification process, I personally tested this prompt with stifling and difficult petitions from the *Annals of the Joseon Dynasty*, Machiavelli's *The Prince*, and even Kant's notoriously difficult philosophical texts. The results were astounding. What appeared on the surface to be dry characters pledging loyalty to a king were revealed to be implicit, bloody political battles and fierce power dynamics aimed at purging opponents. The AI perfectly identified those subtle tensions and deconstructed the text as if it were a vivid historical drama unfolding before my eyes. It doesn't just provide reading comprehension; it offers a massive sense of immersion as if you are directly conversing with the author and being pulled into the era they lived in.

**💡 Pro Tip & Constraint Control:**
Depending on the nature of the text and the historical background, try tuning the **Role** variable even more sharply. For example, when deciphering Eastern classics, refine it to `[A History Professor specializing in Joseon Dynasty factional politics]`; for Western philosophical documents, use `[An expert in modern Enlightenment thought]`. This will dramatically increase the resolution and depth of the "Reading Between the Lines" output.

Furthermore, it is crucial to strategically choose the AI model depending on whether the original text is a literary piece full of metaphors and poetic analogies, or a logical and rigid philosophical/political declaration. In my tests, **Claude 3.5 Sonnet's** context comprehension was overwhelmingly superior for capturing the subtle nuances of complex philosophical reasoning or literary texts. On the other hand, **GPT-4o** showed more stable performance when quickly cross-referencing vast amounts of historical facts and deriving objective information. Thanks to the strong constraints of the prompt, hallucinations rarely occurred in either model, but I recommend cross-checking with expert sources if using the output for academic purposes or citations. I hope you complete your own deep intellectual exploration through this powerful tool that restores the 'feelers of time' embedded in the text.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can it instantly interpret originals written entirely in Classical Chinese (Hanja)?**
  - A: Yes, it is perfectly capable. Specifically, if you input vast Classical Chinese sources like the *Annals of the Joseon Dynasty* or the *Diaries of the Royal Secretariat*, it provides expert-level modern translations along with in-depth background explanations. However, if the document is an image (photo, scan) rather than text, you will first need to convert it into text data using an AI that supports OCR (Optical Character Recognition), such as ChatGPT Plus.

- **Q: I'm worried the AI might plausibly make up historical facts.**
  - A: To prevent exactly that, I have placed very strong anti-hallucination instructions in the **Warning** section of the Pro prompt. Nevertheless, when using the output for important academic citations or official publications, you must go through a cross-check using professional reference books.

- **Q: The paraphrasing feels too modern, losing the heavy and solemn 'flavor' of the original.**
  - A: If you want a result closer to a literal translation that preserves the nuance and tone of the original source rather than the context, try changing the "natural paraphrasing focused on conveying meaning" part of the constraints to **"literal translation that preserves the tone and style of the original as much as possible."**

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1.  **Combination of Role and Context:** I gave the AI a specific persona of a **'Classical Philologist'** rather than a vague translator. By clearly presenting the difficulties the user is facing (difficult sentence structure, lack of background) as Context, I guided the AI to perfectly adjust its explanation level to be user-friendly.
2.  **Enforced Systematic Output Structure:** I forced a systematic 4-step analysis framework: Translation, Reading Between the Lines, Key Terms, and Historical Significance. This prevents the disaster of the AI simply tossing a translation and ending the analysis.
3.  **Anti-Hallucination Safety Device:** Considering the nature of historical sources where fact-checking is vital, I added a strong constraint (Warning) to "not arbitrarily make up information that is not certain," significantly increasing the reliability and integrity of the information.
