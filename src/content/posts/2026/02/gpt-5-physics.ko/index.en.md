## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the **[variables]** in brackets to suit your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version (Standard)

Useful when you need to quickly scan just the core summary of a paper.

> **Role:** You are a `[Science Journalist]`.
>
> **Task:** Clearly summarize the core discovery of the following `[Paper Text or Link]` and how it will change the world into 3 points that even a middle school student can understand instantly.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you want to dissect a paper to its bones—from its academic background to critical limitations and practical insights for your job.

> **Role:** You are a Nobel-winning senior researcher and the ultimate mentor who can explain even the most difficult concepts using brilliant analogies.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: I am an `[IT Planner/Marketer]`, and I need to understand this paper to quickly apply the latest tech trends to my business.
> - Goal: To clearly see the core value and limitations of this discovery without any prior knowledge or understanding of professional jargon.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Deeply analyze the provided `[Paper Text or Link]` and explain the items below in order.
> 2. **Core Research Achievement:** Condense and summarize exactly what was discovered in 1–2 sentences.
> 3. **Easy Analogy:** Explain this complex discovery intuitively by comparing it to a `[Familiar Object or Situation from Daily Life]`.
> 4. **Practical Implications:** Specifically predict what kind of tectonic shift this discovery will cause in my job or industry within the next 5 years.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Write in a clean Markdown format to maximize readability.
> - For mobile readability, never use tables; instead, organize information using high-readability bullet points.
> - Do not leave complex equations or difficult professional jargon as they are; always include easy-to-understand explanations in brackets.
> - Be sure to **bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not arbitrarily exaggerate or invent ripple effects not specified in the paper. For parts that lack evidence or are ambiguous, clearly state that they are 'still under verification.' (To prevent hallucinations)

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: How should I input dozens of pages of vast English research papers?**
  - A: You can directly upload the original paper's PDF file to the chat window (I strongly recommend using Claude 3.5 Sonnet or GPT-4o) or copy the text and paste it at the bottom of the prompt. If the file size is too large or exceeds the context length limit, simply extracting and inputting just the **Abstract** and **Conclusion** sections will still allow you to easily extract over 80% of the overall insights.

- **Q: What if the AI's summary contains false information (hallucinations)?**
  - A: To strictly prevent that, I have set **"do not exaggerate or invent"** as a strict rule in the constraints section. Nevertheless, if you must use this data for a critical company decision, I highly recommend asking a follow-up cross-verification (Double Check) question: *"Quote the exact original text from the paper (page and paragraph) that this summary is based on."*
