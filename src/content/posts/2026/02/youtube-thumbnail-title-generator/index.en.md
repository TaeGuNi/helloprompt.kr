>    - **Composition:** (e.g., Close-up face, split screen)

>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Output the results in a clean Markdown format.
> - Do not generate generic advice; be highly specific to the provided topic and demographic.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - Avoid clickbait that does not deliver on its promise. The title must remain relevant to the core topic.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work for educational or B2B channels, or is it just for entertainment?**
  - A: It works flawlessly for both! For B2B or educational content, simply adjust the `[Topic]` and `[Audience Needs]` (e.g., "SaaS founders looking to reduce churn"). The core psychology of the "Curiosity Gap" applies to every niche.

- **Q: Will using these titles make my channel look like cheap clickbait?**
  - A: There is a massive difference between _clickbait_ (promising something you don't deliver) and a _great hook_ (promising something amazing and actually delivering it). As long as your video fulfills the promise made by the AI-generated title, viewers will be satisfied, not deceived.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works)

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning the persona of a "1M Subscriber Strategist," the AI adopts a highly competitive, growth-focused mindset rather than giving generic, safe advice.
2. **Forced Constraints:** Mandating specific frameworks (Curiosity Gap, Negativity Bias) guarantees variety and prevents boring, descriptive titles.
3. **Visual Extraction:** By asking for Composition, Expression, and Text separately, the AI provides an actionable blueprint for your thumbnail designer (or yourself), bridging the gap between text generation and visual design.
