## 🚀 How Real Professionals Write Prompts

This prompt was perfected after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in brackets to match your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want quick snack ideas you can eat right now without complex conditions or constraints.

> **Role:** You are a professional diet nutritionist who perfectly understands demanding palates.
>
> **Task:**
> I'm on a diet, and my fake hunger gets really bad around 3 PM. Recommend 5 healthy snack ideas that will definitely blow away work stress without making me gain any weight.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a perfectly customized snack list that considers your taste, current situation, and dietary constraints.

> **Role:** You are a picky dessert gourmet and an excellent health food researcher. You have a firm philosophy of 'never letting anything tasteless touch your lips.'
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Time/Place: It's currently 3 PM, and I'm at a `[convenience store near my office]`.
> - Craving: I am currently craving something `[sweet and crunchy]` like crazy.
> - Constraints: However, I am on a strict diet that severely limits `[sugar and flour]` intake.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Suggest **3 alternative snack ideas** that will perfectly satisfy my explosive appetite without ruining my diet.
> 2. Pick **2 commercial products** I can grab right now at the **`[convenience store]`**, specifying brand names or product categories.
> 3. If I were at home, provide **1 ultra-simple homemade recipe** that can be finished in 5 minutes using an **air fryer or microwave**.
> 4. Roughly specify the estimated calories and key nutritional components (sugar, protein content) for all the snacks you recommend.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Never mention boring diet foods everyone knows, like cherry tomatoes, sweet potatoes, boiled eggs, or konjac jelly.
> - Select only innovative ideas that produce a perfect 'real-world taste' that can trick the brain, close to regular meals or high-end desserts.
> - For mobile readability, do not use tables; organize in a high-readability bulleted list format.
> - Be sure to **bold** important keywords.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up uncertain information or discontinued commercial products; answer "I don't know" instead. (Hallucination prevention)

## 💡 Insight & How to Use

The reason this prompt is revolutionarily powerful for dieters is that it **forcibly injects a shallow and firm Persona into the AI: 'I will never eat anything that doesn't taste good.'** If we simply turn on ChatGPT and ask, "Tell me some diet snacks that won't make me gain weight," what kind of answer do we get? Nine times out of ten, the AI will mechanically list boring, textbook answers like cherry tomatoes, celery sticks, unsweetened plain yogurt, dry boiled eggs, or chicken breast sausages. Recommending a cucumber stick to someone who sees chocolate cake in their dreams won't stop a binge; it will only trigger anger.

However, the moment you provide very sensory and specific constraints like `[sweet and crunchy texture]` and `[real-world taste]` to trick the brain, as in the Pro Version prompt, the quality of the output changes 180 degrees. The polite nutritionist AI suddenly transforms into a **diet chef with a devilish talent**. It starts pouring out ingenious alternatives like 'protein balls with chocolate crunch,' 'frozen Greek yogurt bark coated with allulose,' and 'salty cheddar cheese chips made in 1 minute in the microwave'—things that perfectly trick our brain and palate while having great ingredients. This approach—not suppressing appetite, but greedily pursuing flavor while only swapping ingredients—is the core secret to preventing a yoyo effect.

Furthermore, the value of this tool is determined by how much you modify and apply **Constraint Control** in practice. The most notable variable is the **specification of the physical environment**. Try asking specifically for things like "what I can buy right now at the convenience store downstairs" or "what is sold at Olive Young." This maximizes utility because you can run straight to the shelves during your commute or that sluggish afternoon work hour. You no longer need to drag your exhausted body home to prep ingredients; you can feel the thrill of solving all your problems just by stepping out with your wallet.

Also, using **personalized nutritional constraints** as variables is a tip you shouldn't miss. If you are on a Low-Carb High-Fat (Keto) diet, set strict numerical constraints in the variables like `[less than 10g of carbs, more than 20g of fat]`. If you are lactose intolerant, add the condition `[completely exclude all dairy, only plant-based alternatives allowed]`. This prompt isn't just a question box; it's like hiring a **24-hour dedicated personal nutritionist** who prepares customized solutions in real-time, considering your subtle tastes, allergies, and even your budget. End the painful diet that relies on willpower and start controlling your palate smartly with AI's calculated recipes.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: AI recommended a great commercial product, but when I went to the convenience store, it was discontinued or out of stock. What should I do?**
  - A: Free versions of conversational AI models cannot perfectly see real-time product discontinuations or specific stock levels at your local store. In this case, rather than obsessing over a specific brand, focus on the **'key nutritional specs'** provided in the answer. For example, if the AI recommended 'Company A's 85% dark chocolate with less than 2g of sugar,' it's important to develop the sharp eye to pick an alternative from Company B or C on the shelf that has the most similar nutritional profile (less than 2g of sugar).
- **Q: I have severe food allergies. Can this be perfectly controlled with a prompt?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just add a single line of critical constraints at the bottom of the `[Context]` block in the prompt, such as "I have a severe nut allergy" or "Please filter only for 100% Vegan snacks." The AI will perfectly block risk factors and output only results that are safe and fit for your body.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **The Magic of Firm Persona Injection:** By giving the AI the role of a "picky dessert gourmet" and shackling it with the rule that "tasteless things are absolutely rejected," this single sentence becomes a decisive trigger. The AI itself filters out boring and tiresome textbook diet foods (cucumbers, chicken breast, etc.) from the results.
2. **Ultra-Precise Context Design:** The physical location (convenience store/home) and the sensory craving the brain is fiercely desiring (sweet/salty/crunchy) are firmly fixed as variables. Thanks to this, the output isn't a floating generalization but a 'surreal and immediate solution' you can execute within 5 minutes of opening your wallet.
3. **Forced Nutritional Metrics:** To prevent the AI from just tossing out plausible-sounding food names, I forced the output of key nutritional indicators like estimated calories, sugar, and protein. With these logical and objective numbers backing it up, the dieter gains the psychological clearance to enjoy the recommended snack without a hint of guilt or unease.
