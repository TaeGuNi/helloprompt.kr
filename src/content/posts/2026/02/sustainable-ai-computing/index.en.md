**🌱 Green AI Architecture Transition Roadmap Report**

**1. Analysis of Major Energy Waste Factors**
Currently, '100,000 image generations per day' are likely processed on GPU instances that are always on, regardless of real-time traffic. Standby power consumption and the use of heavy original models (FP32) are the primary causes of waste.

**2. Three-Step Concrete Solutions**
*   **Model Optimization:** Quantize models to FP16 or INT8 using TensorRT or ONNX. This drastically reduces power consumption while increasing inference speed.
*   **Resource Management:** Consider migrating to low-power custom chips dedicated to AI inference, such as AWS Inferentia.
*   **Scheduling:** For asynchronous image generation tasks that don't require real-time response, process them in batches using idle resources during nighttime or through Spot Instances.

**3. Expected Benefits**
*   **Estimated Energy Savings:** Approx. 35% - 50% (Varies by actual traffic and implementation level)
*   **Qualitative ESG Impact:** Carbon reduction figures can be quantitatively recorded in the Sustainability Report, enhancing the brand image as a leading eco-friendly AI company.

## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in brackets to suit your situation.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly check the overall direction of optimization.

> **Role:** You are a top expert in cloud infrastructure and Sustainable AI (Green AI).
>
> **Task:** Suggest 3 key optimization measures that can drastically reduce server power consumption and carbon emissions for the `[AI service or model name]` we are currently operating.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a detailed report reflecting actual infrastructure architecture and specific migration strategies.

> **Role:** You are a senior 'Green AI Architect' and ESG specialist consultant for a global big-tech company.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: Our company is currently operating `[AI model or task, e.g., 100,000 image generations per day]` in a `[Cloud environment, e.g., AWS]` environment. However, power consumption and cloud infrastructure maintenance costs are exploding exponentially, necessitating an urgent architecture improvement for ESG management.
> - Goal: To derive a roadmap for transitioning to a 'Sustainable AI Computing Architecture' that maximizes energy efficiency while minimizing service performance degradation.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Conduct an in-depth analysis of the most critical energy waste factors based on our current infrastructure state.
> 2. Provide immediately actionable practical solutions from three core perspectives: `[Model optimization technique, e.g., Quantization]`, `[Hardware or cloud resource management]`, and `[Task scheduling and batch optimization]`.
> 3. Summarize the estimated energy saving rate (%) and qualitative benefits from an ESG perspective for a management report.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - The output format should be a summary report using Markdown bullet points and **Bold text** for high readability on mobile screens. (Do not use wide tables that cause horizontal scrolling.)
> - Exclude overly academic theories and specify names of concrete tools or frameworks (e.g., ONNX, TensorRT, etc.) that practitioners can review for adoption tomorrow.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not invent unverified figures or exaggerated cost-saving effects. If an accurate prediction is difficult, mark it as "Varies by situation." (Anti-hallucination)
