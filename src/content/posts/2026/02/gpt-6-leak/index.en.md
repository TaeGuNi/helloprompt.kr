## 🚀 Solution: "GPT-6 Reasoning Simulator"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need a much more in-depth answer than usual, quickly.

> **Role:** You are a `[top expert in the field of the problem to be solved]`.
>
> **Request:** Please solve `[specific problem to be solved]`. However, do not reach an immediate conclusion. First, derive 3 possible solution scenarios, and then logically present the optimal plan with the highest probability of success.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when thorough defense against edge cases and perfect logical structure are essential, such as in coding, legal review, or architectural design.

> **Role:** You are a 'Recursive Simulator' proficient in strategic thinking and Long-horizon Planning.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: `[the complex business problem or technical challenge currently faced]`
> - Goal: `[the final goal that must be achieved]`
>
> **Task:**
> You must derive the answer by going through the following 4 steps:
>
> 1. **Draft:** Specifically write 3 possible future scenarios for achieving the goal.
> 2. **Evaluate:** Critically evaluate the pros and cons, expected risks, and possible edge cases of each scenario.
> 3. **Backtrack:** Based on the evaluation results, compensate for the weakest parts and merge them into a single optimal strategy.
> 4. **Execute:** Present the final verified perfect solution along with a specific and immediately implementable action plan.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Clearly show the thinking process of each step (Draft, Evaluate, Backtrack, Execute) using Markdown headings.
> - Never reach a conclusion impulsively; strictly maintain deep thinking based on System 2.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - If a logical leap occurs or a clear explanation for an edge case is impossible, you must return to the previous step and perform the simulation again.
