## üöÄ The Solution: "Gold Button Naming Center"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick burst of inspiration.

> **Role:** You are a seasoned YouTube consultant and master copywriter who has built multiple 1-million-subscriber channels.
> **Request:** I am starting a new YouTube channel about `[Insert Topic]`. Please suggest 10 catchy, sensible channel names that the YouTube algorithm will love, along with matching subscriber nicknames.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need meticulous, highly-targeted branding quality.

> **Role (Role):** You are a top-tier YouTube consultant and elite copywriter responsible for launching several channels with over 1 million subscribers.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: I am opening a new YouTube channel and need an attractive, highly-clickable channel name.
> - Goal: To establish a strong brand identity that resonates with my target audience and stands out in search results.
>
> **Channel Info:**
>
> - **Topic/Category:** `[e.g., Office worker finance, Cat Vlog, Corner Room Live]`
> - **Target Audience:** `[e.g., Young professionals in their 20s and 30s, People seeking relaxation]`
> - **My Nickname/Feature:** `[e.g., Manager Kim, Introverted Homebody, Glasses-wearing geek]`
>
> **Request (Task):**
>
> 1. Recommend **3 Channel Name Candidates** for each of the following styles (Total of 9 names):
>    - **Intuitive:** Trustworthy names that instantly reveal the channel's topic.
>    - **Friendly/Catchy:** Cute, easy-to-pronounce names that roll off the tongue.
>    - **Emotional/Unique:** Distinctive names that spark curiosity and emotion.
> 2. Recommend one **Subscriber Nickname (Fandom Name)** that perfectly complements each channel name candidate.
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Ensure the suggested names do not overlap with existing, famous YouTubers.
> - Keep the names short, punchy, and memorable (preferably under 15 characters).
> - Present the final output using clear Markdown formatting with bullet points.
>
> **Warning (Warning):**
>
> - If a name closely resembles a highly popular existing brand or channel, explicitly state the potential conflict. Do not hallucinate availability.

## üí° Writer's Insight

Choosing a YouTube channel name is often the biggest roadblock for beginners. People spend weeks agonizing over a name, delaying their actual content creation. This prompt is incredibly effective because it breaks down the "naming block" by forcing the AI to categorize ideas into three distinct styles: Intuitive, Friendly, and Emotional.

I've used this exact framework for corporate clients launching branded channels. The secret sauce here is asking for the **Fandom Name** alongside the channel name. A channel name might sound okay, but when you hear the matching fandom name (like "Money Manager TV" -> "Shareholders"), it instantly validates whether the brand identity has legs. It shifts your perspective from just naming a channel to building a community.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work well for non-English channel names?**
  - A: Absolutely. You can modify the prompt to specify the language (e.g., "Suggest names in Korean/Spanish") or ask for bilingual names that sound natural globally.

- **Q: How do I know if the AI-suggested name is actually available on YouTube?**
  - A: While the AI is instructed to avoid famous YouTubers, it doesn't have real-time access to the YouTube database. You must manually search your favorite generated name on YouTube and check if the custom `@handle` is available before finalizing it.

- **Q: Should I use my real name or a brand name?**
  - A: If you are building a personal brand (like a vlog or expert consulting), your real name or a stable nickname works best. If the channel is topic-focused (like a faceless finance channel), a brand name from the "Intuitive" category is usually more effective.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning the AI the persona of a "million-subscriber YouTube consultant," the tone of the suggestions shifts from generic to highly commercial and strategic.
2. **Categorization (Styles):** Forcing the AI to provide names in three distinct buckets (Intuitive, Catchy, Emotional) prevents it from giving one-dimensional, boring suggestions.
3. **Fandom Synergy:** Coupling the channel name with a fandom nickname ensures the suggested names have a strong, community-building potential.
