The biggest enemy of any diet is an **unrealistic meal plan**. Avocado toast, quinoa salads, Greek yogurt parfaits... the meals we see on Instagram look great, but a typical fridge is more likely to have eggs, tofu, kimchi, and onions. If you can't make a meal with what's in your fridge, you won't last three days.

By telling an AI **"the ingredients in my fridge," "my daily calorie goal," and "any allergies/disliked foods,"** you can get a <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">weekly meal plan that maximizes your existing stock while perfectly balancing calories and nutrients</span> in just 5 minutes. It even generates a shopping list automatically.

## 🚀 How Experts Write Prompts

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Nutritionist]`.
>
> **Request:** Create a `[1,500 kcal per day]` diet plan for 5 days (Mon–Fri). Focus on Korean-style meals.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are an `[Expert Dietitian with a Master's degree in Sports Nutrition]`. You are well-versed in Korean eating habits and recipes, and you can precisely design meal plans based on calorie and macro (P/C/F) balance.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Target: `[30s male, office worker, Height 175cm, Weight 82kg, Goal 75kg]`
> - Activity Level: `[Gym 2x/week (Cardio+Weights), mostly sedentary daily life]`
> - Daily Calorie Goal: `[1,500 kcal]`
> - Macro Ratio: `[Protein 30%, Carbs 45%, Fat 25%]`
> - Fridge Ingredients: `[10 eggs, 2 blocks of tofu, kimchi, onions, carrots, brown rice, gochujang, soybean paste]`
> - Allergies/Dislikes: `[Shrimp allergy, dairy okay, spicy food okay]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Weekly Meal Plan:** Write a 4-meal plan (Breakfast-Lunch-Snack-Dinner) for 5 days (Mon–Fri).
> 2. **Nutritional Info:** Indicate the calories and grams of P/C/F for each meal.
> 3. **Shopping List:** Compile a list of only the missing ingredients with estimated prices.
> 4. **Cooking Difficulty:** Mark each menu with ⭐ (5 mins), ⭐⭐ (15 mins), or ⭐⭐⭐ (30 mins) cooking time.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Never use shrimp.
> - Ensure variety so that one ingredient doesn't appear as the main item for more than 3 consecutive days.
> - Limit lunch menus to items that can be packed as a lunch box.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - This meal plan is an AI-generated reference. If you have specific conditions (diabetes, kidney disease, etc.), please consult a medical professional before starting.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The core of this prompt is the **"fridge ingredients."** Most AI diet requests end at "make me a 1,500 kcal plan." If you do that, the AI will recommend <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">ingredients detached from reality</span>, like avocado, quinoa, or salmon. By telling it you have "eggs, tofu, and kimchi," you get a plan that is actually executable and sustainable.

Pro-tip: The constraint "menus that can be packed as a lunch box" is surprisingly powerful. The biggest reason office workers fail their diets is lunch. If you bring a lunch box instead of eating at the cafeteria or nearby restaurants, calorie control becomes much easier.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Will I actually lose weight following an AI diet plan?**
  - A: Weight loss occurs when you maintain a caloric deficit (calories burned > calories consumed). The AI simply helps with the math; the actual execution depends on your willpower. Consult a doctor based on your health status.

- **Q: Can I use this if I want to gain muscle?**
  - A: Yes. Just change the `[Goal]` to "Lean Mass Up (Muscle gain + fat loss)" and increase the macro ratio to "Protein 40%."

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Specification of Realistic Constraints:** Fridge ingredients, allergies, and cooking times are all real-world constraints. Giving these to the AI results in a plan that is "actually doable" rather than just "theoretically perfect but impossible to execute."
2. **Precise Macro Control:** By specifying the P/C/F ratio, the AI attempts to balance the numbers for each meal. This is the difference between a "vaguely healthy diet" and a "scientifically designed diet."
