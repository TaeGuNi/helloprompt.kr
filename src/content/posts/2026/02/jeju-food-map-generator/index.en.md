## 🚀 The Solution: "Gourmet Map Prompt"

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you need to quickly find a great spot nearby.

> **Task:** Recommend exactly 3 `[cafes]` near `[Aewol-eup, Jeju Island]` that have an amazing `[ocean view]`. Please find places that aren't overly crowded and have convenient parking.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a perfectly vetted list of establishments that seamlessly fits your travel itinerary.

> **Role:** You are a strict Michelin Guide inspector and an expert in local data analysis.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Target Area: `[Jeju Island West Coast (Hyeopjae to Aewol to Hallim) coastal road]`
> - Category: `[Cafe (renowned for exceptional desserts)]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Strictly curate places that maintain a Google Maps rating of 4.2+ OR a KakaoMap rating of 4.0+.
> 2. Rigorously exclude any establishments with a high volume of blog reviews containing keywords like "sponsored," "compensated," or "provided for free."
> 3. You must indicate whether each location is a "No Kids Zone" and detail its "Parking Availability."
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Output the results as a highly readable bulleted list. (Fields: Cafe Name | 1-Line Review | Recommended Menu | Parking & Kids)
> - Exclude places that are permanently closed or lack reviews from the last 3 months.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not hallucinate. If information is uncertain, explicitly state "No Information Available."

## 💡 Writer's Insight

The ultimate killer move for restaurant search prompts is utilizing **Negative Prompting** and **Cross-Verification**. If you simply ask the AI to "recommend a good place," it will instinctively fetch the most heavily SEO-optimized marketing posts available online.

Therefore, you must apply sharp, specific constraints—such as "Exclude places with over an hour of waiting," "Exclude places with poor cost-effectiveness," or "Must simultaneously meet high rating thresholds on both Google Maps and KakaoMap." Utilizing a real-time search AI like Perplexity is especially powerful here; it can actively filter out mandatory "sponsored" disclosures in blog reviews, effectively bypassing Instagram tourist traps to uncover genuine local favorites.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: What if the restaurant the AI recommended is actually closed when I arrive?**
  - A: AI training data and even real-time searches might not capture sudden closures with 100% accuracy. For a foolproof plan, we highly recommend doing a quick final check of the curated restaurant names on Google Maps or Apple Maps to confirm they are "Currently Open."

- **Q: Can I use this prompt when traveling abroad?**
  - A: Absolutely! Simply swap the variables to something like `[Shibuya, Tokyo]` and `[Yakitori]`. However, to get the most accurate local recommendations, add a constraint instructing the AI to base its search on the dominant regional review platform—such as "Tabelog" for Japan or "Yelp" for the US.

---

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Cross-Platform Verification:** By forcing the AI to meet high rating standards across multiple platforms (like Google Maps and KakaoMap) simultaneously, it perfectly filters out establishments that manipulate their ratings on a single site.
2. **Explicit Ad Filtering:** Instructing the AI to exclude specific disclosure keywords like "sponsored" or "compensated" proactively prevents it from regurgitating spammy, paid review data.
3. **Practical Metadata Requirements:** The prompt maximizes real-world utility by forcing the AI to output crucial logistical info—like "Parking Availability" and "No Kids Zone" policies—which are just as important to a traveler as the quality of the food.
