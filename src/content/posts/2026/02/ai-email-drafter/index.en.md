## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

This is a prompt completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[variables]` in brackets according to your situation to deploy it immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

A lightweight prompt perfect for immediate use when writing quick replies for simple content or routine informational emails.

> **Role:** You are a competent business secretary.
>
> **Request:** Write a polite email to Assistant Manager Kim at our client company proposing to postpone this Friday's meeting to next Monday at 14:00. The reason is a change in the internal executive schedule.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when reporting to important clients or executives, or when you need to exercise perfect writing skills in situations requiring high-level communication skills, such as price increases, schedule delays, or payment demands.

> **Role:** You are a veteran B2B communicator with 10 years of experience and a professional secretary.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Recipient: `[Manager Park at Client Company]`
> - Sender: `[Me (Assistant Manager Kim)]`
> - Purpose: `[Sending quote and notice of review deadline]`
> - Key Content:
>   1. `[Attached is the requested quote for the 2026 new project]`
>   2. `[Considering our long-term partnership, a special 5% additional discount is applied to this case only]`
>   3. `[Request feedback and confirmation by next Wednesday (2/18) at 18:00]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Write in accordance with perfect business email etiquette, considering the difference in rank between the recipient and the sender.
> 2. Maintain a 'polite and professional' tone and manner that is not too stiff but inspires confidence.
> 3. Suggest 3 versions of the email title (Title) that are clear and savvy enough to make the recipient want to click, ensuring they don't miss the email.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Organize logically with a greeting, introduction, body, conclusion, and closing.
> - For parts requiring fact-checking like dates, amounts, and names, use `[ ]` brackets so I can edit them directly.
> - Do not overuse unnecessary flowery language; write with high readability so the core content can be grasped at a glance.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never make up additional conditions or situations that are not certain. (Hallucination prevention)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight)

The true value of this prompt shines overwhelmingly in moments when you need to engage in **'uncomfortable communication,'** rather than just simple routine emails. We often lose our composure in business situations where emotions are easily mixed, such as demanding payment, politely refusing an unreasonable request, or defending against service complaints. Unintentionally, our tone can become aggressive, making the text thorny, or conversely, we may fail to deliver the main point (Due Date, required documents, etc.) because we are overly concerned about the other party's feelings. The human brain is severely limited in producing rational and calm text in stressful situations.

AI, however, is not swayed by emotions. It produces only perfectly dry and professional results within strictly controlled **Constraints**. For example, even if raw emotions like "Seriously, when are you going to pay the overdue amount? You're not even picking up the phone" are boiling in your head, when you convey this to the AI, it elegantly translates it into perfect business language that is dry yet polite, hitting the core: "Please check the payment schedule for last month's settlement once more. A quick reply is needed due to the internal accounting closing schedule."

The most noteworthy part of the Pro Version prompt is **Variable Control (`[ ]`)**. Many people explain the situation at length in prose when asking AI to write an email. But if you do that, AI often makes up irrelevant content (Hallucination) or misses the points you want to emphasize. Structuring the **Context** and **Key Content** strictly in a list format, as in the prompt above, and swapping variables as if filling in blanks makes the **probability of malfunction close to 0%**. The core principle of this prompt is to modularize repetitive and consumptive tasks to build a perfect template that can respond immediately to any situation by replacing just a few words.

Furthermore, the instruction in the **Constraints** to `use [ ] brackets for parts requiring fact-checking like dates, amounts, and names` is a powerful cheat code to prevent human error. It prevents the disaster of copying and sending fake dates or amounts that the AI plausibly made up to a client. After copying the generated draft, you just need to search for the `[` symbol in your editor and fill in the blanks. This significantly reduces the chance of making mistakes, allowing you to take advantage of both the convenience of text generation by AI and the safety of fact-checking by a human. The time spent staring wide-eyed at numbers, worrying if they are wrong just before sending an email, will disappear forever.

Finally, don't forget to specify the **rank difference between the recipient and the sender**. Unlike English, Korean business emails require an overwhelmingly high level of 'Context' in relationships. A collaboration email sent to an "intern in another department" must be completely different from a contract extension email sent to the "CEO of an external partner of 10 years" in terms of vocabulary choice, sentence length, and the temperature of the closing remarks. Just by entering this one variable correctly, the AI pulls out a perfect tone and manner like a quick-witted ace assistant manager. Because it judges and adjusts the level of the email and the degree of politeness for each situation itself, the user doesn't need to engage in unnecessary agonizing. In any crisis, this prompt will be a steady and powerful shield and spear. ## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can English business emails be written with the same prompt?**
  - A: Absolutely. Just add a single line to the "Task" item: `Use business English for the output language and include natural idioms often used by native speakers.` Beyond simple machine translation, you can get sophisticated, native-level results that are ready to be deployed in a global business environment.

- **Q: How should I modify it when sending to internal team members or a boss instead of an external client?**
  - A: Specifically state the relationship between the recipient and the sender as `[Team Leader]` and `[Team Member]` in the "Context" section. Then, fine-tune the tone and manner of the "Task" to something like `concise with key points in a report style` or `soft but polite` to complete an email perfectly targeted for internal communication.

- **Q: I'm worried it might look too much like it was written by AI.**
  - A: Because we placed a strong constraint to 'exclude unnecessary flowery language' within the prompt, ChatGPT's characteristic translation-ese or exaggerated modifiers are drastically reduced. If you take the draft generated by AI and slightly add the friendly greetings or unique closing remarks you usually use, it becomes completely indistinguishable from something written directly by a human.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1.  **Role (Power of Role Assignment):** By giving the AI a strong persona as a '10-year veteran B2B communicator,' we forced it to use high-level vocabulary and highly refined expressions optimized for a business environment instead of general conversational style.
2.  **Context (Specification of Situation and Relationship):** By clearly setting the relationship (rank) between the receiver and the sender, we cleanly solved the problem of honorifics and titles, which is the most difficult and sensitive part of Korean business communication.
3.  **Constraints (Prevention of Human Error through Blank Templating):** We instructed it to enclose proper nouns, dates, amounts, etc., that must change every time, in `[ ]` symbols. This fundamentally blocks critical mistakes where a professional might omit key information or send incorrect content during the final fact-checking process.
