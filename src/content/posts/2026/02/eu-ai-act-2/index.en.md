## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was completed after dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompts below and fill in the `[Variables]` in the brackets according to your project's situation to put them into practice immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this lightly when you want to quickly identify overall potential regulatory violations of your project.

> **Role:** You are a global IT compliance expert well-versed in the EU AI Act.
> 
> **Request:** Read the following `[Generative Video Project Overview]` and summarize three items that potentially violate regulations based on the EU AI Act 2 standards effective in 2026, along with corresponding response measures.
>
> **Generative Video Project Overview:**
> `[Enter project content or service plan briefly here]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need a detailed risk analysis report suitable for reporting to the legal team or issuing work instructions to the development/design teams before an actual service launch.

> **Role:** You are a 'Senior AI Compliance Officer' who in-depthly analyzes the European Union's latest AI regulation (EU AI Act 2) and preemptively defends against corporate legal risks.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: Our company plans to launch a new Generative Video service targeting the global market.
> - Goal: To seamlessly identify potential legal risks (transparency obligations, high-risk classification, etc.) latent in the project plan and establish defense strategies immediately applicable to practice.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Based on the provided `[Service Plan]`, strictly review compliance with the core provisions of the EU AI Act 2.
> 2. Clearly classify risk levels as 'High / Medium / Low' and provide specific solutions (Action Items) for each risk.
> 3. Conduct an in-depth analysis focusing particularly on watermark display obligations, deepfake disclosure obligations, and whether biometric information is processed.
>
> **Input Data:**
>
> - Service Plan: `[Enter detailed service plan, data collection methods, output formats, etc. specifically here]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - For mobile readability, never use tables; instead, write in a highly readable bulleted list format, clearly distinguishing Risk Factors, Potentially Violating Clauses, Specific Issues, and Action Items.
> - Ensure important keywords are **bolded**.
> - Clearly state at the bottom of the output that this analysis does not replace legal advice (Disclaimer).
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Never make up legal clauses or precedents that are not certain. For ambiguous parts or those requiring verification, clearly state "Additional legal review required." (To prevent hallucinations)

## 💡 Author's Commentary (Insight & How to Use)

The true value of this prompt goes beyond mechanically summarizing complex legal clauses; it lies in the ability to **directly apply the specific and raw context of 'our actual service plan' to the strictest AI regulatory network for a stress test.**

The reason many companies struggle with regulatory response is the deep gap between law and technology. Developers and planners do not understand legal terminology, and lawyers do not accurately grasp API call methods or the structure of data pipelines. This is where bottlenecks occur, and time and costs are wasted exponentially.

This prompt is an excellent translator and primary filtering tool that fills that gap. Especially in the Pro version, it is designed to sharply point out elements that practitioners might inadvertently introduce to improve user experience (UX) but could become lethal poison. By forcing analysis on **'transparency obligations (watermarking methods, deepfake pre-notification timing, etc.)'** and **'biometric information processing (facial recognition, emotion analysis, etc.)'**—the most sensitive areas in generative video services—it ensures the AI model hits the core risks rather than just skirting around them.

When using this prompt in practice, **the most important tip is how to write the `[Service Plan]` variable.** If you simply write "AI video editing app," the AI can only give a predictable answer.
You must describe the data flow and UI/UX policies as specifically as possible, including **1) what data is input (e.g., user selfie photos, voice data), 2) what model it passes through internally (e.g., a custom fine-tuned model based on Stable Video Diffusion), and 3) what form the final output is provided to the user (e.g., a 15-second short-form video in MP4 format, no on-screen watermark).**

If 'High Risk' is derived from the analysis, do not panic; instead, engage in further conversation with the AI using that result. If you follow up with a question like, *"Suggest 3 UI design alternatives that satisfy the mandatory watermark application you pointed out without significantly harming the user experience (UX),"* you can find amazing compromises that achieve both regulatory compliance and business goals.

Just executing this prompt once when the draft plan is completed can **drastically save the massive time and communication costs** that would otherwise be spent ping-ponging emails with the internal legal team or external law firms. When attending a legal team meeting, there is a world of difference between going empty-handed and asking, "Can we do this?" versus bringing a primary risk analysis report derived from this prompt and saying, "We've added a watermark process to the UI because these regulatory issues are expected; please review if this will be a sufficient legal defense logic."

Furthermore, this prompt is not only useful during the planning stage of new services. Get into the habit of running this prompt whenever you update an existing service with new features, just as you would perform a regression test. You can use it like, "This is the plan for the 'User voice-based avatar lip-sync' feature being added in this sprint. In addition to previous analyses, please further review if this feature violates the AI Act 2."

The legal environment in the AI era is changing as fast as code updates. Perfectly safe services do not exist; only flexible organizations that constantly monitor risks and respond quickly survive. Do not view 'regulation' merely as an obstacle holding back business. Instead, by embedding a preemptive and thorough compliance response structure into your system, use it as the most powerful weapon to prove your service's **'overwhelming legal stability and reliability'** to investors and the global market. This prompt will be your best partner in taking that bold first step.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Assigning a Senior AI Compliance Officer Persona:** To prevent the AI from acting as a simple information-retrieval chatbot, I completely replaced its **Lens** to evaluate risks from the perspective of a strictly conservative and defensive legal expert. This leads to much sharper and more practical points.
2. **Forced Targeting of Core Regulatory Clauses:** I clearly pointed out the **'Achilles' heel'** in the generative video field, such as watermarking and deepfake disclosure obligations, which have the highest risk of fines, and instructed them within the prompt. This maximized the resolution and accuracy of the analysis.
3. **Hallucination Prevention Mechanism:** By setting a strong constraint (Warning) to "never make up legal clauses that are not certain," I fundamentally blocked the generation of incorrect legal information that could be a fatal blow to corporate decision-making.
