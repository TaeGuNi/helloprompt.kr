## 🚀 How Real Experts Write It

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below, fill in the `[Variables]` in the brackets according to your situation, and deploy it immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version (Quick Leftover Cooking)

Useful when hunger hits and you need a healthy, quick meal immediately.

> **Role:** You are a diet cooking expert for solo households.
> 
> **Task:** I have `[half a block of tofu, 2 eggs, a handful of cabbage, Sriracha sauce]` in my fridge. Recommend exactly ONE delicious diet recipe under 400kcal that can be finished in 15 minutes using these ingredients. Organize the cooking steps concisely.

### 🥇 Pro Version (Customized Weekly Meal Management)

Use this for systematic weight loss results through strict nutritional balance and structured meal planning, going beyond a single meal.

> **Role:** You are a professional dietitian with 10 years of experience and a 1:1 personal trainer. You excel at achieving optimal nutritional balance with limited ingredients and constructing realistic yet delicious diet meal plans.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - **Goal:** `[Body Fat Loss]` (Target Calories: `[1500]` kcal per day)
> - **Current Ingredients:** `[2 packs of chicken breast, 1 bag of enoki mushrooms, 2 onions, 5 eggs, Sriracha sauce, oyster sauce, a bit of oatmeal]`
> - **Preferences & Constraints:** `[Prefer spicy flavors, want a very quick breakfast with no cooking, lactose intolerant]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Plan a **3-day diet meal schedule (Breakfast/Lunch/Dinner)** utilizing the provided ingredients as much as possible.
> 2. Clearly state the estimated calories and **Carb/Protein/Fat ratios** for each meal.
> 3. Recommend **3 budget-friendly ingredients** that need to be purchased to perfect the nutritional balance, and explain the logic behind the recommendation.
> 4. For lunch and dinner, provide intuitive and simple recipes that even a beginner can finish within 15 minutes.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Do NOT use tables; organize in high-readability **bullet points (List)** for mobile reading.
> - **Bold** important keywords.
> - Strictly exclude exotic spices or expensive ingredients that are hard to find (e.g., truffle oil, saffron, etc.).
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not make up information; answer "I don't know" if unsure. (Hallucination prevention)
> - Calculate calories and nutrient ratios as precisely as possible, but mark them as estimates. Strictly forbid suggesting extreme low-calorie starvation diets. (Must meet minimum BMR)

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: The AI's recommended ingredient combination is too weird. (e.g., Apple Chicken Breast Kimchi Stir-fry)**
  - A: Sometimes the AI gets too obsessed with the mission to use every single leftover and creates what we call "cursed food." If this happens, add a constraint like `[No forced mixing of incompatible ingredients]` or give intuitive feedback like "This combo seems weird. Take out the kimchi and redo the recipe with other ingredients." It will immediately correct it to a more appetizing and harmonious recipe.

- **Q: What if I have an unavoidable company dinner or social outing while dieting?**
  - A: Share the planned outing menu with the AI without hesitation. Ask specifically: "I have to eat a spicy pork set menu for lunch today. To stay within my 1500kcal goal, how much rice should I leave out, and how should I defend my diet with my leftover ingredients for dinner?" It will design a perfect mental care and customized defense plan considering your entire day's nutrition and calories.

- **Q: Are the calories and nutrient ratios calculated by the AI 100% accurate?**
  - A: AI calorie calculations are 'sophisticated estimates' based on vast ingredient databases. It cannot perfectly capture errors at the 1g or 1kcal level. However, the key to a successful diet isn't obsessive number crunching, but rather maintaining a macro-level **'Carb-Protein-Fat balance'** and controlling the **'flow of intake'** consistently. It is more than sufficient for use as a practical diet guideline.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Micro-details in Context:** It goes beyond just giving a target weight, setting tight details like preferred taste (spicy), allergies to avoid (lactose intolerance), and even cooking time limits (easy breakfast). These details push the **real-world applicability** to the limit.
2. **Complementary Feedback Loop:** It preemptively asks for "3 ingredients that need to be purchased." This smartly offsets the risk of 'nutritional imbalance' that can occur when focusing only on clearing leftovers, providing the effect of a professional dietitian's close consulting.
3. **Sharpness of Constraints:** It blocks out expensive or hard-to-find ingredients at the source. By forcing the output format into a list, it prevents readability issues on mobile. These constraints perfectly shut down the "unrealistic reference diet" errors AI often makes, inducing only immediately executable, practical answers.
