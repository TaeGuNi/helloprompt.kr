## 🚀 How the Pros Do It

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to politely request a task or nudge a schedule without offending the other party.

> **Role:** You are a business communication expert working at a US Fortune 500 company.
>
> **Task:**
> Please revise the sentence below into the **most polite and professional** expression so it can be sent to a boss or an important client. Suggest 3 different versions so I can compare the nuances.
>
> **Context:** `[Give me the report by tomorrow.]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need subtle emotional control and high-level business strategy, such as for rejections, apologies, or price negotiations.

> **Role:** You are a global business negotiation expert and a communication consultant with a Harvard MBA.
>
> **Context:**
> - Background: `[Discussing a contract with an overseas client. Their proposal is good, but a price reduction is needed due to budget issues.]`
> - Goal: `[Maintain a friendly relationship while persuading them to cut the price by 10%.]`
> - My Draft: `[Your proposal is good. But price is expensive. Discount 10% please.]`
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Completely rewrite my draft into a very natural and sophisticated business email that a native speaker would read.
> 2. Provide 3 options for the Subject line along with the Body of the email.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Tone: Gentle but Firm. Be careful not to sound rude.
> - Structure: First, praise the excellence of their proposal (Sandwich Technique), then politely lead to a price reduction by citing budget constraints as a valid reason.
> - Vocabulary: Actively use high-end idioms and 'cushioning' expressions that native speakers frequently use in business environments.

## 💡 Author's Insight

This prompt is my own **"secret weapon"** that I have used most frequently and powerfully in my own work when collaborating with global teams. Beyond simple one-dimensional translation, the true value of AI is 100% realized in awkward situations, such as polite apologies for delays or firm rejections of unreasonable demands. When you need to respect the other party's feelings while clearly carrying out your core intent and business purpose, this prompt becomes a perfect strategic shield and a sharp spear.

In particular, the **'Sandwich Technique (Positive-Negative-Positive)'** explicitly applied in the Pro Version is a high-level communication strategy that native speakers use daily in actual global business negotiations and feedback. This technique, which cleverly taps into human psychology, delivers the bitter 'negative' message of a rejection or a demand between two pieces of soft, sweet 'positive' bread—such as expressions of praise or gratitude. The moment you explicitly request this structure from the AI, a blunt and stiff draft like "Discount please" is instantly transformed into the language of an elegant and persuasive elite negotiator.

Furthermore, to use this prompt more flexibly in practice, it is important to develop a sense of **Constraint Control**. If the output generated by the AI feels too authoritative or overly heavy and stiff compared to your usual speaking style, try adding specific instructions to the constraints at the bottom, such as: _"Considering that we have already exchanged emails several times, please refine it into a slightly more warm and casual tone."_ Conversely, when you need to draw a firm line because the other party continues to make unreasonable demands, you can adjust it by saying: _"Since legal liability must be clarified, please lower the tone to be strictly factual and formal to avoid any room for misunderstanding."_

AI spits out exponentially better results the more specific the details of the **'Context'** you provide. Instead of just saying "Cut the price," try adding background details like: "Because our department's budget was cut by 15% this year, we absolutely need a 10% price reduction from your side to get this project approved." Based on this context, the AI will weave a perfect business excuse into an elegant English sentence. Go beyond the limits of a translator, keep your own Harvard-educated communication secretary by your side, and seize the initiative in business communication.

Additionally, if you feel the output is too long and lacks readability, try adding a command to the constraints: _"Summarize the entire email into 3 paragraphs or less so it can be read at a glance on mobile devices, and organize the core requests into bullet points to make them stand out."_ A clean and organized format that saves the time of a busy global partner will drastically reduce the probability of your email being 'ghosted' and will be the magic key to eliciting a swift reply. As these experiences of fine-tuning accumulate, you will evolve beyond a simple AI user into a true prompt engineer and an outstanding global business communicator.
