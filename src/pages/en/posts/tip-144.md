### ‚úÖ After (Prompt Result: Restaurant Analogy)

**[Situation Analogy]**
API is a **'Restaurant Waiter (Server)'**.
Customers (You) don't go directly to the chef (Server) and shout "Give me pasta", right?
Instead, you order looking at the menu (Document) through the waiter.
Then the waiter takes the order sheet to the kitchen, receives the dish, and brings it to you.
You don't need to know whether there is a fire show or chopping in the kitchen. You just need to call the waiter.

**[1:1 Matching]**
| Restaurant Situation | IT Technology |

| **Waiter** | **API** (Intermediary) |
| **Chef** | Server (Database) |
| **Menu** | API Document (Manual) |
| **Dish** | Data (Response) |

**[One Sentence Summary]**
"A smart clerk who runs errands between two programs that don't speak the same language."
