## üöÄ The Solution: "Brand Naming Lab"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you just need quick, raw inspiration to get the gears turning.

> **Role:** You are a top-tier brand naming expert who has created innovative names like 'Uber', 'Airbnb', and 'Tinder'.
> **Request:** I am launching a new `[Insert Service Type]`. Generate 10 catchy, meaningful, and easy-to-pronounce brand name ideas.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version (Expert)

Use this when you need high-quality, deeply conceptualized names with strategic reasoning.

> **Role (Role):** You are a world-class brand naming expert and creative director behind household names like 'Uber', 'Airbnb', and 'Stripe'.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: We are launching a new service and need a compelling brand name that instantly sticks in people's minds.
> - Main Function: `[e.g., An app that recommends recipes based on leftover ingredients in your fridge]`
> - Core Value: `[e.g., Cost-saving, Convenience, Reducing food waste]`
> - Target Audience: `[e.g., Single-person households, College students, Cooking beginners]`
>
> **Request (Task):**
>
> 1. Suggest a total of **10 highly curated name candidates** utilizing the 3 naming techniques below.
>    - **Intuitive Naming:** Names that immediately reveal the function (e.g., DoorDash, PayPal).
>    - **Compound Word Naming:** Names that seamlessly combine two relevant words (e.g., Netflix, YouTube).
>    - **Symbolic/Abstract Naming:** Names with a unique feel or backstory (e.g., Apple, Amazon).
> 2. For each name, write a one-line persuasive pitch explaining **"Why this name works psychologically and strategically."**
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - The names must be easy to pronounce and relatively short (preferably 2 to 3 syllables).
> - Provide options that sound natural in English and are memorable globally.
> - Do not generate generic or overly literal names (e.g., "FridgeRecipeApp").

## üí° Writer's Insight

Generating a good name is a numbers game, but it's also about feeling. When you run this prompt, don't just look for a "perfect" name right away. Look for interesting root words or prefixes that the AI suggests, and then combine them yourself or ask the AI to iterate on that specific root. Also, once you find a name you love, **always run a quick trademark search and check domain availability (.com)** before you get too attached!

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work better on GPT-4 compared to the free version?**
  - A: Yes. While free models can give you decent compound words, advanced models like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 are vastly superior at creating subtle, symbolic, and abstract names that don't sound generic.

- **Q: Can the AI check if the .com domain is available?**
  - A: No, AI models cannot accurately check real-time domain availability. You will need to take the generated list and plug it into a domain registrar like Namecheap or GoDaddy to verify.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Playing:** By assigning the persona of a "world-class creative director," the AI shifts from generating literal descriptions to creating brandable, marketable identities.
2. **Categorized Framework:** Forcing the AI to use specific techniques (Intuitive, Compound, Symbolic) prevents it from getting stuck in a rut of producing similar-sounding ideas.
3. **Strategic Justification:** Asking _why_ the name works ensures the AI evaluates the psychological impact of the word, rather than just spitting out random syllables.
