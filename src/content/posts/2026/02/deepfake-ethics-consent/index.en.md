### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need sophisticated practical guidelines that go beyond simple declarations and cover the organization's practical response manual and technical/legal defense systems.

> **Role:** You are the AI Ethics Officer and Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: We are in a state of 'technological gap' where damage from non-consensual deepfake pornography and fake news exploiting generative AI is skyrocketing.
> - Goal: To build strong ethics guidelines and a crisis response manual that can fundamentally block deepfake misuse within `[Organization/Service Name]` and respond immediately when problems occur.
>
> **Task:**
>
> Please write a comprehensive guideline including the following items:
>
> 1. Core Principles: Clearly state principles of content generation based on 'consent' and protection of human dignity.
> 2. Technical Response: Propose technical control measures such as watermarking, metadata insertion, and introduction of detection algorithms.
> 3. Sanctions and Legal Action: Procedures for applying the zero-tolerance principle and disciplinary/reporting actions in case of regulation violation (creation and distribution).
> 4. Victim Relief Protocol: A 3-step crisis response scenario leading from report reception to content blinding and connection to legal/psychological support.
> 5. Keep the `[Organization/Service Name]` part in the form of brackets (`[ ]`) so that users can enter their own company name directly.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Organize the output structure as a highly readable manual using Markdown headings (`###`) and bullet points (`-`).
> - Strictly exclude academic paper styles and maintain a firm and clear policy tone (Corporate Policy Tone) that working-level staff can declare as internal regulations right away.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Write in accordance with universal human rights and global ethical standards that do not conflict with current laws in actual countries. Do not explain specific national laws definitively. (Prevention of hallucination)

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

When facing deepfake-related issues in practice, the point I feel most poignantly is that a **fast and firm internal process** is much more important than perfect detection technology. No matter how excellent an AI detection algorithm is introduced, new bypass technologies are bound to appear constantly. When the technical defense line is breached, the only weapon that can minimize damage and control the situation is a pre-prepared **clear guideline and response manual.** This prompt puts the most powerful weapon in your hands to save that 'Golden Time.'

A common mistake many companies make when establishing an AI code of ethics is that they only stay at pleasant, abstract declarations. Vague phrases such as 'We comply with AI ethics' or 'We respect the rights of users' do not become any guidance when an actual crisis occurs. The point where this prompt differentiates itself is that it replaces abstract moral norms with specific and firm **'Actionable Protocols'** that work immediately in practice. The **'Victim Relief Protocol (Report-Delete-Support)'** requirement specified in the prompt induces the construction of a powerful system that covers everything from simple sanctions to follow-up relief without any gaps.

In particular, please pay attention to the Constraints section of the prompt. Here, we forced the maintenance of a **firm and clear policy tone (Corporate Policy Tone).** This is a very important variable control strategy. Because AI models basically prefer a kind and explanatory tone, asking them to write a guideline might result in an academic or apologetic nuance. However, internal regulations or terms of use must be written in a firm style without a hint of ambiguity. The output generated through this constraint has the highest level of practicality that can be immediately announced on the internal bulletin board or reflected in the terms of use without additional tone and manner modifications.

Also, when applying it to practice, you can vary the prompt in various ways to suit the characteristics of the organization in addition to the `[Organization/Service Name]` variable. For example, if the target users are primarily teenagers, try adding the background 'A platform that requires special attention to the protection of minor users' to the **Context** section. The model will propose more powerful and delicate response measures equivalent to child and youth protection laws. Or if it is a global service, you can preemptively defend against compliance risks by adding specific instructions in the **Warning** to reflect the regulatory trends of specific countries (e.g., EU's GDPR, USA's FOSTA-SESTA, etc.).

Even if you lack the budget or development personnel to link a perfect deepfake detection solution to your service right now, there is no need to be frustrated. Just by strongly declaring the **'Zero-Tolerance principle for the creation and distribution of non-consensual content'** internally and externally at the organizational level and stipulating strict sanctions for violations, you can exert enormous psychological deterrence on potential abusers. Even if the system is not perfect, if the will is firm, the defense line is built. This prompt will be the best partner to complete that firm will into the fastest and most professional form of text. Put it into practice today.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can the guidelines written by AI have actual legal effect?**
  - A: They can exert sufficient effect as internal regulations and terms of use, and become a strong defense mechanism proving that the company 'fulfilled its duty to protect users' in case of future disputes. However, it is recommended to go through a review by an internal legal team or a professional lawyer before external distribution or official regulation.

- **Q: Can't I perform deepfake video detection itself with an LLM prompt?**
  - A: There are clear limits to determining the authenticity of a video with only a text-based Large Language Model (LLM) prompt. For practical blocking, it is much more accurate and safe to link a dedicated detection AI pipeline (e.g., FakeCatcher, etc.) that captures the fine distortions of the video's frequency and pixels.
