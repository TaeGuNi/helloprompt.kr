> _"I'm not really sure what Palantir even does, and the P/E ratio is over 100? Isn't this a bubble?"_

Palantir is one of the most **controversial stocks** in the US market. This company, which supplies data analysis platforms to the CIA and the US Department of Defense, has seen its stock price soar as commercial revenue grew explosively following the launch of its AI Platform (AIP) in 2024. The problem is the extreme valuation, with a Forward P/E ratio **exceeding 100x**.

The individual investor's dilemma is clear: "What Palantir does seems amazing, but <b>isn't a P/E of 100x too expensive?</b>" To answer this, one must comprehensively analyze why Palantir's business model shouldn't be evaluated with traditional SaaS P/E ratios, what position AIP holds in the enterprise AI market, and whether the <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">long-term stability of government contracts</span> justifies the premium.

This prompt separates Palantir into "Government" and "Commercial" segments and independently evaluates their respective growth trajectories, margin structures, and TAM. In particular, it tracks the **high expansion revenue (Net Dollar Retention)** generated by AIP's "bootcamp" sales model as a key variable.

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **Evaluate with Rule of 40:** For high-growth SaaS, it is more reasonable to use the Rule of 40—the sum of "Revenue Growth Rate + FCF Margin"—rather than P/E.
2. **AIP Bootcamp Effect:** A 72% conversion rate and 138% NDR are top-tier levels within the SaaS industry.
3. **Valuation Risk:** The current price assumes "perfect execution," so volatility following earnings surprises or misses is extreme.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The core design of this prompt is the **reframing toward the "Rule of 40."** While the number 100x P/E is shocking, adding a 33% revenue growth rate to a 28% FCF margin results in a Rule of 40 score of **61**. In the SaaS industry, a score over 40 is classified as "healthy growth," and over 50 as a "top-tier company." By shifting the frame this way, the <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">perspective completely changes</span> from "a 100x P/E is expensive" to "a Rule of 40 score of 61 can justify the premium."

However, the critical question here is: **"Can this growth be sustained?"** While AIP bootcamp's 72% conversion rate and 138% NDR are amazing figures, it's necessary to distinguish whether this is a result of market expansion (TAM growth) or a one-time initial adoption demand. If you ask the AI about "TAM penetration rate and growth sustainability," you can get structural answers to this core question.
