## üí° Writer's Insight

Getting the AI to pull the camera back is notoriously difficult. Image generation models have an inherent bias toward close-ups and eye-level portraits because that comprises the vast majority of their training data.

To break this habit, you cannot just type "high angle" once. You must **stack your camera keywords**. Combining terms like `aerial drone shot`, `bird's eye view`, and `looking down` creates a compound effect that forces the AI's "virtual camera" into the sky. Furthermore, specifying lens types like `14mm wide-angle lens` or `panoramic` helps stretch the canvas to encompass the massive scale you are aiming for.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does this work the same way in DALL-E 3 as it does in Midjourney?**
  - A: Yes, but DALL-E 3 is much more obedient to conversational instructions. You can simply command, "Draw this from a drone's perspective looking straight down," whereas Midjourney requires heavy, comma-separated keyword stacking (e.g., `drone view, top-down, aerial`).

- **Q: How do I stop the AI from blurring the edges of the city?**
  - A: AI often applies a shallow depth of field (bokeh) to scenes shot from above, resulting in a miniature "tilt-shift" effect. To prevent this and keep the entire city crisp, add keywords like `deep depth of field`, `f/16 aperture`, and `infinite focus`.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

3.  **Role Play:** Assigning the "drone cinematographer" persona aligns the AI's latent space with professional aerial photography aesthetics.
