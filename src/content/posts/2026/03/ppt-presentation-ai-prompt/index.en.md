## 📝 AI-Powered PPT: AI Handles Everything from Storyline to Slide Structure

- **🎯 Recommended for:** Office workers who frequently write reports/proposals, college students presenting team projects
- **⏱️ Time Required:** 4 hours of agonizing over PPT structure → 5 minutes with AI storyline
- **🤖 Top Performance:** GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 2.5 Pro

- ⭐ **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆
- ⚡️ **Effectiveness:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 🚀 **Utility:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

> _"I have an executive report tomorrow and need to fill 30 slides. I have the content in my head, but I'm stuck staring at blank slides, not knowing how to organize them."_

The real problem with PPT presentations isn't the **design**. Design can be solved with templates. The real issue is the **storyline (logical structure)**—the question of <b>"In what order should I speak to persuade the audience?"</b> A good presentation flows naturally through "Status → Problem → Cause → Solution → Expected Effects," and establishing this structure usually consumes about 60% of the total work time.

By providing the AI with the purpose, audience, and key message of the presentation, a structure consisting of <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">Title-Key Message-Supporting Data</span> for each slide can be generated in just 5 minutes. You can even receive the presentation script (speaker notes) for each slide.

## ⚡️ 3-Line Summary (TL;DR)

1. **3-Act Structure (Why-What-So What):** By applying Hollywood screenwriting techniques to your PPT, the audience naturally follows the flow of "Why is it needed? → What are we doing? → What are the effects?"
2. **Slide-by-Slide Structure:** Receive specific content for each slide, including titles, key messages, supporting data, and speaker notes.
3. **Preparing for Counter-Questions:** Prepare for anticipated questions and model answers that executives might ask, ensuring you remain composed during the Q&A session.

## 🚀 This is How True Experts Write

### 🥉 Basic Version

> **Role:** You are a `[Presentation Expert]`.
>
> **Request:** Create a slide structure for a `[SaaS Work Automation Implementation Proposal]` PPT. Organize it into 15 slides.

### 🥇 Pro Version

> **Role:** You are a `[McKinsey-style Strategy Consultant]`. You have experience with over 500 executive presentations and are an expert in storyline design based on the MECE principle and the Pyramid Principle.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Presentation Purpose: `[Approval for SaaS Work Automation Platform Implementation]`
> - Audience: `[3 C-Level Executives, 20-minute presentation + 10-minute Q&A]`
> - Key Message: `[160 hours/month of manual work → 320 million KRW/year savings via automation]`
> - Current Data: `[Survey results of repetitive tasks by team, comparison table of 3 solutions, ROI estimation]`
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Storyline:** Create titles and key messages for 15 slides using a 3-act structure (Why-What-So What).
> 2. **Slide Details:** For each slide, write (a) Title, (b) 1-line key message, (c) Recommended visualization (Chart/Table/Icon), and (d) Speaker notes (30-second script).
> 3. **Q&A Defense:** Prepare 5 anticipated questions executives might ask along with model answers.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Only one key message per slide. Strictly adhere to "One slide, one story."
> - Limit speaker notes to an amount that can be spoken within 30 seconds.
> - Utilize numbers and data as much as possible, and avoid abstract expressions (e.g., "many," "significant").

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to use)

The core of this prompt is the **"Audience."** A PPT presented to C-level executives is completely different from one shared with teammates. Executives are only interested in "Why we should do it" and "How much it saves," so technical details should be moved to the appendix. If you input the `[Audience]` information accurately, the AI will automatically design a <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">structure tailored to that audience's interests</span>.

Real-world tip: The key is limiting the speaker notes to 30 seconds. 15 slides × 30 seconds = 7 minutes 30 seconds. Combined with the Q&A, it fits perfectly into a 20-minute presentation. Exceeding the allotted time is the biggest mistake in executive presentations.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Does the AI create the actual PPT file directly?**
  - A: This prompt is for creating the **content structure (storyline)**. To create the actual PPT file, you can input this structure into tools like Gamma.app, Tome, or PowerPoint Copilot for automatic generation.

- **Q: Can this be used for proposals or reports as well?**
  - A: Yes. By changing the `[Presentation Purpose]` to "Writing a proposal" and requesting a "Chapter structure" instead of slides, you can achieve the same effect.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Persuasiveness of the 3-Act Structure:** The flow of Why (Problem) → What (Solution) → So What (Expected Effects) aligns with **human cognitive processing**. By first creating curiosity with "Why?" and then presenting the answer, persuasiveness is maximized.
2. **Application of the MECE Principle:** "One slide, one story" is an application of MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) in presentations. It ensures messages are delivered without overlap or omission.
