## üöÄ The Solution: "Bulletproof Wedding Budget Planner"

### ü•â Basic Version

Use this when you need a quick, high-level budget breakdown to start your planning.

> **Role:** You are an expert financial advisor and veteran wedding planner.
> **Task:** Create a detailed wedding budget checklist and financial breakdown for a total budget of `[$X,XXX]`. Be sure to include a list of commonly forgotten hidden costs.

<br>

### ü•á Pro Version

Use this for a meticulous, customized, and highly detailed financial defense strategy against aggressive wedding upselling.

> **Role:** You are an elite wedding planner and a strict financial advisor. Your primary goal is to protect the couple from upselling, hidden fees, and unnecessary expenses while ensuring they have a beautiful, memorable wedding.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Current Situation: We are planning our wedding and have a strict total budget of `[Total Budget, e.g., $30,000]`. Our expected guest count is approximately `[Guest Count, e.g., 100]`.
> - Priorities: We care most about `[Top Priority 1, e.g., Food and Catering]` and `[Top Priority 2, e.g., Photography]`, and we are completely willing to compromise on `[Low Priority, e.g., Floral Arrangements]`.
> - Target Goal: A comprehensive, realistic budget allocation that includes a buffer for unexpected costs, plus a defensive checklist of questions to ask vendors to avoid hidden fees.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. **Budget Breakdown:** Allocate the `[Total Budget]` across all major wedding categories (Venue, Catering, Attire, Photography/Videography, Decor, Entertainment, etc.) using percentages and exact dollar amounts based on our priorities.
> 2. **The "Hidden Cost" Warning List:** Identify at least 10 common hidden costs or upselling tactics specific to our priorities, budget, and guest count.
> 3. **Vendor Negotiation Guide:** Provide 3-5 specific, hard-hitting questions we must ask vendors before signing contracts to prevent surprise charges (e.g., cake cutting fees, corkage, overtime rates, taxes, and mandatory gratuities).
> 4. **Cost-Cutting Alternatives:** Suggest 3 realistic, creative ways to save money on our `[Low Priority]` category without making the event look cheap.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Output the budget breakdown as a well-structured markdown list.
> - Ensure the tone is practical, protective, and highly realistic. Do not sugarcoat the costs of the modern wedding industry.
> - Set aside a mandatory 10-15% contingency fund before allocating the rest of the budget.
>
> **Warning:**
>
> - Do not include unrealistic cost estimates based on outdated data. If our budget is objectively too small for a specific request, clearly state the reality and provide alternative solutions.

## üí° Writer's Insight

The wedding industry is notorious for the "wedding tax" and aggressive upselling tactics. Couples often start with a simple spreadsheet, only to realize months later that they completely forgot to budget for vendor meals, postage for heavy invitations, dress alterations, or "++" (plus plus) service charges that add over 20% to the catering bill.

This prompt is incredibly effective because it doesn't just give you a generic list; it forces the AI to act as a **defensive planner**. By clearly defining your priorities and non-negotiables upfront, the AI tailors the budget to reflect what you actually care about, while aggressively highlighting the hidden traps where vendors typically extract more money. I highly recommend running this prompt _before_ you tour a single venue or speak to a single vendor. It gives you the baseline financial literacy needed to negotiate confidently.

## üôã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can I use this for a destination wedding?**
  - A: Absolutely! Just add "Destination Wedding in `[Location]`" to the Context section. The AI will immediately adjust the categories to include travel, accommodation, welcome bags, and local legal fees for the ceremony.

- **Q: What if my budget is extremely tight (e.g., under $5,000)?**
  - A: The AI handles micro-budgets brilliantly. It will skip traditional, expensive venues and instead suggest parks, restaurant buyouts, digital invitations, and DIY alternatives, effectively reshaping the entire checklist to fit your financial reality without judgment.

- **Q: Does this work for different cultural weddings that last multiple days?**
  - A: Yes. If your culture has specific multi-day events or traditional ceremonies (like a Mehndi, Tea Ceremony, or specific gifting customs), simply mention them in the Context. The AI will expand the budget categories to accommodate those specific traditions.
