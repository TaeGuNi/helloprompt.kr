> _"How do I say no to a friend asking for money without hurting their feelings? How do I break up with someone as painlessly as possible? Is your mind cluttered, leaving you unable to even write the first line?"_

There are defining moments in life when words are necessary—or when they must be delivered in writing. We often find ourselves staring at a KakaoTalk chat window or a blank email screen, watching the cursor blink for tens of minutes, or even hours, as we agonize over what to say.

**"Will they be offended if I use this word?"**
**"Does this sound too cold?"**
**"But if I'm too vague, they might ask again later..."**

The stress is particularly intense when a close friend suddenly asks for a large loan, when you need to end a relationship without causing more pain, or when you have to write a sincere apology to a boss or client after a critical mistake. We suffer because our desire to maintain a precious relationship clashes violently with our firm goal (rejection, breakup, or apology).

The most fatal issues we face in these delicate situations are **'emotional overflow'** and the **'loss of objectivity.'** Writing in a state of heightened emotion—be it anxiety, guilt, or anger—often leads to rambling sentences that stray far from our original intent. In an attempt to defend ourselves, we might spiral into pathetic excuses, or in an attempt to avoid misunderstanding, we might come across as a cold-blooded robot by being too clinical.

We often mistakenly think, "If I write it this way, they'll understand my sincerity." However, written text lacks the sender's facial expressions or tone of voice, meaning it carries the risk of being interpreted in a completely different way depending on the recipient's current mood. We have seen countless times how a poorly written sentence or a poorly chosen word can act as a butterfly effect, permanently severing a 10-year friendship or escalating into an irreversible emotional battle.

**A high-wire act of balancing cold logic and warm empathy**—you no longer have to spend sleepless nights writing and deleting dozens of times.

The fastest, wisest, and safest solution is to delegate the first draft to an **'AI Ghostwriter'** that can perfectly filter your emotions and utilize thoroughly calculated phrasing. This prompt is not just a writing assistant. It is the perfect **'Emotional Buffer'** that cools down your complex, tangled feelings and prevents the catastrophe of a broken relationship.

An AI equipped with a sophisticated prompt takes your core message (rejection, apology, confession, etc.) as the skeleton and builds the flesh of the sentence by choosing the most refined and polished vocabulary to minimize hurting the other person's feelings. It calculates even the subtle nuances that humans might miss, finding the optimal 'temperature'—not too cold, yet not too clingy.

The moment you introduce this prompt into your professional and daily life, your life will become surprisingly peaceful. Instead of a headache, you can receive a logical, warm, and perfectly balanced draft in just one minute.

All you need to do is take that solid draft and add **one or two spoonfuls of minor details or unique memories** known only to you and the recipient. Experience the miraculous change of shaking off the heavy burden that has been weighing on your chest for days and pressing the 'send' button with a light and comfortable heart. Clear communication without misunderstanding will protect your precious relationships even more firmly.

## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

This is the prompt perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variables]` to fit your situation for immediate use.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this lightly when you just need a quick draft with a general nuance.

> **Role:** You are a psychological counselor who delicately touches people's hearts and a bestselling epistolary author.
> 
> **Task:** Write a message to `[Acquaintance]` politely `[refusing a request for a loan]`. Make it not too cold, but convey my intention firmly.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This prompt is highly recommended when you need a high level of detail and a strategic approach to achieve your goal perfectly without offending the other person.

> **Role:** You are a psychological counselor and professional ghostwriter who perfectly understands the subtle nuances of human relationships. You must write while calculating the emotions the recipient will feel from every single word.
>
> **Context:** I am about to send an important letter/message to `[Target]`. It is a very delicate situation where my sincerity must be delivered well without being distorted or misunderstood.
>
> **Letter Information:**
> - **Recipient:** `[e.g., A friend of 10 years, a partner of 3 years, a distant coworker, a client contact person]`
> - **Core Purpose:** `[e.g., A polite refusal to lend money, a firm warning about repeated mistakes, a request for an honest conversation to overcome a relationship slump]`
> - **My Emotions & Attitude:** `[e.g., Sorry but forced to refuse because my situation is also hard, angry but keeping it rational, thankful and affectionate but saying goodbye]`
> - **Must-include Content/Episode:** `[e.g., The excuse that my mortgage interest recently went up, the argument we had during our last trip to Jeju Island]`
>
> **Task:**
> 1. Reflecting all the above conditions, write a **natural and sincere letter draft**.
> 2. Construct the sentences so that my intention (rejection, apology, breakup, etc.) is 100% clear without ambiguity, while minimizing any hurt to the recipient's feelings.
> 3. Provide two versions so I can choose based on the situation: **Version 1 (Soft and Indirect Tone)** and **Version 2 (Clear and Direct Tone)**.
>
> **Constraints:**
> - Never use robotic, "translated-style" phrasing that reveals it was written by an AI (e.g., "I hope for your understanding," "I am informing you through this text").
> - Use natural, spoken-style language that perfectly fits the sentiment of daily Korean messenger apps (KakaoTalk) or emails.

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight & How to Use)

The most powerful weapon and core philosophy of this prompt is **'Outsourcing Emotion.'** Because humans are emotional creatures, it is impossible to write objectively and rationally while overwhelmed by extreme stress, guilt, or anger. Writing while emotions are high might feel cathartic in the moment, but it often becomes a 'dark history' that makes you cringe the next morning or a weapon that leaves indelible scars on the other person.

Entrusting the first draft to an AI is a process of filtering your heightened emotions through the safest and most logical filter. The AI restores the reason and logic we lost to emotion and acts as the best **shield** to smooth over the situation with organized sentences.

AI's ability shines overwhelmingly in high-difficulty writing that requires both **'Justification'** and **'Empathy'** simultaneously, such as rejections, apologies, or breakups. For example, instead of one-dimensional and dry expressions like "I have no money" or "I'm sorry," it produces **buffer sentences** that allow the other person to nod in agreement without feeling slighted. It comforts the other person's difficult situation first and then logically, yet warmly, explains that there are realistic constraints that prevent you from helping despite your desire to do so.

**However, here is a crucial tip: Never copy and paste the AI-written text exactly as is.**

No matter how perfect the grammar or brilliant the vocabulary, an AI-written text will inevitably lack **'human warmth.'** Use the AI's organized sentence structure and skeleton, but be sure to manually type and add one or two **very small and personal details shared only by you and the recipient.**

For example, try putting "the time you were hospitalized for gastritis" in the `[Must-include Content/Episode]` variable. Or, try changing the first line of the AI's draft from "Jihun" to a nickname or a friendly title you usually use. The moment you add a purely personal memory like, "Remember when we struggled because of that flat tire on our Jeju trip?", the robotic nuance disappears completely. The recipient will 100% perceive it as a letter you wrote with deep sincerity after agonizing all night, and this small **'Human Touch'** will elevate the prompt's quality to the ultimate level.

**Professional Tip on Variable Control:**
The `[My Emotions & Attitude]` variable is the most important steering wheel for the overall atmosphere of the letter. Instead of just writing "Firmly," the quality of the AI's output will increase exponentially if you give detailed emotional instructions like **"Firmly and clearly stating that I absolutely cannot lend the money due to my realistic limits, while feeling sorry and regretful."** The more specifically and honestly you pour out your inner thoughts to the AI, the more accurately the AI will speak for your heart.

Lastly, I strongly and actively recommend using **Anthropic's Claude 3.5 Sonnet model** rather than ChatGPT for writing that requires such emotional and delicate nuances. Among existing AI models, Claude is by far the best at reading subtle Korean emotional lines, understanding hidden meanings between the lines, and using natural spoken-style language that isn't overly artificial. Clear your cluttered mind right now and paste this prompt into Claude's input window. You will experience the magic of your worries being perfectly resolved in just one minute.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: I'm hesitant to press the send button because I'm afraid it will look like it was written by AI.**
  - A: That is exactly why the `[Must-include Content/Episode]` variable is absolutely vital. Inserting a specific memory, past event, or proper noun shared only by the two of you will make the robotic feel characteristic of AI vanish like magic. Specifying 'daily messenger sentiment' in the final constraints is also a key defensive mechanism to prevent stiff, translated-style phrasing and draw out a natural spoken tone.

- **Q: Can this be used for very formal business emails to a boss or a client?**
  - A: Absolutely. it works perfectly. Change the `[Target]` to 'Work Boss' or 'Important Client Contact,' and modify `[My Emotions & Attitude]` to 'Extremely polite and formal, while clearly defending our reasons for the issue.' It produces surprisingly clean drafts for difficult business apologies or resignation emails.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Maximizing a Powerful Persona:** I assigned the AI the role of a **'Psychological Counselor'** rather than just a ghostwriter. This causes the AI to finely adjust its reasoning direction to minimize the emotional impact on the recipient with every word choice.
2. **Clear Structuring of Variables (RTFC Compliance):** I designed the input to separate the letter's target, purpose, attitude, and episode into independent blocks. This design optimizes the AI's ability to build a solid skeleton (purpose) and then layer on the flesh (episode, attitude) in a more three-dimensional and organized way.
3. **Providing Multiple Options (Task 3):** In human relationships, there are times when a soft, indirect rejection is needed, and times when a firm line needs to be drawn to eliminate room for misunderstanding. By requesting two opposite tones at once, the user is given the freedom to choose the temperature that best fits the current situation after seeing the output.
