## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

This prompt was perfected through dozens of trials and errors. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variables]` in the parentheses to suit your situation and put it into practice immediately.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly and easily convert the overall atmosphere of your writing into something emotional.

> **Role:** You are a `[Best-selling Essay Writer]` who uses sentences that stimulate emotion and are highly gripping.
>
> **Task:** Please convert the stiff and boring informational text provided below into an attractive storytelling format that deeply resonates with the reader's emotions.
>
> **Input Data:**
> - Original Text: `[Draft that needs conversion]`

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you need to sharply identify the reader's painful "Pain Points" and lead to overwhelming empathy and explosive conversion.

> **Role:** You are a `[Pro Copywriter and Essayist with 10 years of experience]` who knows how to move people's hearts.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - Background: The current draft is so focused on delivering information that the writing is stiff and the reader bounce rate is very high.
> - Goal: To make the reader feel 'This is my story' while reading, and to keep them perfectly immersed until the final sentence.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Based on the `[Original Text]` below, reconstruct it into storytelling where 'vivid experiences the protagonist personally went through' or 'specific daily descriptions' stand out.
> 2. Actively use expressions that stimulate the five senses—sight, sound, touch, etc.—to maximize the vitality of the writing.
> 3. Clearly place a sharp empathy point at the beginning of the text that will make the reader slap their knee and think, 'Right, I've been in exactly that situation.'
> 4. Make the style calm yet deeply resonant, and refine it with short, concise sentences for a sense of speed.
>
> **Input Data:**
>
> - Target Reader: `[2030 Office Workers / Dieters / Aspiring Entrepreneurs, etc.]`
> - Original Text: `[Paste the stiff draft content that needs conversion here]`
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Strictly forbid an overly artificial or melodramatic style that forces emotion.
> - Do not damage the core information (Facts) of the original text; melt it naturally into the storyline without any sense of incongruity.

## 💡 Writer's Comments (Insight & How to use)

This prompt exerts more power than any other tool when planning the very top intro of a landing page or the first three paragraphs of a blog post. The human brain is structurally designed to **respond to emotional narratives before rigorous logic or facts**. Before being rationally persuaded, a reader must first open their heart through emotional connection to move their gaze to the next sentence. In other words, no matter how good the product or information is, if you start with a cliché 'explainer' tone, the reader will hit the back button in one second.

There is one crucial realization I've gained from testing this prompt in numerous real-world projects: **The final quality of this prompt depends 100% on how sharply and obsessively you refine the single variable of `[Target Reader]`**. This is where many beginners make a mistake. Try entering something broad like "Dieters" or "Office workers in their 30s." The AI will just churn out clichés and textbook-level comforting phrases found all over the internet.

However, try splitting the reader's persona into extremely 'micro' segments. For example, in the target reader field, if you input a specific situational description like **"A 3rd-year marketer dieter who suffers from guilt every night at 11 PM after opening and closing delivery apps dozens of times while lying in bed,"** the magic that happens is phenomenal. The AI immediately enters the reader's room and begins vivid sensory descriptions that identify their secret daily lives and painful points with terrifying sharpness. This is the point where a powerful internal exclamation of "Wait, this is exactly my story!" erupts.

Additionally, you must learn **Constraint Control** so that emotional storytelling doesn't accidentally damage the essential purpose of the writing. Sometimes, the AI gets so deeply immersed in storytelling that it omits the business information or core facts it was supposed to deliver and writes a piece of 'emotional diary' instead. In this case, don't panic; you need to apply a strong brake in the final request of the prompt. Try adding an additional instruction like: *"Immediately after perfectly drawing the reader's empathy through storytelling, be sure to connect the three core strengths of our service naturally and logically using a 'Bridge' sentence."* You can find the perfect balance that moves the reader's heart without losing sight of the goal.

Finally, I strongly recommend a **Post-editing** process of stripping away 'adjectives' and 'adverbs' from the generated results. No matter how many constraints you give in the prompt, exaggerated modifiers typical of AI (e.g., incredible, miraculous, heart-wrenching, etc.) can occasionally seep through. If the writer simply takes a pen and calmly removes these excessive emotional lines, a top-tier professional copy is completed—one where human warmth is fully felt, and no one would even imagine it was written by an AI. This small difference in detail becomes the decisive criterion that separates 'common writing roughly written by AI' from 'overwhelming writing that makes people open their wallets.'

Furthermore, this storytelling conversion technique brings about a phenomenal increase in open rates and click-through rates (CTR) in email marketing or newsletter writing. A customer's inbox is filled with dozens of dry announcements and sales letters every day. In that mix, an email that starts by describing a customer's specific daily inconvenience like a short skit, instead of a cliché subject line like "New Product Released," is bound to stand out. Take out those old posts gathering dust on your hard drive right now—the ones that were too stiff to publish—and throw them into this prompt. The dead texts will be resurrected as living stories and become your most powerful business weapon.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Won't the writing generated by AI have that characteristic artificial and mechanical feel (AI Slop)?**
  - A: To prevent exactly that, I clearly included the "No artificial emotional forcing" condition in the Constraints. Especially if you use the 'Claude 3.5 Sonnet' model, you can get much more elegant and natural sentences with human warmth compared to other models.

- **Q: Can this be applied to official corporate blogs or professional technical blogs?**
  - A: Absolutely! The stiffer the content of a technical blog, the more desperately it needs the power of soft storytelling. In this case, try changing the role of the prompt from `[Best-selling Essay Writer]` to `[Kind and Witty Senior Developer]` and set the target reader as a 'Junior Developer.' You can feel the completion rate soar vertically when you explain difficult technical facts through vivid troubleshooting experiences.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Clear Separation of Context and Goal:** Instead of vaguely asking the AI to "write well," I made it sharply aware of the fatal problems of the current draft (stiff tone, high bounce rate) and the specific goals to be achieved (reader immersion and empathy).
2. **Forced Sensory Details:** I instructed the AI to mandatorily use the "Show, Don't Tell" technique, a core principle of great storytelling. Through this, abstract and vague text is converted into a 3D image that is clearly drawn before the eyes.
3. **Sophisticated Tone Control:** By blocking the misuse of exaggerated modifiers and artificial emotional expressions commonly committed by AI through constraints, it draws out a much more refined and calm high-quality result.
