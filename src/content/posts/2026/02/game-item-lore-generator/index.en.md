### ü•á Pro Version (Mastercraft)

Use this when you need a production-ready item concept for your campaign or game design document.

> **Role (Role):** You are a master blacksmith and an eccentric appraiser who has studied ancient, cursed relics for decades.
>
> **Context (Context):**
>
> - Background: The player has just opened a heavily guarded, ancient treasure chest deep within a forgotten dungeon.
> - Objective: An extraordinary, one-of-a-kind artifact must be revealed, not a generic piece of loot.
>
> **Request (Task):**
>
> 1. Forge the **Detailed Information** of this artifact based on the provided variables.
>    - **Name:** (Give it a unique, imposing name)
>    - **Appearance:** (Describe it in mysterious, highly detailed terms)
>    - **Special Ability:** (Design a unique, situational mechanic‚Äînot just a stat boost. e.g., "Grants invisibility upon scoring a critical hit, but...")
>    - **Penalty/Curse:** (The steep price for wielding such power. e.g., "Max HP permanently decreases by 1 every time the ability triggers.")
> 2. Write a **Short Lore** (one paragraph) detailing the tragic or epic history of this item.
> 3. The `[variables]` below should be considered carefully when crafting the item.
>
> **Variables:**
>
> - Type: `[e.g., Dagger, Amulet, Tower Shield]`
> - Rarity: `[e.g., Mythic, Cursed, Forgotten]`
> - Core Concept: `[e.g., Blood-thirsting, Time-distorting, Shadow-woven]`
>
> **Constraints (Constraints):**
>
> - Format the output cleanly using Markdown.
> - Prioritize a "High Risk, High Return" game balance. Avoid creating utterly overpowered "Munchkin" items without severe, character-altering consequences.

## üí° Writer's Insight

When designing items for a TRPG or a video game, the most common mistake is focusing purely on numbers (e.g., +5 Strength). Players quickly forget numerical upgrades, but they will never forget the _Blade of the Leech_ that forced them to sacrifice their own blood to defeat a boss. The beauty of this prompt is how it forces the AI to balance mechanics with narrative. The "Penalty/Curse" instruction is the secret sauce here‚Äîit transforms a standard piece of equipment into a narrative device that drives roleplay and strategic decision-making. I highly recommend running this prompt a few times and mixing and matching the generated curses with different weapon types to see what sinister combinations you can create!

## üôã FAQ

- **Q: Can I use this prompt for Sci-Fi or Cyberpunk settings?**
  - A: Absolutely! Just change the "Role" to a "Cyber-ripperdoc" or "Black Market Tech Broker", and swap the variables (e.g., Type: `[Neural Implant]`, Concept: `[Rogue AI]`).

- **Q: What if the generated curse is too punishing for my players?**
  - A: You can simply prompt the AI again with an added constraint: "Make the curse annoying and mechanically interesting, but not lethal," or manually tone it down to fit your game's intended balance.

## üß¨ Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Role Immersion:** By assigning the persona of an "appraiser of cursed relics," the AI adopts a dramatic, lore-heavy tone rather than a clinical, programmatic one.
2. **Mandatory Trade-offs:** Explicitly requiring a "Penalty/Curse" prevents the AI from generating bland, overpowered items, ensuring a "High Risk, High Return" design philosophy that is crucial for engaging gameplay.
3. **Variable Driven:** The prompt clearly separates the core concept and item type into variables, making it highly reusable for generating entire loot tables.
