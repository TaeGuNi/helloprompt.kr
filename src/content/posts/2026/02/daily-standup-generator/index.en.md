**The pressure as 10 AM approaches is not a pain you feel alone.**
Every morning at 10 AM, you log into Slack or Zoom to share what you did yesterday and what you'll do today in the Daily Standup. Do you look forward to this short meeting, or do you dread it? Many junior developers, planners, designers, and marketers feel intense psychological pressure as their turn approaches. "What did I do yesterday?" Your mind goes blank, and you frantically scramble through Jira tickets, note apps, or Slack history. **The disaster of a bad standup: The trap of declining trust and wasted time**
The problem is that these disorganized reports don't just end with 'personal embarrassment.' To put it bluntly, your teammates don't want to hear your TMI (Too Much Information), long-winded stories, or detailed tales of failure. What about from the perspective of a leader or Scrum Master? An honest but unrefined report like "I spent all day struggling yesterday" is a fatal mistake that erodes trust in you. What a leader truly wants to know in a standup is **not 'how much you suffered yesterday,' but 'what problem did you solve yesterday, and how can the team help you today to move the project toward its goal?'**
This inefficient and emotional communication repeated every morning ultimately degrades the productivity of the entire team and undermines the essence of Agile—identifying and responding to problems quickly. Every time a meeting ends, you find yourself falling into a pit of deep regret and self-loathing, thinking, "I shouldn't have made that excuse earlier" or "I should have spoken more like a pro." ## 📊 Proof: Satisfaction in the Results (Before & After)

### ❌ Before (The pain we used to suffer)

Yesterday, there was a payment error, so I struggled all day. The external API kept disconnecting. So, for now, I put in some retry code because it was urgent. Today, I have to upload this to the server and see if it works well. There's a meeting with the planning team in the afternoon. Oh, and the test server is so slow right now it's hard to work, so the infrastructure team needs to check it out.

### ✅ After (The perfect transformation)

```text
**[Yesterday's Tasks]**
Yesterday, I focused on debugging intermittent errors in the payment module. I identified that external API timeouts were the cause and implemented retry logic to temporarily resolve the issue for system stability.

**[Today's Tasks]**
Today, I plan to deploy and monitor the payment logic I fixed yesterday. In the afternoon, I have a requirements review meeting with the planning team.

**[Blockers / Help Needed]**
The current response delay on the test server is causing a bottleneck in the QA schedule. It would be a great help if the infrastructure team could check this issue to restore the test environment.
```

1. **Automated Achievement Packaging:** Reconstructs sentences to focus on solved results (achievements) rather than the struggling process (the grind).
2. **Perfect Format Standardization:** Enforces the standard structure of Yesterday / Today / Blockers that teammates find easiest to digest.
3. **Clear Help Requests:** Instead of emotional appeals, it objectively specifies exactly who needs to provide what help to solve the problem.

## 🚀 This Is How Real Pros Write

### 🥉 Basic Version

Useful when you need to quickly toss a status update in text on messengers like Slack or Teams.

> **Role:** You are a skilled Scrum Master.
>
> **Task:** Based on my notes, write a clean Daily Standup message to share on Slack.
>
> **My Notes:**
>
> - Yesterday: Fixed login API, changed DB schema, team dinner
> - Today: Integration for signup page, design team meeting
> - Issue: AWS server connection is unstable

### 🥇 Pro Version

Highly recommended when you need to present verbally in a video meeting or want to leave a more professional and proactive impression in meetings attended by leaders.

> **Role:** You are a senior developer with excellent communication skills in a Silicon Valley Agile team.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - I spent all day yesterday struggling with an error of unknown cause, and I'm worried it might look like I didn't make much progress.
> - I want to look like an 'expert' who proactively solved the problem, rather than sounding like I'm making excuses or complaining.
>
> **Input Data (My Notes):**
>
> - Yesterday: Spent 4 hours catching a bug in the payment module. The cause was an external API timeout. Eventually implemented retry logic as a temporary fix.
> - Today: Plan to deploy and monitor yesterday's fix. Meeting with the planning team for requirements review in the afternoon.
> - Blockers: The test server is so slow right now that QA is constantly being delayed. Need the infrastructure team to check.
>
> **Task:**
>
> 1. Based on the data above, write a **Script for verbal presentation** that can be read within 1 minute.
> 2. Strictly exclude emotional expressions like "I struggled with doing ~" and reconstruct the sentences to be **Achievement and Problem-Solving centered**, such as "Identified the external API timeout issue and implemented retry logic to build defensive code."
> 3. Write the Blockers with a nuance that politely but firmly requests support from the infrastructure team.
>
> **Constraints:**
>
> - Use Markdown for the output format and strictly follow the structure below.
>   - **[Yesterday's Tasks]**: (Focus on core achievements, within 2 sentences)
>   - **[Today's Tasks]**: (Specific plans, within 2 sentences)
>   - **[Blockers / Help Needed]**: (Call-to-action style request)
> - Write in a natural, spoken style (I did..., I plan to...).

## 💡 Author's Comment (Insight)

**The essence of a standup is cool-headed Synchronization (Sync), not an emotional vent.**
A standup meeting is absolutely not an emotional reporting session to prove to your colleagues "I worked so late and suffered like this" or to get comforted. In the Agile Scrum methodology, the real purpose of the daily meeting is to **quickly and accurately sync with the entire team on 'what I broke through to achieve the team's goals and where bottlenecks are currently occurring.'**
Many junior and mid-level practitioners make the mistake of rambling about the difficulties and processes they went through (the so-called 'grind time'). But to be blunt, your colleagues are overwhelmingly focused on 'how resolving that issue positively impacts today's sprint schedule' rather than which Stack Overflow post you looked at to fix a code error. This is where the true value of this prompt lies. By using AI as a sort of cold-headed editor and emotional filter, it **removes your fatigue, complaints, and disorganized excuses, refining only the facts, achievements, and actionable next steps.**

**Injecting perfect context through variable control (Context & Variables)**
The reason this prompt achieves top-tier performance unlike typical prompts on the market is that it is meticulously designed with powerful variable control mechanisms: `[Context]` and `[My Notes]`. If you had simply given ChatGPT a one-dimensional instruction like "Clean up my notes for a standup meeting," the AI would likely have spit out a soulless, robot-like summary.
However, we explicitly injected the speaker's hidden needs and anxieties into this prompt: "I'm psychologically worried it might look like I didn't make much progress" and "I want to look like an expert who led the problem-solving." An AI given such human psychological context and a clear target image begins to perform sophisticated **Strategic Framing** beyond simple sentence correction.
For example, it perfectly substitutes a very negative input like 'wasted 4 hours failing to find a solution' with a positive and proactive achievement like 'tested defensive logic to ensure system stability by analyzing causes from multiple angles.'
When you use this in practice, try actively modifying this `[Context]` variable to suit your situation. Try adding detailed instructions like "Today is a very delicate situation where I have to officially inform the team of a launch delay for the main project I'm working on and ask for their understanding," or "The whole team is cruising without critical issues this week, so write it in a light and relaxed tone to brighten the morning atmosphere." You'll be able to get a perfectly customized script with surprisingly fine-tuned nuances.

**Communication skills for requesting Blockers elegantly and firmly**
Another core part of this prompt that must be noted is the detailed design of `[Blockers / Help Needed]`. In actual work settings, the hardest moment is when you have to ask for a favor or say, "I can't proceed because of this, so please help." This is because if you choose your words poorly, it can sound like you're shifting responsibility to others or whining like a child.
But a true professional's communication style must be different. This prompt forces the blockers to be written as a "Call To Action (CTA) request" in the Constraints stage. As a result, a raw, emotional complaint like "I'm annoyed and it's hard to work because the test server is so slow" is dramatically transformed into a polite yet clear request for cooperation: "It would be a great help if the infrastructure team could check the issue to restore the test environment, which would significantly reduce the overall QA schedule."
This is not just about changing words. By cleverly shifting the arrow of blame from a person ('You guys aren't managing the server') to the problem itself ('The bottleneck of response delay'), you can experience high-level psychological magic that smoothly leads inter-departmental collaboration toward the common goal of 'problem-solving' without unnecessary emotional drain.

Seriously, try applying such a well-refined standup script to your team meetings consistently for just three days. Your fellow developers and project leaders will strongly perceive you as a 'true professional who doesn't get swayed by small emotions and focuses all energy on solving problems.' I highly recommend you enjoy the most certain and explosive personal branding boost you can get with just a 1-minute investment.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can non-developer roles (marketers, designers) use this prompt too?**
  - A: Of course. Just change the 'Role' part of the Pro prompt to "Competent Senior Performance Marketer" or "Senior Product Designer" and input your own work notes. You'll get a perfectly customized script that fits your role's characteristics and terminology.

- **Q: What if the script comes out too different from my usual way of speaking, like a machine?**
  - A: Try explicitly adding a condition at the bottom of the Constraints: `My usual tone: Write it in a friendly and soft style like "I did this, and I'm thinking of doing that."` You'll get a much more natural result that doesn't feel out of place.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Specification of Context:** It makes the AI aware of the speaker's psychological anxiety, such as "worrying about appearing to lack progress." Through this, the AI avoids writing sentences defensively or passively and reconstructs them into an active and confident tone.
2. **Exclusion of Emotion and Achievement Substitution:** Instead of simply listing hours of struggle (4 hours of grinding), it explicitly instructs to substitute this with solving ability (building retry logic), vertically raising the quality of the report.
3. **Call To Action (CTA) Blockers:** Beyond a simple complaint like "The server is slow," it makes you present a clear direction for action, such as "Need the infrastructure team to check," maximizing the efficiency of the meeting.
