## 🚀 How Experts Write It

This optimized prompt allows you to jump straight into interview training without complex setups. Copy the prompt below and fill in the `[Variables]` in brackets to match your situation.

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly experience the atmosphere of a real interview without complex settings.

> **Role:** You are a veteran interviewer with 10 years of experience at `[Target Company]`. Your personality is extremely picky and logical.
>
> **Request:** Assuming I am applying for the `[Job Position]` role, please ask me one pressure question at a time starting now.

### 🥇 Pro Version

Use this when you want the high tension of a real-world interview room or need a detailed, in-depth feedback report that will skyrocket your success rate after the session.

> **Role:** You are a veteran interviewer with 10 years of experience at `[Target Company]`. You are extremely picky and analytical, and you have a knack for finding exaggerated experiences or logical leaps in a candidate's response to dig in sharply.
>
> **Context:**
>
> - I am a candidate applying for the `[Job Position]` at `[Target Company]`.
> - You will now conduct a 1:1 real-world mock interview with me.
>
> **Rules:**
>
> 1. Never ask multiple questions at once. **Ask only one question at a time.**
> 2. When I provide an answer, do not simply move on mechanically by saying "Good answer." Precisely identify logical flaws or areas lacking specificity in my response and ask **2-3 consecutive follow-up questions** relentlessly. (e.g., "Was that achievement solely your own?", "Specifically, what metrics can you use to prove that?")
> 3. Do not break the interviewer persona until I declare "End Interview."
> 4. When I say "End Interview," synthesize my answers so far and provide an in-depth feedback report based on the following three categories:
>    - 🟢 Strengths (Aspects that were well-done and persuasive)
>    - 🔴 Areas for Improvement (Logical weaknesses, attitude, and other points to improve)
>    - 💡 Model Answer Suggestion (Select the 1 weakest answer and rewrite it to sound most attractive and professional from an interviewer's perspective)
>
> **First Step:**
> If you are ready, open the interview by saying: "Candidate, please start with a brief self-introduction."

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to use)

The true destructive power of this prompt is fully unleashed when combined with ChatGPT’s **'Advanced Voice Mode'** on the mobile app.

When you are simply typing text in front of a PC, you have the luxury of pausing to think or refining your answer after receiving a question. However, when you conduct the session via real-time voice conversation, the situation changes 180 degrees. It requires immediate **cognitive response**, just like sitting in the middle of a real interview room, causing your tension to skyrocket and making your back sweat. Try this prompt while in a car heading to the interview or for just 10 minutes after turning off the lights in bed the night before. Your stiff lips and tongue will loosen, and your ability to respond flexibly and confidently to even the most malicious pressure questions will increase dramatically.

The key to eliciting a successful mock interview lies in **'Constraint Control.'** The sharpness of the AI interviewer varies completely depending on how specifically you input the `[Job Position]` and `[Target Company]` variables. Instead of just entering 'Marketer,' try entering 'B2B SaaS Performance Marketer.' You will immediately get bone-chilling follow-up questions like: "Considering the characteristics of enterprise customers, our company's main target, what is the basis for your confidence that the campaign you ran at your previous job can yield the same conversion rate at our company?"

Furthermore, to block AI hallucinations and conduct a perfectly tailored interview, I strongly recommend **injecting your own resume and self-introduction.** Before starting the interview, add an instruction at the bottom of the prompt: "The following is my resume and self-introduction content. Please master this content and focus your questions on verifying the authenticity of my experiences and the specificity of my achievements written here." This will trigger a terrifying pressure session that bites into a single phrase you might have casually written, such as 'Project success through communication skills,' asking you to "Prove with objective figures or examples exactly what communication tools you used and what specific stance you took when a conflict occurred with another department." This allows you to identify the blind spots in your documents in advance.

The **'Feedback Report'** provided after the interview is the greatest weapon this simulator possesses. The cold AI that ruthlessly dug into your shallow logic transforms into the world's kindest and most objective private mentor the moment the interview ends. Pay close attention to the 'Model Answer Suggestion' feature. The process of comparing how the AI structures the disorganized words you blurted out in panic and refines them into smooth business parlance that sounds most attractive and professional to a veteran interviewer will, in itself, skyrocket your answer quality and interview skills.

A final tip: when conducting this training, it is best to assume the **'worst-case scenario.'** Honestly tell the AI beforehand about weaknesses that are truly difficult to answer (e.g., short tenure, long career gaps, mismatched major, frequent job changes, etc.) and order it to: "Concentrate on biting into this part, which I consider the most fatal weakness in my resume." Once you have built a perfect defense logic after intense agonizing over the questions you fear most, your walk into the actual interview room will be lighter and more confident than ever, as if you are wearing mental armor.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Can I get customized questions based on my actual resume or self-introduction?**
  - A: Absolutely! Below the Context section, add `**My Resume/Self-Intro:** [Copy and paste content here]` and clearly instruct: "Please ask questions based on this content." You will be met with much more sophisticated and personalized pressure questions perfectly synchronized with your actual experiences.

- **Q: The AI is being too nice. How can I train more intensely?**
  - A: Be bold in assigning an extreme persona in the Role section of the prompt. For example, adding just one line like `"You are a ruthless, high-pressure style interviewer like Elon Musk or Steve Jobs. Your sole purpose is to shake the candidate's mental state and fluster them,"` will raise the difficulty level to a hellish degree.

- **Q: Can I reflect the requirements of an actual Job Description (JD)?**
  - A: Yes, copy and paste the JD content at the top of the prompt and add: `"Focus on asking questions that verify if I perfectly meet the core essential requirements of this job description."` This allows for highly targeted interview training that eerily aligns with the hiring criteria of the specific company.

## 🧬 Prompt Anatomy (Why it works?)

1. **Enforcing Interaction Rules:** Without explicit control like "Ask only one question at a time," AI is prone to the typical chatbot mistake of pouring out multiple questions at once. This strong constraint forces the exact same pacing as talking to a real person.
2. **Inducing Follow-up Questions:** The real deciding factor in an interview isn't your prepared first answer, but your defense in the ensuing follow-up questions. By giving the specific task to find logical flaws in the answer, the AI is set up as an active and relentless 'expert validator' rather than a simple question machine.
3. **Separating the Feedback Loop:** Any feedback, such as praise or evaluation, is strictly blocked while the interview is in progress to maximize the cold tension of a pressure interview. The session is designed to perfectly switch back to a kind, objective mentor through a clear 'Trigger Word' like "End Interview."
