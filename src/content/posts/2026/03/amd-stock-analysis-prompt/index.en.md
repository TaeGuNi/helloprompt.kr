> _"Nvidia is too expensive to buy now. If AMD rises to become the number two in the AI GPU market, couldn't it offer even greater returns?"_

AMD is a company that has demonstrated one of the most **dramatic turnarounds** in the semiconductor industry. Under the leadership of CEO Lisa Su, they broke Intel's monopoly in the CPU market, driving their data center CPU market share up to 25%. Now, they are challenging Nvidia's H100/B100 in the AI GPU market with the <b>MI350 series</b>.

The core question for investors is this: "Can AMD capture a significant share of the AI GPU market?" With NVDA building a solid moat through the CUDA ecosystem, the key variables are how much AMD's <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">ROCm software stack</span> has caught up and whether AMD's traditional weapon of **price-to-performance** will be effective in the AI market.

This prompt analyzes AMD from the **"NVDA Rival" perspective**. Rather than an independent corporate analysis, it is designed as a **relative comparison** frame with NVDA. By simultaneously tracking market share gaps, technical gaps, and valuation gaps, it verifies the validity of the investment thesis: "Closing the gap for AMD → Rising stock price."

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to Use)

The most important point to note in this prompt is the **"Valuation Inversion Phenomenon."** Many investors think, "AMD is cheaper than NVDA, so let's buy AMD," but in reality, **AMD's Forward P/E (38x) is higher than NVDA's (35x)**. This means the market has already reflected a "gap-closing premium" into AMD.

In other words, <span style="color:var(--color-cyber-cyan)">for AMD to rise further, it needs "proof of performance" that its market share has actually increased</span>. The expectation that "market share will increase" is already baked into the current price. By calculating the "fair price at 20% market share" in this prompt's scenario analysis, you can reverse-calculate how much market share expansion is already priced in.

## 🙋 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

- **Q: Can AMD beat NVDA in GPUs like it beat Intel in CPUs?**
  - A: In the CPU market, Intel's software lock-in was weak, but in the GPU market, CUDA acts as a very strong lock-in. Since switching costs are much higher than in CPUs, it's difficult for the exact same pattern to repeat. However, **multi-sourcing demand** (customers wanting to reduce dependence on NVDA) is AMD's opportunity.

- **Q: Are Intel's AI accelerators not a threat?**
  - A: Currently, Intel's Gaudi series has a market share of less than 2% and is not a significant threat. However, if you add Intel to the `[Comparison Target]` in the prompt, a three-way comparative analysis is possible.
