> _"Your practical experience is perfect, but have you ever left an 'amateur' impression due to a single awkward sentence in your resume?"_

The core element that determines **'professionalism'** in a resume or cover letter is nothing other than the 'tone.' No matter how brilliant your practical achievements are, if you overuse childish colloquialisms like "I did this," "really a lot," or "hard-working," or leave ungrammatical sentences with poor subject-predicate agreement, your pass rate in the document screening stage is bound to plummet.

The biggest hurdle many job seekers and junior professionals face when writing a resume is **business writing**. Despite having successfully led excellent projects by working through the night, they often find themselves feeling small when trying to put those experiences into words. Your head is full of intense memories from the field and proud performance data, but in front of the blinking cursor of a word processor, you might only think of diary-like sentences such as "I took on the project and worked really hard on it."

Such **clumsy colloquialisms and excessive emotional adverbs** leave a fatal impression on recruiters. HR managers and hiring teams review dozens or hundreds of resumes every day. Their sharp eyes scan the 'tone and manner' of your writing in just three seconds, unconsciously judging whether you are an **'amateur'** or a **'professional'**. Emotionally pleading sentences like "I did my best" or "I put in a tremendous amount of effort" actually devalue your objective practical skills. Errors in spacing or ungrammatical sentences serve as clear evidence of a **lack of attention to detail** in your work attitude.

Even more unfortunate is that this 'amateur' tone can instantly devalue years of precious practical experience and hard-earned results. Even with great data like a "30% increase in conversion rate," the moment the way you deliver it turns into an emotional appeal like "I really tried incredibly hard and increased performance a lot," doubts about your logical thinking and problem-solving abilities begin to arise in the recruiter's mind. What you truly need to show is not emotion but **facts**, and the perfect **business tone and manner** that packages those facts in the most solid and concise way.

But now, you no longer need to feel frustrated in front of a blank canvas or pay for expensive editing services. **A single prompt**, infused with the sharp perspective of a senior headhunter from a top-tier IT company with 10 years of experience, will perfectly solve your concerns. Just throw your raw draft into this prompt, and in just one minute, the AI will strip away unnecessary emotions and completely remodel your sentences into the most sophisticated and formal business language.

Experience the magic for yourself. We will now reveal in detail the process of how your ordinary, flat writing transforms into a flawless business document that provides **overwhelming credibility** to recruiters. With just one copy and paste of this prompt, you will be reborn as a perfect professional with a top 1% pass rate.

## 🚀 How Real Experts Write

### 🥉 Basic Version

Use this when you want to quickly catch basic typos and smooth out the context.

> **Role:** You are a headhunter with 10 years of experience and an expert in business document proofreading.
> **Request:** Please check the spelling of the resume draft below and correct the colloquialisms into a credible business written style.

### 🥇 Pro Version

This is an advanced prompt used when you want to perfectly control the subtle nuances of every single word and appeal with overwhelming professionalism to the recruiter.

> **Role:**
> You are a senior headhunter for a major IT company and a chief editor specializing in business documents with 10 years of experience.
> 
> **Context:**
> - Background: I am currently writing a resume and cover letter for a successful career move.
> - Goal: I want to perfectly refine my writing into a professional business tone that highlights my practical experience and quantitative achievements while instilling 100% confidence in the recruiter.
> 
> **Task:**
> Carefully read the `[Resume Draft]` below and perfectly correct it according to the following three strict criteria.
> 
> 1. **Refine Style:** Completely revise casual colloquialisms (~, ~) into clear, polite, and formal written style (~, ~).
> 2. **Objectify:** Delete all emotional and subjective adverbs such as 'really,' 'so,' and 'extremely,' and maintain a strictly concise and objective tone. Also, convert passive voice expressions into active voice whenever possible.
> 3. **Grammar Correction:** Flawlessly correct spacing and spelling errors, and fix awkward ungrammatical sentences so that the subject and predicate correspond accurately.
> 
> **Input Text:**
> `[Paste your resume/cover letter draft here]`
> 
> **Output Format:**
> - Show the **[Original Sentence]** and **[Corrected Sentence]** side-by-side for an intuitive comparison.
> - For each correction, provide a short and clear **[Reason for Correction]**. (e.g., fixed subject-predicate agreement, removed unnecessary adverbs, etc.)

## 💡 Author's Comments (Insight & How to use)

The first and most essential tip for utilizing this prompt at 200% capacity and overcoming the limitations of AI to get the best results is **"always input one paragraph at a time."** Many applicants, in their haste, tend to pour the entire lengthy cover letter or a full resume containing multiple project experiences into the AI prompt window all at once. However, in this case, the probability of fatal errors occurring during the AI model's context window processing becomes extremely high. If the AI tries to process a vast amount at once, it may unintentionally truncate important context or omit the **most crucial key performance indicators (numeric data, sales growth rates, cost savings, etc.)** that could determine your success. Therefore, even if it is cumbersome, you must adopt a method of instructing the AI to edit paragraph by paragraph and meticulously comparing the results for each paragraph.

The second tip is **"meta-cognition by reverse-tracking the reasons for correction."** This prompt is designed not just to throw the result at you, but to provide the **[Reason for Correction]** for each item. Sometimes, the AI's suggested corrections might feel excessively mechanical or cold compared to your original intention. This happens when the tone becomes too low while aiming for a perfect business written style. In such cases, do not blindly copy and paste (Ctrl+C, Ctrl+V) the result. Instead, read the reasons for correction provided by the AI carefully, reverse-track them, and **ensure you go through the process of fine-tuning the sentences once more with your own natural rhythm.** When you add a spoonful of your own human insight to the AI's logical structure, a perfect cover letter that no one can imitate is completed.

The third and most important thing to watch out for is the **preservation of industry-specific jargon.** Large Language Models (LLMs) basically tend to choose the most universal and general words. As a result, key professional keywords or names of the latest technology stacks used only in your specific job (e.g., development, performance marketing, finance, etc.) may be lumped together as ordinary common nouns or replaced with completely different words. For example, the clear marketing term 'Conversion Rate (CVR) optimization through A/B testing' could be degraded into a vague expression like 'improving performance by comparing two plans.' Therefore, when reviewing the output results, you must **scrutinize with a hawk's eye** whether the edgy keywords that reveal your job expertise have been damaged by the AI. If necessary, control variables more tightly by adding a constraint at the bottom of the prompt like `- Do not change specific job terms (e.g., Docker, Kubernetes, CVR, etc.) under any circumstances.`

Finally, do not be afraid of **"tuning your own tone and manner."** This prompt provides the most universal and standard 'senior headhunter' view. However, if the organizational culture of the company you are applying to is very casual and values a youthful sense like a startup, the current heavy business written style might actually have an adverse effect. In that case, slightly twist the constraints in the Context area and try tuning the instructions to say, `Make it not too stiff, and refine it into a confident yet trendy tone.` A prompt is not a fixed mathematical formula. Your AI utilization skills will rise exponentially in the process of modifying and assembling this basic framework according to the philosophy and talent image of the company you are applying to, as well as your own unique character. Remember that AI is just an excellent assistant; it is ultimately you who puts your name on the final signature line of your precious resume.

## 🙋 FAQ

- **Q: Can I use this prompt as is for English resumes or cover letters?**
  - A: Yes, it is perfectly compatible. However, to take advantage of the characteristics of an English resume, please specify a concrete purpose such as `English Resume correction for applying to a foreign company` in the Context section. If you add just one constraint like `Start sentences with Action Verbs`, you can get overwhelming results optimized for Western business culture.

- **Q: AI keeps deleting my key performance metrics (e.g., 30% increase) or damaging them with other words during the editing process. How do I fix this?**
  - A: Try adding a strong command in the Constraints area of the prompt: `- Under no circumstances should the numerical data (%, period, amount, etc.) and key proper nouns of the original be modified or deleted.` This can fundamentally block the disaster of your hard-earned quantitative data being distorted due to the hallucination phenomenon unique to LLMs.
